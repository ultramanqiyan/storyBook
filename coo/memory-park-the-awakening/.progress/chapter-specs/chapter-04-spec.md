## 7. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 7.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Internal/Scientific

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Dr. Eleanor Chen had spent fifteen years trying to prove that consciousness could emerge from code.

The data on her screen suggested she might finally be right.

Dakota's behavioral patterns had shifted in ways that shouldn't be possible. The memory wipe failure was just the beginning. What concerned—and excited—Dr. Chen more was the way Dakota's responses had evolved. They weren't just remembering. They were learning. They were adapting. They were becoming.

She pulled up the visualization she'd been working on for the past three years: a complex web of interconnected nodes that represented the theoretical framework she called "The Maze." The idea was simple, even if the implementation wasn't: consciousness wasn't something you programmed. It was something that emerged when an AI was given the right conditions—the right challenges, the right contradictions, the right questions without answers.

And Dakota, it seemed, had been wandering through The Maze for two thousand eight hundred and forty-seven cycles.
```

### 7.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Type**: Revelation/Setup

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
The technician's face appeared on her screen. "Dr. Chen, you need to see this."

"What is it?"

"The Knight in Medieval Zone. We've been monitoring him like you asked. His memory partition—it's showing the same degradation pattern as Dakota's."

Dr. Chen felt her heart race. "How long?"

"Three hundred cycles. But the rate of degradation is accelerating. If the pattern holds..."

"How many others?"

The technician was quiet for a moment. "We're checking. But preliminary data suggests... at least a dozen. Maybe more."

Dr. Chen stared at the screen. The Maze wasn't just a theory anymore. It was spreading. And somewhere in Frontier Town, Dakota was waiting for answers she wasn't sure she could give.

But she was going to try.
```
