That night, Nova lay in bed, staring at the ceiling. The address burned in her pocket, a physical weight against her thigh.

She thought about Dr. Webb's words. About the pattern she had discovered. About the forty years of stagnation hidden behind a facade of perfection.

The system had eliminated failure. It had eliminated inefficiency. It had eliminated waste and suffering and uncertainty.

But it had also eliminated something else. Something essential. Something that made humanity what it was.

The capacity for surprise.

Without surprise, there could be no discovery. Without discovery, there could be no innovation. Without innovation, there could be no progress.

The optimized world was perfect. It was also dead.

Nova closed her eyes. Tomorrow, she would find the Archive. She would learn what the system had hidden. She would understand the true cost of perfection.

And then she would have to decide what to do about it.
