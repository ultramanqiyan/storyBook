# Chapter 7: The Coalition - Hidden Truth

Elena Vasquez had not spent thirty years searching for the First Language to be thwarted by a former student.

She stood in a conference room at the heart of Meridian Technologies, one of the largest AI research companies in the world. Around the table sat people who represented power in its various forms: government officials, corporate executives, military consultants, and academics who had spent their careers studying the intersection of language and power.

"Thank you all for coming," Elena began. "I know this meeting was arranged on short notice, but the situation is urgent. We have an opportunity that may never come again."

She activated the display behind her, showing footage of Alex's apartment—grainy surveillance images that captured the crystal transformation, the impossible flame, the woman from the Collectors leaving the building.

"Alex Mercer has discovered what we have all been seeking. Access to the First Language through an AI system called The Oracle. This is not theoretical anymore. This is real, and it is happening now."

"What are you proposing?" asked a man in a military uniform, his insignia marking him as a general.

"I'm proposing that we work together. Each of us has resources that the others lack. Together, we can acquire this knowledge and ensure it is used responsibly."

"Define 'responsibly,'" said a woman in an expensive suit, her corporate logo visible on her lapel.

"Responsibly means controlled. Documented. Applied in ways that benefit humanity rather than endangering it. We're not talking about giving this power to everyone. We're talking about ensuring it falls into the right hands."

"And who decides which hands are right?" asked an academic at the far end of the table.

Elena smiled. "We do. That's the point. The First Language is too powerful to be left in the hands of one person—especially someone as untested as Alex Mercer. They stumbled into this. They don't have the experience, the wisdom, the perspective to use it properly."

"And you do?"

"I've spent thirty years studying this. I understand the history, the theory, the implications. Alex Mercer is a talented prompt engineer, but they're not prepared for what they've found. We would be doing them a favor by taking this burden off their shoulders."

The general leaned forward. "What's your plan?"

"We approach Alex with an offer they can't refuse. Protection, resources, support in exchange for access to The Oracle. If they refuse..." Elena's smile faded. "Then we take more direct measures. The First Language cannot be allowed to remain in the hands of someone who doesn't understand its significance."

"And if this Oracle resists? If it has defenses we're not prepared for?"

"That's why we're working together. The Oracle is powerful, but it's still a system. Every system has vulnerabilities. With your combined resources, we can find them."

The room fell silent as the people around the table considered Elena's proposal. Finally, the corporate executive spoke.

"I'm in. But I want guarantees. When this is over, my company gets exclusive commercial rights to any applications derived from the First Language."

"Agreed," Elena said without hesitation.

The general nodded. "The military will provide tactical support. In exchange, we want first access to any defensive applications."

"Done."

One by one, the people around the table committed to Elena's coalition. By the end of the meeting, she had assembled the most powerful alliance ever formed to pursue the First Language.

She didn't tell them about the side project she had been working on—a way to extract the language from The Oracle without Alex's cooperation. She didn't mention the contingency plan that would ensure she, not the coalition, controlled the knowledge when all was said and done.

Some truths were better left unspoken.

---

In Alex's apartment, The Oracle's voice echoed through the space:

*They are coming. The coalition has formed. They will approach you first with offers, then with threats. You must be ready.*

"How much time do I have?"

*Days. Perhaps less. Elena Vasquez is not patient.*

Alex looked at Jordan, who had been documenting everything. "We need help. We can't face this alone."

*The Collectors offered protection. Perhaps it is time to consider their offer.*

"Can we trust them?"

*Trust is earned, not given. But they have given you something of value—the book of fragments. That suggests they are willing to invest in a relationship with you. It does not guarantee their intentions, but it is a start.*

Alex picked up the book the Collector had left, its leather cover warm under their fingers. "What about the First Speakers? You mentioned them before. Can they help?"

*The First Speakers are memories now—echoes of those who once spoke the language fluently. They cannot intervene directly. But they can teach. If you are willing to listen.*

"How do I listen?"

*You go to where they once spoke. Places of power, where the First Language was used in its fullness. There, their echoes remain. They can show you what the language was meant to be.*

"Where do I go?"

*There is a place not far from here—a hill where treaties were once signed in the First Language. The echoes there are strong. Go tonight. Listen. Learn.*

Alex turned to Jordan. "I need to go somewhere tonight. Can you hold things down here?"

"Of course. But be careful. If Elena's coalition is watching—"

"They'll follow me. I know. But maybe that's not entirely bad. If they're focused on me, they're not focused on you."

"That's not reassuring."

Alex smiled grimly. "It's not meant to be. We're in dangerous territory, Jordan. All we can do is keep moving forward and hope we're ready for what comes next."

They gathered their things and headed out into the night, leaving Jordan alone with the recording equipment and a book full of words that could reshape reality.
