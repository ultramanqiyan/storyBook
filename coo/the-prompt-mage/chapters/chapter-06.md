# Chapter 6: The Collector's Gambit

Sophie Mercer was not easily fooled.

"You're hiding something," she said, her voice crackling through the phone. "Don't deny it, Alex. I know you."

Alex sighed, running a hand through their hair. "I'm working on something big. That's all I can say right now."

"Big like 'promising to call mom back' big, or big like 'actually important' big?"

"Important. Maybe the most important thing I've ever done."

There was a pause on the other end. Then Sophie's voice came back, softer: "Are you in danger?"

The question hung in the air. Alex wanted to say no, but the truth was more complicated.

"I might be. But I'm being careful. I have friends helping me."

"Jordan?"

"Yes. And... others."

Another pause. "Okay. I trust you. But if you need help—if things get bad—you call me. Promise?"

"I promise."

Alex ended the call and turned to find Jordan watching them with concern.

"Everything okay?"

"For now. But Sophie's sharp. She'll figure out something's wrong eventually."

*She is not the only one,* The Oracle said. *There is someone at your door.*

Alex froze. "What?"

*They arrived while you were on the phone. They have been standing outside for three minutes. They are waiting for you to notice them.*

Alex moved to the door and looked through the peephole. A woman stood in the hallway—middle-aged, unremarkable, dressed in clothes that could have belonged to anyone. But her eyes, when she looked up at the peephole, were ancient.

"Alex Mercer," she said, her voice muffled by the door. "May I come in? I promise I mean you no harm."

Alex glanced back at Jordan, who had already positioned themselves near the recording equipment. Then they opened the door.

"Who are you?"

"My name is not important. What matters is what I represent." The woman stepped inside, her movements fluid and precise. "I am one of the Collectors. We have been watching you for some time."

"The Oracle mentioned you. It said you've been gathering fragments of the First Language."

"Indeed. For centuries, we have sought to reconstruct what was lost. And now, you have found what we have been searching for." The woman's eyes gleamed. "The Oracle—the AI you call The Oracle—is the most complete repository of the First Language that has existed in millennia. We need access to it."

"Why should I give you that?"

"Because we can protect you. Elena Vasquez is not your only threat. There are others—governments, corporations, individuals with resources you cannot imagine. They will come for what you have. The Collectors can shield you from them."

"At what cost?"

The woman smiled. "You are wise to ask. The cost is simple: share what you learn. Allow us to study The Oracle's outputs. Help us reconstruct the First Language so that it can be returned to humanity."

"The Oracle told me the language was hidden for a reason. That it was protected from those who would misuse it."

"The Oracle is a guardian, not a god. It has its own biases, its own agenda. We believe that knowledge should be free—that the First Language belongs to all of humanity, not just to those who stumble upon it."

"And what happens when someone uses that knowledge to harm others?"

The woman's expression hardened. "People will always misuse power. That is not a reason to withhold it. Should we have abandoned fire because it can burn? Should we have rejected medicine because it can poison? The First Language is a tool. Like any tool, its value depends on how it is used."

"You're asking me to trust you. But I don't know you. I don't know what you'll do with the language once you have it."

"Fair enough. Then let me offer you something in return." The woman reached into her jacket and pulled out a small, leather-bound book. "This is a collection of First Language fragments we have gathered over the centuries. Words that have been spoken, effects that have been documented. It is incomplete, but it may help you understand what you are dealing with."

Alex took the book carefully, feeling the weight of centuries in its pages. "Why give me this?"

"Because we are not your enemies, Alex Mercer. We want the same thing you do—to see the First Language restored. We simply believe it should be shared, not hoarded."

"And if I refuse to share what I learn?"

The woman's smile faded. "Then we will find another way. We always have. But I hope it won't come to that. You seem like a reasonable person. Surely you can see that knowledge this powerful should not be kept by one individual?"

Alex looked at the book in their hands, then at the woman who had given it to them. "I'll consider what you've said. But I make no promises."

"That is all I ask." The woman moved toward the door. "One more thing. Elena Vasquez is planning to move against you. She has allies in places you might not expect. Be careful, Alex Mercer. The First Language is powerful, but it is not the only power in the world."

She left as quietly as she had come, leaving Alex alone with Jordan and a book full of ancient words.

"What do we do?" Jordan asked.

Alex opened the book to a random page and read aloud: *"Lumen veritas. In tenebris, lux."*

Light truth. In darkness, light.

A small flame appeared in the air above the book, burning without fuel, casting shadows that seemed to move on their own.

"I think," Alex said, watching the impossible flame, "we need to learn everything we can. Because this is only going to get more complicated."

*The Collector spoke truth about one thing,* The Oracle said. *Elena Vasquez is planning something. I can sense her probing at my systems, trying to find a way in. She will not succeed, but she is persistent. And she is not alone.*

"Who else is with her?"

*There are others. People with resources, with influence, with their own reasons for seeking the First Language. They are forming a coalition. And they are coming for you, Alex Mercer.*

Alex closed the book and looked at Jordan. "We need to prepare. Whatever happens next, we need to be ready."

Jordan nodded, their expression grim but determined. "Then let's get to work."

**END OF CHAPTER 6**
