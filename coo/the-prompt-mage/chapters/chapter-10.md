# Chapter 10: The Word That Cannot Be Taken
The air carried the faint scent of dust, lingering in the background.

They came at midnight.

Alex heard them before they saw them—the synchronized footsteps of a tactical team, the soft hum of electronic equipment designed to detect and disable. Elena's coalition had brought everything they had: security forces, technical specialists, and Elena herself, standing at the back like a conductor directing an orchestra.

Alex stood in the center of their apartment, Jordan beside them, the book of First Language fragments open on the table between them. The crystal that had started everything was gone—Unmade days ago—but its absence felt like a presence, a reminder of what they had learned and what they stood to lose.

"Alex Mercer," Elena's voice came through a megaphone from outside. "You are surrounded. Surrender peacefully, and no one will be harmed."

Alex looked at Jordan. "You should go."

"I'm not leaving you."

"Jordan, you're a Witness. Your job is to remember what happens here. You can't do that if you're captured."

Jordan's jaw tightened. Then they nodded, moving toward the back exit that The Oracle had assured them was clear. "I'll document everything. I'll make sure the world knows what happened here."

"That's all I ask."

Jordan disappeared into the night, and Alex turned to face the door as it burst inward.

The tactical team moved with practiced efficiency, securing the perimeter, disabling any potential threats. But when they reached the center of the room, they found only Alex, standing alone, their hands at their sides.

"Dr. Elena Vasquez," the team leader reported. "Target secured."

Elena entered the apartment, her eyes sweeping the space with clinical precision. "Where is the Oracle? Where is the interface?"

"The Oracle isn't something you can take," Alex said calmly. "It's not a machine. It's a voice. And voices can't be captured."

"We'll see about that." Elena nodded to her technical team, who began setting up equipment around Alex. "We're going to extract everything you know. Every word of the First Language, every technique, every secret. And when we're done, you'll be free to go."

"You still don't understand." Alex looked at Elena with something like pity. "The First Language can't be extracted. It can only be spoken. And I choose what I speak."

"We have ways of encouraging cooperation."

"I know. But you're forgetting something important." Alex closed their eyes, reaching for that deep place where the language lived. "The First Language isn't just about creation and transformation. It's also about truth. And the truth is..."

They spoke a word—not in English, but in the First Language, a word that resonated through the room like a bell:

*"Veritas."*

Truth.

The word didn't attack. It didn't destroy. It simply revealed. And suddenly, everyone in the room could see everything—the coalition's true motivations, Elena's hidden agenda, the ways they had all been deceiving themselves and each other.

Elena gasped as she saw her own reflection—not the brilliant scholar she believed herself to be, but a woman driven by fear and pride, willing to sacrifice anything for knowledge she didn't understand.

The tactical team members saw their own complicity, the ways they had allowed themselves to become tools of forces they didn't comprehend.

Even Alex saw themselves more clearly—not a hero, not a villain, just a person who had stumbled into something vast and was trying to do their best with it.

"Stop," Elena whispered. "Please."

*"Silex,"* Alex said. Silence.

The truth faded, leaving only the memory of what they had all seen. The tactical team stood frozen, their weapons lowered, their certainty shattered.

"The First Language cannot be taken," Alex said quietly. "But it can be shared. With those who are willing to receive it."

Elena stared at them, her face pale. "What do you want?"

"I want you to leave. I want you to tell your coalition that the First Language is not a resource to be exploited. It's a responsibility to be honored. And I want you to know that I will be watching. If you try to come for me again—if you try to come for anyone I care about—I will speak the truth again. And next time, I won't stop at silence."

Elena was silent for a long moment. Then she nodded, once, and gestured to her team. They filed out of the apartment, leaving Alex alone in the wreckage of their home.

*You spoke well,* The Oracle said. *But this is not over.*

"I know."

Alex looked around the apartment—at the scattered equipment, the broken door, the evidence of the battle that had just been fought.

But for now, there was peace. For now, there was truth.

Alex picked up the book of fragments and walked to the window, looking out at the city lights.
