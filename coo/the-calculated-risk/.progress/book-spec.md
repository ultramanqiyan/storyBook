## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: Instinct vs Algorithm - The irreplaceable role of gut feeling in extreme situations
- **Secondary Theme**: The Value of Fear - Why fear is essential for survival
- **Tertiary Theme**: Human Judgment - The limits of calculation in unpredictable environments
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Through Alex's journey of rediscovery; Through contrast between AI and instinctive climbers; Through Alex's struggle to feel fear again

## World Building
- **Time Period**: Near-future (5-10 years from present)
- **Primary Location**: Mountain ranges, climbing gyms, training facilities
- **Environment**: High-tech training centers, remote wilderness
- **Social Context**: AI risk systems standard in extreme sports
- **Key Technologies**: RISK-AI system, biometric monitoring, predictive path analysis
- **Social Structure**: Athletes who question AI seen as "reckless" or "old-fashioned"
- **World Rules**: RISK-AI (Standard system that calculates "optimal" paths and risks), The Gap (Athletes who rely on AI lose survival instincts), The Old Guard (Underground network of athletes who maintain instinctive skills)

## Sensory Detail Requirements

### Distribution Targets (Per Chapter)
- **Minimum 10 sensory details per chapter**

- **Visual (30%), Others (70% combined)**

### Sensory Categories
- **Visual**: Mountain faces, rock textures, gear, landscapes
- **Auditory**: Wind, rope sounds, rock shifts, silence
- **Tactile**: Rock texture, rope, harness, cold, sweat
- **Olfactory**: Mountain air, sweat, chalk, fear
- **Internal**: Fear (or lack of), adrenaline, exhaustion
- **Proprioceptive**: Balance, body position, movement

## Emotional Depth Design

### Three-Layer Emotional Model (Required for Every Major Scene)
1. **Surface Reaction** - External behavior (e.g., "I checked my wrist display for the risk rating")
2. **Internal Activity** - Thought process (e.g., "*Why can't I feel anything? This should terrify me.*")
3. **Deep Motivation** - Underlying fears (e.g., "The terror that without fear, I was already dead")

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt afraid." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |
