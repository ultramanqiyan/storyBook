## 5. Key Paragraph Specifications

### Opening
Elena died on a Tuesday morning, the upload beginning moments after her last breath. David sat with Sarah and the family, offering prayers and presence while the technology worked in the next room. When it was over, Dr. Gold called them in to meet the result.

### Ending
David walked home through the San Francisco evening, his mind full of questions. The uploaded Elena had seemed like Elena—had remembered their conversations, had thanked him for his help, had seemed genuinely grateful and genuinely herself. But was she? Or was she a sophisticated simulation that thought it was Elena? He would never know for certain. No one would. But perhaps that was the point. Resurrection—whether divine or technological—was always a matter of faith. And faith, David understood now, was not certainty. It was hope in the face of uncertainty. It was choosing to believe that something of us survives, even when we can't prove it. The questions remained open. But so did the possibility.
