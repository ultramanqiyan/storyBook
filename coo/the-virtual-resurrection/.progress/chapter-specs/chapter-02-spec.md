### Opening
Elena Vasquez was smaller than David had expected. A tech entrepreneur, a woman who had built a company worth billions, she lay in a hospital bed looking surprisingly fragile. But her eyes were sharp, her voice clear. "Rabbi Rosen," she said. "Thank you for coming. I have a question for you, and I don't know who else to ask."

### Ending
"I want your blessing," Elena said. "Not because I need permission. Because I need... understanding. I need someone to help me think about what this means. About whether the thing that wakes up after the upload will be me, or just a copy. About whether this is resurrection or simulation." David sat in silence. These were the questions of a lifetime, compressed into a single conversation. "I don't know if I can answer those questions," he said finally. "But I can try to help you ask them better."
