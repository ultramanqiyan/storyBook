### Opening
Rabbi David Rosen walked through the hospital corridors, his pager silent for once, the morning light filtering through the windows. Fifteen years as a chaplain had taught him to be present with dying patients, to sit with families in their grief, to find words when there were no words. But the message he had received that morning was unlike anything in his experience.

### Ending
David drove toward the hospital where Elena Vasquez was a patient, his mind racing. A consciousness upload. A digital afterlife. A woman seeking his blessing for something that blurred every line between life and death, body and soul. He didn't know what he would say. He didn't even know what he believed about it. But he knew he had to try to understand.
