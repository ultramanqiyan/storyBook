## 5. Key Paragraph Specifications

### Opening
David sat in his study, surrounded by books. The Talmud, Maimonides, modern Jewish philosophy—all of them had something to say about resurrection, about the afterlife, about what survives death. But none of them had imagined anything like this.

### Ending
The tradition offered questions, not answers. The rabbis had debated resurrection for centuries—was it bodily or spiritual? Did the resurrected body have the same identity as the original? What was the relationship between body and soul? But they had never imagined a resurrection that was technological, a continuity that was digital. David would have to find his own way through this, guided by tradition but not limited by it.
