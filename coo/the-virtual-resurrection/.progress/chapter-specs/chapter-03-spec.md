## 5. Key Paragraph Specifications

### Opening
Dr. Benjamin Gold was exactly what David had expected: young, brilliant, and utterly confident in his technology. "The process is quite straightforward," he said, leading David through the lab. "We map the neural connections, transfer the pattern to a digital substrate, and initiate consciousness. The result is... continuity. A preservation of identity."

### Ending
David walked out of the lab, his mind reeling. The technology was real. It worked. But the questions it raised were staggering: If Elena's consciousness was uploaded, would the result be Elena? Would it have her memories, her personality, her soul? Or would it be a sophisticated simulation, a copy that thought it was her? And what did that mean for resurrection, for identity, for everything he thought he knew about death and continuity?
