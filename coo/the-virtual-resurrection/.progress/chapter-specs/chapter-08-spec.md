### Opening
David sat by Elena's bedside, the afternoon light softening the hospital room. "I saw the simulation," he said. Elena nodded. "And?" "And I don't know if it's you. I don't know if anyone can know." Elena smiled, a sad, knowing smile. "That's the most honest thing anyone has said to me about this."

### Ending
They sat in silence for a long time, two people facing mortality and the unknown. Finally, Elena spoke: "I'm going to do it. Not because I'm certain it's the right choice. But because I have to choose something, and this is the choice that feels like hope." David nodded. "Then I will be with you. Not because I understand. But because you shouldn't be alone with this." Elena reached for his hand. "Thank you, Rabbi. For not giving me answers. For helping me ask better questions."
