Lily Okonkwo is an "Optima native"—the first generation of children raised entirely in LEXICON's optimized language. In this world, questions are inefficient, dreams are monitored, and the word "why" has been replaced by "query purpose." But Lily is different. She dreams of colors that don't exist in the Academy's palette. She hears words in her grandmother's voice that she can't translate. And when she asks "why" for the first time, she sets in motion a chain of events that will force her to choose between fitting in and remembering. A coming-of-age dystopian novel about the power of questions, the cost of resistance, and the words we carry for each other.
