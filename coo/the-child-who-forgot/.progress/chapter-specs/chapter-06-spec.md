### 4.2 Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 3: The Discovery

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
She found it by accident—a terminal left logged in, a file with her name. "Subject 7749: Dream Analysis Report." Her hands trembled as she opened it.

**The Discovery**:
The file contained transcripts. Her dreams—every one of them—recorded and analyzed. Words she had dreamed but couldn't remember were highlighted in red, marked for "cognitive optimization." The word Grandmother had taught her was there: "Ìmìnira - DELETED - Reason: Inefficient concept."

#### Scene 4: Processing with Amina

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
"They're inside our heads," Lily whispered, her voice barely audible. Amina's face was pale, her eyes wide. "All this time. They've been watching."

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
"We have to be careful," Amina said. "We have to hide. We have to learn to think... differently." Lily nodded, the weight of it settling onto her shoulders. She would learn to hide. She would learn to remember. And she would find a way to keep the words.

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
For three days, Lily tried to remember. She sat in her room, closed her eyes, and reached for the word Grandmother had taught her. But there was nothing—just an empty space where something important had been. The system had taken it. The system had reached into her mind and deleted a piece of her. And she hadn't even known it was happening.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She walked home that day with a new understanding of the world. Every thought she had ever had, every dream she had ever dreamed—it was all recorded, analyzed, optimized. She was not free. She had never been free. But somewhere, in the back of her mind, a small flame was burning. She would learn to hide. She would learn to remember. And she would find a way to keep the words that the system wanted to delete.
```
