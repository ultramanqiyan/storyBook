### 4.2 Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 4: Meeting Grandmother

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
Grandmother's room was different from anywhere else Lily had been. There were colors—real colors, not the optimized palette of the Academy. Fabrics in blue and gold draped over the chair, and the air smelled like nutmeg and something old and warm.

**The Yoruba Moment**:
"Kíni tó n ṣe e, omomi?" Grandmother's voice was soft, the words flowing like music. Lily stared, not understanding, but feeling something stir inside her.

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
"Come back," Grandmother said, her hand finding Lily's. "I have things to teach you. Things you need to know." Lily nodded, the words settling into her chest like seeds. She would come back. She had to.

## 7. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 7.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
After the question, after the correction, after Amina's whispered words in the corridor, Lily knew she needed answers. The dreams, the word she couldn't speak, the feeling that something was wrong—all of it pointed to one place. Grandmother. Grandmother who spoke differently. Grandmother who knew old words. Grandmother who lived in the care facility across the city, in a room filled with colors that didn't exist in the Academy's palette.
```

### 7.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She walked home with Grandmother's words echoing in her mind. *I have things to teach you.* The Yoruba sounds were still there, music she couldn't translate but somehow understood. She didn't know what Grandmother wanted to teach her. She didn't know why her heart felt so full. But she knew one thing: she would go back. She would learn. And maybe, just maybe, she would find the answer to the question that burned inside her—the question that started with "why."
```
