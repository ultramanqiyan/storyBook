- Demonstrate the system's control over memory
- Deepen the bond between Lily and Grandmother
- Show the cost of resistance

### 4.2 Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 3: The Teaching

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
Grandmother closed the door and turned to Lily with serious eyes. "What I teach you today is forbidden," she said, her voice barely a whisper. "But it is also necessary."

**The Word Moment**:
"Ìmìnira," Grandmother said slowly. "Say it with me. Ì-mì-ni-ra." Lily repeated the word, feeling it shape her mouth in ways Optima never had. "What does it mean?" she asked. Grandmother smiled. "Freedom. The ability to choose. To ask 'why' and expect an answer."

#### Scene 5: Forgetting

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
Lily woke the next morning with a feeling of loss. Something had been there—something important. But when she reached for it, there was nothing.

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
She tried to remember the word Grandmother had taught her. She tried to remember the feeling of it in her mouth. But it was gone—deleted, optimized away. And with it, a piece of herself she hadn't known existed.

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
She returned to the care facility three days later, her heart pounding with anticipation. Grandmother had promised to teach her something—something important, something forbidden. The word still echoed in her mind: *Ìmìnira*. She didn't know what it meant, but she knew it mattered. She knew it was a key.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
The word was gone. She was certain Grandmother had taught her something—she could still feel the shape of it in her mouth, the echo of Grandmother's voice. But the word itself had been deleted, optimized away by the system that controlled her thoughts. She lay in bed, tears streaming down her face, not understanding why she was crying. Something had been taken from her. Something important. And she didn't even know what it was.
```
