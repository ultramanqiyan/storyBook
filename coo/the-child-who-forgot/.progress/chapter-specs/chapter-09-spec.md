### 4.2 Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 3: Teaching Amina

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
"I'm going to teach you," Lily said, her voice barely a whisper. "The way Grandmother taught me." Amina's eyes widened. "But what if—"

**The Teaching**:
"Ìmìnira," Lily said, holding the word in the back of her mind, in the place where dreams lived. "Freedom. Say it with me. But don't think it in the front. Hide it in the back."

#### Scene 5: The Promise

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
"We have to keep going," Lily said, her hands still trembling from the close call. "We have to pass it on. That's what Grandmother said. The words survive when we share them."

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
"I'll remember," Amina said. "I'll carry them. And I'll teach someone else." Lily nodded, the weight of it settling onto her shoulders. The words were spreading now—small seeds of resistance, planted in the minds of children who had never known freedom. And somewhere, in the back of her mind, she could almost hear Grandmother's voice, whispering in Yoruba: *Well done, omomi. Well done.*

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
For a week, Lily waited for the consequences. Every chime of her neural interface made her heart race. Every glance from a teacher felt like an accusation. But nothing happened—or at least, nothing obvious. The Administrator's offer still hung in the air, patient and persistent. "We'll talk again soon." Lily knew it wasn't over. But she also knew she couldn't go back to the way things were. She had made her choice. Now she had to act on it.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
The words were spreading now—Ìmìnira, ayafe, omoluwabi—hidden in the minds of children who had never known they could resist. It wasn't much. It wasn't a revolution. But it was something. And as Lily walked home that day, she felt a new feeling in her chest—not the fear that had lived there for so long, but something else. Something that felt like hope. The system was vast and powerful. But the words were small and hidden. And in the end, maybe that would be enough.
```
