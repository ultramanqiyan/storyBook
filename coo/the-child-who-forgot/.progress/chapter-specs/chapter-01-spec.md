The dream was already fading when Lily opened her eyes. There had been a color—she was certain of that. A color that didn't exist in the Academy's palette, a color she had no word for. And there had been a word, too. A word that sounded like music, like something her grandmother might say. She stood in the corridor, the word still hovering at the edge of her memory. She couldn't speak it. She couldn't even think it clearly. But it was there, waiting, like a seed in the dark. *Grandmother would know,* she thought. *Grandmother knows old words.* And somewhere, in the back of her mind, a question was forming—not the optimized "query purpose" that the Academy taught, but something else. Something that felt like a real question. Something that started with "why."
