### 4.2 Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 3: The Lesson

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
The lesson was about "Cognitive Efficiency Protocols"—how to recognize and redirect inefficient thought patterns. Lily sat at her station, her heart pounding. Today was the day.

#### Scene 4: The Question

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
Her hand rose before she could stop it. The teacher's eyes found her, pleasant and expectant. "Lily. Query purpose."

**The Question Moment**:
"Teacher," Lily said, her voice trembling. "Why—" The word felt strange in her mouth, a shape she had never made before. "Why do we not learn about the old words?"

**The Correction**:
The teacher's smile didn't waver. "Lily. 'Why' is an inefficient cognitive pattern. The correct formulation is 'query purpose.' Would you like to rephrase?"

#### Scene 5: Aftermath

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
Amina found her in the corridor, her face pale. "You asked 'why,'" she whispered. "I've never heard anyone say that word before."

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
"Something is wrong," Lily said, the words barely audible. "I don't know what, but something is wrong." Amina nodded, her eyes wide. And in that moment, Lily knew she wasn't alone.

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The dream had come again—not the same dream, but similar. Colors that didn't exist. Words that sounded like music. And this time, a voice. Grandmother's voice, saying something Lily couldn't quite hear. She woke with the word "why" on her lips, and it felt like a key turning in a lock she hadn't known was there.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She walked home that day with a new feeling in her chest—not the shame of the correction, but something else. Something that felt like a small, hard stone. She had asked "why." She had been corrected. But the question hadn't gone away. It was still there, burning in her mind. And she knew, with a certainty she couldn't explain, that she would ask it again.
```
