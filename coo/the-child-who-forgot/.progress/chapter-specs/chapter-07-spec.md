### 4.2 Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 3: Learning to Hide

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
"The system reads your thoughts through your interface," Grandmother explained, her voice low. "But it cannot read what you do not think." Lily frowned. "How can I not think something?"

**The Technique**:
"You must learn to hold words in a different part of your mind," Grandmother said. "Not in the front, where the interface watches. But in the back, in the place where dreams live. That is where the old words survive."

#### Scene 4: The Warning

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
"There is something you must know," Grandmother said, her face serious. "The words I teach you—they are dangerous. If the system discovers them, it will delete them. And perhaps... it will delete you."

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
"Be careful, omomi," Grandmother said, using the Yoruba word for "my child." "The words are precious. But you are more precious. Do not let them take you." Lily nodded, the weight of it settling onto her shoulders. She would be careful. She would be strong. And she would remember.

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
She returned to Grandmother with a new purpose. The discovery had changed everything—she knew now that the system was watching, that her thoughts were not her own. But Grandmother had promised to teach her. Grandmother had promised to show her how to remember. And Lily was ready to learn.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She walked home with the words burning in her mind—not in the front, where the interface could see, but in the back, in the place where dreams lived. *Ìmìnira.* Freedom. *Ayafe.* Beloved. *Omoluwabi.* A person of good character. Three words, hidden in the dark corners of her mind, waiting to be remembered. She held them like precious stones, turning them over and over, feeling their weight. The system could delete what it could see. But it could not delete what it could not find.
```
