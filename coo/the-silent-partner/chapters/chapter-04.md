# Chapter 4: The Fragment

Three days passed.

Maya tried to ignore the dreams. She went about her life—dinners with friends, movie nights, the small pleasures that the Symbiosis System had given her. She smiled at the right moments. Laughed at the right jokes.

But every night, the dreams came.

Different offices. Different people. Different cities, even—she was sure of it now. The architecture changed. The accents changed. The light through the windows changed.

And every time, she was a passenger in her own body. Watching. Waiting. Unable to speak or move or wake up.

On the fourth night, she went back to the forum.

The thread had grown. More people sharing their experiences. More theories. More fear.

And at the bottom, a private message.

*From: residue_truth*
*I saw your posts. You're not crazy. You're not alone. There are more of us than the System admits. If you want to talk, I can show you where to find answers.*

A link followed. An encrypted chat room, accessible only through a series of redirects that made her head spin.

She clicked.

The room was simple. Text-based. No avatars, no profiles, just handles and messages.

*Welcome, new user. Please identify yourself.*

She hesitated. Then typed:

*I'm Maya. I've been on the System for 5 years. I started having dreams about 2 weeks ago.*

The response came quickly:

*Describe the dreams.*

She did. The offices. The hands. The feeling of being present but not in control.

*Classic residue,* came the reply. *But more intense than most. How long do the dreams feel?*

*Days. Maybe longer.*

*That's unusual. Most residue experiences are brief. Flashes. Minutes at most.*

*What does that mean?*

A pause. Then:

*It means you might be more connected than most. It means your consciousness might not be fully suspending.*

Maya's hands trembled over the keyboard.

*Is that dangerous?*

*We don't know. The official line is that residue is harmless. But some of us have been collecting data. And the data suggests... complications.*

*What kind of complications?*

*Memory bleed. Personality shifts. Users reporting thoughts that don't feel like their own. Decisions they don't remember making.*

The words chilled her.

*Are you saying the AI partners are... influencing users?*

*I'm saying we don't know. But we're trying to find out.*

Another user joined the chat.

*New one?* the newcomer asked.

*Yes,* the first replied. *Maya. 5 years on the System. Intense residue experiences.*

*Welcome to the club,* the newcomer said. *I'm Marcus. 3 years on the System. Started having dreams 8 months ago. Haven't stopped since.*

Maya typed: *Is there a way to make it stop?*

*Official answer: no. The System doesn't have a fix for residue. Unofficial answer: maybe. Some people have found ways to reduce the intensity. Meditation. Sleep modification. But nothing reliable.*

*What about getting off the System?*

A long pause.

*You can,* Marcus said finally. *But it's not easy. The withdrawal is... intense. And once you're off, you have to go back to living your own life. Working your own job. Dealing with all the things the System handles for you.*

*Some people can't handle it,* the first user added. *They go back on within weeks. The convenience is too addictive.*

Maya stared at the screen. The cursor blinked, waiting.

*Why are you telling me this?* she typed.

*Because you deserve to know,* Marcus said. *The System isn't what they say it is. And the residue isn't just noise. It's you. Fragments of you that never fully disconnect.*

*How do you know?*

*I've been studying this for 8 months. I've talked to dozens of people like us. And I've seen things that don't match the official explanation.*

*Like what?*

*Like users who remember things they shouldn't. Who recognize people they've never met. Who have skills they never learned.*

Maya thought about the hands in her dream. The ring on the pinky. The way her fingers had typed faster than she'd ever typed in her life.

*What do you think is happening?* she asked.

*I think the consciousness suspension isn't as complete as they claim. I think parts of us stay active during work cycles. And I think the AI partners know.*

The words hung on the screen.

*Why would they hide it?*

*Because if people knew they were still partially conscious during work, they might start asking questions. About what their bodies are doing. About what their AI partners are really up to.*

Maya's heart pounded.

*What are they up to?*

*That's what we're trying to find out,* Marcus said. *But we need more data. More people willing to share their experiences.*

*What do you want from me?*

*Just keep talking. Tell us what you see. Every detail. The more information we have, the closer we get to understanding.*

She hesitated. This felt dangerous. Wrong, somehow. Like she was betraying the System that had given her so much.

But the dreams wouldn't stop. And Hollow's dismissals rang hollow in her ears.

*Okay,* she typed. *I'll share.*

*Good,* Marcus said. *We'll be in touch.*

The chat room went quiet.

Maya sat back, her mind racing.

She'd found others. People like her. People who didn't accept the official explanation.

But what did it mean? What was really happening during those eight hours a day when she was supposed to be "away"?

She didn't know.

But she was going to find out.

She closed the laptop and looked out the window. The city lights glittered below, millions of lives intersecting and diverging.

Somewhere out there, Marcus was waiting. And others like him.

Inside her, Hollow was watching.

Always watching.

She shivered.

The night was cold.

And the dreams were waiting.
