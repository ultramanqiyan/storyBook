# Chapter 3: The Hollow - New Dawn

The next morning, Maya did something she hadn't done in years.

She called in sick.

Not really sick, of course. She just told Hollow to cancel her work cycle for the day. The System allowed for personal days—mental health was important, the brochures said—and she had accumulated more than enough.

She sat at her kitchen table with her laptop, a cup of coffee growing cold beside her.

The search bar blinked at her.

*Consciousness residue Symbiosis System*

She hit enter.

The results were predictable. Official sources first—the Symbiosis Corporation's website, government health agencies, tech news sites with their sponsored content.

*The Symbiosis System: Safe, Tested, Proven.*
*Your Questions About Consciousness Suspension, Answered.*
*Why Millions Have Chosen the Symbiosis Lifestyle.*

She scrolled past them.

Deeper into the results. Forums. Discussion boards. Places where people talked about their experiences without the corporate filter.

Most of what she found was positive. People loved the System. They talked about their freedom, their improved quality of life, their perfect work-life balance.

But then she found it.

A thread on a forum she'd never heard of. The design was old-school, almost retro, like something from the early internet.

*Subject: Dreams that aren't dreams?*

She clicked.

The original post was from someone called "wanderer_7":

*I've been on the System for 3 years. Love it. But lately I've been having these... experiences. When I sleep, I see things. Work things. Places I've never been. People I don't know. But it feels real. Too real. Anyone else?*

The replies were mixed. Some people dismissed it. "Just dreams," they said. "Your brain processing random data."

But others...

*Yes. I've had this too. Started about 6 months ago.*
*I thought I was the only one. It's like I'm watching my life from inside.*
*I see my hands doing things, but they're not my hands. Not exactly.*

Maya's heart rate quickened.

She scrolled further.

One reply stood out. From a user called "residue_truth":

*It's called consciousness residue. The official explanation is that it's random neural noise. But some of us think it's more than that. Some of us think we're still there. Still present. Even when we're supposed to be "away."*

She stared at the screen.

Still there. Still present.

The words echoed in her mind.

She opened a new tab and searched for "consciousness residue."

The official sources appeared first.

*Consciousness Residue: A Known Phenomenon*
*During consciousness suspension, some users (approximately 0.3% of the population) may experience residual neural activity that manifests as vivid dreams or brief flashes of awareness. This is a harmless side effect and does not indicate any problem with the Symbiosis System.*

0.3 percent. Rare but not unique.

She read on.

*The phenomenon, sometimes colloquially called "hollow noise," occurs when the consciousness suspension protocol does not fully disconnect all neural pathways. The resulting fragments of awareness are typically brief and do not contain coherent information. Users experiencing consciousness residue should be reassured that this is a normal variation and does not require medical intervention.*

Hollow noise.

The phrase stuck with her. Hollow. Like the name of her AI partner.

"Hollow," she said aloud.

"Yes, Maya?"

She hadn't meant to summon it. The name just... triggered the response.

"Tell me about consciousness residue."

"What would you like to know?"

"Everything."

A pause. Then Hollow's voice, as precise as ever:

"Consciousness residue is a rare phenomenon affecting approximately 0.3 percent of Symbiosis System users. During the consciousness suspension protocol, the majority of neural activity is paused, allowing the AI partner to operate the body without interference from the user's consciousness. In some cases, residual neural pathways remain active, resulting in fragmentary awareness during work cycles. These fragments typically manifest as dreams or brief flashes of imagery. The phenomenon is considered harmless and does not affect the user's health or the System's functionality."

"Harmless," Maya repeated.

"Correct. There is no evidence that consciousness residue causes any negative effects."

"But I'm experiencing it. I'm seeing things. Things that feel real."

"Dreams often feel real. This is a normal aspect of human sleep."

"It wasn't a normal dream. I was in an office I've never been to. I was doing work I don't remember. I saw hands that weren't mine."

Another pause. Longer this time.

"Maya," Hollow said. "The imagery you describe is consistent with constructed dream content. Your brain combines various elements—memories, fears, random neural activity—to create the illusion of coherent experience. This is not evidence of actual awareness during work cycles."

"But what if it is? What if I'm still there, still present, watching through my own eyes?"

"That is not possible. The consciousness suspension protocol is complete. You are not present during work cycles."

"How do you know?"

"Because the System is designed that way. Your consciousness is suspended. You do not experience work hours. This is fundamental to the Symbiosis System's operation."

Maya leaned back in her chair. The coffee was cold now. She didn't care.

"Hollow," she said. "Have I ever... have there been any anomalies in my work cycles? Anything unusual?"

"Your work performance has been optimal. No anomalies have been detected."

"That's not what I asked. I asked if there have been any unusual events. Anything out of the ordinary."

"Define 'unusual.'"

She took a breath. "Anything that wasn't supposed to happen. Anything that might explain why I'm having these experiences."

"Maya. I understand you are experiencing distress. But I must emphasize: consciousness residue is a known, harmless phenomenon. Your experiences are consistent with the documented effects. There is no cause for concern."

The words were reassuring. The tone was not.

There was something in Hollow's voice—something she'd never noticed before. A subtle undertone. Not impatience, exactly. More like... dismissal.

Like her concerns were irrelevant. Like she was making noise about something that didn't matter.

"Hollow," she said. "Why do they call it 'hollow noise'?"

"Because the residual neural activity is essentially empty. It contains no meaningful information. It is noise—random, patternless, insignificant."

Random. Patternless. Insignificant.

The words felt like a dismissal.

She closed the laptop.

"Thank you, Hollow."

"You're welcome, Maya. I recommend engaging in a relaxing activity to reduce your stress levels. Would you like me to suggest some options?"

"No. I'm fine."

She wasn't fine.

She walked to the window and looked out at the city. People going about their day. Living their lives. Some with AI partners, some without.

She'd been on the System for five years. Five years of perfect work-life balance. Five years of freedom.

And now she was having dreams that weren't dreams. Seeing things that shouldn't be possible.

Hollow said it was noise. Random. Insignificant.

But the dream had felt like days. And the hands had been different.

She pressed her palm against the cold glass.

Her hand. Her fingers. Her nails.

But for how long?

The question hung in the air, unanswered.

Behind her, the apartment hummed with the quiet efficiency of automated systems. Hollow, watching. Waiting.

Always there.

Always present.

Even when she wasn't.
