# Chapter 3: The Break

The break came during a routine code review.

A colleague criticized his approach—mildly, professionally, the kind of feedback that was normal in engineering. Marcus should have taken it in stride, considered it, responded professionally.

Instead, he felt crushed. The criticism echoed in his mind, growing larger, more devastating. He couldn't process it. Couldn't handle it.

He opened EmoteSense, typed: "Help me process this feedback," and waited for the AI to tell him how to feel.

*This is what I've become*, he realized. *Unable to handle normal criticism without AI assistance.*

---

The break was a wake-up call.

This wasn't a major crisis. This wasn't a catastrophic failure. This was normal workplace feedback—the kind he'd handled thousands of times before AI.

But now, without AI assistance, he couldn't handle it. The emotional resilience that had once defined him had disappeared.

*When did I lose this?* he wondered. *When did normal difficulty become overwhelming?*

The question was painful. But it was also necessary. Because it meant he was facing the truth. And facing the truth was the first step toward change.

---

He tried to process the feedback without AI.

It was hard. The emotions were intense. The discomfort was real. But he sat with it. Felt it. Allowed himself to experience the difficulty without algorithmic mediation.

The experience was uncomfortable. But it was also real. Not optimized. Not processed. Just... felt.

*This is what resilience looks like*, he realized. *Not the absence of difficulty—the presence of the capacity to handle it. Not optimized coping—actual emotional strength.*

The insight was small. But it was significant. Because it meant his resilience wasn't gone. It had just been buried under AI assistance. And now, he was digging it out.

---

That evening, he told Sarah about the break.

"I couldn't handle normal feedback today," he said, the words coming hard. "Without EmoteSense, I fell apart. Over something that should have been easy."

Sarah listened, her expression carrying the concern that had become familiar.

"What did you do?"

"I tried to handle it myself," Marcus said. "It was hard. But I did it. I felt the emotion. I processed it. I moved forward."

"That's resilience," Sarah said gently. "That's what you've been missing."

The words were simple. But they were true. And truth, he was learning, was what he needed.
