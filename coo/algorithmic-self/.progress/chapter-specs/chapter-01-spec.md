## 4. Opening

Maggie Sullivan stood before the judge, her voice calm and precise. "Your Honor, the behavioral data speaks for itself. My client's husband has exhibited patterns consistent with what DivorcePredict classifies as 'pre-divorce disengagement' for the past eighteen months." She clicked to the next slide. "These aren't accusations. These are patterns. And patterns, as we know, predict behavior."

---

## 5. Ending

Dr. Chen leaned forward. "What if I told you that DivorcePredict could analyze your own life? Not as a lawyer, but as a person. Would you want to know what it sees?" Maggie hesitated. She'd spent her career knowing other people's marriages better than they did. But her own? "How accurate is it?" she asked. "For personal prediction? Eighty-seven percent." Maggie thought of Tom, of their quiet dinners, of the distance she'd been ignoring. "I'll try it."
