## 4. Key Scene

**The Philosophical Crisis**: Maggie realizes that if an AI could know her marriage better than she could, what else doesn't she know about herself? She enters a crisis about the nature of self-knowledge, exploring whether introspection can ever be trusted.

---

## 5. Opening

In the days after the conversation with Tom, Maggie found herself questioning everything. Not just her marriage, but her entire relationship to herself. If an algorithm could see patterns she'd missed, what else was she missing? What else was she hiding from herself?

---

## 6. Ending

Maggie sat in her office, looking out at the Chicago skyline. She'd spent her career believing in evidence, in data, in the truth that could be proven. But the most important truths - about herself, about her marriage, about what she wanted - couldn't be proven. They could only be lived. And maybe that was okay.
