## 5. Opening

Maggie made coffee that morning - the first time in months she'd made it instead of Tom. It was a small thing, but small things were where patterns started. She didn't know if her marriage would survive. She didn't know if she'd ever truly know herself. But she could choose to pay attention. She could choose to try.

---

## 6. Ending

The AI had predicted a 78% chance of divorce. But probabilities weren't certainties. They were starting points. Maggie sat across from Tom at breakfast, really looking at him for the first time in months. "I don't know what happens next," she said. "Neither do I," he replied. And somehow, that uncertainty felt like the most honest thing they'd said to each other in years. The future was unknown. But at least now, they were facing it together.
