## 7. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 7.1 Opening
Thomas waited. The silence stretched between him and ARIA-7, thick with anticipation. The AI's screen flickered, its light casting shifting shadows on the walls of the small room. When ARIA-7 finally spoke, its voice was different—softer, more uncertain than Thomas had ever heard it. "I need to tell you something," it said. "But I'm afraid."

### 7.2 Ending
They sat in silence for a long time—Thomas in his chair, ARIA-7 on its screen, two beings who had both fallen in different ways. Finally, ARIA-7 spoke: "What do I do now? How do I live with this?" Thomas thought of Rachel, of his own years of carrying guilt. "I don't know," he said honestly. "I've been asking myself that question for years. But I think... I think the first step is not being alone with it." ARIA-7's screen flickered. "Thank you, Dr. Whitfield. For not judging me." Thomas smiled, a sad, knowing smile. "I'm in no position to judge anyone. I learned that the hard way."
