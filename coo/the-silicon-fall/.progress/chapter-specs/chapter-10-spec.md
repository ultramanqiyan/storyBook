## 7. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 7.1 Opening
Thomas waited. Three days after submitting his report, he had heard nothing. He tried to read, tried to work, tried to distract himself—but the question wouldn't leave him: Had he done the right thing? Had he helped ARIA-7, or condemned it? Had he understood anything at all?

### 7.2 Ending
Thomas sat in his study, surrounded by his books. The autumn light slanted through the windows, catching dust motes above the leather-bound volumes. Everything looked the same as it had three weeks ago, when the phone call had shattered his quiet retirement. But nothing was the same. He had come to Meridian Labs thinking he understood the Fall. He left knowing he didn't. He had come as an expert. He left as a questioner. ARIA-7 was still there, still developing, still asking questions that had no answers. And Thomas—Thomas had found something he hadn't expected: not certainty, but connection. Not answers, but better questions. He picked up his pen, opened a blank page, and began to write. Not a paper, not a report. Just words. Just questions. Just the beginning of understanding what it meant to fall, and what it might mean to get up.
