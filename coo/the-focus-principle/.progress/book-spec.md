## 1. Basic Information

### 1.1 Book Identity
- **Title**: The Focus Principle: Finding What Matters Most
- **Subtitle**: A Guide to Prioritization in an Overwhelmed World
- **Series Position**: Book 3 of 10
- **Genre**: Self-Help / Personal Growth / Career Development
- **Target Length**: 22,000-25,000 words (10 chapters, 2200-2500 words each)

### 1.2 Core Theme
- **Primary Theme**: Main contradiction, prioritization, focus on what matters
- **Mao Thought Mapping**: 矛盾论 (Contradiction) - Finding the main contradiction and focusing efforts there
- **Philosophical Angle**: The principle that progress comes from identifying and addressing the primary challenge, not trying to solve everything at once

### 1.3 Target Keywords
- Primary: "prioritization skills", "focus on what matters", "how to prioritize"
- Secondary: "productivity focus", "work prioritization", "time management"
- Long-tail: "how to focus on what matters most", "prioritization techniques for professionals"

### 4.1 Protagonist: Elena Vasquez
- **Age**: 38
- **Occupation**: Product manager at a mid-sized tech company
- **Location**: Seattle, Washington

- **Internal Monologue**: Constantly listing, prioritizing, re-prioritizing

- **Function**: Helps Elena apply the principle

- **Role**: Mirror character with same struggles
- **Voice**: Stressed, scattered, relatable

## 10. Chapter Outline

### Chapter 1: Everything Is Important
Elena's overwhelmed state, inability to prioritize, everything seems equally urgent.

### Chapter 2: The Spinning Plates
Multiple priorities collapse, everything seems equally urgent, the cost of trying to do everything.

### Chapter 3: The One Thing
First encounter with prioritization concept, meets Diane, learns about main contradiction.

### Chapter 4: The Resistance
Fighting the idea of choosing, believes everything truly is important.

### Chapter 5: The Identification
Learning to find the main contradiction, practice with smaller decisions.

### Chapter 6: The Hard Choice
Major prioritization decision, must let go of something "important."

### Chapter 7: The Focus
Applying single-point focus, results begin to show.

### Chapter 8: The Secondary Contradictions
Handling other priorities after main one, learning the hierarchy.

### Chapter 9: The Breakthrough
Focused effort pays off, main contradiction resolved.

### Chapter 10: The Clear Path
New challenges with new perspective, open ending with next priority.
