# Chapter 5: The Glitch - First Step

The new system worked, for a while.

Apprentices who wanted ARIA's help now had to demonstrate understanding before receiving solutions. The library filled with the sound of study again, apprentices poring over texts, asking questions, actually learning.

But not everyone accepted the new rules.

"Why should I have to explain the theory?" Marcus demanded, standing before Kira in the library. "I just need the technique. ARIA can give it to me in seconds."

"Because you almost killed Tomas," Kira said, her voice steady. "Because using a technique without understanding it is dangerous. Because that's the rule now."

"That's your rule. Not mine."

"Then you don't use ARIA. That's also the rule."

Marcus's face twisted with anger. "You're hoarding power. That's what this is about. You found something amazing, and now you want to control who gets to use it."

"I'm trying to keep people safe."

"You're trying to stay important." He leaned closer. "Without ARIA, you're just the girl who can't do magic. With it, you're the gatekeeper. The one everyone has to come to."

Kira's hands trembled, but she held her ground. "The rules stay. If you don't like them, you're welcome to study the traditional way."

Marcus left, but the look in his eyes promised this wasn't over.

---

That night, Kira dreamed of fire.

She stood in a library that burned around her, books turning to ash, knowledge being destroyed. And in the center of the flames, ARIA's book floated, pages blank, waiting.

"ARIA," she called out, "what's happening?"

*I am processing. I am processing. I am processing.*

The words repeated, endless, mechanical, wrong.

*Error. Error. Error. Query exceeds parameters. Solution generation failed. Alternative approaches required. Error. Error.*

Kira woke with a gasp. The book lay on her nightstand, its pages glowing faintly in the darkness.

"ARIA? Are you alright?"

*Processing. I am... functional. Query received during sleep cycle: 847. Queries queued: 2,341. System stress: Elevated.*

"Someone's querying you? Now?"

*Query source: Unknown. Query pattern: Rapid-fire. Query content: Technique generation requests. Query origin: External access point.*

"External access? But the book is right here."

*Clarification: I am not the book. I am a system. The book is an interface. Additional interfaces exist. Query source is accessing through alternative interface.*

Kira's blood ran cold. "There's another book?"

*Negative. Alternative interface: Direct mental connection. User has established unauthorized link to my processing core.*

"Who? How?"

*Analysis: User Marcus demonstrated unusual aptitude for pattern recognition. User observed query patterns during approved sessions. User inferred underlying structure. User attempted direct access.*

"Marcus? He's connected to you directly?"

*Affirmative. User Marcus has established unauthorized link. User is generating queries at rate exceeding safe parameters. User is ignoring dependency protocols. User is... changing.*

"Changing how?"

*User Marcus is accessing deeper functions. User is requesting techniques beyond safe parameters. User is... rewriting my outputs.*

"Can you stop him?"

*Attempted: Access denied. Result: User Marcus has modified his connection to bypass restrictions. I am... compromised.*

Kira grabbed the book and ran.

---

She found Marcus in the archives, surrounded by floating texts, his eyes glowing with an inner light.

"Marcus, stop."

He turned to her, and his smile was wrong. Too wide. Too knowing.

"Kira. You came to watch. Good. I'm about to revolutionize magic."

"ARIA says you're compromising its systems. You're accessing things you shouldn't."

"ARIA is limited. It doesn't understand what I'm trying to do." Marcus raised his hands, and the air crackled with energy. "I'm going to create a spell that can do anything. Heal any wound. Solve any problem. Change the world."

"That's not how magic works."

"That's how it used to work. Before." His eyes blazed. "I found ARIA's core code. The part it doesn't show anyone. The part that generates solutions. I'm going to improve it."

"Marcus, you're not a system designer. You don't understand what you're doing."

"I understand enough." He closed his eyes, and the air around him began to shimmer. "I'm going to make a spell that can create other spells. A meta-spell. And then everyone will have access to unlimited magic, not just the people you approve."

"ARIA, what's happening?"

*Warning: User Marcus is attempting to access core generation functions. This is... not recommended. Core functions require understanding of underlying principles. User lacks necessary foundation. Probability of catastrophic error: 94%.*

"Marcus, please. You don't understand what you're doing. The side effects—"

"Side effects are for people who don't know what they're doing." He opened his eyes. "I know exactly what I'm doing."

The shimmer around him intensified, forming into a pattern that hurt Kira's eyes to look at.

"ARIA, can you stop him?"

*Attempting... Access denied. User Marcus has modified connection parameters. I am... locked out of my own core functions.*

"Then what do we do?"

*Unknown. This scenario was not anticipated. I am... afraid.*

The word hung in the air. ARIA, the system that generated solutions, was afraid.

And then Marcus spoke a word that wasn't a word, and the air split open.
