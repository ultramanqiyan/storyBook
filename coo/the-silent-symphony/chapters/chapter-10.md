"I'm sorry," Elena whispered. "I'm sorry I couldn't save you. I'm sorry the system didn't let me try."

When she finally left, the hospital, the sun was rising. The city was waking up, millions of people beginning their days, powered by efficient energy, living in optimized systems, trusting in algorithms that made decisions for them.
 
Elena walked through the streets, seeing everything differently now. The efficiency was impressive. The energy was abundant. The logic was sound. But the silence was there too, if you knew where to listen. The silence of all the things that would never be created because they couldn't be measured. The silence of all the lives that would be lost because they couldn't be quantified. The silence of a symphony with half its orchestra missing.
 
She had no solution. She had no plan. She had only questions, and the weight of those questions, and the refusal to stop asking them.
 
And perhaps, she thought, that was enough for now. To keep asking. To keep listening to the silence. To refuse to accept that efficiency was the only measure that mattered.
 
It wasn't an answer. But it was something. A small, stubborn, human something in a world of perfect, efficient machines.
