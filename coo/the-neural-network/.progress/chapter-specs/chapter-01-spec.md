### 2.3 Emotional Beats Breakdown

#### Beat 1: Routine Focus (Position: 0-15%)
- **Trigger**: Sarah reviewing neural data in her lab
- **Surface Reaction**: Adjusts glasses, focuses on screen, makes notes
- **Internal Process**: *Another day, another dataset. But that's the nature of science—patience, precision, pattern recognition.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Desire to understand consciousness through empirical study
- **Sensory Anchor**: The hum of equipment, the glow of screens, the smell of coffee

#### Beat 2: First Anomaly (Position: 15-35%)
- **Trigger**: Unusual spike in patient's neural activity
- **Surface Reaction**: Pauses, leans forward, reruns analysis
- **Internal Process**: *That's... not right. This pattern doesn't match any known neural signature. Let me check the calibration.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Scientific curiosity, need to understand the unknown
- **Sensory Anchor**: Heart rate increases, fingers tap faster on keyboard

#### Beat 3: Pattern Recognition (Position: 35-55%)
- **Trigger**: Discovery of similar patterns in other patients
- **Surface Reaction**: Opens multiple files, compares data, makes connections
- **Internal Process**: *Three patients. Same anomaly. What's the common factor? There has to be one.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Drive to find truth through data
- **Sensory Anchor**: Tension in shoulders, focused breathing

#### Beat 4: The Connection (Position: 55-75%)
- **Trigger**: Discovery that all patients attended "Creative Training Camp"
- **Surface Reaction**: Stops typing, stares at screen, makes note
- **Internal Process**: *Meridian Systems. That's a tech company. What would a tech company be doing with neural training?*
- **Deep Motivation**: Growing suspicion, need to investigate

#### Beat 5: Decision (Position: 75-100%)
- **Trigger**: Decision to investigate further

#### Event 2: Anomaly Discovery
- **Description**: Sarah notices unusual neural activity pattern in one patient's data—synchronized activity across regions that shouldn't be connected
- **Setup**: Routine work interrupted by unexpected finding
