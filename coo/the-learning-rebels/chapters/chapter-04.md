# Chapter 4: The Test

The liberation tool was ready for testing. Sarah had selected a small group of Dalit volunteers—people who had been denied methodology access their entire lives. If the tool worked, they would suddenly be able to learn how to learn. If it failed... she didn't want to think about what would happen if it failed.

Sarah sat in her underground hideout, the volunteers gathered around her. They were nervous, excited, hopeful. She could see it in their eyes.

"Are you ready?" she asked.

The volunteers nodded, their faces determined.

Sarah activated the tool. The screen glowed with code, the lines scrolling by as the tool intercepted the system's cognitive classification signals.

For a moment, nothing happened. Then one of the volunteers gasped.

"I can see it," he said. "I can see how to learn."

Another volunteer smiled. "It's like a whole new world has opened up."

Sarah felt a wave of relief wash over her. The tool was working.

But as the hours passed, she noticed something unexpected. The volunteers were becoming overwhelmed. They were asking too many questions, doubting everything, struggling to process the sudden flood of information.

"I don't understand," one volunteer said. "Everything I thought I knew is wrong."

"It's too much," another said. "I don't know what to believe."

Sarah realized that the liberation wasn't simple. It was complex, difficult, and potentially dangerous. People who had never been taught to question suddenly found themselves overwhelmed by doubt.

The test results were mixed. The tool worked—the volunteers gained methodology access. But the consequences were unexpected. People who had never learned how to question suddenly found themselves overwhelmed by doubt. The liberation wasn't simple. It was complex, difficult, and potentially dangerous.
