# Chapter 9: The Burden

Three days had passed since Sarah had discovered the consequences of liberation. Three days of carrying the knowledge like a stone in her chest, of looking at her tool and seeing not freedom but potential chaos. She had created something that could change the world—and might destroy it.

Sarah sat in her underground hideout, the hum of servers filling the small space. She sipped her coffee, the bitter taste matching her mood. She thought about the simulation results, about how the sudden introduction of methodology access would cause social collapse.

She thought about the volunteers, about how they had struggled with the sudden freedom of methodology access. She thought about the resistance, about how they were ready to fight for cognitive freedom. She thought about the system, about how it was designed to maintain social stability at all costs.

Sarah felt the weight of the decision on her shoulders. If she released the tool, she would be responsible for the social collapse that followed. If she didn't, she would be responsible for the continued oppression of cognitive stratification.

She thought about her father, about how he had made a similar decision when he preserved science. She thought about the consequences he had faced, about how he had lost his career, his reputation, his freedom.

Sarah stood at the window of her apartment, looking out at the city lights. Millions of people, living in cognitive stratification, unable to learn how to learn. She could change that. She could give them methodology access. But the cost might be everything.

There was no right answer. There was only the choice she would have to live with.
