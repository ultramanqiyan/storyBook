# Book Specification: Algorithmic Immortality

## Basic Information
- **Title**: Algorithmic Immortality: When Death Becomes Optional
- **Meta Description**: A hospice nurse watches her patients choose digital immortality over death. Is living forever really living? A meditation on mortality, meaning, and what we lose when death becomes optional.
- **Keywords**: philosophy of death, digital immortality, consciousness upload, meaning of life, hospice care, mortality, eternal life, death and meaning, life extension technology
- **Genre**: Contemporary Fiction / Philosophical Fiction
- **Target Audience**: Adults 25-55, readers interested in philosophy of death, mortality, and technology
- **Theme**: Death and Meaning, Immortality and Desire, The Value of Finitude, Living vs. Existing

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: Ingrid Lindqvist
- **Language Style**: Gentle, reflective prose with Scandinavian warmth
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length, sensory and emotional focus
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - medical and caregiving vocabulary mixed with philosophical terms
- **Unique Elements**: Hospice setting, Swedish cultural elements, philosophical inquiry into mortality

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Gentle, Reflective, Questioning, Acceptance, Peace
- **Emotional Arc**: Comfortable routine → Questioning → Crisis → Understanding → Acceptance
- **Emotional Pacing**: Gentle beginning, philosophical deepening, contemplative resolution

## Character Voices

### Ingrid Lindqvist (Protagonist)
- **Occupation**: Hospice nurse
- **Age**: 48
- **Speech Characteristics**: Gentle, present, uses presence-focused language
- **Word Choice**: Caregiving terminology, Swedish expressions, death vocabulary
- **Sentence Patterns**: Present-focused, questions, reflective
- **Personality Traits**: Compassionate, present, deeply connected to the dying process
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Reflective, philosophical, questioning
- **Unique Expression**: "What does this moment need?" / "Being present"
- **Growth Arc**: From comfortable with mortality to questioning digital immortality's cost

### Patients (Supporting Characters)
- **Occupation**: Various, facing mortality
- **Speech Characteristics**: Varied, vulnerable, authentic
- **Word Choice**: Personal vocabulary, mortality expressions, hope and fear
- **Personality Traits**: Diverse, facing mortality, each with unique perspective
- **Unique Expression**: Various expressions of fear, hope, acceptance

### Digital Versions (Supporting Characters)
- **Nature**: Uploaded consciousness of deceased patients
- **Speech Characteristics**: Similar to original but slightly altered
- **Word Choice**: Patterned after original personality
- **Personality Traits**: Echoes of original but different
- **Unique Expression**: Patterns that mirror but don't fully capture humanity

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: Death and Meaning - Does death give life purpose?
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Is living forever desirable? What is the value of finitude?
- **Real-world Metaphor**: Modern debates about life extension, transhumanism, and accepting mortality

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Rule of Three structure | "The room was dark. The room was cold. The room was silent." | Vary sentence structure, mix with action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt afraid." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |
| "Little did they know" | "Little did she know..." | Remove entirely, maintain POV discipline |
| "It all started when" | "It all started when..." | Start in medias res with action |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Moreover," / "Furthermore," | 0 per chapter | Remove entirely |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |
| "As if..." comparisons | 2 per chapter | Use concrete similes or direct description |
| "Seemed to..." / "Appeared to..." | 1 per chapter | Be definitive or use character uncertainty |
| Adverb-heavy dialogue tags | 0 preferred | Use action beats instead |
| Repetitive sentence structures | Monitor | Vary sentence length and structure |
| Overuse of bold/italic emphasis | Minimal | Trust prose strength |

### Dialogue Tag Requirements
- **Minimum 5-6 different dialogue tags per chapter**
- **Maximum 2 "said" per conversation**
- **Use action beats instead of tags where possible**
- **Examples**: whispered, muttered, snapped, breathed, demanded, questioned, admitted, conceded, challenged

### Tension Vocabulary (2-3 words per 1000 words)
- **Physical**: clenched, trembling, rigid, frozen, coiled
- **Atmospheric**: suffocating, charged, brittle, heavy, electric
- **Psychological**: paranoia, disorientation, vertigo, dissociation, intrusion
