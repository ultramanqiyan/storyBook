## 3. Opening
Ingrid couldn't stop thinking about digital Sarah. If death was optional now, what did that mean for her work? What did it mean for life? She'd spent twenty years helping people die well. But if death could be avoided, was there still such a thing as dying well?

---

## 4. Ending
Ingrid realized that digital immortality wasn't really immortality. It was something else - a continuation, a preservation, a kind of afterlife that existed in servers rather than heaven. The question wasn't whether it was real. The question was whether it was meaningful. And that was a question Ingrid couldn't answer.
