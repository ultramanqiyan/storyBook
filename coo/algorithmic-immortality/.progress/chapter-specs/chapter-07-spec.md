## 3. Opening
Ingrid spent her days off reading philosophy. Heidegger on being-toward-death. Epicurus on why death is nothing to us. The Stoics on accepting mortality. She was looking for an answer to the question that haunted her: if death could be avoided, did life still have meaning?

---

## 4. Ending
Ingrid found an answer, or something close to one. Death gave life its shape - its urgency, its preciousness, its meaning. Without death, life might just go on forever, shapeless and endless. But with digital immortality, death wasn't really gone. It had just changed form. And maybe that was okay.
