## 3. Opening
Mr. Chen was the first patient Ingrid had cared for who chose digital immortality. "I'm not ready to go," he told her. "My granddaughter is having a baby next year. I want to see her grow up." The technology was new, expensive, and controversial. But for those who could afford it, death had become optional.

---

## 4. Ending
Ingrid watched Mr. Chen's family say goodbye - not to death, but to his physical form. In a week, he would "wake up" in a digital environment, his consciousness preserved. It wasn't death. But it wasn't life either. It was something new. And Ingrid wasn't sure if it was beautiful or terrifying.
