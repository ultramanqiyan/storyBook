## 3. Opening
Margaret was eighty-three, dying of heart failure, and she had no interest in digital immortality. "I've lived a good life," she told Ingrid. "I'm ready to go. Really go." Ingrid had heard this before. But now, with digital immortality as an option, choosing actual death felt different. It felt like a statement.

---

## 4. Ending
Margaret died peacefully, surrounded by family, her story complete. Ingrid sat with her until the end, honoring her choice. Not everyone wanted to live forever. Not everyone wanted to be preserved in code. Some people just wanted to finish their story and let go. And that was okay too.
