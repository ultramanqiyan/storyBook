## 3. Opening
Ingrid Lindqvist sat beside the dying woman, holding her hand the way she had held hundreds of hands before. This was her work - not to cure, but to accompany. Not to fight death, but to help people meet it with dignity. After twenty years as a hospice nurse, she knew that death wasn't the enemy. It was simply the end of a story.

---

## 4. Ending
Ingrid drove home through the Minneapolis evening, thinking about Mrs. Patterson. She'd died peacefully, surrounded by family. It was a good death - the kind Ingrid had learned to appreciate. But something was changing. More and more patients were talking about digital immortality. More and more were choosing to upload rather than die. And Ingrid wasn't sure how she felt about that.
