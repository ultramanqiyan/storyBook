# Book Specification: The Ghost Writer's Thesis

## Basic Information
- **Title**: The Ghost Writer's Thesis: An Academic Mystery of Borrowed Minds
- **Meta Description**: When PhD candidate Elena Martinez discovers her "original" research questions were suggested by an AI, she must confront a terrifying truth: she's forgotten how to be curious.
- **Keywords**: academic integrity, AI writing, original research, PhD thesis, academic AI, research methodology, scholarly writing
- **Genre**: Academic Thriller / Psychological Drama / Near-Future Fiction
- **Target Audience**: 22-40 years, academics, graduate students, researchers, readers interested in AI ethics and education
- **Theme**: Originality vs AI, The Death of Curiosity, Academic Integrity in the AI Age

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: First-person
- **POV Character(s)**: Elena Martinez
- **Language Style**: Intellectual, increasingly uncertain, ultimately hopeful; academic precision early, more questioning later
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Complex sentences early; simpler, questioning sentences later
- **Vocabulary Level**: Advanced (academic terminology) mixed with accessible prose
- **Unique Elements**: Research notes, academic conference scenes, library atmosphere; linear timeline with flashbacks

## World Building
- **Time Period**: Near-future (5-10 years from present)
- **Primary Location**: Prestigious research university
- **Environment**: Academic halls, libraries, conference venues
- **Social Context**: AI research tools normalized; "original thinking" seen as inefficient
- **Key Technologies**: SCHOLAR-AI research assistant, automated literature review, AI-generated research questions
- **Social Structure**: Academics who question AI dependency seen as "gatekeepers" or "romantics"
- **World Rules**: SCHOLAR-AI (Standard research tool that suggests questions, methods, and hypotheses), The Gap (Researchers who rely on AI lose ability to generate original questions), The Archive (Hidden collection of pre-AI research showing what curiosity looked like)

## Emotional Depth Design

### Three-Layer Emotional Model (Required for Every Major Scene)
1. **Surface Reaction** - External behavior (e.g., "I stared at my thesis document, cursor blinking")
2. **Internal Activity** - Thought process (e.g., "*These questions aren't mine. They never were.*")
3. **Deep Motivation** - Underlying fears (e.g., "The terror that I had become a vessel for AI-generated thoughts")

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt afraid." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |
