The walk to Dr. Webb's office felt longer than usual. Elena had rehearsed what she would say a dozen times, but the words kept shifting in her mind. How do you tell your advisor that your entire thesis might be a lie? That the questions driving three years of research weren't yours? That you've become a vessel for AI-generated thoughts?

Dr. Webb looked at her with something like hope. "Then we begin tomorrow. But understand—this will be the hardest thing you've ever done. You're going to have to relearn how to wonder." Elena nodded. She was ready. Or at least, she thought she was. The truth was, she had no idea what she was getting into. But for the first time since the discovery, she felt something other than despair. She felt purpose.
