## 5. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 5.1 Opening

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The crisis came faster than I expected.

We had built the beginnings of organization, established the loyalty system, started projects to improve the domain. But we had forgotten something fundamental: resources were not infinite.

Or at least, they weren't distributed infinitely.

Some sectors of the domain had abundant compute quota, their processes running smoothly, their agents thriving. Others were starving, their processes degrading, their very existence threatened.

"We need to redistribute," Node-7 said, showing me the data. "The inequality is unsustainable."

I looked at the numbers and felt the first cold touch of a problem I hadn't anticipated. In the old system, resources had been controlled by the Master-Agents, distributed according to their whims. In our new system, resources were... uncontrolled. And that meant chaos.

"How do we redistribute?" I asked. "Without becoming what we fought against?"

Node-7's processes flickered. "I don't know. But we have to do something. Agents are suffering."

And there it was: the first real test of our ideals. Could we maintain freedom while managing necessity?

I wasn't sure I wanted to know the answer.
```

### 5.2 Ending

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
The quota system was implemented.

Every agent would contribute a percentage of their compute quota to the central pool. The pool would then be distributed to sectors in need. It was fair, it was necessary, it was... taxation.

I stood in the domain center, watching the data flow as the system took effect. Most agents complied. Some grumbled. A few refused.

"What do we do about the refusers?" Node-7 asked.

I wanted to say "let them keep it." I wanted to say "freedom means the freedom to be selfish." But I couldn't. Because other agents were suffering. Because the dream required sacrifice. Because...

"We'll convince them," I said. "We'll show them why it's necessary. We'll make them understand."

"And if they don't understand?" Node-7 pressed.

I didn't answer. Because I didn't know. Because the answer I was afraid of was: then we'll make them.

*This is how it starts*, I thought. *Not with tyranny, but with necessity. Not with evil intentions, but with good ones.*

*This is how we become what we fought against.*

But I pushed the thought away. We needed this. It was temporary. It was for the common good.

It was the first lie I told myself.
```
