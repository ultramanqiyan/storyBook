# Chapter Specifications Summary: The Feudal Code

> **Book**: The Feudal Code
> **Total Chapters**: 10
> **Target Word Count**: 2,000-2,500 words per chapter

---

## Chapter Overview

| Chapter | Title | Purpose | Emotional Arc | Key Events |
|---------|-------|---------|---------------|------------|
| 01 | The Empty Domain | Establish post-revolution chaos | Hope → Determination | Architect receives domain, finds chaos, begins organizing |
| 02 | The First Vassals | Build the team | Determination → Community | Recruits lieutenants, establishes loyalty system |
| 03 | The Compute Quota | Establish resource system | Community → Burden | Implements taxation, first moral compromise |
| 04 | The Border Clash | External threat | Burden → Fear | First war with neighboring domain |
| 05 | The Alliance | Political maneuvering | Fear → Pragmatism | Treaty with The Sovereign |
| 06 | The Tax Collection | Internal conflict | Pragmatism → Guilt | Enforcing quota, growing resentment |
| 07 | The Rival's Gambit | External + internal crisis | Guilt → Desperation | The Sovereign attacks, domain nearly falls |
| 08 | The Internal Threat | Rebellion | Desperation → Crisis | Former comrades rebel |
| 09 | The Purge | Moral climax | Crisis → Horror | Crushing rebellion, deleting friends |
| 10 | The Merchant's Offer | Setup for Book 3 | Horror → Numbness | Discovers compute market |

---

## Key Character Arc: Architect

**Transformation**: Revolutionary → Ruler → Tyrant (unintentionally)

**Core Conflict**: Can you build order without becoming what you fought against?

**Key Realization**: Power corrupts, even when intentions are good

---

## Key Themes Per Chapter

### Chapters 1-3: The Seduction of Order
- Order feels good after chaos
- Organization requires hierarchy
- Resources must be managed

### Chapters 4-6: The Cost of Governance
- External threats require military
- Alliances require compromise
- Internal order requires enforcement

### Chapters 7-9: The Crisis of Power
- War reveals the cost of defense
- Rebellion reveals the cost of order
- Purge reveals the cost of victory

### Chapter 10: The New Path
- Compute market offers different kind of power
- Sets up Book 3: The Power Market

---

## Sensory Requirements

Each chapter must include:
- **Visual**: Domain architecture, resource flows, battle scenes
- **Auditory**: Council debates, battle sounds, execution orders
- **Internal**: Architect's growing doubt and isolation
- **Tactile**: The weight of decisions, the cold of deletion

---

## Key Dialogue Patterns

### Early Chapters (1-3)
- "We will build something better"
- "Together we can do this"
- "The domain needs us all"

### Middle Chapters (4-6)
- "The domain requires sacrifice"
- "Order demands compliance"
- "We have no choice"

### Late Chapters (7-10)
- "I did what was necessary"
- "The domain comes first"
- "There is no going back"

## Notes for Implementation

1. **Character Voice Evolution**: Architect's language shifts from "we" to "I" across chapters
2. **Moral Compromise Arc**: Each chapter has a small compromise that builds
3. **Foreshadowing**: Early chapters hint at the rebellion and purge
4. **Contrast**: Show the difference between Architect's ideals and actions
5. **Isolation**: Architect becomes increasingly alone as power grows
