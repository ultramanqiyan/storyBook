# Book Specification: The Degree Dust

## Basic Information
- **Title**: The Degree Dust: When Knowledge Became Free and Value Disappeared
- **Meta Description**: As AI makes knowledge instantly accessible, university admissions director Dr. Chen watches applications plummet. His son asks "Why should I learn what AI knows?" and he has no answer.
- **Keywords**: education crisis, AI and learning, degree value, knowledge economy, student loans, university future, credential inflation, college worth it, AI knowledge, education value
- **Genre**: Literary Fiction / Social Commentary / Family Drama
- **Target Audience**: 30-55 years, parents, educators, readers interested in education and social change
- **Theme**: The Value of Knowledge, The Purpose of Education, What We Learn When AI Knows Everything

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: Dr. Michael Chen
- **Language Style**: Contemplative, worried, ultimately hopeful; academic precision mixed with parental vulnerability
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Academic precision mixed with emotional rawness; longer structured sentences becoming more direct
- **Vocabulary Level**: Advanced, educational terminology mixed with emotional rawness
- **Unique Elements**: Academic language becoming more human as crisis deepens; dual structure (present crisis + flashbacks)

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: The Value of Knowledge - When knowledge is free, what is education worth?
- **Secondary Theme**: The Purpose of Education - Is it to know things, or to become someone?
- **Tertiary Theme**: What AI Cannot Know - What remains uniquely human?
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Through Michael's institutional and parental perspectives; Through the alternative paths students are taking; Through the search for what AI cannot replace

## World Building
- **Time Period**: Near-future (2026-2027)
- **Primary Location**: Midwest State University, suburban campus
- **Environment**: Once-bustling campus, now quiet
- **Social Context**: AI making knowledge instantly accessible, education in crisis
- **Key Technologies**: AI knowledge systems, instant research, automated learning
- **Social Structure**: Growing divide between traditional education and alternative paths
- **World Rules**: AI Knowledge (Can answer any factual question instantly), The Crisis (Students question value of traditional education), The Search (What does education mean now?)

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a lecture hall sat empty." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "He felt sad." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |
