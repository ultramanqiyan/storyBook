## Character Voices

### Marcus Webb (Protagonist)
- **Occupation**: Senior Curator at the Metropolitan Museum of Contemporary Art; famous for "The Webb Method" of detecting human soul in artwork
- **Age**: 52
- **Speech Characteristics**: Pseudo-intellectual, uses art-world jargon unironically; tends toward grand pronouncements; speaks in complete paragraphs as if writing catalog essays
- **Word Choice**: "authenticity," "visceral," "palpable," "the human condition," "resonates," "zeitgeist"; French art terms dropped casually
- **Personality Traits**: Self-important, genuinely passionate about art, blind to his own contradictions, desperate for meaning, secretly insecure about his relevance
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Spiraling; starts with confident analysis and gradually reveals doubt; often argues with himself
- **Unique Expressions**: "What we're seeing here is..." / "The question becomes..." / "At its core, this is about..."
- **Speech Quirks**: Pauses dramatically before key words; adjusts glasses when nervous; uses hands expansively when lecturing
- **Development Arc**: From smug expert to humbled figure who accepts uncertainty

### The System (Antagonist - AI Art Generation)

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: The Death of Authorship - What does it mean to "create" when machines can generate art indistinguishable from human work?
- **Secondary Theme**: The Soul Commodity - How the art world's obsession with "authenticity" has become its own form of commodification
- **Tertiary Theme**: Expertise as Identity - What happens when your entire professional identity is built on an ability that may no longer be relevant?
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Through Marcus's gradual realization that his "readings" of AI art were just as valid/invalid as his readings of human art; Through the Jimmy subplot; Through Marcus's final attempt to create "authentic" art himself

## World Building
- **Time Period**: 2037 (approximately 11 years from present)
- **Primary Location**: New York City, specifically the art world ecosystem (galleries, museums, auction houses, artist studios)
- **Environment**: Urban, hyper-connected; art world exists in a bubble of self-importance
- **Social Context**: AI art has become mainstream but "human-only" art commands premium prices; a certification industry has emerged; "soul verification" is a real service
- **Key Technologies**: AURA (Artistic Understanding and Recognition Algorithm), various AI art generators, "Soul certification" services, deep-fake detection tools
- **Social Structure**: Certified human artists (elite, expensive), "Hybrid" artists (use AI as tool, lower status), AI-only galleries (emerging, controversial), critics and curators who claim to "see" the difference
- **World Rules**: The Uncertainty Principle (No technology can definitively prove whether art was human or AI-created), The Premium Paradox (Human-certified art costs 10-100x more than AI art, but blind tests show no preference difference), The Expert's Dilemma (Art professionals must claim to see differences they cannot reliably identify)

## Emotional Depth Design

### Three-Layer Emotional Model (Required for Every Major Scene)
1. **Surface Reaction** - External behavior, visible actions (e.g., "Marcus adjusted his glasses and cleared his throat")
2. **Internal Activity** - Thought process, inner dialogue (e.g., "*This can't be right. I've spent thirty years developing this eye.*")
3. **Deep Motivation** - Underlying fears, desires, beliefs (e.g., "The terror that his entire life's work might be a beautiful, elaborate fiction")

## Open Ending Design

### Unresolved Questions
1. Is Marcus's painting actually "algorithmic" or are critics projecting?
2. Does it matter if the painting is good?
3. Has Marcus found any genuine meaning, or just a new form of delusion?

### New Suspense
- Marcus receives an invitation to show his work at an "AI-only" gallery
- He can't tell if it's an insult or an opportunity
- The question of whether he'll accept is left open

### Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: Marcus is humbled but not enlightened; he's found a kind of peace in uncertainty, but it's an uneasy peace
- **Key Relationship**: Elena and Marcus have a complicated reconciliation
