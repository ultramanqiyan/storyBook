### 3.2 Event Descriptions

#### Event 1: Portfolio Review
- **Description**: Marcus reviews portfolios, applying his method
- **Setup**: The mountain of submissions for "Humanity's Last Breath"
- **Execution**: Show his process - intuitive, confident, possibly arbitrary
- **Consequence**: He rejects many, finds few candidates
- **Connection to Theme**: The arbitrariness of "authenticity"

#### Event 2: Elena's Challenge
- **Description**: Elena confronts Marcus at a gallery opening
- **Setup**: She's become a successful podcaster, skeptical of the whole industry
- **Execution**: Sharp dialogue, Marcus defensive, she plants seeds of doubt
- **Consequence**: Marcus is rattled but won't admit it
- **Connection to Theme**: The voice of doubt he can't silence

#### Event 3: Discovery of Jimmy
- **Description**: Marcus finds Jimmy Okonkwo's portfolio
- **Setup**: Among hundreds of submissions, one stands out
- **Execution**: Marcus feels the "spark" - raw, authentic, human
- **Consequence**: He's excited, vindicated
- **Connection to Theme**: The search for authenticity

#### Event 4: Meeting Jimmy
- **Description**: Marcus visits Jimmy's studio
- **Setup**: Young artist, supposedly self-taught, raw talent
- **Execution**: Jimmy is evasive about his process; Marcus ignores the signs
- **Consequence**: Marcus commits to featuring Jimmy
- **Connection to Theme**: Willful blindness

#### Event 5: Commitment
- **Description**: Marcus officially selects Jimmy for the exhibition
- **Setup**: The centerpiece of "Humanity's Last Breath"
- **Execution**: Professional announcement, internal unease
- **Consequence**: The die is cast
- **Connection to Theme**: The cost of denial

## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The portfolios arrived in waves. First the emails - hundreds of them, each with attachments that crashed his server twice. Then the physical submissions, boxes upon boxes that filled his office like a paper flood. Marcus had requested "the most authentic human art" for "Humanity's Last Breath," and the art world had delivered. Or at least, they'd delivered what they thought he wanted to see. He picked up the first portfolio and began.
```

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
Marcus signed the final paperwork and sat back. Jimmy Okonkwo would be the centerpiece of "Humanity's Last Breath." A young, self-taught artist with raw talent and authentic vision. Exactly what the exhibition needed. Exactly what Marcus needed. He ignored the small voice that asked why Jimmy had no early work, why his process was so vague, why the studio had smelled faintly of something that wasn't paint. Tomorrow, he would visit the verification lab. Tomorrow, he would have proof. Tonight, he would believe.
```
