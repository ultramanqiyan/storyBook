## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening
```
Two weeks after his resignation, Marcus bought paints. He hadn't painted since art school - thirty years of looking at other people's work, analyzing it, judging it, but never making anything himself. Now, standing in the art supply store, he felt like an imposter. Which, he supposed, was appropriate.
```

### 6.2 Ending
```
He submitted the painting to a small gallery - not the Met, not anything prestigious, just a neighborhood place that accepted open submissions. The email felt like a confession. "Untitled, by Marcus Webb." No credentials. No reputation. Just a painting. He waited for a response.
```
