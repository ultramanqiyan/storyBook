## 5. Key Scene: Dr. Chen's Office

**Basic Information**
- **Location**: Dr. Sarah Chen's office, verification lab
- **Time**: Afternoon
- **Characters Present**: Marcus Webb, Dr. Sarah Chen
- **Atmosphere**: Clinical, honest, uncomfortable

**Narrative Function**
- **Purpose**: Introduce Dr. Chen; deliver the uncomfortable truth
- **Information Revealed**: Verification is essentially faith-based
- **Character Development**: Marcus's denial; Dr. Chen's intellectual honesty

**Key Dialogue**:
```
"Dr. Chen, your machines are supposed to detect AI-generated art."
"They detect patterns, Mr. Webb. Patterns that suggest AI involvement. But patterns can be created by humans too. And AI can learn to avoid the patterns we detect."
"So the verification is..."
"An educated guess. A professional opinion. Not so different from your Webb Method, I imagine."
```

## 7. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 7.1 Opening
```
The Authenticity Verification Laboratory occupied the top floor of a glass tower in Midtown. Marcus had passed it a hundred times without noticing - just another anonymous corporate facade. But inside, it was all white walls and humming machines and people in lab coats who looked like they knew things he didn't. He hated it already.
```

### 7.2 Ending
```
Outside, the city was gray and cold. Marcus walked toward the subway, Dr. Chen's words echoing in his head. "Not so different from your Webb Method." He would prove her wrong. He would prove himself right. The exhibition would be a success, Jimmy would be vindicated, and all of this - the labs, the machines, the doubt - would be forgotten. He hoped.
```
