# Chapter 01: The Darkness I Knew

Marcus had never seen a photograph, but he had taken thousands. He knew light by its warmth, shadow by its absence, and beauty by the way people's voices changed when they looked at his work.

First-time clients were always nervous. They didn't understand how a blind man could take photographs, and they weren't sure they trusted the process.

"Ms. Chen," Marcus said, rising from his chair and extending his hand. "Welcome. Please, make yourself comfortable."

"Thank you." Her voice was higher than he had expected, with a slight accent he couldn't quite place. "I've heard a lot about your work. My sister had you photograph her wedding last year."

He didn't know. He might never know. But the question was there, persistent, insistent, refusing to be ignored.

The simulation ended an hour later. Marcus sat in the chair, his mind still reeling from what he had experienced. He had seen his studio, his photographs, his sister's face. He had seen the world for the first time, constructed by a machine, filtered through algorithms and patterns.

And he wasn't sure how he felt about it.

"What do you think?" Dr. Reyes asked, her voice gentle.
