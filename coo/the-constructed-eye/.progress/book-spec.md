## 2. Narrative Style

### 2.1 Perspective
- **Point of View**: First-person
- **POV Character(s)**: Marcus Webb
- **POV Consistency**: Single, consistent throughout

### 2.2 Narrative Structure
- **Dual Structure**: Yes
- **Structure Type**: Present timeline + Internal perceptual journey
- **Interweaving Method**: External events trigger reflections on perception; memories of his pre-implant "imagined" world emerge

### 2.3 Language Style
- **Overall Tone**: Wonder, doubt, philosophical, increasingly urgent
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Descriptive when describing the visual world, questioning when reflecting on its nature
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate to advanced (perceptual psychology, art terminology, emotional vocabulary)
- **Unique Stylistic Elements**: Contrast between the "optimized" visual world and the "real" gray world; descriptions of color as both revelation and question

## 5. Thematic Depth

### 5.1 Core Themes
1. **Perception as Construction**: Do we see the world as it is, or as we construct it?
2. **The Comfort of Illusion**: Is a beautiful lie better than an ugly truth?
3. **The Nature of Reality**: If we can never know "objective" reality, does it matter?

### 5.2 Philosophical Exploration
| Question | Exploration Approach |
|----------|---------------------|
| Is perception discovery or construction? | Marcus's experience of IRIS's "optimizations" |
| Can we choose between truth and beauty? | The option to disable optimizations |
| What is "real" perception? | The impossibility of knowing what others see |
