## 4. Opening

The client was a collector who had worked with Sofia for years. He trusted her judgment, her eye, her craft. When Sofia showed him both restorations - hers and the AI's - she expected him to appreciate the human touch, the subtle imperfections that marked her work as authentic. Instead, he studied both pieces for a long moment and said, "The AI one. That's the one I want."

---

## 5. Ending

Sofia stood in her workshop after the client left, looking at her own restoration. The one the client had rejected. It was good work - she knew that. But the AI's work had been better. More accurate. More faithful to the original. And the client had chosen it. What did that mean for her craft? What did it mean for the idea of authenticity?
