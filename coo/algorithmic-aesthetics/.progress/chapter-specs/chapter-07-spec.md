## 4. Opening

Sofia didn't go to her workshop for three days. For the first time in forty years, she couldn't face the work. What was the point? If AI could do it better, if she couldn't even tell the difference, then what value did her craft have? She'd spent her life pursuing authenticity, and now she wasn't sure what that meant.

---

## 5. Ending

On the fourth day, Sofia returned to her workshop. She walked past the restored pieces, past the AI analysis, and stopped at an unrestored chair in the corner. It was badly damaged, almost beyond repair. The AI had recommended against restoring it - too little original material remained. But Sofia had kept it anyway. She sat down and looked at it, really looked at it, for the first time in months. And she saw something the AI hadn't seen: the story in the damage.
