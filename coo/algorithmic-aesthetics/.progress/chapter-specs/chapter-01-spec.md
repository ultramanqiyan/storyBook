## 4. Opening

Sofia Reyes ran her hand along the arm of the Victorian chair, feeling the grain of the wood beneath her fingertips. This was what forty years of craft had taught her: wood had a memory, a history, a story that could be read if you knew how to listen. The chair had been damaged by water, its finish ruined, one leg broken. But beneath the damage, Sofia could see what it had been - and what it could be again.

---

## 5. Ending

Sofia stepped back from the completed restoration, the way she always did. The chair looked as it had a hundred years ago - not new, but whole. Not perfect, but authentic. This was her craft: not to make things new, but to help them remember what they had been. Tomorrow, she would start another piece. And the day after that, another. This was her life, and she loved it.
