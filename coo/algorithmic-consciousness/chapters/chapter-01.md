Rosa shook her head. "That's what you thought. I asked what you felt. Where was the nervousness? Was it in your stomach? Your chest? Your throat? Did your hands shake? Did your heart race? Did you feel cold? Hot?"

Marcus frowned, his brow furrowing as he considered the question. "My stomach," he said finally. "It was... tight. Like a knot. And my hands were sweating."

"Good." Rosa stepped closer to him. "That's where emotion lives, Marcus. Not in the word 'nervous.' In the body. The body doesn't lie. It doesn't know how to perform. It only knows how to respond."
