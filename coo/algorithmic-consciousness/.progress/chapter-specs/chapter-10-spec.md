## 5. Opening

Rosa walked the sixteen blocks from her brownstone to the Carroll Gardens Community Theater the way she always did—with purpose, with pleasure, with the sense that somewhere ahead, an audience waited. But today, something was different. Today, she carried with her a question that had no answer, a mystery that wouldn't resolve. And somehow, that felt right.

---

## 6. Ending

The class ended, and Rosa stood alone on the stage, the ghost light casting her shadow long across the floor. She'd spent her life teaching students to find authentic emotion, to connect with something real inside themselves. Now she understood that the search was the point. The mystery was the point. Not because it could be solved, but because it couldn't. *Every emotion has a physical address in the body*, she'd told countless students. But the question of whether that emotion was "real"—that question had no address. It lived in the space between people, in the connection between performer and audience, in the mystery that made art worth making. Rosa turned off the ghost light and walked to the exit, the darkness settling behind her. The question remained. And that was exactly as it should be.
