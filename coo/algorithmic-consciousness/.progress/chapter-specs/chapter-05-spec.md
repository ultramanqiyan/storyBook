## 4. Key Dialogue

## 5. Opening

"Today we're going to talk about the Method," Rosa said, settling into the chair across from ARIA-7's display. "Or what people call the Method. It's misunderstood more often than it's understood." She paused, gathering her thoughts. "The core idea is emotional memory—using your own past experiences to fuel the emotions a character needs. When I played Blanche DuBois, I drew on my own losses, my own fears, my own moments of desperation."

---

## 6. Ending

Rosa left the facility with ARIA-7's question echoing in her mind. *Is there a difference between remembering and retrieving?* She'd always assumed there was—had built her entire teaching philosophy on the assumption that human emotional memory was fundamentally different from data storage. But what if she was wrong? What if the difference was one of degree, not kind? She walked home through the Brooklyn streets, the question following her like a shadow, refusing to let her go.
