## 5. Opening

Rosa had performed it herself, decades ago, and had taught it to countless students. ## 6. Ending

"It was technically perfect," Rosa said to David afterward, her voice carefully neutral. "But I need to think about what I'm seeing." David nodded, his expression eager. "That's exactly what we need from you, Ms. Moretti. Your expertise, your judgment." Rosa walked to the exit, her mind racing. The performance had been good. Better than good. But something had nagged at her throughout, a question she couldn't quite articulate. Was it that ARIA-7's performance was too perfect? Too controlled? Or was it something else—something about the nature of performance itself that she'd never had to examine before?
