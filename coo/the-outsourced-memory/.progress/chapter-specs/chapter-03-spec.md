### 2.3 Emotional Beats Breakdown

#### Beat 1: New Morning Routine (Position: 0-20%)
- **Trigger**: Mike's morning with AI assistance
- **Surface Reaction**: Efficient preparation, checking AI recommendations
- **Internal Process**: *This is so much easier. Why did I resist?*
- **Deep Motivation**: Relief, validation of choice
- **Sensory Anchor**: Phone as first reach, not coffee

#### Beat 2: Efficient Day (Position: 20-40%)
- **Trigger**: Successful day with AI assistance
- **Surface Reaction**: Multiple showings, all go smoothly
- **Internal Process**: *Three showings, three matches. I used to spend days on this.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Pride in efficiency, ignoring what's missing
- **Sensory Anchor**: Absence of walking, absence of physical notes

#### Beat 3: Susan's Concern (Position: 40-60%)
- **Trigger**: Susan notices Mike is different
- **Surface Reaction**: Defensive, dismissive
- **Internal Process**: *She doesn't understand. This is progress.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Fear of being questioned, denial
- **Sensory Anchor**: Susan's worried eyes, his own defensiveness

#### Beat 4: Free Weekend (Position: 60-80%)
- **Trigger**: Mike has weekend free for first time in years
- **Surface Reaction**: Relaxing, enjoying time with Susan
- **Internal Process**: *I should have done this years ago.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Reward for efficiency, ignoring trade-off
- **Sensory Anchor**: Absence of neighborhood walks

#### Beat 5: Something Missing (Position: 80-100%)
- **Trigger**: Quiet moment, Mike feels subtle emptiness
- **Surface Reaction**: Brushes it off
- **Internal Process**: *What am I forgetting? No, nothing. Everything's fine.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Denial, first whisper of loss
- **Sensory Anchor**: Phantom sensation of walking, quickly dismissed

## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Action/Internal - New routine

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Mike reached for his phone before he reached for the coffee. Three weeks ago, that would have felt wrong. Now it felt like breathing. The HomeMatch app opened automatically, displaying his morning briefing: four showings scheduled, all AI-matched, all predicted to close within the week. He scrolled through the property summaries, noting the key points the AI had highlighted for each client. No need to walk the houses first. No need to build his own mental map. The data was all there, clean and organized.
```

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Type**: Internal - Subtle loss

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
He stood at the window, looking out at the neighborhood he used to know like the back of his hand. The streets were familiar, but distant now - something he saw from a window, not something he walked. A phantom sensation tugged at his feet, the urge to feel the pavement, to read the houses with his body instead of his phone. He ignored it. Monday would bring more matches, more efficiency, more success. He turned away from the window and picked up his phone. A notification glowed: new client, new match, 94% confidence. He smiled and opened it.
```
