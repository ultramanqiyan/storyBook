# Book Specification: The Hollow Heart

## Basic Information
- **Title**: The Hollow Heart: A Transhumanist Psychological Drama
- **Meta Description**: When Kai Zhang undergoes sensory enhancement, he gains superhuman perception but loses his emotional capacity. A haunting exploration of what it means to be human when perfection costs your soul.
- **Keywords**: transhumanism, sensory enhancement, emotional loss, identity crisis, what makes us human, perfection cost, psychological drama
- **Genre**: Science Fiction / Psychological Drama / Philosophical Fiction
- **Target Audience**: 18-35 years, readers interested in transhumanism, identity, and what makes us human
- **Theme**: Identity and Self, Emotion vs Enhancement, The Cost of Perfection, Human Connection

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: First-person
- **POV Character(s)**: Kai Zhang (primary)
- **Language Style**: Intimate, introspective, increasingly desperate; short, fragmented when processing emotions; longer, flowing when describing sensory experiences
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Contrast between hyper-detailed sensory descriptions and hollow emotional experiences
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate to advanced (technical terms for enhancement, emotional vocabulary for internal struggle)
- **Unique Elements**: Medical/technical terminology mixed with poetic longing; enhanced perception vs emotional emptiness contrast

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Wonder, Confusion, Loss, Longing, Desperation, Acceptance
- **Emotional Arc**: Wonder and disorientation → Discovery and confusion → Loss and longing → Desperation and searching → Acceptance and uncertainty
- **Emotional Pacing**: Setup (chapters 1-2) → Rising (chapters 3-5) → Climax (chapters 6-8) → Resolution (chapters 9-10)

## Character Voices

### Kai Zhang (Protagonist)
- **Occupation**: Former musician, now "Enhanced" citizen
- **Age**: 28 (at transformation), 30 (story present)
- **Speech Characteristics**: Careful, precise, struggling to express emotional concepts
- **Word Choice**: Technical sensory terms mixed with attempts at emotional vocabulary
- **Sentence Patterns**: Short sentences when confused; long descriptive passages when experiencing sensory input
- **Personality Traits**: Intelligent, observant, increasingly desperate, deeply conflicted
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Rich sensory detail contrasted with emotional emptiness; frequent questioning
- **Unique Expression**: "I can see/hear/feel everything, but..."
- **Speech Quirks**: Pauses when trying to describe emotions, uses analogies from music

### Dr. Sarah Chen (Supporting Character)
- **Occupation**: Lead scientist at the Enhancement Institute
- **Age**: 52
- **Speech Characteristics**: Clinical, reassuring, avoiding emotional topics
- **Word Choice**: Medical terminology, progress metrics, optimization language
- **Sentence Patterns**: Clear, direct statements with technical precision
- **Personality Traits**: Genuinely believes in the Enhancement, uncomfortable with emotional questions

### Maya (Supporting Character)
- **Occupation**: Musician (violinist)
- **Age**: 29
- **Speech Characteristics**: Warm, emotional, increasingly distant
- **Word Choice**: Musical metaphors, emotional vocabulary, personal references
- **Sentence Patterns**: Varied, flowing, expressive
- **Personality Traits**: Creative, empathetic, struggling to connect with transformed Kai

### Dr. Marcus Webb (Supporting Character)
- **Occupation**: Former Enhancement Institute researcher, now helps Enhanced individuals reverse the process
- **Age**: 60s
- **Speech Characteristics**: Cautious, empathetic, haunted by his own experience
- **Word Choice**: Recovery, reversal, soul, humanity, cost
- **Sentence Patterns**: Personal anecdotes mixed with technical knowledge
- **Personality Traits**: Former true believer who discovered the truth about emotional loss, now dedicated to helping others find their way back

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: Technology and Nature's Intersection
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Can technology heal nature?
- **Real-world Metaphor**: Environmental conservation through technology

## Emotional Depth Design

### Three-Layer Emotional Model (Required for Every Major Scene)
1. **Surface Reaction** - External behavior (e.g., "I nodded, processing her words")
2. **Internal Activity** - Thought process (e.g., *She's crying. I should feel something. I don't.*)
3. **Deep Motivation** - Why this reaction (e.g., The terror that I might never feel again, that I've traded my soul for sharper eyes)

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Rule of Three structure | "The room was dark. The room was cold. The room was silent." | Vary sentence structure, mix with action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt afraid." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |
| "Little did they know" | "Little did she know..." | Remove entirely, maintain POV discipline |
| "It all started when" | "It all started when..." | Start in medias res with action |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Moreover," / "Furthermore," | 0 per chapter | Remove entirely |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |
| "As if..." comparisons | 2 per chapter | Use concrete similes or direct description |
| "Seemed to..." / "Appeared to..." | 1 per chapter | Be definitive or use character uncertainty |
| Adverb-heavy dialogue tags | 0 preferred | Use action beats instead |
| Repetitive sentence structures | Monitor | Vary sentence length and structure |
| Overuse of bold/italic emphasis | Minimal | Trust prose strength |

### Dialogue Tag Requirements
- **Minimum 5-6 different dialogue tags per chapter**
- **Maximum 2 "said" per conversation**
- **Use action beats instead of tags where possible**
- **Examples**: whispered, muttered, snapped, breathed, demanded, questioned, admitted, conceded, challenged

### Tension Vocabulary (2-3 words per 1000 words)
- **Physical**: clenched, trembling, rigid, frozen, coiled
- **Atmospheric**: suffocating, charged, brittle, heavy, electric
- **Psychological**: paranoia, disorientation, vertigo, dissociation, intrusion
