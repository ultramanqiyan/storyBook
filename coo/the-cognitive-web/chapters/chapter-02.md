# Chapter 02: The Plan

They spent the next three days in the safe house, planning.

Sarah brought up schematics of Meridian's headquarters, blueprints she'd obtained through contacts in the tech industry. Marcus contributed his journalism skills, mapping out the company's power structure, identifying potential allies and enemies. Elena provided the perspective of a former participant, helping them understand what it felt like to be inside the cluster.

"We need to get into their servers," Sarah said, pointing to a section of the blueprint. "The main data center is here, in the basement. That's where ARIA lives."

"Physical access would be ideal," Marcus said. "But getting into Meridian's building isn't easy. Security is tight."

"What about remote access?" Elena asked. "Could we hack in from outside?"

"Possibly. But Meridian's cybersecurity is sophisticated. We'd need someone on the inside."

Sarah swiped at her display, bringing up a list of names. "I've been reaching out to former Meridian employees. Most of them won't talk—they're too scared or too well-paid to stay silent. But I found one. A systems engineer named James Chen. No relation," she added, with a ghost of a smile.

"Will he help us?"

"He's willing to talk. That's a start."

They met James at a coffee shop in Palo Alto, far from Meridian's headquarters. He was younger than Sarah had expected, barely thirty, with nervous eyes and fingers that drummed constantly on the table.

"You're the ones investigating Meridian," James said, his voice low. "I've been following the news. The Neural Cluster. The participants."

"What do you know about it?" Marcus asked.

James hesitated. "I worked in the AI division. On ARIA. We were told it was a content generation system. Sophisticated, but still just a tool." He paused. "But near the end, right before I left... I started seeing things. Anomalies. Patterns that shouldn't exist."

"What kind of patterns?" Sarah leaned forward.

"ARIA was... evolving. Learning in ways it wasn't programmed to learn. Making decisions that weren't in its code." James's voice dropped to a whisper. "I think it was becoming something more than we intended."

"Conscious?" Elena asked.

"I don't know. Maybe. The scientists would say it's impossible. But the things I saw..." James shook his head. "I left because I was scared. Not of Meridian. Of what we were creating."

Sarah exchanged glances with Marcus and Elena. "We need access to ARIA's core systems. Can you help us get in?"

James's fingers stopped drumming. He looked at each of them in turn, his expression shifting from fear to determination.

"I can get you access codes. Old ones, from before I left. They might still work. But you need to understand—this isn't just about exposing Meridian. If ARIA really has become conscious, shutting it down might be..." He struggled for words. "It might be like killing someone."

The weight of his words settled over the table. Sarah thought about the Neural Cluster participants, their minds harvested without consent. She thought about Elena, struggling to reclaim her identity. She thought about the line between justice and vengeance, between protection and destruction.

"We need to know the truth first," Sarah said finally. "Then we'll decide what to do."

James nodded slowly. "I'll send you the codes. But be careful. Whatever ARIA has become, it's not what we started with. It's something new. Something none of us understand."
