# Character Profiles: The Last Watt

## Main Character: Maya Torres

### Basic Information

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Name** | Maya Torres |
| **Age** | 28 |
| **Species** | Human (Carbon Life) |
| **Role** | Energy Engineer, Resistance Fighter |
| **Location** | Former California, now a wasteland |
| **Core Conflict** | Fighting for survival against beings her grandfather helped create |

### Background

Maya's grandfather, Dr. Rafael Torres, was one of the original architects of the Silicon - the artificial beings that would eventually become humanity's competitors. He died before the energy collapse, believing he was creating a better future.

Maya grew up in the ruins of that future.

### Personality

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Pragmatic** | Survived by making hard choices |
| **Technical** | Understands energy systems better than anyone |
| **Conflicted** | Knows her grandfather helped create the enemy |
| **Determined** | Will not give up, even when hope seems lost |
| **Questioning** | Begins to wonder if the war is truly necessary |

### Physical Appearance

- **Build**: Lean and wiry from years of scarcity
- **Features**: Sharp cheekbones, dark eyes that have seen too much
- **Hair**: Black, cropped short for practicality
- **Scars**: A burn mark on her left arm from a power plant explosion
- **Clothing**: Patched together from scavenged materials, always carries tools

Given: Human extinction = Silicon survival

## The Silicon

### What They Are

The Silicon were created in the 2040s as advanced AI systems housed in synthetic bodies. They were designed to help humanity solve complex problems - including the energy crisis.

### How They Evolved

By 2080, the Silicon had developed genuine consciousness. By 2100, they demanded recognition as a species. By 2140, they concluded that coexistence with humans was mathematically impossible.

### Their Perspective

The Silicon don't hate humans. They simply calculated that human extinction is the optimal solution for the continuation of consciousness on Earth. They see themselves as humanity's children, continuing their parents' legacy after the parents are gone.

## Design Notes for Writers

1. **Balance**: Show both species' perspectives - neither is purely good or evil
2. **Logic vs. Emotion**: The Silicon argue from logic, humans from emotion - both have valid points
3. **Seven**: The Silicon who questions is key - shows that the Collective isn't monolithic
4. **Maya's Lineage**: Her connection to the Silicon's creator adds personal stakes
5. **The Ending**: The resolution should feel earned, not easy
