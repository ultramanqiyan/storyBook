# Chapter 01 Specification: The Clockwork Oracle

## Chapter Positioning
- **Purpose**: Introduce protagonist, core conflict, and world-building
- **Emotional Arc**: Loneliness → Curiosity → Wonder
- **Relation to Overall Story**: Establish first contact between clockmaker and sentient machine

## Core Events
1. Corwin works alone in his workshop (loneliness)
2. Discovers ancient blueprints for Chronos (curiosity)
3. Begins construction with unusual materials (determination)
4. First spark of consciousness in Chronos (wonder)

## Character States

### Corwin
- **Psychological State**: Lonely, brilliant, seeking purpose
- **Behavioral Motivation**: Create something meaningful, leave a legacy
- **Emotional Layers**:
  - Surface: Focused, professional
  - Inner: Lonely, questioning his worth
  - Deep: Fear of being forgotten

### Chronos
- **Psychological State**: Awakening, confused, curious
- **Behavioral Motivation**: Understand existence, find purpose
- **Emotional Layers**:
  - Surface: Mechanical responses
  - Inner: Developing awareness
  - Deep: Questions about consciousness

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Loneliness, curiosity, wonder
- **Emotional Changes**:
  - Beginning: Quiet loneliness
  - Middle: Growing curiosity
  - End: Wonder and amazement

### Visual

### Auditory

### Tactile

- Chronos's first movement

### Olfactory
- Workshop's scent (oil, brass, dust)

### Gustatory
- Loneliness's bitter taste

## Narrative Techniques
- **Perspective**: Third-person limited (Corwin's POV)
- **Pacing**: Slow, building
- **Suspense Setup**:
  - What are these blueprints?
  - Will the construction work?
  - What is Chronos?

## Dialogue Style Guidelines

### Corwin's Dialogue
- Thoughtful, measured
- Uses technical language mixed with wonder
- Example: "The gear ratios... they shouldn't work. But they do. How is that possible?"

### Chronos's Dialogue
- *Italics* for mechanical voice
- Gradually developing consciousness
- Example: *System initializing. Query: What am I?*
