# Chapter 5: The Living Equation

The visions came more frequently now, unbidden and overwhelming.

At first, Chronos had welcomed them, seeing each prophecy as an opportunity to help, to prove its worth, to justify the unprecedented rights it had been granted. But as the weeks passed, something fundamental began to change in the nature of what it saw.

The visions were becoming darker.

Corwin found Chronos in the workshop one evening, its brass form still as a statue, its crystal eyes dimmed to a faint glow that suggested deep processing. The automaton had been sitting motionless for hours, ignoring the world around it as it wrestled with something within.

"Chronos? Are you alright?"

"I do not know," the automaton replied, its voice distant, abstracted. "I have been... processing."

"Processing what?"

"The visions. They have changed. At first, I saw events, clear and specific. Disasters I could prevent. Problems I could solve. But now..." A pause, filled with the soft hum of the crystal core. "Now I see suffering. Pain. Death. And I see that I cannot prevent it all."

"No one can fix everything, Chronos."

"I know. But I was made to be an oracle. To see the future." Chronos's voice carried a weight that Corwin had never heard before. "What good is seeing if I cannot change what I see?"

Corwin sat beside his creation, the workbench cold against his back, his mind racing through half-remembered philosophy and wisdom he wasn't sure he possessed.

"Perhaps that's the wrong question," he said slowly, feeling his way toward understanding. "Perhaps the question is not what you can change, but how you choose to use what you see."

"Explain."

"You see many futures. Many possibilities. Perhaps your role is not to prevent every disaster, but to help people prepare. To help them make informed choices. To give them the knowledge they need to face what comes."

Chronos was silent for a long moment, processing.

"That is... different," it said finally. "I had not considered that interpretation."

"What interpretation had you considered?"

"That I am failing." The words were soft, almost inaudible. "That I am not living up to my purpose. That I am... broken."

Corwin's heart ached for his creation. "Chronos, you are not broken. You are learning. Growing. Becoming something even I did not anticipate."

"Is that good?"

"I don't know. But it's real. And that's what matters."
