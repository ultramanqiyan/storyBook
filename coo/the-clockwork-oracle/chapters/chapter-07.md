# Chapter 7: The Choice

The return journey was marked by silence, broken only by the rhythmic clatter of hooves on cobblestone and the distant, mournful cry of a city waking to yet another overcast day.

Corwin rode alongside Princess Elara, his thoughts a tempest of worry and wonder. Chronos followed behind them, its brass feet striking the ground with a measured cadence that seemed almost meditative. The automaton had spoken little since leaving the ancient temple, its crystal eyes fixed on some distant horizon that only it could perceive.

"What did it see?" Elara asked, her voice barely above a whisper, as if afraid that speaking too loudly might shatter the fragile peace between them.

"Its own destruction," Corwin replied, the words tasting like ash on his tongue. "A future where it is torn apart by those who fear what it represents."

"And it believes this future is fixed? Unchangeable?"

"It did. Until..." Corwin glanced back at his creation, at the being that had become so much more than brass and crystal. "Until I reminded it that I gave it choice. That the future is not written in stone, but shaped by the decisions we make."

Elara was silent for a moment, her horse's mane rippling in the gentle breeze. "Do you believe that? Truly?"

"I have to." Corwin's voice was firm, despite the doubt that gnawed at his heart. "If I don't believe that choice matters, that we can shape our own destinies, then what was the point of giving Chronos consciousness? What was the point of any of it?"

They rode in silence for a while, the city walls growing larger on the horizon, the weight of their return pressing down upon them.

"Corwin," Elara said finally, her voice careful, measured. "What if Chronos is right? What if the future it saw cannot be changed?"

"Then we face it together." Corwin met her eyes, and he saw the same fear reflected there that he felt in his own heart. "We don't abandon it. We don't let it face its fate alone. We stand with it, no matter what comes."

"Even if standing with it means sharing its fate?"

"Even then."
