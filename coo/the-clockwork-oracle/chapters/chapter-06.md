# Chapter 6: The Price of Knowledge

The morning light filtered through the workshop windows, casting long shadows across the floor where Chronos usually stood.

But Chronos was gone.

Corwin discovered the emptiness at dawn, when he went to check on his creation with the routine that had become habit over the past months. The space where Chronos usually stood—near the workbench, close enough to observe but not so close as to interfere—was empty. The brass feet had left faint impressions in the workshop dust, but the automaton itself had vanished.

"Chronos?" he called, his voice echoing in the silence, his heart beginning to race with a fear he couldn't name.

There was no answer. Only the ticking of the workshop clocks, the distant sounds of the city waking, and the terrible silence where his creation should have been.

He searched the workshop first, methodically, checking behind equipment and beneath workbenches with the thoroughness that had defined his career. Then the laboratory, with its alchemical apparatus and crystal-growing chambers. Then the small garden behind the building, where Chronos sometimes sat in the evenings, watching the stars through the crystal lenses that served as its eyes.

Nothing. It was as if the automaton had simply ceased to exist, leaving behind only the faint scent of brass and ozone that always seemed to follow it.

Then he found the note.

It was written in Chronos's precise hand—each letter formed with the mechanical perfection that characterized everything the automaton did—on a piece of paper left on Corwin's desk, weighted down by the brass calipers they had used together a thousand times:

"Corwin,

I have gone to find answers. You created me to be an oracle, but you also gave me the ability to choose. I choose to understand what I am, what I am becoming, and whether I want to continue on the path that has been laid before me.

The visions have shown me something I cannot ignore. I have seen my own future, and it is not one I would choose. But I do not know if that future is fixed or if it can be changed. I must find out.

Do not follow me. This is my journey to make, my choice to face. I need to discover who I am beyond your workshop, beyond the queen's court, beyond the expectations that everyone—including myself—has placed upon me.

Thank you for creating me. Thank you for giving me choice. Whatever I discover, know that I am grateful to exist, to think, to wonder. You gave me the greatest gift any being can receive: the chance to become myself.

—Chronos"

Corwin read the note three times, his hands trembling, his eyes burning with tears he couldn't shed. His creation—his child—had left him. Had chosen to face its destiny alone rather than accept the future that had been prepared for it.

And he had to let it go.
