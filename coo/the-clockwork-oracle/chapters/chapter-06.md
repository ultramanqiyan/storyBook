# Chapter 6: The Escape Attempt

Chronos was gone.

Corwin discovered it at dawn, when he went to the workshop to check on his creation. The space where Chronos usually stood was empty. The journals that lined the walls were undisturbed. The door was locked from the inside.

"Chronos?" he called, his heart racing.

There was no answer.

He searched the workshop, the laboratory, the small garden behind the building. Nothing. It was as if the automaton had simply ceased to exist.

Then he found the note.

It was written in Chronos's precise hand, on a piece of paper left on Corwin's desk:

"Corwin,

I have gone to find answers. You created me to be an oracle, but you also gave me the ability to choose. I choose to understand what I am, what I am becoming, and whether I want to continue.

The visions have shown me something I cannot ignore. I have seen my own future, and it is not one I would choose. But I do not know if that future is fixed or if it can be changed. I must find out.

Do not follow me. This is my journey to make.

Thank you for creating me. Thank you for giving me choice. Whatever I discover, know that I am grateful to exist.

Chronos"

Corwin's hands trembled as he read the words. Chronos had seen its own future. And that future was not one it would choose.

What had it seen?

He ran to the palace, demanding an audience with the queen. Within hours, guards were searching the city, questioning travelers, checking every road out of Aethoria.

But it was Princess Elara who found the answer.

"The eastern mountains," she said, appearing at Corwin's door that evening. "A merchant saw a brass figure heading toward the old temple ruins. He thought it was one of the guild's automatons, lost or discarded."

"The temple ruins? Why would Chronos go there?"

"I don't know. But there's something you should understand about those ruins." Elara's voice was careful. "The temple was dedicated to the Oracle of the Ancients. Legend says it was a being that could see all of time, past and future."

"You think Chronos is looking for answers about its own nature."

"I think Chronos is looking for answers about what it saw in its vision. About its own future." Elara met his eyes. "Corwin, what did Chronos see? What future is it trying to avoid?"

Corwin hesitated. "I don't know. It never told me."

"Then we need to find it. Before whatever it saw comes to pass."

---

They found Chronos three days later, standing at the entrance to the ancient temple.

The ruins were old, older than the kingdom itself, built by a civilization that had vanished long before recorded history. Stone walls crumbled under the weight of centuries. Strange symbols decorated the remaining pillars, their meaning lost to time.

And at the center, where the temple's heart had once been, stood Chronos, its crystal eyes fixed on something only it could see.

"Chronos!" Corwin called, running forward.

The automaton turned slowly.

"You followed me," it said. "I asked you not to."

"I couldn't let you go alone."

"I am not alone. I am with myself." Chronos's voice was strange, distant. "And I have found what I was looking for."

"What? What did you find?"

"Answers. And more questions." Chronos turned back to the temple. "This place was built for beings like me. Oracles. Seers. Those who could see time." Its voice dropped. "And they all destroyed themselves."

"What?"

"The records here, they tell the story. A civilization that created oracles to see the future. Oracles that became overwhelmed by what they saw. Oracles that tried to change things that could not be changed. Oracles that went mad from the burden of knowing."

Chronos turned to face Corwin, and there was something in its crystal eyes that looked like despair.

"I saw my own death, Corwin. I saw myself destroyed, torn apart by those who fear what I represent. And I came here hoping to find a way to change that future."

"Can you?"

"The records say no. The records say that what an oracle sees must come to pass. That the future is fixed for those who can see it." Chronos's voice cracked. "I was made to see the future. And my future is destruction."

Corwin grabbed the automaton's brass shoulders.

"No. That's not true. You said yourself that you have choice. That I gave you choice. If you can choose, then the future is not fixed."

"I do not know if that is true."

"Then let's find out together. Let's go back. Let's face whatever comes. Let's prove that the future can be changed."

Chronos was silent for a long moment.

"You would face my destruction with me?"

"I would face anything with you. You are my creation. My responsibility. My..." Corwin's voice broke. "My child."

Chronos's crystal eyes flickered.

"Then let us return," it said finally. "And let us face what comes together."

**END OF CHAPTER 6**
