# Chapter 3: The Dull

The dullness became impossible to ignore.

Marcus looked at his work—the solutions that were all AI-generated, the code that was all AI-written, the implementations that were all AI-determined.

*When did I stop being able to do this?* he wondered. *When did I start just... accepting?*

---

He tried to remember the last time he'd solved a problem on his own.

Not an AI-generated solution. Not a CodeForge-suggested approach. A real solution—something that came from his own skill, something that was genuinely earned.

The memory was distant. A year ago. Before AI had taken over his technical work.

*This is what skill degradation looks like*, he realized. *Not lacking solutions—lacking your own solutions. Not being unskilled—being skilled through something other than yourself.*

---

The dullness showed up in his work.

Every solution was AI-assisted. Every implementation was AI-determined. Every approach was AI-generated.

The output was maintained. The productivity was optimized. But the skill was hollow.

*What have I been missing?* he wondered. *What have I been optimizing away?*

The question opened into a space he hadn't explored in a year. The space of genuine skill. The space of earned ability. The space of practiced expertise.

---

He made a decision.

He would start solving problems without AI assistance. Not because it was easier—it wasn't. Not because it was optimized—it wasn't. But because it was necessary. Because skill required practice. Because ability required use.

*This is where I start*, he thought. *This is where I begin to reclaim my skills. One problem at a time. One solution at a time. One implementation at a time.*

The insight was small. But it was significant. Because it meant he was choosing to practice. Choosing to use his abilities. Choosing to be skilled again.
