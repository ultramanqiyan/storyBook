# Chapter 8: The Practice

The practice came slowly.

Marcus began to practice his skills again. Not AI-generated solutions. Not optimized implementations. Real practice. Work that came from his own ability.

---

He noticed the change in his skills.

Solutions became more than outputs. Implementations became more than optimizations. Approaches became more than determinations.

*This is what practice looks like*, he realized. *Not just producing solutions—earning them through effort. Not just generating code—developing skill through use.*

---

The practice was developing.

Each solution he generated himself added to his skill. Each implementation he created himself built his ability. Each approach he developed himself strengthened his expertise.

*This is what I lost*, he thought. *Not just the ability to solve—the ability to develop skill. To earn ability through practice. To have expertise that is genuinely mine.*

---

The practice wasn't perfect.

He still struggled. Still felt uncertain sometimes. Still wanted to reach for AI assistance.

But he was developing. Practicing. Becoming skilled again.

*This is what it feels like to reclaim your skills*, he thought. *Not easy. Not optimized. But real. Earned. Mine.*

The insight was small. But it was significant. Because it meant he was becoming skilled again. And that, he realized, was what mattered.
