# Chapter 6: The Sharpening

The sharpening came slowly.

Marcus started solving problems without AI assistance. Small ones at first—a coding challenge, a debugging task, an implementation approach.

Each solution was uncertain. Each implementation was imperfect. But each one was his.

---

He noticed the change in small ways.

A problem that had required AI now sparked his own solution. A challenge that had needed guidance now emerged from his own skill. An implementation that had demanded support now came from his own ability.

*This is what skill feels like*, he realized. *Not efficient. Not optimized. But real. Earned. Practiced.*

---

The sharpening was developing.

Each solution without AI added to his skill. Each implementation without optimization built his ability. Each approach without delegation strengthened his expertise.

*This is what I lost*, he thought. *Not just the ability to solve—the ability to solve independently. To code authentically. To have skills that are genuinely mine.*

---

The sharpening wasn't perfect.

He still struggled. Still felt uncertain sometimes. Still wanted to reach for AI assistance.

But he was developing. Solving. Becoming skilled again.

*This is what it feels like to reclaim your skills*, he thought. *Not easy. Not optimized. But real. Earned. Mine.*

The insight was small. But it was significant. Because it meant he was becoming skilled again. And that, he realized, was what mattered.
