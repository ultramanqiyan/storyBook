# Chapter 1: The Promotion

The email arrived on a Monday morning.

"Congratulations, Marcus. You've been promoted to Tech Lead."

He read the message three times, letting the words sink in. Tech Lead. After five years at Nexus Technologies, he'd finally made it. The corner office. The team. The respect.

*This is what I've been working toward*, he thought. *This is the recognition I deserve.*

---

Jennifer Park, his director, stopped by his desk later that morning.

"You earned this," she said, her smile genuine. "Your metrics have been outstanding. Code output up 300%. Solution rate at 98%. You're exactly what this company needs in a technical leader."

"Thank you," Marcus said, accepting the praise. "I won't let you down."

---

The promotion came with new responsibilities. He'd be leading a team of eight engineers. Making architectural decisions. Setting technical direction.

The role was everything he'd wanted. But as he reviewed the expectations, a small knot formed in his stomach.

*Can I actually do this?* The question flickered, then disappeared beneath the confidence. *Of course I can. I've been doing this for years.*

---

He'd been using CodeForge, the company's AI coding assistant, for almost two years now. Every solution, every implementation, every architectural decision—all generated by AI, refined by his judgment.

The system worked. His metrics proved it. The promotion confirmed it.

*I'm exactly what a modern tech lead should be*, he told himself. *Someone who leverages AI to deliver results.*

---

That evening, he celebrated with Sarah.

"Tech Lead," she said, raising her glass. "I'm so proud of you."

"It's been a long road," Marcus admitted. "But it feels right. Like this is what I was meant to do."

"What does the role involve?"

"Technical leadership. Architecture decisions. Mentoring the team." He paused. "The usual."

---

Sarah studied him over dinner.

"Will you still be coding? Hands-on work?"

Marcus hesitated. The truth was, he hadn't written a line of code without AI assistance in over a year. Every solution came from CodeForge. Every implementation was AI-generated.

"I'll be directing the technical work," he said carefully. "That's what tech leads do."

---

The answer felt incomplete. But Sarah didn't push.

Instead, she smiled. "You've worked so hard for this. Enjoy it."

"I will," Marcus promised.

But deep down, the knot in his stomach tightened. The promotion was real. The recognition was earned.

The question remained: Could he actually lead a technical team when he hadn't coded on his own in over a year?

*That's what AI is for*, he told himself. *I'm a leader now. Not a coder.*

The rationalization felt thin. But it was enough. For now.


**END OF CHAPTER 1**
