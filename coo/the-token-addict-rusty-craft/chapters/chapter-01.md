# Chapter 1: The Rust

One year into his AI journey, Marcus noticed the rust.

Not physical rust—skill rust. The abilities that had once been sharp were dulling. The expertise that had once been refined was coarsening.

*When did I start getting worse?* he wondered. *When did the skills become so rusty?*

---

He'd always been skilled. The tech industry required it—technical depth, problem-solving ability, architectural understanding. He'd built expertise over years of practice, honed abilities through constant use.

Now, the skills were fading. Replaced by AI-generated solutions. AI-suggested approaches. AI-determined implementations.

*This is what happens when you outsource skill*, he realized. *When AI handles the technical parts, you lose the capacity to do them yourself.*

---

The rust showed up in small ways first.

A coding problem that once would have been straightforward now required AI assistance. An architectural decision that once would have been intuitive now needed AI guidance. A technical challenge that once would have been manageable now demanded AI support.

Each skill was fading. Each ability was coarsening. Each expertise was dulling.

*What happened to my skills?* he wondered. *What happened to the abilities I built over years?*

The question was uncomfortable. But it was also necessary. And asking it, he realized, was the first step toward understanding what he'd lost.

---

He tried to remember the last time he'd solved a technical problem without AI assistance.

The memory was distant. A year ago, before CodeForge. He'd had skills that were genuinely his. Abilities that came from practice. Expertise that was earned through use.

Now, every solution was AI-generated. Every approach was AI-suggested. Every implementation was AI-determined.

*This is what skill degradation looks like*, he realized. *Not lacking skills—lacking your own skills. Not being unskilled—being skilled through algorithms.*

The insight was uncomfortable. But it was also clarifying. And clarity, he was learning, was what he needed.
