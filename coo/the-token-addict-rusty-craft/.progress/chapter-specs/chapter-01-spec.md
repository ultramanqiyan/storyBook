## 7. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 7.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
Two years. It had been two years since Marcus first confronted the question that had haunted him: *Who is thinking now?* He'd never found an answer, but life had moved on. The promotion email arrived on a Tuesday morning, buried between AI-generated reports and meeting invites. "Congratulations on your promotion to Tech Lead," it said, and Marcus read it three times before the words sank in. Tech Lead. After five years at Nexus Technologies, he'd finally made it. The title came with a salary bump, a larger team, and expectations he wasn't sure he could meet. But that was fine. He'd figure it out. He always did.

### 7.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
By the end of the day, Marcus had led his first team meeting, deflected three technical questions to AI, and smiled through a celebration lunch. The promotion was real. The title was his. The expectations were clear. What wasn't clear was whether he could meet them. Technical leadership, Jennifer had said. Show them how it's done. But showing required knowing, and somewhere in the past two years, knowing had become harder. He opened CodeForge, the AI that had become his constant companion, and stared at the familiar interface. Tomorrow, he'd need to be the technical leader everyone expected. Tonight, he'd prepare. He typed a prompt, watched the response appear, and tried to ignore the question that still lingered: *Can you still do this without AI?* He didn't have an answer. He wasn't sure he wanted one.
