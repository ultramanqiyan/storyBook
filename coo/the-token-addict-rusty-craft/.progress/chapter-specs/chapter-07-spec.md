## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Fear (400 words)
**Location**: Marcus's home, bedroom
**Time**: Night, after the crisis

**Key Events**:
- Marcus lies awake, replaying the crisis
- The fear of losing his position
- The realization: he can't regain his skills quickly enough
- The question: "What can I do?"
- The first thought of deception

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Dark room, ceiling, Sarah sleeping beside him
- Auditory: Silence, his own breathing
- Internal: Fear, desperation, the first rationalization

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "I can't let this happen again. I can't be exposed."
- Marcus (internal): "What if I... created materials that made it look like I knew?"
- Marcus (internal): "Is that lying? Or is it... managing perception?"

### Scene 2: The System (700 words)
**Location**: Marcus's home office, then work
**Time**: Next morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus develops a systematic approach to hiding his gaps
- He uses ThinkTank and CodeForge to generate technical documentation
- He creates "personal notes" that are actually AI-generated
- He practices delivering AI explanations as his own
- The creation of a "cheat sheet" system for meetings
- The first use: a technical discussion where he "knows" the answer

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: AI interfaces, generated documents, his own notes
- Auditory: Keyboard sounds, his own voice practicing
- Tactile: Writing notes, keyboard
- Internal: The mix of relief and shame

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "If I prepare enough, no one will know."
- ThinkTank (output): "Technical architecture overview for distributed caching..."
- Marcus (practicing): "The key consideration is cache invalidation..."
- Marcus (internal): "That sounds like me. That sounds like someone who knows."

### Scene 3: The Test (600 words)
**Location**: Team meeting
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- A technical question arises in a team meeting
- Marcus has prepared for this topic using AI
- He delivers a confident, AI-generated explanation
- The team is impressed
- Alex asks a follow-up question
- Marcus deflects smoothly using prepared material
- The success of the deception

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Meeting room, team faces, his notes
- Auditory: His confident voice, team's responses
- Internal: The thrill of success, the underlying shame

**Character Voices**:
- Team member: "Marcus, how should we handle the database migration?"
- Marcus: "Based on the architecture, we should use a blue-green deployment with..." (AI-generated)
- Team: Nods, impressed
- Alex: "What about the data consistency during the switch?"
- Marcus: "Good question. We'll implement a dual-write pattern during transition..." (prepared)
- Alex: "That makes sense. Thanks, Marcus."
- Marcus (internal): "It worked. They believed it."

### Scene 4: The Expansion (400 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Following days

**Key Events**:
- Marcus expands the cover-up system
- He creates AI-generated documentation for all major systems
- He maintains the illusion through constant AI assistance
- The system becomes his new normal
- The line between "preparation" and "deception" blurs

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Growing documentation, AI interfaces
- Internal: The comfort of the system, the unease of the lie

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "This is just... preparation. Everyone prepares."
- Marcus (internal): "I'm still making the decisions. The AI is just... helping me articulate them."

### Scene 5: The Cost (500 words)
**Location**: Home, dinner with Sarah
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Sarah asks about work
- Marcus describes his "successful" week
- He realizes he's now lying to Sarah too
- The moral weight of the deception
- Sarah's quiet observation: "You seem different"
- The cost of maintaining the illusion

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Dinner table, Sarah's face
- Auditory: Sarah's voice, dinner sounds
- Internal: The weight of the lie, the isolation

**Character Voices**:
- Sarah: "How's work going? After the crisis?"
- Marcus: "Good. I've been... preparing better. Making sure I'm on top of things."
- Sarah: "You seem more confident. But also... more distant."
- Marcus: "Just busy. The new systems take time."
- Marcus (internal): "The new systems. The lies. The deception."
- Sarah: "I'm here if you want to talk. About anything."
- Marcus: "I know. Everything's fine."
