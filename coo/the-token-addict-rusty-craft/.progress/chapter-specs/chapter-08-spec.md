## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Comfort (400 words)
**Location**: Nexus Technologies, Marcus's office
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus's routine is now smooth
- The deception system works
- He receives praise for his "technical leadership"
- The comfort of the illusion
- But something feels wrong

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Office, screen, familiar interfaces
- Auditory: Praise from colleagues
- Internal: Comfort mixed with unease

**Character Voices**:
- Colleague: "Marcus, great architecture review yesterday."
- Marcus: "Thanks. Just doing my job."
- Marcus (internal): "But it wasn't my job. It was ThinkTank's."

### Scene 2: The Moment (800 words)
**Location**: Team meeting, then Marcus's office
**Time**: Mid-morning

**Key Events**:
- A new project requires hands-on technical work
- Marcus realizes he can't do it himself
- He would need to delegate everything
- The moment of clarity: he is no longer an engineer
- He is an "AI translator" - someone who translates AI output into human language
- The philosophical weight of this realization
- The question: "What am I?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Meeting room, project requirements, his own hands
- Auditory: Discussion, his own silence
- Tactile: The feeling of his hands - hands that no longer code
- Internal: The crushing recognition

**Character Voices**:
- Jennifer: "Marcus, I need you to prototype this feature. Hands-on."
- Marcus: "I'll... assign it to the team."
- Jennifer: "No, I need you to do it. Show technical leadership."
- Marcus (internal): "I can't. I literally cannot do this."
- Marcus: "Let me... scope it first."
- Marcus (internal, later): "I'm not an engineer anymore. I'm a translator. I translate AI output into human language. That's all I am."

### Scene 3: The Grief (600 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office, then car
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus sits with the recognition
- He remembers who he used to be
- The grief for his lost skills
- The anger at himself
- The mourning of his identity
- The question: "Can I ever get it back?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Old code he wrote, his reflection
- Auditory: Silence, his own breathing
- Internal: Grief, anger, mourning

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "I used to be able to build anything. Now I can only translate."
- Marcus (internal): "When did I become this? When did I let go?"
- Marcus (internal): "Is it too late? Can I learn again?"
- Marcus (looking at old code): "I wrote this. I understood this. Who was that person?"

### Scene 4: The Conversation (600 words)
**Location**: Home, living room
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Marcus talks to Sarah about his realization
- He admits, for the first time, what he's become
- Sarah listens without judgment
- The relief of honesty
- The grief shared
- Sarah's question: "What do you want to be?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Living room, Sarah's face
- Auditory: Sarah's voice, the quiet of home
- Internal: The relief of honesty, the grief

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "Sarah, I need to tell you something."
- Sarah: "What is it?"
- Marcus: "I'm not... I'm not an engineer anymore. Not really."
- Sarah: "What do you mean?"
- Marcus: "I can't code. I can't architect. I can only translate what the AI produces. I'm a fraud."
- Sarah: "You're not a fraud. You're... adapting."
- Marcus: "But at what cost? I've lost who I was."
- Sarah: "Maybe. Or maybe you're becoming someone new."
- Marcus: "I don't know if I want to be this new person."

### Scene 5: The Acceptance (400 words)
**Location**: Home, bedroom
**Time**: Night

**Key Events**:
- Marcus lies awake, processing the day
- The grief is still present, but something else too
- A tentative acceptance: "This is who I am now"
- The question: "What do I do with this?"
- The first step toward a new identity

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Dark room, ceiling
- Auditory: Silence, Sarah's breathing
- Internal: Grief, acceptance, the question

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "I am an AI translator. That's my reality."
- Marcus (internal): "Can I be honest about that? Can I live that truth?"
- Marcus (internal): "Or do I keep pretending?"
- Marcus (internal): "Tomorrow, I'll decide. Tonight, I'll grieve."
