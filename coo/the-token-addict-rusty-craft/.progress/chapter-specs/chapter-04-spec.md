## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Routine (400 words)
**Location**: Nexus Technologies, Marcus's office
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus's typical morning: ThinkTank, CodeForge, delegation
- The comfort of the routine
- A sense of competence based on AI output
- The calm before the storm

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Screen glow, familiar interface
- Auditory: Keyboard clicks, office hum
- Internal: Comfort, routine, false confidence

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Another productive day ahead"
- The routine: Check ThinkTank, review AI suggestions, delegate

### Scene 2: The Question (800 words)
**Location**: Team meeting room
**Time**: Mid-morning

**Key Events**:
- Alex presents a technical implementation
- He asks Marcus a specific architecture question
- Marcus tries to answer but can't
- He deflects to ThinkTank, but Alex presses
- The moment of exposure: Marcus doesn't know
- The team notices the hesitation

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Alex's diagram, team's faces, Marcus's screen
- Auditory: Alex's voice, the silence after the question
- Tactile: Sweat on palms, tension in shoulders
- Internal: Panic, shame, the gap exposed

**Character Voices**:
- Alex: "Marcus, how should we handle the race condition in the cache layer?"
- Marcus: "Let me... think about that..."
- Alex: "But you designed this architecture. How did you handle it before?"
- Marcus (internal): "I didn't. ThinkTank did."
- Team member: "Is everything okay, Marcus?"

### Scene 3: The Deflection (500 words)
**Location**: Meeting room, then Marcus's office
**Time**: Immediately after the meeting

**Key Events**:
- Marcus deflects: "Let me review the documentation"
- The meeting ends awkwardly
- Marcus retreats to his office
- He uses ThinkTank to understand his own architecture
- The shame of learning from AI what he should know

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Closed door, ThinkTank interface
- Auditory: Silence, then keyboard sounds
- Internal: Shame, anger at himself, the hollow feeling

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "How do I not know my own architecture?"
- ThinkTank (output): "The cache layer uses a write-through pattern with..."
- Marcus (internal): "I should know this. I should KNOW this."

### Scene 4: The Cover-up (500 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus prepares a response using AI
- He practices explaining the architecture
- He convinces himself he "knew it all along"
- The first lie to himself: "I just needed a reminder"
- The pattern of cover-up begins

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Notes on screen, architecture diagrams
- Tactile: Writing notes, keyboard
- Internal: Rationalization, self-deception

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "I'll just... refresh my memory"
- Marcus (practicing): "The cache layer uses write-through because..."
- Marcus (internal): "See? I knew that. I just needed to recall it."

### Scene 5: The Return (400 words)
**Location**: Team area
**Time**: Late afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus returns to the team with "the answer"
- He delivers the AI-generated explanation
- Alex accepts it, but something has shifted
- The team's perception of Marcus has cracked
- Marcus feels the fragility of his position

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Team's faces, Alex's expression
- Auditory: His own voice delivering borrowed knowledge
- Internal: The hollowness of the performance

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "After reviewing, the cache layer should use..."
- Alex: "Thanks, Marcus. That makes sense."
- Alex (expression): A flicker of doubt
- Marcus (internal): "They believed it. But for how long?"
