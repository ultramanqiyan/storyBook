## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Normal Morning (300 words)
**Location**: Nexus Technologies, Marcus's office
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus's routine morning
- The calm before the storm
- A brief moment of confidence from yesterday's "success"
- The pager goes off

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Screen, coffee cup, office
- Auditory: Pager sound, sudden alarm
- Internal: Calm, then sudden alarm

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Maybe yesterday wasn't so bad..."
- Pager: [URGENT] Production incident - all hands on deck

### Scene 2: The War Room (800 words)
**Location**: Emergency conference room
**Time**: Immediately after

**Key Events**:
- The team gathers for the production incident
- A critical system is down, affecting users
- Alex and other engineers dive into the code
- Marcus tries to follow but can't understand the details
- He asks ThinkTank for help, but there's no time
- The team works frantically while Marcus watches
- Jennifer enters, expects Marcus to lead
- Marcus tries to contribute but his suggestions are wrong or obvious
- The team ignores him and solves it themselves

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Multiple screens, stressed faces, code scrolling
- Auditory: Multiple voices, keyboard fury, tension
- Tactile: Cold sweat, grip on chair
- Internal: Panic, helplessness, shame

**Character Voices**:
- Alex: "The issue is in the connection pool. We need to..."
- Marcus: "Have you tried... restarting the service?" (obvious suggestion)
- Alex: "Yes, Marcus. That was the first thing."
- Engineer: "I found it! The race condition in..."
- Marcus (internal): "I should be the one finding this."
- Jennifer: "Marcus, what's your assessment?"
- Marcus: "The team is... handling it well."
- Jennifer (expression): Disappointment

### Scene 3: The Failure (600 words)
**Location**: War room, continuing
**Time**: During the crisis

**Key Events**:
- The crisis continues for hours
- Marcus tries to use AI to understand the problem
- By the time he understands, the team has moved on
- He offers a suggestion that's already been tried
- Alex's subtle look of pity
- The moment Marcus realizes he's not helping
- He steps back, watching his team work without him

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: The blur of screens, his own useless hands
- Auditory: The team's technical discussion, his own silence
- Internal: The crushing weight of uselessness

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "What about the timeout configuration?"
- Alex: "We adjusted that an hour ago, Marcus."
- Marcus (internal): "An hour ago. I'm an hour behind. I'm hours behind."
- Engineer: "We're close. I can feel it."
- Marcus (internal): "I used to be the one who could feel it."

### Scene 4: The Resolution (400 words)
**Location**: War room
**Time**: Late afternoon

**Key Events**:
- The team solves the crisis
- Alex gets credit for the fix
- Jennifer thanks the team, looks at Marcus
- The unspoken question: "What did you contribute?"
- Marcus's hollow congratulations
- The team's relief, Marcus's shame

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Relieved faces, Alex's tired smile
- Auditory: Celebration sounds, Jennifer's voice
- Internal: The hollow feeling of being present but useless

**Character Voices**:
- Alex: "We got it. The fix is deployed."
- Jennifer: "Great work, everyone. Alex, excellent job."
- Marcus: "Good work, team." (hollow)
- Jennifer (to Marcus): "I'd like to discuss this later."

### Scene 5: The Confrontation (500 words)
**Location**: Jennifer's office
**Time**: After the crisis

**Key Events**:
- Jennifer questions Marcus's role in the crisis
- "You seemed... disconnected from the technical work"
- Marcus makes excuses: "I was coordinating"
- Jennifer's doubt is visible
- The warning: "I need a technical lead who can lead technically"
- Marcus's fear: his position is at risk

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Jennifer's face, office setting
- Auditory: Jennifer's concerned voice
- Internal: Fear, defensiveness, the threat to his identity

**Character Voices**:
- Jennifer: "Marcus, I noticed you weren't hands-on during the crisis."
- Marcus: "I was coordinating the response, making sure resources were allocated."
- Jennifer: "Alex was the one who found the fix. You're the Tech Lead."
- Marcus: "I was providing oversight..."
- Jennifer: "I need you to be more than oversight. I need technical leadership."
- Marcus: "I understand. It won't happen again."
- Marcus (internal): "But how? I can't do what I can't do."
