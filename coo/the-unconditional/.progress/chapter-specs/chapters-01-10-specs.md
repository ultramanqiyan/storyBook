## Chapter 01: The Waiting

### 章节定位

- **作用**：Establish Buddy's world, his relationship with Sarah, and the loneliness of his daily life
- **情绪曲线**：平静 → 孤独 → 短暂的喜悦
- **与整体故事的关系**：Sets the baseline of "normal" before the AI arrives

### 核心事件

1. Morning routine - Sarah leaves for work (Brief joy → Loneliness begins)
2. The long wait - Hours pass, Buddy experiences time as a dog does (Patience → Restlessness)
3. The Return - Sarah comes home, but the joy is brief (Joy → Disappointment)

### 角色状态

#### Buddy

- **心理状态**：Content but lonely, accepting this as normal
- **行为动机**：Love for Sarah, hope for her return
- **情感层次**：
  - 表面：Wagging tail, waiting by the door
  - 内心：*She will come back. She always comes back.*
  - 深层：Fear that this is all there is, fighting it with faith

### 情感基调

- **整体氛围**：Quiet, domestic, melancholic
- **情感变化**：
  - 开头：Calm, routine
  - 中间：Growing loneliness, restlessness
  - 结尾：Brief joy, then settling back into waiting

### 感官细节要求（每类至少1项）

#### 嗅觉

- Sarah's morning scent (coffee, perfume, stress)
- The apartment's smell changing as hours pass
- The scent of other dogs from the window

#### 听觉

- Sarah's footsteps leaving
- The silence of the empty apartment
- Distant sounds of life outside

#### 触觉

- The cold floor where Buddy lies
- The warmth of Sarah's brief touch before leaving
- The leash (a symbol of connection and restraint)

#### 视觉

- Light moving across the floor
- The door (always watching)
- Sarah's tired smile

#### 味觉

- Dry kibble (routine, mechanical)
- A treat (rare, special)

### 叙事技巧

- **视角**：First-person, Buddy's perspective
- **节奏**：Slow, emphasizing the long hours
- **悬念设置**：
  - Why does Buddy wait so patiently?
  - What does he think about during these hours?
  - Is this really a happy life?

### 对话风格指南

#### Buddy's internal monologue

- Short, simple sentences
- Present tense
- Focus on sensory experience
- 示例：*She's leaving. The door closes. I wait.*

## Final Notes

### The Open Ending

The story must end with Buddy at the threshold - the door open, Sarah on one side, the world on the other. The reader should feel:

1. The weight of the choice
2. The complexity of love
3. The question: "What would I do?"
4. The question: "What is my pet thinking?"

### Key Themes to Reinforce

- Unconditional love vs. conditional love
- Freedom vs. belonging
- The consciousness of animals
- Human responsibility to those who love us

### What NOT to Do

- Do NOT resolve the ending
- Do NOT make Sarah a villain
- Do NOT make the AI evil
- Do NOT preach or moralize
- Do NOT give easy answers
