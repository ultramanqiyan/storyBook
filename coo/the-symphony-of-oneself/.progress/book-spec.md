# Book Specification: The Symphony of Oneself

## Basic Information
- **Title**: The Symphony of Oneself: When Multiple Selves Become One
- **Meta Description**: A neuroscientist discovers she has seven distinct personality fragments living in her mind, each with unique memories and desires. A psychological drama about identity, integration, and the symphony of self.
- **Keywords**: multiple personalities, identity integration, psychological drama, consciousness symphony, seven selves, fragmented mind, self-discovery
- **Genre**: Psychological Fiction / Science Fiction
- **Target Audience**: Adults 25-55, readers interested in identity, consciousness, psychological drama
- **Theme**: Identity Integration, The Symphony of Self, Wholeness vs. Fragmentation

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: Third-person limited (shifting between the seven selves)
- **POV Character(s)**: Dr. Maya Chen and her seven fragments
- **Language Style**: Varied per self—scientific, emotional, logical, creative
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Each self has distinct sentence patterns
- **Unique Elements**: Seven distinct voices in one mind, symphony metaphor throughout

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Discovery, Conflict, Harmony, Integration, Acceptance
- **Emotional Arc**: Fragmentation → Discovery → Conflict → Negotiation → Integration → Harmony
- **Emotional Pacing**: Building toward orchestral harmony

## Character Voices

### Dr. Maya Chen (Host)
- **Speech Characteristics**: Calm, analytical, mediating voice
- **Word Choice**: Scientific terminology, balanced perspective
- **Personality Traits**: The core self seeking integration
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Observational, seeking harmony

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt afraid." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Moreover," / "Furthermore," | 0 per chapter | Remove entirely |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt |

### Dialogue Tag Requirements
