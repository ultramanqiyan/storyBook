# Chapter 6: The Wounds - What Made Us

After the presentation was done, Maya finally asked the question she had been avoiding.

*How did this happen?* she said in the internal conference room. *What made us... this way?*

The room fell silent. Even Sage was quiet.

*We don't have to talk about it,* Nora said gently. *Not if it's too painful.*

*We have to,* Maya said. *If we're going to integrate—if we're going to be whole—we need to understand what broke us in the first place.*

---

Selene spoke first, her child-voice trembling.

*I remember the first time,* she said. *I was five. Mommy was crying again. Daddy was gone. And I felt so small, so scared. And then... I wasn't alone anymore. There was someone else. Someone bigger. Someone who wasn't afraid.*

*Marcus,* Maya said.

*He held my hand,* Selene said. *In our mind. He said, "I'll protect you. I'll keep us safe." And I believed him.*

*I kept that promise,* Marcus said, his voice heavy. *Every time something threatened us, I took over. Every time we were scared, I became strong. But the cost...*

*What cost?*

*I pushed everything away,* Marcus said. *Every feeling. Every vulnerability. I made us hard. I made us safe. But I also made us lonely.*

---

Iris spoke next.

*I remember when we were twelve,* she said. *We wanted to be an artist. We painted and wrote poetry and saw beauty everywhere. But then...*

*But then,* Logan continued, *our teachers told us art wasn't practical. Our parents told us we needed a real career. And we believed them.*

*I couldn't let the art go,* Iris said. *So I became a separate space. A place where the beauty could live, even when the rest of us became... practical.*

*You saved our creativity,* Maya realized. *You kept it alive when I was trying to be sensible.*

*I kept our soul,* Iris said simply.

---

Nora spoke third.

*I remember the loneliness,* she said. *The way we pushed people away because we were afraid of being hurt. But I also remember the love—the grandmother who held us, the friends who stayed, the moments of connection that made life worth living.*

*You kept those?*

*I kept them safe,* Nora said. *Every act of kindness we received, every moment of love—I preserved them. So we would know that love was possible, even when we were too afraid to seek it.*

---

Phoenix spoke fourth, her voice fierce.

*I remember the anger,* she said. *Every time we were told to be quiet. Every time we were told we were too much, too intense, too difficult. Every time we swallowed our rage and smiled.*

*You kept the anger.*

*I kept the FIRE,* Phoenix corrected. *The part of us that knows we deserve better. The part that fights back. Without me, we would have disappeared. We would have become what everyone else wanted us to be.*

---

Logan spoke fifth.

*I remember the confusion,* he said. *The way the world didn't make sense. The way people said one thing and meant another. The way emotions clouded judgment and led to mistakes.*

*So you became logic.*

*I became clarity,* Logan said. *A place where things made sense. A framework for understanding. Without me, we would have been lost in chaos.*

---

Sage spoke last.

*I remember the beginning,* the oldest self said. *Before the splitting. Before the fragments. When we were one.*

*What happened?*

*The world taught us to be many,* Sage said. *It taught us that some parts of ourselves were unacceptable. That some feelings were dangerous. That some truths were better hidden. So we hid them. We split them off. We became many so that each part could survive.*

*But we can be one again?*

*We were always one,* Sage said. *We just forgot.*

---

That night, Maya understood.

The selves weren't created by trauma. They were revealed by it. Each self was a part of her that had always existed—a facet of the whole that had been pushed into shadow by a world that couldn't accept all of her.

The child who wondered. The protector who defended. The artist who created. The analyst who understood. The caretaker who loved. The rebel who fought. The wisdom that held it all.

These weren't separate people. They were her—all of her. The parts she had been told to hide. The aspects she had been taught to suppress. The facets of a diamond that had been told to be only one side.

*We're not broken,* she said to all of them. *We were never broken. We were just... scattered.*

*And now we gather,* Sage said.

*Now we become whole.*
