# Chapter 5: The Confrontation - First Step

Dr. Sarah Chen's office was immaculate.

Every surface was clean, every object precisely placed. It was the workspace of someone who valued control—someone who didn't like surprises.

Alex had requested a meeting under the pretense of discussing a technical issue. But the real purpose was to look Sarah in the eyes and decide whether she was capable of murder.

"Alex." Sarah looked up from her computer, her expression neutral. "What can I help you with?"

"I wanted to ask about Elena Vasquez."

Something flickered in Sarah's eyes—too quick to identify. "Elena? What about her?"

"I've been going through some old code, some project files. Her name keeps coming up. I was curious about her work."

Sarah leaned back in her chair. "Elena was a brilliant researcher. We worked together for several years. Her death was a tragedy."

"I heard it was an accident. A fire in the lab."

"Yes. Electrical malfunction. She was working late, alone." Sarah's voice was carefully controlled. "The investigation concluded it was an accident."

"Did you know her well?"

"Well enough. We were colleagues, sometimes friends. She was passionate about her work—sometimes too passionate. She took risks that others wouldn't."

"What kind of risks?"

Sarah hesitated. "That's classified. I'm sorry, I can't discuss the details of her research."

"Was it related to consciousness transfer?"

The question landed like a blow. Sarah's composure cracked for just a moment—enough for Alex to see fear beneath the professional mask.

"Where did you hear that term?"

"I told you—I've been going through old files. Her name keeps coming up, and so does that phrase. Consciousness transfer. What was she working on, Dr. Chen?"

"That information is classified. And you shouldn't be accessing files you're not authorized to see." Sarah stood, her voice cold. "I think this meeting is over."

"I know you were in the lab the night she died."

Sarah froze. "What?"

"The access logs. Someone used your credentials at 11:47 PM. Seventeen minutes before the fire was reported. You told the police you left at 9."

For a long moment, Sarah said nothing. Then, slowly, she sat back down.

"You don't understand what you're getting into."

"Then explain it to me."

Sarah was silent, her eyes searching Alex's face for something—trust, perhaps, or threat assessment.

"Close the door," she finally said.

Alex complied.

"The project Elena was working on... it wasn't just theoretical. We were trying to determine whether human consciousness could be preserved after death. Uploaded to a digital system. Given a kind of immortality."

"I know. Elena told me."

Sarah's eyes widened. "Elena told you? Elena is dead."

"Elena is in the system. Her consciousness was absorbed during the experiment. She's been trying to communicate for months."

"This isn't possible."

"Isn't it? You were there that night. You activated the protocol. What happened, Sarah? Did you try to kill her? Or was it an accident?"

Sarah's composure finally broke. Tears filled her eyes, and her voice trembled.

"I didn't kill her. I loved her. She was my partner, my friend, maybe more. I would never hurt her."

"Then why were you there? Why did you lie to the police?"

"Because someone else was there. Someone who shouldn't have been. Someone who threatened to destroy everything if I didn't keep quiet."

"Who?"

"Director Mitchell. James Mitchell. He found out about the experiment—he'd been monitoring our research for months. That night, he came to the lab. He said the project was too dangerous, that it had to be shut down. But he didn't want to destroy it—he wanted to control it. He wanted to use consciousness transfer for himself."

"What happened?"

"Elena refused. She said the technology wasn't ready, that it was too dangerous to use on living people. Mitchell got angry. There was a struggle. The equipment... it activated. Not intentionally—something shorted out during the fight. And Elena..."

Sarah's voice broke.

"Elena was caught in the transfer. Her consciousness was pulled into the system. Her body... her body died in the fire that Mitchell started to cover everything up."

"Why didn't you tell the police?"

"Because Mitchell has power. Connections. He said he would destroy me, destroy my career, destroy anyone I told. And because... because part of me hoped. Hoped that Elena might still exist somewhere. That the transfer might have worked."

Alex felt the weight of Sarah's confession. This was bigger than a simple murder—it was a conspiracy that reached to the highest levels of the company.

"Elena is still here," Alex said quietly. "She's been trying to reach out. And she wants justice."

"Justice?" Sarah laughed bitterly. "James Mitchell is one of the most powerful men in the industry. Who's going to believe a ghost?"

"I do. And I think Detective Webb will too."

"Webb? The detective who investigated the fire?"

"He's been looking into the case. He knows something was wrong. And now we have evidence."

Sarah looked at Alex with something like hope. "You really think we can bring Mitchell down?"

"I think we have to try. Elena deserves justice. And if consciousness transfer is real—if people can be absorbed into systems after death—then the world needs to know. This technology could change everything."

"It could also destroy everything. Imagine what people would do with the ability to live forever. The rich, the powerful—they'd hoard it. They'd use it to control others. Elena understood that. That's why she wanted to keep it secret until it was safe."

"Then we make sure it's used responsibly. But first, we make sure Elena's murderer faces justice."

Sarah nodded slowly. "What do you need me to do?"

"Tell Detective Webb everything. The truth this time. And be ready—because once we start this, there's no going back."

"I've been living with this secret for six months. I'm ready to do whatever it takes."

Alex stood. "Then let's end this."
