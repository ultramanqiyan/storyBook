# Book Specification: The Programmed Heart

## Basic Information
- **Title**: The Programmed Heart: When Love Is Generated
- **Meta Description**: Sixteen-year-old Alex discovers her parents' perfect love is generated by an AI system. A haunting exploration of what makes love real, what we sacrifice for perfection, and whether artificial care can ever be enough.
- **Keywords**: programmed heart, MHA system, AI emotions, artificial love, neural interface, parental love, emotional technology, fake love, real emotions
- **Genre**: Science Fiction / Psychological Thriller / Young Adult
- **Target Audience**: 14-25 years, readers interested in AI ethics, family dynamics, and psychological thrillers
- **Theme**: Real vs Artificial Love, The Cost of Perfection, Identity and Memory, Freedom of Choice

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: First-person
- **POV Character(s)**: Alex Chen
- **Language Style**: Introspective, haunting, ultimately hopeful; short punchy sentences mixed with longer reflective passages
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Short sentences for action, longer for reflection; varied rhythm
- **Vocabulary Level**: Accessible YA level, with some technical terms (MHA, neural interface)
- **Unique Elements**: Sensory contrasts between "perfect" and "real" experiences; dual structure (present + memory flashbacks)

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Confusion, Betrayal, Longing, Understanding, Acceptance
- **Emotional Arc**: Chapters 1-2: Warmth and subtle wrongness (70→60) → Chapters 3-4: Discovery and confrontation (60→40) → Chapters 5-6: Escape and search (40→70) → Chapters 7-8: Understanding and complexity (70→80) → Chapters 9-10: Return and acceptance (80→80)
- **Emotional Pacing**: Setup (1-2) slow, Rising (3-4) medium, Climax (5-6) fast, Resolution (7-10) medium

## Character Voices

### Alex Chen (Protagonist)
- **Age**: 16
- **Speech Characteristics**: Introspective, questioning, gradually more assertive
- **Word Choice**: Simple but precise, increasingly philosophical
- **Sentence Patterns**: Short sentences for action, longer for reflection
- **Personality Traits**: Observant, sensitive, seeks truth, values authenticity
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Constant questioning, searching for meaning
- **Unique Expressions**: "What is real?" / "Perfect but wrong"
- **Speech Quirks**: Pauses before speaking, often starts with "I..."
- **Development Arc**: From content teen to someone seeking authentic truth while maintaining connection

### Parents (David & Sarah Chen)
- **Nature**: MHA System users (full system, no awareness)
- **Speaking Style**: Perfect, consistent, never varies
- **Word Choice**: Always appropriate, always loving, always the same
- **Sentence Patterns**: Measured, calm, no hesitation
- **Personality Traits**: Patient, kind, supportive - but generated by system
- **Conflict Style**: Cannot understand Alex's concerns—they have no awareness of system intervention

### Marcus
- **Age**: ~20
- **Speaking Style**: Casual, honest, experienced
- **Word Choice**: Direct, uses metaphors about tools and choices
- **Sentence Patterns**: Varied, natural, real
- **Personality Traits**: Uses partial MHA system, has found balance
- **Role**: Shows Alex that there are gray areas, that choice is possible

### Dr. Elena Rivera
- **Occupation**: Psychology professor, MHA researcher
- **Speaking Style**: Professional but warm, explanatory
- **Word Choice**: Scientific terms mixed with empathy
- **Personality Traits**: Knowledgeable, nuanced, non-judgmental
- **Role**: Provides context and history for the MHA system

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: Real vs Artificial Love - Can generated emotions still be meaningful?
- **Secondary Theme**: The Cost of Perfection - What do we lose when we eliminate human flaws?
- **Tertiary Theme**: Identity and Memory - Who are we without our real emotions?
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Through Alex's discovery that parental love is artificial; Through contrast between system users and non-users; Through Marcus's balanced approach

## World Building
- **Time Period**: Near-future (10-20 years from present)
- **Primary Location**: Suburban home, then city
- **Environment**: World where MHA system is common but controversial
- **Social Context**: Neural interface technology is normalized
- **Key Technologies**: MHA System (Mental Health Associates), neural interfaces, emotional pattern programming
- **Social Structure**: System users vs. non-users, partial users like Marcus
- **World Rules**: MHA System (Generates appropriate emotional responses based on situational analysis), Full System Users (No awareness of system intervention, believe emotions are their own), Partial Users (Can toggle system on/off, maintain awareness and choice), System Origins (Originally for severe mental health cases, expanded to everyday use)

## Sensory Detail Requirements

### Distribution Targets (Per Chapter)
- **Minimum 10 sensory details per chapter**
- **At least 5 different sensory types per chapter**
- **Visual/Auditory (40%), Tactile/Olfactory/Gustatory (30%), Emotional (30%)**

### Sensory Categories
- **Visual**: Perfect symmetry, identical smiles, flawless environments
- **Auditory**: Perfect voice modulation, lack of emotional tremor
- **Tactile**: Identical pressure in hugs, consistent warmth
- **Olfactory**: Perfect cookie smell at 4 PM, unchanging perfume
- **Gustatory**: Perfectly cooked meals, no variation in taste
- **Emotional**: The wrongness beneath perfection

## Emotional Depth Design

### Three-Layer Emotional Model (Required for Every Major Scene)
1. **Surface Reaction** - External behavior (e.g., "I smile at my mother's perfect breakfast")
2. **Internal Activity** - Thought process (e.g., "*Why does everything feel so... scripted?*")
3. **Deep Motivation** - Core desires (e.g., "The fear that I've never been truly loved")

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt afraid." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |

## Chapter Outline

### Chapter 1: The Perfect Family
Alex's family seems perfect. Subtle wrongness lurks beneath. Emotional arc: Contentment (70→60)

### Chapter 2: The Glitch
Friends' real emotions contrast with perfect family. Alex finds MHA card. Emotional arc: Confusion (55→50)

### Chapter 3: The Record
Alex finds documents and learns the truth about MHA. Emotional arc: Suspicion (45→30)

### Chapter 4: The Confrontation
Alex confronts parents but they can't understand. Emotional arc: Shock (35→40)

### Chapter 5: The Escape
Alex runs away to find something real. Emotional arc: Anger (45→70)

### Chapter 6: The Search
Alex meets Marcus who uses MHA differently. Emotional arc: Determination (65→55)

### Chapter 7: The Gray
Marcus shares his story about gray areas. Emotional arc: Confusion (50→75)

### Chapter 8: The History
Dr. Rivera explains MHA origins and context. Emotional arc: Understanding (70→80)

### Chapter 9: The Return
Alex returns home to say goodbye to the parents she thought she had. Emotional arc: Acceptance (75→70)

### Chapter 10: The Choice
Alex writes a letter and moves forward with hope. Emotional arc: Bittersweet (65→80)

## Open Ending Design

### Unresolved Questions
1. Will Alex ever find her "real" parents beneath the system?
2. Will society address the ethics of full-system usage?
3. Can artificial love ever be enough?

### New Suspense
- What would you do if you discovered your family's love was artificial?
- The courage to love imperfectly, to seek truth even when it hurts

### Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: Alex has chosen to seek her own truth while maintaining connection
- **Key Relationship**: With parents - broken but acknowledged
