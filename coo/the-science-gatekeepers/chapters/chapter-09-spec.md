## 4. Opening and Ending

### Opening
```
The archive was complete. Marcus stared at the encrypted files, the culmination of months of secret work. Thousands of scientific papers, preserved in their original form, ready to be released to anyone who wanted to see them. All he had to do was activate the distribution protocol.
```

### Ending
```
Marcus sat in the darkness of his home office, the simulation results glowing on his screen. The numbers were clear: if he released the archive, the sudden introduction of scientific uncertainty would destabilize society. People who had been taught that science was absolute truth would suddenly discover that everything they knew was provisional, questionable, uncertain. The chaos would be incalculable. There was no good choice. There was only the choice he would have to live with.
```

## 5. Key Revelation

### The Consequence
- People taught science as absolute truth would face cognitive crisis
- Scientific uncertainty would spread through society
- Institutions built on "settled science" would be questioned
- Social stability would be threatened

### The Moral Horror
- Truth might be more dangerous than lies
- Liberation might be worse than oppression
- There is no clean solution
