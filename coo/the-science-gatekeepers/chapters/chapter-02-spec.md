## 4. Opening and Ending

### Opening
```
The Shudra Education Center was a model of efficiency—clean lines, soft lighting, and rows of students absorbing knowledge through their neural interfaces. Marcus stood at the back of the classroom, observing a lesson on photosynthesis. The simplified version, of course. No mention of the complex biochemistry, the electron transport chain, the evolutionary history. Just: "Plants use sunlight to make food."
```

### Ending
```
Marcus left the Education Center with a heaviness in his chest. The student's question echoed in his mind: "But how do we know it's true?" The teacher's answer: "Because the system says so." That wasn't science. That was religion dressed in a lab coat. And somewhere in his fifteen years of curation, Marcus had helped make it possible.
```
