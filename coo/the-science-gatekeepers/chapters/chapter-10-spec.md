## 4. Opening and Ending

### Opening
```
Three days had passed since Marcus had discovered the consequences of his archive. Three days of carrying the knowledge like a stone in his chest, of looking at his work and seeing not liberation but potential chaos. The archive sat on his server, ready to deploy. All he had to do was decide.
```

### Ending
```
Marcus stood at the threshold, the archive ready, the truth preserved. On one side: scientific integrity, the right to question, the foundation of knowledge. On the other: social stability, the prevention of chaos, the system he had served. There was no right answer. There was only the choice he would make, and the world that would exist because of it.

He reached toward the interface. His fingers hovered over the activation key.

And then—

His daughter Sarah's voice echoed in his memory: "Dad, why do you always have to question everything?"

Marcus smiled bitterly. Because that's what science is, he thought. That's what it's always been.

He made his choice.
```
