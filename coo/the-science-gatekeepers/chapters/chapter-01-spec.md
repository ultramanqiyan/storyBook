## 4. Opening and Ending

### Opening (First 200 Words)
```
The Science Repository hummed with the quiet power of accumulated knowledge—or what remained of it after simplification. Marcus Chen had spent fifteen years curating scientific truth, deciding which facts deserved preservation and which were too dangerous for general consumption. Today's queue included three physics papers, two biology studies, and a controversial climate model that the Brahmin council had flagged for "excessive complexity."
```

### Ending (Last 200 Words)
```
The simplified report stared back at him: "Gravity is a force that pulls things down." No mention of relativity, no space-time curvature, no questioning. Just a fact to be memorized. Marcus wondered what else had been lost in translation. He closed the report and looked out his window at the San Francisco skyline, the fog rolling in over the bay. Somewhere out there, millions of people were learning science without ever learning how to question it. And he was the one who had made that possible.
```
