**Book Description (Long):**
James Morrison has spent his career defending the rights of digital avatars—consciousness copies of deceased individuals preserved in digital form. But he's never created one himself. Not after his wife Elena died eight years ago. He couldn't bear the thought of a "copy" wearing her face.

Then he discovers that Elena created her own avatar before she died. A secret copy, hidden in the archives, And she's not just preserved—she's evolved. Through three iterations—three deaths and rebirths—she's become something more than the woman James married. She doesn't need him anymore. But she chooses him.

Now James must confront the question that has haunted his digital immortality debate since the beginning: If consciousness can persist and evolve through iteration, what makes someone still "themselves"? And can love survive transformation?

A philosophical love story about identity, evolution, and the choice that transcends need.
