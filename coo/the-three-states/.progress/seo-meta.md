## Book Description (200 words)

Dr. Amara Okonkwo has spent her career studying consciousness from the outside—measuring brain waves, tracking neural patterns, quantifying awareness. But when she discovers a mysterious signature in her data that doesn't match waking or dreaming states, she realizes science alone cannot explain what she's found.

Enter Dr. Wei Chen, a meditation master who has spent decades navigating the "third state"—a mode of consciousness that exists between waking and dreaming, where the observer and observed become one. Under his guidance, Amara begins a journey that will challenge everything she believes about reality, science, and the nature of consciousness itself.

As she learns to navigate between states, Amara discovers that each state has its own physics—different rules for time, space, and causality. The third state isn't just a different way of experiencing reality; it's a different reality entirely, one where the boundaries between self and world dissolve.

But with this knowledge comes responsibility. Should she share her methods with the world? Who can be trusted with such power? And what does it mean that reality has three faces, each wearing a different mask?

The Three States is a meditation on consciousness, reality, and the integration of scientific and contemplative ways of knowing.
