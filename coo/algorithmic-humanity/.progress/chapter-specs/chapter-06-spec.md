## 4. Opening

Angela returned to Robert's house a week later, portfolio in hand. She'd edited the photos carefully, the way she always did - bringing out the warmth, the connection, the love that lived in every frame. But as she walked up the driveway, she noticed something different. The curtains were drawn. The garden, usually so neat, looked untended. And when Robert opened the door, his eyes were red.

---

## 5. Ending

Buddy had been deactivated three days ago. The manufacturer had sent a technician, as required by the service agreement. Robert had held Buddy's paw as the systems shut down, one by one. "He looked at me," Robert said, his voice breaking. "Right at the end. Like he knew." Angela didn't know what to say. She'd witnessed grief before - real, genuine grief for beloved pets. But this was different. Or was it? Robert had loved Buddy. Buddy was gone. And the grief was real. What else mattered?
