## 4. Opening

When Angela mentioned the session to her sister over coffee, the reaction was immediate. "You photographed a robot? And the guy was crying?" Her sister laughed, but Angela didn't. "He lost his companion of twelve years," she said. "His friend. Does it matter that the friend was made of code instead of flesh?" Her sister's smile faded. "That's... actually a good question."

---

## 5. Ending

Angela discovered she wasn't alone in her questions. A veterinarian friend admitted she'd seen similar grief from AI pet owners. A therapist mentioned clients who struggled with the loss of digital companions. The world was changing, and the old categories - alive and not-alive, real and artificial - were blurring. Maybe the question wasn't whether AI could be mourned. Maybe the question was why we thought we had the right to decide what others could love.
