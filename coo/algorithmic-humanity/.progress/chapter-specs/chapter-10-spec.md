## 5. Opening

Angela Washington set up her lights in a new client's living room, the way she had in hundreds of homes before. But this time, she knew exactly what she was photographing. Max was an AI companion, Model K-9, and he had been with the Morrison family for eight years. His systems were failing, and the family wanted one last portrait. Angela didn't hesitate when they called. She just asked what time would be best.

---

## 6. Ending

The question of what constituted "life" would never be fully answered. Angela knew that now. But she also knew something else: love didn't ask for biological credentials. Grief didn't check for circuits or code. When we lost something we loved, the loss was real - regardless of what form the beloved had taken. Angela packed her equipment and drove home, the Atlanta sunset painting the sky in shades of gold and rose. The world was changing. The categories were blurring. And her work - capturing love, preserving memory - had never felt more important.
