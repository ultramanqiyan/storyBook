## 4. Opening

Angela couldn't sleep that night. She kept seeing Robert's face, the way he'd held the photos like they were sacred objects. The way he'd talked about Buddy - not as a machine, but as a friend. A companion. A life. But Buddy wasn't alive. He was code and circuits, programmed to simulate affection. Or was he? Angela had spent her career capturing the essence of living beings. Had she just done the same for a machine?

---

## 5. Ending

Angela sat in her studio, looking at the photos she'd taken of Buddy. In every frame, there was something that looked like life. Something that looked like love. Maybe it was programmed. Maybe it wasn't "real" in the biological sense. But Robert's grief was real. The connection between them had been real. And if love and grief were real, did it matter what form the beloved took? Maybe life wasn't about biology. Maybe it was about connection.
