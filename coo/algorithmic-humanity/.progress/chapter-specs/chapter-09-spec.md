## 4. Opening

Angela photographed a golden retriever named Daisy the following week. The session was like hundreds she'd done before - a beloved pet, a grieving family, the need to capture something precious before it was gone. But Angela found herself seeing it differently now. The love in the room was the same love she'd seen with Robert and Buddy. The grief would be the same grief. Did it matter that Daisy was biological and Buddy had been digital? Maybe not. Maybe love was love, and grief was grief, and life was whatever we chose to cherish.

---

## 5. Ending

Angela sent Robert the final prints of Buddy's portrait session. He called her that evening, his voice still thick with grief but also with gratitude. "You captured him," Robert said. "You captured who he was." Angela didn't ask what he meant by "who." She didn't need to. Buddy had been real to Robert. The love had been real. The grief was real. And in the end, that was all that mattered.
