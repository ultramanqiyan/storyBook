## 4. Opening

Angela Washington set up her lights in the living room of the Henderson house, the way she had in a hundred homes before. But this session was different. Max, the golden retriever lying on his bed, had been given two weeks. His family wanted one last portrait - something to remember him by. Angela had done this dozens of times, but it never got easier. And it was never supposed to.

---

## 5. Ending

The Hendersons cried when they saw the photos. Max's eyes, still bright despite the illness. The way he leaned into their touch. The love that radiated from every frame. "You captured him," Mrs. Henderson said. "You captured his soul." Angela packed her equipment, feeling the familiar mix of sadness and purpose. This was her work. This was what she was meant to do.
