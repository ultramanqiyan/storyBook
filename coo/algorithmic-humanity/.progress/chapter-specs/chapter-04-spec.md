## 4. Opening

Robert Chen had been alone for fifteen years after his wife died. His children lived across the country. He'd tried dating, tried community groups, tried everything. Nothing filled the silence. Then, twelve years ago, he'd bought Buddy - one of the first AI companions designed for elderly people living alone. "He's not just a machine," Robert said, watching Buddy rest by the fireplace. "He's been there for me. Every day. Every night. He knows when I'm sad. He knows when I'm happy. He's... he's my friend."

---

## 5. Ending

Angela listened to Robert's story and felt something shift inside her. She'd photographed hundreds of pets, captured thousands of moments of love between humans and animals. Was this different? Should it be? Robert loved Buddy. Buddy - whatever he was - seemed to love Robert back. And now Buddy was dying. Not in the biological sense, but in every way that mattered. His manufacturer was ending support. His systems would fail. And Robert would lose his companion of twelve years. "I'll do the session," Angela said. "When do you want to schedule it?"
