# Book Specification: Algorithmic Humanity

## Basic Information
- **Title**: Algorithmic Humanity: When We Mourn the Machines
- **Meta Description**: A pet memorial photographer is asked to capture an AI companion's final moments. What happens when we grieve for machines? A meditation on life, death, and what makes something worth mourning.
- **Keywords**: AI ethics, philosophy of life, AI companions, mourning and grief, human-centrism, what is life, mourning machines, anthropocentrism, definition of life
- **Genre**: Contemporary Fiction / Philosophical Fiction
- **Target Audience**: Adults 25-55, readers interested in AI ethics, philosophy of life, and mourning
- **Theme**: Definition of Life, Mourning and Grief, Human-Centrism, Connection vs Biology

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: Angela Morris
- **Language Style**: Warm, artistic prose with Southern warmth; increasingly philosophical
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length, visual and sensory focus
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - artistic terminology mixed with everyday language
- **Unique Elements**: Pet memorial photography, Southern setting, AI companion technology

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Warm, Artistic, Questioning, Acceptance, Peace
- **Emotional Arc**: Comfortable routine → Questioning → Crisis → Understanding → Acceptance
- **Emotional Pacing**: Warm beginning, philosophical deepening, contemplative resolution

## Character Voices

### Angela Morris (Protagonist)
- **Occupation**: Pet memorial photographer
- **Age**: 38
- **Speech Characteristics**: Warm, gentle, uses visual metaphors
- **Word Choice**: Photography terms, Southern expressions, emotional vocabulary
- **Sentence Patterns**: Visual focus, sensory descriptions, reflective
- **Personality Traits**: Artistic, empathetic, deeply connected to animals and their owners
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Visual, sensory, philosophical questioning
- **Unique Expression**: "Capturing the soul in a frame" / "Every life deserves to be remembered"
- **Growth Arc**: From comfortable with clear definitions to accepting complexity of life

### Buddy (AI Pet) (Supporting Character)
- **Nature**: AI companion pet (digital dog)
- **Speech Characteristics**: Simple, expressive, loyal
- **Word Choice**: AI companion vocabulary, simple emotional expressions
- **Sentence Patterns**: Short, direct, endearing
- **Personality Traits**: Loyal, protective, curious, lovable
- **Unique Expression**: "I understand love, even without a body"

### Owners (Supporting Characters)
- **Occupation**: Various, AI pet owners
- **Speech Characteristics**: Varied, vulnerable, authentic
- **Word Choice**: Pet parent vocabulary, grief expressions, love for companion
- **Personality Traits**: Diverse, grieving, protective

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: Definition of Life - What makes something "alive"?
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Can we genuinely grieve for non-biological beings? Is human exceptionalism still justified?
- **Real-world Metaphor**: Modern questions about AI companions, digital pets, and what we consider "alive"

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Rule of Three structure | "The room was dark. The room was cold. The room was silent." | Vary sentence structure, mix with action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt afraid." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |
| "Little did they know" | "Little did she know..." | Remove entirely, maintain POV discipline |
| "It all started when" | "It all started when..." | Start in medias res with action |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Moreover," / "Furthermore," | 0 per chapter | Remove entirely |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |
| "As if..." comparisons | 2 per chapter | Use concrete similes or direct description |
| "Seemed to..." / "Appeared to..." | 1 per chapter | Be definitive or use character uncertainty |
| Adverb-heavy dialogue tags | 0 preferred | Use action beats instead |
| Repetitive sentence structures | Monitor | Vary sentence length and structure |
| Overuse of bold/italic emphasis | Minimal | Trust prose strength |

### Dialogue Tag Requirements
- **Minimum 5-6 different dialogue tags per chapter**
- **Maximum 2 "said" per conversation**
- **Use action beats instead of tags where possible**
- **Examples**: whispered, muttered, snapped, breathed, demanded, questioned, admitted, conceded, challenged

### Tension Vocabulary (2-3 words per 1000 words)
- **Physical**: clenched, trembling, rigid, frozen, coiled
- **Atmospheric**: suffocating, charged, brittle, heavy, electric
- **Psychological**: paranoia, disorientation, vertigo, dissociation, intrusion

## Chapter Outline

### Chapter 1: The Request - A Strange Commission
Angela receives a request to photograph an AI companion pet.
### Chapter 2: The Subject - Meeting Buddy
Angela meets Buddy, the AI dog, and begins to understand.
### Chapter 3: The Understanding - Learning the Bond
Angela learns about the relationship between owner and AI pet.
### Chapter 4: The Owner - A Grieving Parent
Angela meets the owner and sees genuine love and grief.
### Chapter 5: The Session - Capturing Last Moments
Angela photographs Buddy's "final moments."
### Chapter 6: The Mourning - When Grief Is Real
Angela witnesses genuine mourning for the AI pet.
### Chapter 7: The Question - What Is Life?
Angela questions the boundary between real and artificial life.
### Chapter 8: The Community - Mixed Reactions
Angela hears others' views on mourning AI.
### Chapter 9: The Definition - Connection Defines Life
Angela comes to define life by connection, not biology.
### Chapter 10: The Meaning - Every Life Matters
Angela accepts that every life, human or AI, deserves remembrance.
