## 2. Narrative Style

### 2.1 Perspective
- **Point of View**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: Angela Morris
- **POV Consistency**: Single POV throughout

### 2.2 Language Style
- **Overall Tone**: Warm, artistic prose with Southern warmth; increasingly philosophical
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length, visual and sensory focus
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - artistic terminology mixed with everyday language

## 4. Thematic Depth

### 4.1 Core Themes
1. **Definition of Life**: What makes something "alive"?
2. **Mourning**: Can we genuinely grieve for non-biological beings?
3. **Human-Centrism**: Is human exceptionalism still justified?

### 4.2 Philosophical Exploration
| Question | Exploration Approach |
|----------|---------------------|
| What defines a life worth mourning? | Angela photographs an AI "pet" for end-of-life memorial |
| Is human uniqueness still meaningful? | The genuine grief for an AI challenges human-centrism |
| What is the boundary of life? | The line between biological and digital life blurs |

1. Was the grief for the AI pet "real"?
