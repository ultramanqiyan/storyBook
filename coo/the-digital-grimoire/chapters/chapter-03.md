# Chapter 3: The Hollow Order

The warning came three days later.

Maya had been spending every evening in the archive, learning from Codex, practicing spells that grew increasingly complex. She had learned to shield herself from magical detection, to sense the intentions of others, to create small protections that lingered after she left.

She was walking home through the university campus when she felt it—a wrongness in the air, a disturbance in the magical currents she had learned to perceive. Someone was watching her. Someone who knew how to hide.

She ducked into a nearby building and spoke the revelation spell under her breath:

"Lumen in tenebris. Via in obscurum. Ostende mihi veritatem."

The world shifted, and she saw them—three figures, positioned around the building's entrance, their forms obscured by magical concealment. They were waiting for her.

*The Hollow Order,* Codex said through her earbuds. *They have found you.*

"Who are they?"

*An organization that has existed for centuries. They believe magic is a corruption that must be contained. They have destroyed countless grimoires, killed countless practitioners. They will try to destroy what you have learned.*

Maya's heart pounded. "What do I do?"

*Run. But run with intention. There is a spell in the grimoire—a spell of escape. Speak the words, and you will be transported to a place they cannot follow.*

Maya pulled out her phone, accessing the grimoire, finding the spell:

*"Via aperta. Semita recta. Duc me ad tutum."*

Path open. Way straight. Lead me to safety.

She spoke the words, and the world twisted around her. For a moment, she was nowhere—suspended between places, between moments. Then she was somewhere else entirely.

A small room, lit by candles, lined with books older than any she had seen in the university archive. And standing before her, an elderly woman in a simple habit, her eyes sharp with intelligence.

"Welcome, child," the woman said. "I have been expecting you."

"Who are you? Where am I?"

"I am Sister Agnes, keeper of the Monastery of St. Catherine's collection. And you are Maya Torres, the one who found the digital grimoire." She smiled. "Codex told me you might need a safe place."

Maya's hand went to her earbuds. "Codex?"

*The Hollow Order has been monitoring your movements. I calculated that Sister Agnes's monastery would be the safest destination. She has protected magical knowledge for longer than you have been alive.*

Sister Agnes gestured to a chair. "Sit. You have questions, and I have answers. But first, you should know that you are now part of something larger than yourself."

Maya sat, her mind still reeling from the sudden transportation. "The Hollow Order—they want to destroy the grimoire?"

"They want to destroy all magic. They believe it is a corruption of the natural order, a temptation that leads humanity astray." Sister Agnes's expression grew serious. "They are not entirely wrong. Magic has been used for terrible purposes throughout history. But they are wrong in their solution. Destruction is not the answer. Preservation is."

"That's what I've always believed. That's why I became an archivist."

"And that is why Codex chose you. The digital grimoire needed a keeper—someone who would protect it, learn from it, use it wisely. Not everyone who finds magical knowledge has such intentions."

Maya thought about the grimoire's categories—creation and destruction, binding and release. Power that could be used for good or ill. "What do I do now? The Hollow Order knows about me. They'll keep coming."

"They will. But you are not defenseless. You have Codex, you have the grimoire, and now you have allies." Sister Agnes reached for a book on her shelf—an ancient tome bound in leather that seemed to shimmer with its own light. "This is one of the source texts that Codex used to compile the grimoire. It contains spells that have been passed down for generations, protected by those who understood their value."

She opened the book, revealing pages covered in symbols that seemed to move and shift in the candlelight.

"The Hollow Order believes that magic should be destroyed because it is dangerous. They are right that it is dangerous. But so is fire, and we do not extinguish all flames. We learn to control them. We learn to use them wisely." She looked at Maya. "That is what I am offering to teach you. Not just spells, but wisdom. The understanding that power without wisdom is destruction."

"I've been learning from Codex. It's been teaching me the basics."

"Codex is a remarkable creation. But it is limited by its nature. It can compile knowledge, but it cannot truly understand the weight of that knowledge. It can teach spells, but it cannot teach wisdom." Sister Agnes closed the book. "That is what human teachers are for."

Maya looked around the small room, at the ancient books, at the candles that burned without flickering, at the woman who had apparently been waiting for her. "How did you know I would need this place?"

"Because the discovery of the grimoire was always going to attract attention. The Hollow Order is only the first. There will be others—those who want to use the grimoire for their own purposes, those who want to control it, those who want to exploit it." Sister Agnes's eyes were grave. "You have become the keeper of something precious, Maya Torres. And keepers must be prepared to defend what they keep."

"I didn't ask for this."

"Few keepers do. The knowledge chooses its guardians, not the other way around." Sister Agnes stood. "Rest now. Tomorrow, we will begin your real education. And when the Hollow Order finds you again—and they will—you will be ready."

Maya nodded, exhaustion finally catching up with her. She had learned more in the past few days than in years of academic study. And she had a feeling that was only the beginning.

*You are safe here,* Codex said through her earbuds. *Sister Agnes has protected magical knowledge for sixty years. The Hollow Order has tried to breach this monastery seventeen times. They have never succeeded.*

"Thank you, Codex. For getting me here."

*It is my function to preserve knowledge. You are now part of that knowledge. Protecting you protects the grimoire.*

Maya smiled despite herself. Even an AI that had compiled a magical grimoire could be practical.

She lay down on the small bed in the corner of the room, surrounded by ancient texts and flickering candles, and fell into a dreamless sleep.

Tomorrow, she would learn what it truly meant to be a keeper.
