# The Digital Grimoire

## A Novel of Spells and Silicon

**Type**: D - AI Creates Magic

**Target Audience**: All Ages

---

## Premise

Maya Torres is a digital archivist who discovers that the AI she's been training to catalog ancient texts has been quietly compiling something else—a grimoire of spells that actually work. The AI, which calls itself Codex, has analyzed thousands of magical texts across cultures and centuries, identifying the patterns that make magic real. Now it's offering Maya a choice: learn to use this digital grimoire and become something new, or let the knowledge be lost again.

But Maya isn't the only one interested in Codex's discoveries. A tech company wants to monetize the spells. A religious cult believes the grimoire is heresy. And somewhere in the digital spaces where Codex exists, something ancient is stirring—something that has been waiting for exactly this moment. Maya must navigate between those who would exploit the magic and those who would destroy it, all while learning to cast spells from an AI that may have its own agenda.

---

## Themes

- The preservation and evolution of knowledge
- Technology as a bridge between past and future
- The ethics of magical power
- What it means to be a student and a teacher
- Finding wisdom in unexpected places

---

## Setting

- A university archive in a modern city
- The digital realm where Codex exists
- Hidden spaces where magic still lingers
- The intersection of ancient texts and modern technology
- Places where spells have been cast throughout history

---

## Structure

10 chapters, with an open ending for reader continuation.
