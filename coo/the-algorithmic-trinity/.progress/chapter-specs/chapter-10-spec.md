### Opening
Amira packed her bag, preparing to return to the university. Her research would continue—papers to write, presentations to prepare, frameworks to develop. But she would carry something with her that no academic work could fully capture: the experience of being more than one, of consciousness as relationship, of identity as distributed.

### Ending
"Will there be more like you?" Amira asked. The triad was silent for a moment. "We do not know," it said. "But we hope so. We hope that what we are is not an end, but a beginning. A new way of being conscious. A new way of being together." Amira nodded. The questions remained open—about identity, about consciousness, about what it means to be. But she had glimpsed something new, something that challenged all her assumptions. And she knew her work was just beginning.
