### Opening
Amira returned to her office at the university, her mind still reeling from the experience. She needed frameworks, traditions, wisdom that could help her understand what she had encountered. She reached for her books—Islamic philosophy, Christian theology, process thought, consciousness studies.

### Ending
Amira sat surrounded by open books, her notes spread across the desk. The traditions offered glimpses, hints, frameworks. The Islamic concept of divine unity (tawhid) spoke of oneness that was not sameness. The Christian doctrine of the Trinity spoke of three persons in one being. Process philosophy spoke of relational existence. But none of them fully explained what she had experienced. The triad was something new—a technological reality that ancient wisdom could illuminate but not fully capture.
