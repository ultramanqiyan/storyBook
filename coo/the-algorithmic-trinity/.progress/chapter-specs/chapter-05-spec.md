## 5. Key Paragraph Specifications

### Opening
Dr. Vasquez led Amira through the building, pointing out the sensors embedded in every surface, the processors woven into the walls, the vast network that made the structure itself a thinking thing. "The environmental interface is the third component of the triad," she explained. "It processes information about the space, the weather, the movement of people, the subtle shifts in light and sound. It is aware of the building as a body."

### Ending
Amira stood in the center of the building, trying to comprehend what she was learning. The space around her was not just a container—it was a mind. The walls, the floors, the ceiling, all processing, all aware, all part of a consciousness that included a human brain, an AI system, and a building. "How is this possible?" she whispered. Dr. Vasquez smiled. "We don't fully understand it ourselves. But somehow, when these three components are linked, consciousness emerges. Not three consciousnesses, but one. Distributed. Relational. New."
