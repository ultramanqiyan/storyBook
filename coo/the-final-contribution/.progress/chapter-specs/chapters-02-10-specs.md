## Chapter 2: The News - When Efficiency Became a Layoff

### Positioning
- **Location in Book**: Setup/Rising action
- **Narrative Function**: Introduce the external threat
- **Relationship to Previous**: Robert's security is challenged
- **Relationship to Next**: Prompts investigation

### Emotional Arc
| Start | End | Key Beats |
|-------|-----|-----------|
| Contentment (72/100) | Unease (60/100) | The announcement → Colleagues leaving → First fear |

### Core Events
1. Company announces AI integration initiative
2. The initiative will replace 30% of workforce
3. Robert's department is "safe for now" but the writing is on the wall
4. Robert sees colleagues packing boxes, the first of many
5. Robert begins to question his three-month timeline

### Key Sensory Details
- Visual: Empty desks where colleagues used to sit
- Auditory: The silence after the announcement
- Tactile: The weight of his own box, just in case
- Internal: The first real fear
- Temporal: Three months suddenly feeling very short

### Thematic Focus
- **The Social Contract**: The company's "loyalty" revealed as conditional
- **The Meaning of Work**: What happens when work is "efficient" but people are displaced?

### Opening Hook
The email arrived at 8:47 AM: "All staff meeting, 9:00 AM, main auditorium. Attendance mandatory."

### Ending Transition
Robert walked to his car, the empty parking spaces beside him like missing teeth. Three months. Would it be enough?

## Chapter 5: The Meeting - Where Numbers Become People

### Positioning
- **Location in Book**: Rising action
- **Narrative Function**: The system speaks
- **Relationship to Previous**: Personal crisis meets institutional response
- **Relationship to Next**: Leads to the choice

### Emotional Arc
| Start | End | Key Beats |
|-------|-----|-----------|
| Guilt (40/100) | Conflict (30/100) | HR meeting → The offer → Moral paralysis |

### Core Events
1. Robert is called to HR for an "early retirement opportunity"
2. Eleanor, the HR director, presents the offer: retire now with full benefits
3. The subtext: the company wants to avoid paying his full pension
4. Eleanor's eyes reveal she knows the system is failing
5. Robert realizes: taking the offer means abandoning others who will need the system

### Key Sensory Details
- Visual: Eleanor's tired face, the offer document, the conference room
- Auditory: Eleanor's measured voice, the hum of fluorescent lights
- Tactile: The paper, cold from the air conditioning
- Internal: Conflict, paralysis
- Temporal: The deadline, approaching

### Thematic Focus
- **The Social Contract**: The company's "offer" vs. the implied contract
- **Intergenerational Justice**: Taking now vs. leaving something for others

### Opening Hook
Eleanor Chen had been in HR for twenty-three years. She'd delivered layoffs, consolations, and early retirement packages with the same professional calm. Today, her hands trembled slightly as she passed the folder across the desk.

### Ending Transition
Robert walked out of the building, the folder under his arm. He had three days to decide. Three days to choose between his security and his principles.
