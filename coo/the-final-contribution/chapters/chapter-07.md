Robert walked to his office and closed the door.

Forty years. He'd spent forty years in this building, watching it change from a place of bustle and ambition to... this. A place where people came to wait for the end.

He sat at his desk and looked at the photographs on the wall. His first day, 1984. The company Christmas party, 1991. The day he made partner, 1998. Each image a frozen moment from a world that no longer existed.

*Let me tell you what it was like*, he thought. *Let me remember.*

Work was work, but it was also something more. It was belonging. It was purpose. It was the knowledge that what you did mattered to someone.


1998. The day he made partner. He was thirty-six years old, standing in Harold's office, trying not to cry.

"You earned this, Robert. You've been with us for fourteen years. You've built relationships, solved problems, made this company better. You're one of us now."

One of us. The words had meant everything. He wasn't just an employee anymore. He was an owner, a stakeholder, a person with a voice in the company's future. He'd worked his whole life for this moment, and here it was, real and solid and his.

The knock on his door was soft. He looked up to see Janet standing in the doorway, her eyes red.

"Of course. Come in."

She sat in the chair across from his desk. They'd started at Morrison & Associates the same year, worked side by side for four decades. She was one of the few people left who remembered Harold, who remembered what the company used to be.

"I got the letter this morning," she said. "My position is being eliminated. They're giving me two weeks' notice."

"I've been thinking about it a lot lately. About what he said. About what this place used to be." She paused. "It wasn't perfect, you know. We worked too hard. We sacrificed too much. But at least it felt like something. At least we mattered to each other."
