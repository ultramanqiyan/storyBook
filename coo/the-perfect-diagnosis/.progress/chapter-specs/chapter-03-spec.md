## 1. Chapter Positioning

### 1.1 Position in Book
- **Overall Location**: Setup (end)

**Primary Objective**: Sarah confronts the reality of what she's lost through a concrete test

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
She couldn't remember. Sarah lay in bed, staring at the ceiling, trying to recall a single diagnostic decision she had made in the past eighteen months that hadn't come from MEDICA. The harder she tried, the more the memories slipped away, like water through her fingers. She remembered the outcomes—correct diagnoses, successful treatments, the green checkmarks on her performance reviews. But the thinking itself? The moment of insight, the flash of intuition, the slow accumulation of evidence leading to a conclusion? Nothing. Just the soft hum of AI in her earpiece, telling her what to do.

She opened the window, letting fresh air into the stale apartment. Somewhere, buried under layers of AI assistance, the doctor she used to be might still exist. She just had to find her. The thought was terrifying—what if there was nothing left?—but it was also the first real thought she'd had in eighteen months that hadn't come from a machine. She would find a way back. She had to. Because the alternative was to accept that Dr. Sarah Chen, the doctor who once could diagnose appendicitis without a second thought, was gone forever.
