### 2.3 Emotional Beats Breakdown

#### Beat 1: Confidence (Position: 0-15%)
- **Trigger**: Reviewing her perfect diagnostic record
- **Surface Reaction**: Checking statistics on tablet, slight smile
- **Internal Process**: "*Another perfect month. The algorithm knows what it's doing.*"
- **Deep Motivation**: Need for validation and control after years of uncertainty
- **Sensory Anchor**: The cool glass of the tablet against her fingers

#### Beat 2: Pride (Position: 15-30%)
- **Trigger**: Dr. Chen's congratulatory comment in the hallway
- **Surface Reaction**: Nodding, accepting praise gracefully
- **Internal Process**: "*They don't understand how easy it is now. But I shouldn't tell them that.*"
- **Deep Motivation**: Relief that she no longer has to struggle with uncertainty
- **Sensory Anchor**: The hum of the hospital's AI systems around her

#### Beat 3: Unease (Position: 30-50%)
- **Trigger**: Dr. Webb's probing question about clinical intuition
- **Surface Reaction**: Dismissing the question, checking her tablet
- **Internal Process**: "*Why does he always ask about the old ways? The system is better.*"
- **Deep Motivation**: Suppressed fear that she's lost something important
- **Sensory Anchor**: The weight of Dr. Webb's gaze, the ticking clock

#### Beat 4: Panic (Position: 50-75%)
- **Trigger**: System failure during critical surgery
- **Surface Reaction**: Frozen, reaching for tablet, nothing works
- **Internal Process**: "*What do I do? What do I do? The numbers aren't there.*"
- **Deep Motivation**: Terror of being exposed as incapable without AI
- **Sensory Anchor**: Cold sweat, racing heartbeat, silence where AI voice should be

#### Beat 5: Helplessness (Position: 75-100%)
- **Trigger**: Realizing she cannot make a basic clinical decision
- **Surface Reaction**: Staring at the patient, hands trembling
- **Internal Process**: "*I don't know. I don't know. I used to know.*"
- **Deep Motivation**: Shame and fear of professional inadequacy
- **Sensory Anchor**: The sterile silence, the absence of guidance

### 3.2 Event Descriptions

#### Event 1: Perfect Record Review
- **Description**: Sarah reviews her diagnostic statistics showing 100% accuracy for 18 months
- **Setup**: Morning routine, checking metrics before rounds
- **Execution**: Tablet displays perfect scores, she nods with satisfaction
- **Consequence**: Establishes her complete reliance on AI
- **Connection to Theme**: Shows the "hollow perfection" of algorithm-dependent medicine

#### Event 2: Colleague Interaction
- **Description**: Dr. Chen congratulates Sarah on her record
- **Setup**: Passing in hallway during rounds
- **Execution**: Brief exchange highlighting the new "normal" of AI medicine
- **Consequence**: Shows how the medical culture has shifted
- **Connection to Theme**: External validation of the system

#### Event 3: Mentor's Warning
- **Description**: Dr. Webb asks Sarah a question about clinical intuition
- **Setup**: Sarah stops by his office for a consult
- **Execution**: He asks what she would do without the system; she deflects
- **Consequence**: Plants seed of doubt about her abilities
- **Connection to Theme**: The old guard vs. new generation

#### Event 4: Emergency Surgery
- **Description**: Sarah is called into emergency surgery
- **Setup**: Critical patient, unexpected complications
- **Execution**: Routine procedure until system glitch warning
- **Consequence**: Sets up the crisis
- **Connection to Theme**: Over-reliance on technology in critical moments

#### Event 5: System Failure
- **Description**: MEDICA-AI goes offline during surgery
- **Setup**: System glitch warning, then complete shutdown
- **Execution**: Sarah freezes, cannot make basic decisions
- **Consequence**: Patient survives by luck, Sarah is shaken
- **Connection to Theme**: The cost of outsourcing clinical judgment

### 6.2 Dialogue Segment Specifications

#### Segment 1: Hallway Validation

**Participants**: Sarah Chen, Dr. James Chen

**Surface Content**: Congratulating Sarah on her record

**Subtext**: Reinforcing the new normal of AI-dependent medicine

**Emotional Undercurrent**:
- Sarah: Pride mixed with hollowness
- Dr. Chen: Genuine admiration

**Power Dynamics**: Equal colleagues

**Dialogue Beats**:
1. Dr. Chen: "Another perfect month, Sarah?"
2. Sarah: "The system makes it easy."
3. Dr. Chen: "Still, 18 months. That's unprecedented."
4. Sarah: "Anyone could do it with MEDICA."
5. Dr. Chen: "Not anyone. You're the model for the new program."

**Action Beats Required**: Checking tablets, walking, nodding

**What Must Be Revealed**: The culture has shifted to AI dependency

**What Must Be Concealed**: Sarah's growing hollowness

**Forbidden Expressions**:
- [ ] "What do you mean?"
- [ ] "You know what I mean"
- [ ] Direct emotion statements ("I feel...")
- [ ] Over-explanation
- [ ] Perfect responses

#### Segment 2: Mentor's Question

**Participants**: Sarah Chen, Dr. Marcus Webb

**Surface Content**: Discussing a patient case

**Subtext**: Dr. Webb testing Sarah's clinical intuition

**Emotional Undercurrent**:
- Sarah: Defensiveness, unease
- Dr. Webb: Concern, patience

**Power Dynamics**: Mentor-student, shifting to equals

**Dialogue Beats**:
1. Dr. Webb: "What does your gut tell you about Mr. Henderson?"
2. Sarah: "My gut? MEDICA says his labs are low concern."
3. Dr. Webb: "But what do you think?"
4. Sarah: "I think the algorithm is more reliable than intuition."
5. Dr. Webb: "Is it?"

**Action Beats Required**: Sarah checking tablet, Dr. Webb watching her

**What Must Be Revealed**: Sarah has lost touch with clinical intuition

**What Must Be Concealed**: Dr. Webb's deeper concerns about the system

#### Segment 3: OR Crisis

**Participants**: Sarah, anesthesiologist, surgical nurse

**Surface Content**: Managing the surgery during system failure

**Subtext**: Sarah's complete inability to function without AI

**Emotional Undercurrent**:
- Sarah: Panic, helplessness
- Team: Confusion, concern

**Power Dynamics**: Sarah losing authority

**Dialogue Beats**:
1. Anesthesiologist: "Dr. Chen, vitals are dropping. What's the call?"
2. Sarah: "I need... let me check the system."
3. Nurse: "System's down. What do we do?"
4. Sarah: [silence, frozen]
5. Anesthesiologist: "Dr. Chen?"

**Action Beats Required**: Monitoring equipment, surgical instruments, frozen hands

**What Must Be Revealed**: Sarah cannot make decisions without AI

**What Must Be Concealed**: How the patient actually survives (luck/intervention)

The tablet buzzed with a new notification. She ignored it. The question Dr. Webb had asked echoed in her mind: What would you do if the system went down? Now she knew. Nothing. She would do nothing.
