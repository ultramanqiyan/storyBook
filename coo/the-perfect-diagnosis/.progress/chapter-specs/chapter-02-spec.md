## 1. Chapter Positioning

### 1.1 Position in Book
- **Overall Location**: Setup
- **Narrative Function**: Sarah confronts her helplessness and begins to understand the depth of her dependency
- **Relationship to Previous Chapter**: Continues from Sarah's realization that she cannot function without AI
- **Relationship to Next Chapter**: Sets up her discovery of what she's lost

### 1.2 Chapter Purpose
**Primary Objective**: Show Sarah's attempt to diagnose without AI and her complete failure

**Secondary Objectives**:
- Deepen the crisis by showing multiple failed attempts
- Introduce the hospital's response to her "malfunction"
- Show the contrast between her past self and present self
- Plant seeds about the "resistance" of doctors who maintain old ways

### 1.3 Key Questions Answered/Raised
- **Answered**: How does Sarah function (or not) after the system failure?
- **Raised**: Is her condition unique or widespread? Can she recover what she's lost?

- **Description**: Sarah attempts to convince herself the incident was a one-time glitch
- **Setup**: Waking up, checking tablet obsessively
- **Execution**: Going through morning routine, avoiding thinking about what happened
- **Consequence**: Sets up false hope before the fall
- **Connection to Theme**: Denial as a stage of confronting dependency

#### Event 2: Failed Diagnosis
- **Description**: Sarah attempts to diagnose a simple case without AI and fails completely
- **Setup**: Routine patient, straightforward symptoms
- **Execution**: She stares at the patient, unable to form a diagnostic thought
- **Consequence**: Humiliation and deeper understanding of her condition
- **Connection to Theme**: The depth of skill atrophy from AI dependency

#### Event 3: Meeting with Dr. Cross
- **Description**: Sarah is called to discuss the OR incident
- **Setup**: Formal meeting, efficiency-focused discussion
- **Execution**: Dr. Cross frames it as a "system issue" not Sarah's fault
- **Consequence**: External validation but internal terror
- **Connection to Theme**: The system protects its own

#### Event 4: Colleague Distancing
- **Description**: Sarah notices colleagues treating her differently
- **Setup**: Passing interactions, subtle changes in behavior
- **Execution**: People avoid eye contact, conversations stop when she approaches
- **Consequence**: Social isolation deepens her crisis

#### Event 5: Night Shift Alone
- **Description**: Sarah takes a night shift to avoid people
- **Setup**: Empty hospital at night, quiet corridors
- **Execution**: She confronts her dependency, reaches for tablet again

### 6.2 Dialogue Segment Specifications

#### Segment 1: Failed Diagnosis

**Participants**: Sarah Chen, Mr. Patterson

**Surface Content**: Attempting to diagnose a cough

**Subtext**: Sarah cannot access basic clinical knowledge

**Emotional Undercurrent**:
- Sarah: Panic, shame
- Mr. Patterson: Confusion, concern

**Power Dynamics**: Doctor-patient, but Sarah has lost authority

**Dialogue Beats**:
1. Sarah: "So, Mr. Patterson, how long have you had this cough?"
2. Mr. Patterson: "About a week now. Started after I got over that cold."
3. Sarah: [staring at tablet, waiting for AI] "And... have you noticed any other symptoms?"
4. Mr. Patterson: "Just the cough. Doctor, are you alright?"
5. Sarah: "I need to... consult with a colleague. I'll be right back."

**Action Beats Required**: Checking tablet, avoiding eye contact, stammering

**What Must Be Revealed**: Sarah cannot function without AI

**What Must Be Concealed**: The depth of her condition

#### Segment 2: System Protection

**Participants**: Sarah Chen, Dr. Helena Cross

**Surface Content**: Discussing the OR incident

**Subtext**: The system protects itself, not Sarah

**Emotional Undercurrent**:
- Sarah: Terror masked by professionalism
- Dr. Cross: Efficient, detached

**Power Dynamics**: Clear hierarchy, Sarah is expendable

**Dialogue Beats**:
1. Dr. Cross: "The incident report has been filed. System malfunction."
2. Sarah: "I... should have been able to—"
3. Dr. Cross: "The system failed, Dr. Chen. Not you. That's the official position."
4. Sarah: "But I couldn't—"
5. Dr. Cross: "We'll monitor the situation. The system has been updated. It won't happen again."

**Action Beats Required**: Tablet checking, professional posture, suppressed trembling

**What Must Be Revealed**: The hospital values the system over individual doctors

**What Must Be Concealed**: The full extent of Sarah's dependency

#### Segment 3: Awkward Avoidance

**Participants**: Sarah Chen, Dr. James Chen

**Surface Content**: Brief hallway encounter

**Subtext**: Both know something is wrong, neither will say it

**Emotional Undercurrent**:
- Sarah: Shame, isolation
- Dr. Chen: Awkwardness, concern he can't express

**Power Dynamics**: Former equals, now uncomfortable

**Dialogue Beats**:
1. Dr. Chen: "Sarah. How are you... holding up?"
2. Sarah: "Fine. Everything's fine."
3. Dr. Chen: "The system's been updated. Should be... smoother now."
4. Sarah: "Right. Good."
5. Dr. Chen: [nods, walks away quickly]

**Action Beats Required**: Avoiding eye contact, quick exit

**What Must Be Revealed**: Word has spread, relationships have changed

**What Must Be Concealed**: The depth of concern or judgment

- [ ] Subtext is present in every exchange
