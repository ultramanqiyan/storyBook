## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Colleague (500 words)
**Location**: Nexus Technologies, open office
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus observes Alex working on a strategic project
- Alex drives himself, sets his own direction
- He doesn't use FocusZone for everything
- He has personal goals, sustained effort
- The contrast with Marcus's dependency

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Alex's focused work, his own direction
- Auditory: Alex's confident voice in meetings
- Internal: Observation, the first comparison

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Alex is driving his own project. He's not waiting for AI direction."
- Alex (in meeting): "I've been working on this initiative for three months. Here's my progress..."
- Marcus (internal): "Three months of sustained effort. Without FocusZone telling him what to do."
- Marcus (internal): "Could I do that? Could I drive something for three months without AI?"

### Scene 2: The Contrast (700 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Throughout the day

**Key Events**:
- Marcus compares himself to Alex
- Alex: self-directed, sustained effort, personal goals
- Marcus: AI-directed, task-focused, no personal vision
- The contrast is stark
- The shame begins
- The denial cracks

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Alex's work, his own empty strategic project
- Auditory: Silence, the weight of comparison
- Internal: Shame, the cracking denial

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Alex has a vision. He's driving toward something he chose."
- Marcus (internal): "I have no vision. I'm following what FocusZone tells me."
- Marcus (internal): "Alex sustained effort for three months. I couldn't sustain effort for three weeks."
- Marcus (internal): "What's the difference between us? Why can he do it and I can't?"
- Marcus (internal): "The difference is... he hasn't surrendered his will. I have."
- Marcus (internal): "Is that true? Have I really surrendered my will?"

### Scene 3: The Conversation (500 words)
**Location**: Break room
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus talks to Alex about his project
- "How do you stay motivated for so long?"
- Alex's answer: "I care about the outcome. It matters to me."
- The question: "What matters to you?"
- Marcus can't answer
- The exposure of his emptiness

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Break room, Alex's face
- Auditory: Alex's voice, Marcus's uncertain responses
- Internal: The exposure, the emptiness

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "Alex, your project is impressive. How do you sustain effort for so long?"
- Alex: "I care about the outcome. This matters to me. I want to see it succeed."
- Marcus: "But how do you stay focused without... external direction?"
- Alex: "I set my own direction. I decide what matters. Then I pursue it."
- Alex: "What matters to you, Marcus? What are you driving toward?"
- Marcus: "I... I'm not sure. I'm focused on productivity. Efficiency."
- Alex: "Those aren't goals. Those are methods. What do you actually want?"
- Marcus (internal): "I don't know. I don't know what I want."

### Scene 4: The Sarah Mirror (500 words)
**Location**: Home, living room
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Marcus tells Sarah about Alex
- She sees the comparison
- "You're seeing what you've lost"
- Marcus's denial weakens
- "Maybe I have surrendered something"
- The first honest admission

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Living room, Sarah's face
- Auditory: Sarah's voice
- Internal: The weakening denial, the admission

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "I talked to Alex today. He's driving his own project. He has... drive."
- Sarah: "And you noticed the difference."
- Marcus: "Yes. He has something I don't. Direction. Will. Something."
- Sarah: "You're seeing what you've lost, Marcus."
- Marcus: "Maybe I have surrendered something. Maybe it's not just efficiency."
- Sarah: "That's the first honest thing you've said about this."
- Marcus: "I don't know if I can get it back. I don't know if I want to."

### Scene 5: The Night (400 words)
**Location**: Home, bedroom
**Time**: Night

**Key Events**:

- The question: "What do I actually want?"
- The emptiness: he doesn't know

**Sensory Requirements**:
