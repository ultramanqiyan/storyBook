## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Pressure (500 words)
**Location**: Nexus Technologies, Marcus's office
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus faces multiple competing priorities
- The pressure to be productive
- The overwhelm of decision-making
- The question: "What should I focus on first?"
- The weight of self-direction

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Multiple screens, task lists, calendar
- Auditory: Notifications, keyboard sounds
- Internal: Pressure, overwhelm, the weight of choice

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "So many priorities. Which one matters most?"
- Marcus (internal): "I need to decide. I need to choose. I need to focus."
- Marcus (internal): "But every choice feels wrong. Every priority competes."

### Scene 2: The Introduction (600 words)
**Location**: Company meeting
**Time**: Mid-morning

**Key Events**:
- Jennifer announces FocusZone deployment
- The promise: "AI that helps you prioritize and focus"
- The demo: FocusZone analyzing tasks, suggesting priorities
- Marcus's initial skepticism: "I can decide for myself"
- The pressure: "Everyone else is using it"
- The temptation: "What if it could help?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Meeting room, FocusZone demo, faces
- Auditory: Jennifer's presentation, audience reactions
- Internal: Skepticism, temptation

**Character Voices**:
- Jennifer: "FocusZone represents the next evolution in productivity. It doesn't just track your tasks - it helps you decide what matters."
- FocusZone demo: "Based on your goals and deadlines, I recommend focusing on Project Alpha first..."
- Marcus (internal): "I don't need AI to tell me what to do. I can decide for myself."
- Colleague: "This is going to save so much time. No more decision fatigue."
- Marcus (internal): "Decision fatigue. Is that what I have?"

### Scene 3: First Use (600 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus opens FocusZone for the first time
- He inputs his tasks and priorities
- FocusZone analyzes and suggests a focus order
- Marcus's surprise: the suggestions are good
- He follows the AI's direction
- The satisfaction: "This is efficient"
- The first surrender: "Let the AI decide"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: FocusZone interface, task lists
- Tactile: Keyboard, mouse
- Internal: Surprise, satisfaction, the first surrender

**Character Voices**:
- FocusZone (interface): "Analyzing your priorities... Recommended focus: Project Alpha, then Team Meeting, then Code Review..."
- Marcus (internal): "That's actually a good order. Better than what I would have chosen."
- Marcus: "Okay. I'll follow this."
- Marcus (internal): "It feels good not to decide. It feels good to just... follow."
- Marcus (internal): "Is this efficiency? Or is this surrender?"

### Scene 4: The Efficiency (500 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: End of day

**Key Events**:
- Marcus has completed more than usual
- The satisfaction of productivity
- The praise from Jennifer: "You're more focused lately"
- The rationalization: "I'm using tools effectively"
- The seed: "What if AI could help with more decisions?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Completed tasks, satisfied expression
- Auditory: Jennifer's praise
- Internal: Satisfaction, the seed of dependency

**Character Voices**:
- Jennifer: "Marcus, you seem more focused lately. Good work."
- Marcus: "Thanks. I've been... prioritizing better."
- Marcus (internal): "I haven't been prioritizing. FocusZone has."
- Marcus (internal): "But that's okay. It's just a tool. I'm still in control."

### Scene 5: The Evening (400 words)
**Location**: Home, with Sarah
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Marcus tells Sarah about FocusZone
- He describes his productive day
- Sarah's question: "But what did YOU want to focus on?"
- Marcus's pause: "I... wanted to be productive"
- The first hint: he didn't choose, he followed

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Home, Sarah's face
- Auditory: Sarah's voice, home sounds
- Internal: The first doubt

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "We got this new tool at work. FocusZone. It helps me prioritize."
- Sarah: "That sounds helpful. What did it have you focus on today?"
- Marcus: "Project Alpha, then meetings, then code review."
- Sarah: "But what did YOU want to focus on? What was your priority?"
- Marcus: "I... wanted to be productive. That was my priority."
- Sarah: "But what about what matters to you? Not just what's efficient."
- Marcus (internal): "What matters to me? I don't... I don't know anymore."
