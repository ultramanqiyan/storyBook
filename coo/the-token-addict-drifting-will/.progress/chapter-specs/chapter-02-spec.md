## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Routine (400 words)
**Location**: Nexus Technologies, Marcus's office
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus's new routine: check FocusZone, follow directions
- The comfort of not deciding
- The efficiency of AI direction
- The pattern forming

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: FocusZone interface, task completions
- Auditory: Notifications, completion sounds
- Internal: Comfort, the pattern

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Check FocusZone. Follow the plan. Complete the tasks."
- FocusZone: "Good morning, Marcus. Here's your focus for today..."
- Marcus (internal): "No decisions needed. Just follow. Just complete."

### Scene 2: The Feedback Loop (700 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Throughout the day

**Key Events**:
- FocusZone provides instant feedback on completions
- "Task completed! You're 15% ahead of schedule"
- The dopamine hit of AI validation
- The pleasure of being "on track"
- The craving for more completions
- The feedback loop: complete → validate → pleasure → repeat
- Marcus's growing addiction to the feedback

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Completion animations, progress bars, praise messages
- Auditory: Completion sounds, notification chimes
- Internal: Pleasure, craving, the addiction

**Character Voices**:
- FocusZone: "Task completed! You're ahead of schedule. Keep it up!"
- Marcus (internal): "That felt good. I want more of that."
- FocusZone: "Excellent focus today. You've completed 8 tasks. Only 4 more to hit your goal."
- Marcus (internal): "I need to hit my goal. I need the completion. I need the validation."
- Marcus (internal): "Is this achievement? Or is this just... feedback?"

### Scene 3: The Escalation (500 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Late afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus seeks more tasks to complete
- He adds minor tasks just to get completions
- The feedback loop escalates
- The addiction deepens
- The question: "Am I productive, or just addicted?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: More tasks, more completions
- Auditory: More notifications
- Internal: The craving, the question

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "I need more completions. I need more feedback."
- Marcus (adding tasks): Minor items, just to complete them
- FocusZone: "Task completed! Productivity score: 94%!"
- Marcus (internal): "94%. I want 100%. I need 100%."
- Marcus (internal): "Is this productivity? Or is this addiction?"

### Scene 4: The Sarah Observation (500 words)
**Location**: Home, dinner
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Sarah notices Marcus's phone checking
- He's checking FocusZone even at home
- "You seem... different. More driven, but also more distracted"
- Marcus's defense: "I'm just being productive"
- Sarah's concern: "Productive at what cost?"
- The first confrontation about AI dependency

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Dinner table, Marcus's phone, Sarah's face
- Auditory: Sarah's voice, phone notifications
- Internal: Defense, the addiction

**Character Voices**:
- Sarah: "You've been checking your phone all through dinner."
- Marcus: "Just checking my productivity. Making sure I'm on track."
- Sarah: "On track for what? You completed all your work tasks."
- Marcus: "I know, but I want to see my score. My progress."
- Sarah: "Your score? Marcus, you're not a machine. You don't have a score."
- Marcus: "FocusZone gives me a productivity score. It helps me stay motivated."
- Sarah: "Motivated by what? By an algorithm? By a number?"
- Marcus (internal): "She doesn't understand. She doesn't feel the satisfaction."

### Scene 5: The Night (400 words)
**Location**: Home, bedroom
**Time**: Night

**Key Events**:
- Marcus checks FocusZone before bed
- The satisfaction of a "perfect" productivity day
- The craving for tomorrow's completions
- The addiction: he can't imagine working without AI feedback
- The question: "What if I couldn't use FocusZone?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Phone screen, FocusZone stats
- Auditory: Silence, phone sounds
- Internal: Satisfaction, craving, the addiction

**Character Voices**:
- FocusZone: "Great day, Marcus! Productivity score: 98%. Tomorrow's plan ready."
- Marcus (internal): "98%. Almost perfect. Tomorrow I'll get 100%."
- Marcus (internal): "What if I couldn't use FocusZone? What would I do?"
- Marcus (internal): "I can't imagine working without it. I can't imagine deciding for myself."
