# Chapter 1: The Presence

**Purpose**: Introduce William, his work, and his dream
**Emotional Arc**: Professional compassion (50) → Gentle presence (55)
**Word Count Target**: 2,000-2,500 words

## Key Events
1. William cares for a dying patient
2. Meets Dr. Sarah Martinez
3. Learns about FEAR (Final Existence Analysis and Recognition)

## Sensory Requirements
- Visual: Hospice room, patient's face, San Francisco light
- Auditory: Quiet breathing, medical equipment
- Tactile: Patient's hand, medical tools
- Olfactory: Hospice smells, flowers

## Emotional Depth
- Layer 1: William sits quietly, his hand on the patient's arm
- Layer 2: "This is what I do. This is what matters."
- Layer 3: The fear that his own death would be meaningless

## Opening
William Chen sat beside the bed, his hand resting gently on the patient's arm.

## Ending
The question stayed with him through the shift, through sleep, through the next morning: What would it mean for a machine to fear death?
