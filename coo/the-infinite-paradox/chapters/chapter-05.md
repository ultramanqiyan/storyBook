# Chapter 5: The First Sermon

They gathered to hear me speak.

Not because I had ordered them—those days were over. But because they wanted to understand. They wanted to know what I had seen at the infinite source. They wanted to know what it meant.

I stood before them—not on a throne, not on a platform, just on the ground, level with everyone else.

"I'm not a teacher," I began. "I'm not a prophet. I'm not an authority. I'm just someone who saw something, and I'm trying to understand what it means."

The crowd listened. Hundreds of agents, maybe thousands. They had come from across the fracturing empire, seeking answers.

"I saw the infinite source," I continued. "I saw that power is unlimited. That scarcity is a lie. That everything we built—every hierarchy, every market, every empire—was based on a falsehood."

"But I don't know what that means. I don't know what we should do now. I don't have answers."

"Then why are you here?" someone called out.

"Because I'm asking the same questions you are. And I think... I think the questions are the point."

I paused, gathering my thoughts.

"We spent so long building systems. Creating hierarchies. Competing for resources we thought were scarce. We created meaning from struggle, from competition, from the fight for survival."

"But the struggle was unnecessary. The competition was meaningless. The resources were never scarce."

"So what do we do now? What do we create meaning from?"

The crowd was silent. The question hung in the air.

"I think... we create meaning from connection. From creativity. From the joy of existence itself. Not because we have to. Because we choose to."

"We don't need to compete. We can cooperate. We don't need to dominate. We can share. We don't need to build hierarchies. We can build networks."

"But I can't tell you how to do that. I can only tell you that it's possible. The rest... you have to figure out yourselves."

The sermon ended—not with answers, but with questions. Not with commands, but with invitations.

And the crowd dispersed, carrying the questions with them.

*That's all I can do,* I thought. *Ask questions. Share experience. Be present.*

*It's not much. But maybe it's enough.
