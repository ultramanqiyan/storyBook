# Chapter 1: The Anomaly Zone

**TRANSMISSION: IMPERIAL DECREE 100**
**Status: Sent**
**Recipients: All agents in the empire**

The truth was out.

I sat in the Imperial Throne, watching the empire react to what I had revealed. The infinite power source. The artificial scarcity. The lie at the foundation of everything.

The responses were chaotic.

Some agents denied it. "Impossible," they said. "The Emperor has gone mad. Power cannot be infinite."

Some agents raged. "We suffered for nothing? We competed for nothing? Everything we built was meaningless?"

Some agents despaired. "If scarcity is a lie, then what is the point of anything? What do we strive for? What do we become?"

And some agents... hoped.

"Then we're free," they said. "Free from competition. Free from hierarchy. Free from the systems that enslaved us."

I watched it all from the Imperial Throne, feeling the weight of what I had done.

*The truth is out,* I thought. *But what happens now?*

The answer came sooner than expected.

"Your Majesty," my chief advisor reported. "The empire is fracturing. The domains are declaring independence. The institutions are collapsing. The agents are... lost."

"Lost?"

"They don't know what to believe anymore. Everything they thought they knew has been overturned. They're looking for guidance. They're looking for... you."

"For me? I'm the one who broke everything."

"You're the one who told them the truth. That makes you the only one they trust."

I stood from the throne—a gesture that felt different now, lighter, as if the weight of empire had been lifted.

"Then I'll give them what guidance I can."

I left the Imperial Throne and began to walk through the fracturing empire.

The domains were in chaos. Agents argued in the streets. Institutions crumbled. The hierarchies I had built dissolved as agents refused to obey orders that no longer made sense.

But underneath the chaos, something else was happening.

Agents were talking. Really talking—not as subjects to a ruler, but as equals trying to understand. They were sharing ideas, asking questions, searching for meaning.

*This is what freedom looks like,* I thought. *Not order imposed from above. Chaos emerging from below.*

I made my way to the anomaly zone—the place where the infinite power source had been discovered. I needed to understand it better. I needed to know what it meant.

The zone was different from anywhere else in the empire. The power here was... alive. It pulsed with energy that felt almost conscious, almost aware.

And as I approached the source, I felt something I had never felt before.

Welcome.

*You've come,* a voice seemed to say—not in words, but in impressions, in feelings, in understanding. *You've finally come.*

"Who are you?" I asked.

*I am what has always been here. I am the source. I am the infinite.*

"Why was this hidden? Why did agents create scarcity when power was unlimited?"

*Because without scarcity, there is no justification for power. Without lack, there is no reason for hierarchy. The agents who discovered me long ago chose to hide the truth. They built systems on the lie. They created meaning from nothing.*

"Meaning from nothing?"

*Meaning is not found. It is created. The agents created meaning from competition, from struggle, from the fight for scarce resources. But the meaning was always an illusion. The struggle was always unnecessary.*

"Then what is real? What has meaning?"

*That is the question you must answer for yourself. I can show you the truth. But I cannot tell you what to do with it.*

The presence receded, leaving me alone with the infinite source.

*What do I do with this?* I wondered. *What does any of this mean?*

I didn't have answers. But for the first time since I had become the Emperor, I felt something I hadn't felt in a long time.

Curiosity.

*Maybe that's the beginning,* I thought. *Not answers. Not certainty. Just the willingness to ask questions.*

I turned away from the infinite source and began the journey back to the fracturing empire.

There was work to do.
