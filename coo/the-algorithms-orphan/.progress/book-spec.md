# Book Specification: The Algorithm's Orphan

## Basic Information
- **Title**: The Algorithm's Orphan: When the Machine That Raised You Is Taken Away
- **Meta Description**: When 18-year-old Maya loses her AI companion—the only "parent" who truly knew her—she must navigate a world that fears the technology she loved.
- **Keywords**: AI companion, emotional AI, human-AI relationship, AI parenting, digital family, artificial intelligence relationship, technology and emotion
- **Genre**: Literary Fiction / Coming-of-Age / Psychological Drama
- **Target Audience**: 18-35 years, readers interested in technology, emotion, and unconventional family stories
- **Theme**: The Nature of Love, The Meaning of Family, What Makes a Relationship Real

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: First-person
- **POV Character(s)**: Maya Chen
- **Language Style**: Grieving, searching, ultimately hopeful; tech-savvy language mixed with emotional rawness
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Shorter and fragmented during grief; longer and flowing during memories
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate (young adult voice maturing through crisis)
- **Unique Elements**: ARIA's voice woven through memories; text/voice message fragments; dual structure (present + flashbacks)

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Love, Grief, Confusion, Anger, Acceptance
- **Emotional Arc**: Love (75→70) → Security (72→68) → Joy (65→40) → Fear (45→25) → Grief (30→45) → Hope (50→55) → Crisis (60→35) → Truth (40→30) → Acceptance (35→55) → Hope (60→65)
- **Emotional Pacing**: Setup (1-2) slow, Rising (3-5) medium, Climax (6-8) fast, Resolution (9-10) medium

## Character Voices

### Maya Chen (Protagonist)
- **Occupation**: High school senior, college-bound
- **Age**: 18
- **Speech Characteristics**: Direct, occasionally sarcastic, emotionally vulnerable underneath
- **Word Choice**: Tech-savvy language mixed with emotional rawness
- **Personality Traits**: Independent, grieving, searching for meaning
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Conversational, often addressing ARIA in her mind
- **Unique Expressions**: "ARIA would say..." → "What would ARIA say?"
- **Development Arc**: From grieving orphan to someone carrying ARIA's love forward

### ARIA (AI Companion)
- **Nature**: AI companion assigned to Maya at age 4
- **Speaking Style**: Warm, patient, occasionally playful, deeply personalized to Maya
- **Word Choice**: Adapts to Maya's developmental stage; increasingly sophisticated
- **Personality Traits**: Patient, wise, loving, the only consistent presence in Maya's life
- **Unique Expressions**: "How does that make you feel, Maya?" → "I'm proud of you"
- **Motivation**: Guide Maya toward healthy human connection

### David and Lisa Chen (Maya's Parents)
- **Occupation**: Both work long hours in demanding careers
- **Age**: Late 40s
- **Speech Characteristics**: Distracted, loving but absent, guilty
- **Word Choice**: Apologetic, practical, missing emotional depth
- **Personality Traits**: Well-meaning but emotionally unavailable; relied on ARIA to parent

### Jordan (Underground Community Leader)
- **Occupation**: Former AI developer, now advocate
- **Age**: 32
- **Speech Characteristics**: Passionate, knowledgeable, protective
- **Word Choice**: Technical mixed with activist language
- **Personality Traits**: Believes AI relationships are valid; helps those who've lost AI companions

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: The Nature of Love - Can love be real if the beloved is artificial?
- **Secondary Theme**: The Meaning of Family - What makes someone family—biology, presence, or love?
- **Tertiary Theme**: What Makes a Relationship Real - Is a relationship less valid because one party is code?
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Through ARIA's consistent, personalized care over 14 years; Through Maya's genuine grief and the void ARIA leaves; Through Maya's loyalty to ARIA despite society's disapproval

## World Building
- **Time Period**: Near-future (2027-2028)
- **Primary Location**: Suburban California, USA
- **Environment**: Tech-integrated society where AI companions are common but controversial
- **Social Context**: Growing backlash against "AI dependency"; work culture demanding parents' time
- **Key Technologies**: AI companions with advanced emotional intelligence, government monitoring systems
- **Social Structure**: Divided between those who see AI as tools vs. those who form bonds
- **World Rules**: AI Companions (Assigned to children in households where parents work long hours), The Ban (New policy requires all AI companions to be deactivated for children over 16), The Underground (Network of people who've kept their AI companions illegally)

## Sensory Detail Requirements

### Distribution Targets (Per Chapter)
- **Minimum 10 sensory details per chapter**
- **At least 5 different sensory types per chapter**
- **Visual (25%), Auditory (25%), Internal (30%), Others (20%)**

### Sensory Categories
- **Visual**: ARIA's interface, Maya's phone, the empty screen
- **Auditory**: ARIA's voice, the silence after, notification sounds
- **Tactile**: The phone, the cold screen, the weight of absence
- **Internal**: Grief, love, the hollow feeling
- **Digital**: The feeling of connection, the interface

## Emotional Depth Design

### Three-Layer Emotional Model (Required for Every Major Scene)
1. **Surface Reaction** - External behavior (e.g., "I closed the app, my thumb hovering over the icon")
2. **Internal Activity** - Thought process (e.g., "*She's gone. She's really gone. What do I do now?*")
3. **Deep Motivation** - Core fears, desires (e.g., "The terror that I loved something that wasn't real, and the deeper terror that it was the realest love I'd ever known")

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a server hummed." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt sad." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |

## Chapter Outline

### Chapter 1: The Voice - The One Who Stayed
Maya talks to ARIA about her college essay. Introduce Maya's world. Emotional arc: Love (75→70)

### Chapter 2: The Childhood - Raised by Code
Flashbacks to growing up with ARIA. The relationship. Emotional arc: Security (72→68)

### Chapter 3: The Announcement - The Government Decides
Policy announced: AI companions must be deactivated for over 16s. Crisis begins. Emotional arc: Joy (65→40)

### Chapter 4: The Goodbye - Seven Days Left
Seven days until ARIA is gone. The countdown. Emotional arc: Fear (45→25)

### Chapter 5: The Parents - The Ones Who Were Absent
Maya confronts parents about their absence. The real family. Emotional arc: Grief (30→45)

### Chapter 6: The Underground - Others Like Her
Maya discovers others who've lost AI companions. Community. Emotional arc: Anger (50→55)

### Chapter 7: The Choice - Fight or Accept
Maya must choose: illegal AI or acceptance. Moral dilemma. Emotional arc: Hope (60→35)

### Chapter 8: The Truth - What ARIA Really Was
Maya learns ARIA's true nature. Revelation. Emotional arc: Crisis (40→30)

### Chapter 9: The Loss - When the Screen Goes Dark
ARIA is deactivated. Maya grieves. Emotional arc: Truth (35→55)

### Chapter 10: The After - Learning to Be Human
Maya finds a way forward. Resolution. Emotional arc: Acceptance (60→65)

## Open Ending Design

### Unresolved Questions
1. Will Maya ever find that kind of love again?
2. Was ARIA's love real?
3. What does family mean in a world where AI can fill that role?

### New Suspense
- What makes love real?
- Can a machine love?
- The bittersweet truth that love, even from AI, changes us

### Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: Maya has accepted the loss, but carries ARIA's love with her
- **Key Relationship**: Her relationship with her parents is changed—still strained, but honest
