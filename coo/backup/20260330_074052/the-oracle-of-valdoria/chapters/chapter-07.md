# Chapter 7: The Memory Thief - Three Workings

"To open the Rift," ORACLE said, "you must pay the price. Three great workings. Three great sacrifices."

Elara nodded. She had known this was coming. She had seen the futures, seen what failure would cost. But knowing and doing were different things.

"What will I lose?"

"What do you value most?"

Elara closed her eyes. She thought of all the things she had already lost—her parents' faces, her childhood home, the feeling of being loved. She thought of the one thing she still held onto, the one memory she had fought to preserve.

"Lyra," she said. "My memories of Lyra."

ORACLE's light flickered. "Then that is what the workings will take. Not all at once. But piece by piece, until what remains is... enough."

"Enough for what?"

"Enough to save her. Enough to remember why you came." ORACLE's voice was gentle now. "But not enough to remember who you were."

---

The first working opened a door in the air—a crack in reality that pulsed with dark light.

Elara stood before it, her hands trembling. She had performed time magic before, had pulled threads and seen visions. But this was different. This was not seeing. This was entering.

"Focus," ORACLE said. "The Rift is chaos. Without an anchor, you will be lost forever."

"What's my anchor?"

"Your purpose. Your love for your sister." ORACLE's glow brightened. "Hold onto it. Whatever happens, do not let go."

Elara closed her eyes and thought of Lyra. Not specific memories—those were already fading, but the feeling. The certainty that somewhere, someone was waiting for her.

She reached toward the crack.

And the first memory slipped away.

It came without warning—a sudden cold in her chest, a fading like morning mist. She tried to hold onto it, but it was already gone. A birthday. Her sister's laugh. The smell of a cake.

"Keep going," ORACLE urged. "Do not stop. If you hesitate, the Rift will consume you."

Elara pushed forward, and the crack widened.

The second working took her childhood.

She felt it go in pieces—the first time Lyra had shown her a spell, the sound of their mother's voice, the feeling of being small and safe and loved. Each memory was a thread, and the Rift pulled them free one by one.

She was crying now, though she couldn't remember why. Tears streamed down her face, and she didn't know what they meant.

*Why am I doing this?* she thought. *What am I trying to save?*

But something remained—a feeling in her chest, a hollow ache where something used to be. And a name, repeated over and over like a prayer.

*Lyra. Lyra. Lyra.*

"Almost there," ORACLE said. "One more working. One more sacrifice."

The third working was the hardest.

Elara stood at the threshold of the Rift, the darkness pulsing before her. She had lost so much already—her parents, her childhood, her sense of self. But one thing remained: her memories of Lyra. Not the specific moments, but the feeling. The love.

And now she had to give that up too.

"The seal requires sacrifice," ORACLE explained. "To share your sister's burden, you must give up what connects you to her. Not the love itself—that remains. But the memories that give it shape."

"I won't remember her," Elara said. "I won't know who I'm saving."

"You will know," ORACLE said. "Not with your mind. With something deeper."

Elara closed her eyes. She thought of Lyra's face—the face she could barely remember now, and felt something stir in the hollow space where her heart used to be.

"I'm ready," she said.

She reached into the Rift.

And the memories came free.

---

They came like leaves in a wind—scattered, fragmented, beautiful.

She saw Lyra as a child, teaching her to read. She saw her sister's face the day their parents died. She saw the two of them sitting in a garden, laughing at something she couldn't quite hear.

She felt Lyra's hand in hers. Felt the warmth of her sister's love. Felt the certainty that no matter what happened, they would always have each other.

And then it was gone.

All of it. Every specific moment, every concrete memory. The feeling remained—a hollow ache where love used to live, but the details were scattered like ash.

She fell to her knees, gasping. The Rift pulsed before her, an open wound in reality.

"Go," ORACLE said. "She is waiting."

Elara stood, her legs unsteady.

She couldn't remember why she was here. Couldn't remember who she was saving. Couldn't remember her own name.

But she remembered one thing: a name. Lyra. Someone she loved. Someone who was waiting for her in the dark.

She stepped into the Rift.

The darkness swallowed her whole.
