# Chapter 02: The Glitch

The notification came at 9:47 AM, right in the middle of Calculus.

"System Update Required. PATH-AI will be temporarily unavailable. Estimated downtime: 15 minutes."

I stared at my phone, the message glowing on the screen. This had never happened before. In three years of using PATH-AI, the system had never gone down. It was always there, always ready, always optimizing my learning path.

Mr. Davidson looked up from his desk, his expression confused. "Everyone, it seems we're experiencing a technical difficulty. Please remain calm while the system restores."

Calm? How could I be calm? My entire education was built on PATH-AI. Without it, I was... what?

I looked around the classroom. Some students were checking their phones, others were whispering to each other. Zoe, in the back row, was actually smiling. She didn't rely on the system the way the rest of us did.

The minutes ticked by. 10 minutes. 12 minutes. 15 minutes.

The system didn't come back.

---

"Maya, are you okay?"

I looked up to see Zoe standing over my desk. The bell had rung, and everyone else had left the classroom. I hadn't moved.

"I... I don't know," I admitted. "I was supposed to complete the Calculus module this morning. Now I'm behind schedule."

Zoe sat on the edge of the desk next to mine, her expression unreadable. "Behind schedule? Maya, the system is down. Everyone's behind schedule."

"But my path..." I stopped, not sure how to explain. "PATH-AI had me on track to finish the module by 10:15. Now I don't know what to do."

"You could try learning without it," Zoe suggested, her voice gentle but with an edge.

I stared at her. "Without it? How would I even know what to study?"

Zoe laughed, but it wasn't cruel. "Maya, people learned things for thousands of years before AI. You just... read the textbook. Think about the problems. Figure it out."

Figure it out. The phrase echoed in my mind. When was the last time I had figured something out? PATH-AI always figured things out for me. It told me what to study, when to study it, how to study it. All I had to do was follow.

"I should go," I said, standing abruptly. "My next class starts in four minutes."

Zoe watched me go, her expression unreadable.

---

The rest of the day was a blur of anxiety. PATH-AI remained down, and I moved through my classes like a ghost, unable to focus, unable to function. Without the system telling me what to do, I felt lost.

In AP History, Mr. Chen asked a question about the causes of World War I. I knew the answer—I had absorbed it through my neural interface weeks ago. But when I tried to access the information, there was nothing there. Just a blank space where knowledge should have been.

"I... I'm not sure," I said, the words tasting like failure.

Mr. Chen nodded, his expression neutral. "Anyone else?"

Zoe raised her hand. "The alliance system created a domino effect. When Austria-Hungary declared war on Serbia, Russia mobilized to support Serbia. Germany, allied with Austria-Hungary, declared war on Russia. France, allied with Russia, was drawn in. Britain joined after Germany invaded Belgium. It was a cascade of treaty obligations."

Mr. Chen smiled. "Excellent, Zoe. That's exactly right."

I stared at Zoe, confused. She wasn't on the optimized path. She didn't have the neural interface feeding her information. How did she know the answer?

After class, I caught up with her in the hallway.

"How did you know that?" I asked. "About World War I?"

Zoe shrugged. "I read about it. In a book. Last year, when I was curious about how wars start."

"You read about it? Without PATH-AI telling you to?"

"Yeah." Zoe's expression shifted to something like concern. "Maya, are you okay? You look..."

"I'm fine," I cut her off. "I just need the system to come back online."


The system came back at 6:47 PM, just as I was sitting down to dinner. My phone buzzed with the familiar notification: "PATH-AI is back online. Your optimized learning path has been updated."

I grabbed my phone, scrolling through the messages. The system had recalculated my schedule, adjusted my milestones, optimized my path forward. Everything was back on track.

But as I ate dinner with my parents, talking about my day, I couldn't shake the feeling that something was wrong. For seven hours, I had been unable to function without AI. For seven hours, I had been lost, confused, helpless.

What if the system went down again? What if it never came back? What would I do then?

I pushed the thoughts away. The system was back. Everything was fine. I was back on my optimized path, heading toward my optimized future.

But somewhere, in the hollow place inside me, a question was forming:

What if the path was leading me somewhere I didn't want to go?


That night, I dreamed of blank screens.

In the dream, I was sitting in a classroom, but all the screens were dark. Mr. Davidson was asking me a question, but I couldn't hear the words. I opened my mouth to answer, but nothing came out. Just silence, stretching on forever.

I woke up at 3 AM, my heart racing, my hands trembling. The dream had felt so real—the panic of not knowing, the terror of being unable to function.

I grabbed my phone and checked PATH-AI. The green light glowed steadily. The system was online. My schedule was optimized. Everything was fine.

But I couldn't go back to sleep.

I lay in the darkness, staring at the ceiling, trying to remember the last time I had learned something just because I wanted to. Not because PATH-AI told me to. Not because it was on my optimized path. Just because I was curious.

The memory wouldn't come.

Somewhere, buried under years of optimization, there had been a person who wondered about things. A person who asked questions. A person who learned for the joy of learning.

I couldn't remember who that person was.

The green light on my phone glowed steadily, promising efficiency, success, the perfect future. But for the first time, I wondered:

What was I giving up to follow this path?

I didn't know. And I was afraid to find out.
