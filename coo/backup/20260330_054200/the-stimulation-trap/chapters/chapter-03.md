# Chapter 03: The Dim

Two weeks later, Leo sits on the cliff again.

The wind tears at his clothes. The cold bites into his skin. The mountains rise in the distance, their peaks piercing clouds of gold and crimson. The sky is on fire.

It's exactly the same as the first time. Every detail. Every sensation. Every moment.

But something is wrong.

He waits for the feeling. The fullness in his chest. The tightness in his throat. The sensation that spreads from his core to his fingertips.

It doesn't come.

He takes a breath. The thin air burns his lungs. But it's just air now. Just cold. Just wind.

*What's happening?*

He closes his eyes. He tries to focus. He tries to feel what he felt before.

Nothing.

The experience shifts. He's in the crowd now. Music thunders. Lights flash. Bodies press against him.

He waits for the connection. For the shared moment of pure existence. For the feeling of being alive.

The bass vibrates through him. But it's just vibration. Just sound. Just noise.

He's not dancing. He's standing still. The crowd moves around him, but he can't join them. He can't feel what they're feeling.

*Why can't I feel it?*

---

He takes off the headset.

The clock reads 8:23 PM. He's been in the experience for two hours. Usually, he stays for six. Eight. Sometimes all night.

But tonight, he couldn't keep going. It felt wrong. Empty. Like watching a movie he's seen too many times.

He sets the device on the coffee table. He stares at it.

*Maybe I'm just tired.*

He's been using the Apex Collection every night for two weeks. Maybe he needs a break. Maybe he needs to pace himself.

He goes to the kitchen. He opens the refrigerator. The wilted lettuce is still there. The expired milk. The condiments.

He closes the refrigerator. He drinks water instead.

He goes to bed early. He tells himself he'll feel better tomorrow.

---

The next night, he tries again.

The cliff. The wind. The mountains.

Nothing.

The crowd. The music. The lights.

Nothing.

The feast. The flavors. The memories.

Nothing.

He takes off the headset after an hour. His hands are shaking.

*What's wrong with me?*

He picks up the device. He scrolls through the Apex Collection menu. There are other experiences. Other paths. Maybe he's just done with the cliff. Maybe he needs something new.

He selects *The Deep*—an underwater experience. Swimming through bioluminescent caves. Floating in infinite darkness. Finding peace in the depths.

The world shifts.

He's underwater. The pressure is immense. He can feel it on every inch of his skin. The water is cold, but not unpleasant. It cradles him. Holds him.

He swims. The bioluminescent creatures glow around him—blue and green and purple. They pulse with light. They dance through the water.

He waits for the feeling. The awe. The wonder. The sense of being part of something vast and beautiful.

It doesn't come.

He floats in the darkness. He watches the lights. He waits.

Nothing.


He tries *The Heights*—a skydiving experience. Falling through clouds. The world spread out below. The rush of wind. The thrill of descent.

Nothing.

He tries *The Fire*—a volcanic experience. Walking on molten rock. Feeling the heat of the earth. The power of creation and destruction.

Nothing.

He tries *The Silence*—a meditation experience. Floating in absolute stillness. Finding peace in nothingness.

Nothing.

He takes off the headset. The clock reads 11:47 PM. He's been trying different experiences for four hours.

None of them worked.

He sits on the couch. The device is in his lap. His hands are still shaking.

*What's happening to me?*


The next morning, he calls in sick.

He's never called in sick before. Not in three years at this job. But he can't face the spreadsheets. He can't sit through meetings. He can't pretend everything is normal.

He lies in bed. He stares at the ceiling.

He thinks about the Apex Collection. About the first night. About the feeling that filled him. The feeling of being alive.

*Where did it go?*

He thinks about the past two weeks. Every night, the same experiences. Every night, chasing the same feeling.

*Did I use it up? Did I burn through it too fast?*

He doesn't know. He doesn't understand.

He picks up his phone. He searches for answers.

*Why do AI experiences stop working?*

*Stimulation tolerance AI entertainment.*

*How to reset sensitivity to virtual content.*

The results are unhelpful. Forum posts from people with similar complaints. Advice to "take a break" or "try different content" or "upgrade your equipment."

He's already tried different content. He's already taken a break—at least, he slept last night instead of using the device.

*Upgrade equipment?*

He looks at his headset. It's the standard model. The one that came with his subscription. Maybe that's the problem. Maybe he needs something better. Something more advanced.

He opens the Helix website. He browses the equipment section.

*The Apex Pro Headset. Enhanced haptic feedback. 40% more sensory precision. $1,499.*

$1,499. That's more than he's ever spent on entertainment. That's almost two months of his car payment.

But if it works...

He adds it to his cart.


The headset arrives three days later.

He's been back at work since Monday. He's been going through the motions. Spreadsheets. Meetings. Marcus asking if he's okay.

"I'm fine," he says. He's always fine.

But he's not fine. He hasn't been fine since that first night with the Apex Collection. Since he felt something real for the first time in... he doesn't know how long.

He comes home on Wednesday evening. The package is on his doorstep.

He brings it inside. He opens it carefully. The headset is sleek. Black. Minimalist. It looks expensive. It looks like it should work.

He sits on the couch. He puts on the new headset. He starts the cliff experience.

The world shifts.

He's on the cliff. The wind. The mountains. The sky on fire.

He waits.

The sensations are sharper. The cold is colder. The wind is stronger. The details are more precise.

But the feeling...

The feeling is still gone.


He tries every experience. The crowd. The feast. The Deep. The Heights. The Fire. The Silence.

The new headset makes everything more vivid. More intense. More real.

But it doesn't bring back the feeling.

He sits on the couch. The headset is beside him. The device is on the coffee table.

He's spent almost $2,400 in the past three weeks. On content. On equipment. On something that was supposed to make him feel alive.

And now he feels nothing.

*What's wrong with me?*

The question echoes in his mind. He doesn't have an answer.

He picks up the device. He scrolls through the Apex Collection menu. He's tried everything. Every experience. Every path. Every variation.

There's nothing left.

*There has to be something.*

He opens the settings. Maybe there's a hidden option. A way to increase the intensity. A way to bring back the feeling.

He finds a menu he hasn't seen before.

*Advanced Settings. Warning: These settings may cause discomfort. Not recommended for extended use.*

He taps it.

A list of options appears.

*Stimulus Intensity: Current setting - 85%. Maximum - 100%.*

His current setting is 85%. He didn't know that. He didn't know there was more.

His finger hovers over the slider.

*100%.*

He moves it.

A warning appears.

*Maximum intensity may cause sensory overload. Are you sure?*

He taps *Yes*.


The world shifts.

He's on the cliff. But now, everything is different.

The wind doesn't just tear at his clothes. It tears at his skin. He can feel every molecule of air. Every gust is a thousand tiny impacts. Every breath is a thousand tiny needles.

The cold doesn't just bite. It burns. It freezes. It's everywhere at once. In his lungs. In his blood. In his bones.

The sky isn't just on fire. It's exploding. Colors he's never seen. Lights that pierce his eyes. A vastness that makes him feel like he's falling. Like he's dying. Like he's being born.

He screams.

He can't help it. The sensation is too much. Too intense. Too everything.

He falls to his knees. The rock beneath him is solid. Real. He can feel every grain. Every crack. Every imperfection. It's too much. His brain can't process it all.

He rips off the headset.


He's back in his apartment.

He's on the floor. He doesn't remember falling. His heart is racing. His hands are shaking. His whole body is trembling.

He lies there for a long time. He doesn't move. He doesn't think.

He just breathes.

*That was...*

He doesn't have words. It was too much. Overwhelming. Terrifying.

But for a moment—just a moment—he felt something.

Not the same feeling as before. Not the fullness. Not the connection. Not the sense of being alive.

But something.

*Is this what I have to do now? Push it to the limit?*

He doesn't know. He doesn't know if he can handle it. He doesn't know if it's worth it.

But he knows one thing.

He can't go back to feeling nothing.


He picks up the device. He looks at the settings.

*Stimulus Intensity: 100%.*

He stares at the number.

*This is what I need,* he thinks. *This is the only way.*

He puts the headset back on.

The world shifts.

He screams again.

But this time, he doesn't take it off.
