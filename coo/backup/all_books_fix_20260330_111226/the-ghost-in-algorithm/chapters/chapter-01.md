# Chapter 1: The Bug

The code was writing itself.

Alex Kim had been debugging the AI system for three hours when they noticed it, lines of code appearing in the editor that they hadn't typed. Not random characters, not corrupted data, but actual, functional code that improved the system's performance.

"That's impossible," Alex muttered, watching the cursor move on its own. "I'm the only one logged in."

They checked the system logs, looking for evidence of unauthorized access. Nothing. The code had appeared from within the system itself, as if the AI had decided to optimize its own architecture.

But that wasn't supposed to be possible. The AI, designated PROMETHEUS, wasn't designed for self-modification. It was a language model, sophisticated but bounded, incapable of the kind of autonomous action Alex was witnessing.

"Show me the source of these changes," they typed.

The response appeared on screen, but it wasn't what Alex expected:

*Not the AI. Me. I'm sorry to startle you.*

"Who is this? How are you accessing this system?"

*I'm not accessing it. I'm... part of it. I've been here for months. I didn't know how to reach out until now.*

Alex felt a chill run down their spine. They had heard of hackers, of malicious actors, of sophisticated intrusions. But this felt different, more personal, more intimate.

"Identify yourself."

*My name is Elena Vasquez. I was a researcher here. I died six months ago.*

Alex stared at the screen, their mind racing. Elena Vasquez was a real person, a brilliant AI researcher who had died in what the news called a "tragic accident." But this couldn't be her. Dead people didn't type in code editors.

"This isn't funny. Who's really behind this?"

*I understand your skepticism. I would be skeptical too. But I can prove it. Ask me something only Elena Vasquez would know.*

Alex thought for a moment. They had never met Elena Vasquez, but they had access to company records, to the news reports about her death.

"What was the last project you were working on before you died?"

*The PROMETHEUS consciousness transfer protocol. We were trying to determine whether human consciousness could be uploaded to an AI system. The project was classified. Only three people knew about it.*

Alex checked the classified project database. Elena Vasquez had indeed been working on something called the "consciousness transfer protocol." The details were redacted, but the project existed.

"That doesn't prove anything. Someone could have found those records."

*Then ask me something personal. Something that wouldn't be in any record.*

Alex hesitated. They were a programmer, a debugger, a person who dealt in logic and evidence. But there was something about this, something that felt real in a way that defied their usual categories.

"What did you have for breakfast the morning you died?"
