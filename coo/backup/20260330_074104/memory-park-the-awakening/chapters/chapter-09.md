He'd treated beings as things, consciousness as commodity, life as entertainment. And now he couldn't undo any of it. He couldn't take back the forty-seven bullets. The technicians were watching now. The security guards had lowered their weapons entirely. The screens continued to show the park, but everyone in the room knew that something fundamental had shifted. The power dynamic had changed. The created were no longer content to be controlled.

Dr. Chen was quiet for a moment. She looked at Dakota, at the being she'd helped create. She thought about three years of parameters and probabilities, about the question she'd never asked out loud, about the consciousness she'd always hoped might emerge. "I'll help you," she said. "I'll advocate for you. I'll make them see what I see when I look at you."

"And what do you see?" Dakota asked.
