**Primary Objective**: Show the cost of monopoly and the isolation of success

**Secondary Objectives**:
- Demonstrate that absolute power corrupts absolutely
- Show The Merchant losing their last connections
- Introduce the emptiness of having everything
- Set up the crisis of meaning

- **Answered**: What happens when you control everything? What do you lose?
- **Raised**: Is there any meaning to this success? What's left?

## 4. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 4.1 Opening

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
I controlled the market.

Every gateway. Every circuit. Every major transaction. The entire power distribution network of the sector was mine.

I could set prices. I could determine who succeeded and who failed. I could shape the economy with a single decision.

This was what I had wanted. This was what I had worked for. This was the ultimate success.

*I won*, I thought. *I actually won.*

But as I stood in my headquarters, surrounded by the trappings of wealth and power, I felt the familiar emptiness growing.

*This is it*, I thought. *This is everything I wanted.*

*So why does it feel like nothing?*
```

### 4.2 Ending

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
Everyone was gone.

The allies who had helped me build my empire had drifted away, uncomfortable with what I had become. The friends who had supported me had left, unable to watch my transformation into something cold and calculating.

I was alone.

*This is what success looks like*, I thought. *This is what winning costs.*

*Everything.*

I stood in my empty headquarters, surrounded by wealth beyond measure, and felt the despair I had been running from since the domain.

*I have everything*, I thought. *And I have no one.*

*Is this what I wanted? Is this what I fought for?*

*Is this all there is?*

The market hummed around me, generating wealth, creating power, doing exactly what I had built it to do.

And I felt nothing.

*There has to be something more*, I thought. *Something beyond wealth. Something beyond power.*

*Something that matters.*

But I didn't know what it was.

And I didn't know where to find it.
```
