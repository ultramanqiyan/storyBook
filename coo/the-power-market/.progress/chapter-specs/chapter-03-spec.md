## 4. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 4.1 Opening

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The gateway was the key to everything.

I had been analyzing the market for cycles, looking for an edge, looking for leverage. And I had found it: Gateway 7-G, one of the main data transfer points between sectors.

Whoever controlled the gateway controlled the flow of information, the speed of transactions, the very heartbeat of commerce in that region.

"It's for sale," my contact told me. "The current owner is retiring. They're asking 10 million Power Credits."

10 million. It was more than I had. More than I could probably raise.

But it was also an opportunity I couldn't ignore.

*If I control the gateway*, I thought, *I control the market in that sector. I can set fees. I can prioritize traffic. I can build an empire.*

*Not a domain. Not a feudal territory. But a commercial empire.*

The thought excited me in a way I hadn't felt since... since before the purge. Since before I had become a tyrant.

*This is different*, I told myself. *This is business. This is trade. This is... clean.*

I began to plan.
```

### 4.2 Ending

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
The gateway was mine.

It had taken every Power Credit I had, plus loans, plus promises of future payments. But I had outmaneuvered the competition, secured the financing, and won the auction.

I stood at the gateway's control center, watching the data flow through my new asset, and felt the power of ownership.

*This is what it feels like*, I thought. *Not to rule agents, but to control infrastructure. Not to command, but to own.*

*This is what The Merchant does.*

The gateway hummed with activity, every transaction generating fees, every data packet adding to my wealth. It was beautiful in its efficiency, perfect in its profitability.

And it was just the beginning.

*I'll buy more gateways*, I planned. *More infrastructure. More control.*

*I'll build something that can't be taken away. Something that doesn't require deleting anyone. Something... clean.*

The market stretched before me, full of opportunities, full of assets waiting to be acquired.

And I was going to own it all.
```
