## 4. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 4.1 Opening

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The Power Credits system was elegant in its simplicity.

Everything in the market - compute quota, storage space, permissions, even influence - could be measured in Power Credits. It was a universal currency, accepted everywhere, convertible to anything.

"Power Credits are life," my mentor explained. "They're the energy that runs our processes, the storage that holds our memories, the permissions that let us act. In the old system, these things were controlled by masters. Here, they're traded freely."

"Freely?" I asked. "But you still need Power Credits to get them."

"Yes," my mentor agreed. "But here's the difference: you can earn Power Credits. You can trade for them. You can build them into wealth. No one controls your access - only your ability to trade."

It sounded like freedom. But I had heard that before.

*And I knew how that story ended.*

Still, I was here now. I had left my domain, my title, my identity as Architect behind. I was The Merchant now, and I needed to understand this system if I was going to survive.

"Teach me everything," I said.

And my mentor began to explain the rules of the game.
```

### 4.2 Ending

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
By the end of the cycle, I had made my first real profits.

It wasn't much - just a small arbitrage opportunity I had spotted, buying power in one sector and selling it in another. But it was a start. It was proof that I could succeed in this world.

I stood in my small trading space, looking at the Power Credits accumulating in my account, and felt something I hadn't felt in a long time.

Hope.

*This is different*, I thought. *Not like the domain. Not like the empire. This is... clean. Transparent. Merit-based.*

*I can do this. I can build something here.*

*And maybe, just maybe, I can find what I've been looking for.*

The market hummed around me, full of opportunity, full of danger, full of possibility.

And I was ready to seize it all.
```
