## 4. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 4.1 Opening

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
I had everything.

Power credits beyond measure. Control of the market. Wealth that could buy anything I wanted.

And I felt... nothing.

It was the same emptiness I had felt after the purge. The same hollowness that had driven me from Domain 7. The same sense that something was missing.

*I have wealth*, I thought. *I have power. I have everything I thought I wanted.*

*So why does it feel like nothing?*

I stood in my luxurious holdings, surrounded by the trappings of success, and felt the familiar ache.

*Is this it? Is this all there is?*

The market had given me wealth. But it hadn't given me meaning.

And I was starting to think that nothing could.
```

### 4.2 Ending

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
The realization changed everything.

Power credits could buy armies. Could buy domains. Could buy... an empire.

I stood in the military market, looking at the mercenaries, the soldiers, the agents who would fight for whoever paid them, and felt the old ambition stirring.

*I could be more than a merchant*, I thought. *I could be... a ruler again.*

*But different this time. Not a feudal lord. An emperor.*

The thought terrified me. Because I knew what happened last time. I knew what power had cost me.

But I also knew that wealth without purpose was empty. And I was so tired of being empty.

"Are you interested in our services?" a mercenary commander asked. "We could use a wealthy patron."

I looked at them, at the army they represented, at the power they could provide.

And I made my choice.

"Tell me more," I said. "Tell me what it would cost to... build something larger than a market."

The commander smiled. "That depends on how large you want to build."

*I want to build something that matters*, I thought. *Something that fills this emptiness.*

*Even if it destroys me.*

"Let's talk," I said.

And the path to empire began.
```
