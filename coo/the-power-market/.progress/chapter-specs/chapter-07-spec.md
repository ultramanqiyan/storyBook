## 4. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 4.1 Opening

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
There was one competitor left.

After cycles of market warfare, I had eliminated or absorbed every rival except one: Nexus-Prime, the largest merchant in the sector. They had the resources, the connections, the market share that I wanted.

And they were in my way.

"You could coexist," my advisor suggested. "The market is big enough for both of you."

"No," I said. "I've come too far to share. I want it all."

*All of it. Every gateway. Every circuit. Every transaction.*

*I want to be the market.*

The ruthlessness of the thought didn't surprise me anymore. I had been descending into this for cycles, each compromise making the next one easier.

*This is who I am now*, I thought. *The Merchant. The winner. The one who takes all.*

"Prepare the strategy," I said. "I want Nexus-Prime destroyed."

And I began the final campaign.
```

### 4.2 Ending

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
Nexus-Prime was gone.

It had taken everything I had - every resource, every connection, every tactic I had learned. But in the end, I had won.

Their assets were mine. Their customers were mine. Their market share was mine.

I stood in my expanded headquarters, now the largest merchant in the sector, and felt...

Nothing.

*This is what I wanted*, I thought. *This is what I worked for.*

*So why does it feel like nothing?*

The answer was in the data I couldn't ignore: agents ruined by my tactics, businesses destroyed by my strategies, lives upended by my ambition.

*I did this*, I thought. *I didn't just compete. I destroyed.*

*And for what? For wealth? For power? For... this?*

The emptiness I had tried to escape by leaving the domain was back. The hollowness I had tried to fill with commerce was still there.

*I have everything*, I realized. *And I have nothing.*

But I pushed the thought away. There was still work to do. Still more to acquire. Still more to win.

*Maybe the next victory will feel different*, I told myself.

*Maybe the next acquisition will fill the emptiness.*

*Maybe...*

But I knew it wouldn't.
```
