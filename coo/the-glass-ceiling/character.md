# Character Profiles: The Glass Ceiling

## Main Character: Alex Chen

### Basic Information

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Name** | Alex Chen |
| **Age** | 24 |
| **Gender** | Non-binary (they/them) |
| **Ethnicity** | Chinese-American (first generation) |
| **Education** | Stanford University, Computer Science |
| **Position** | Junior Product Manager, Meridian Tech |
| **Salary** | $120,000 + equity |

### Background

Alex grew up in a working-class immigrant family. Their parents sacrificed everything to give them opportunities - working double shifts, learning English from TV, pushing Alex toward "stable" careers. Alex was the first in their family to attend college, graduating from Stanford with honors and a chip on their shoulder the size of Silicon Valley.

*They've spent their whole life trying to prove they belong.*

### Personality

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Driven** | Will do whatever it takes to succeed |
| **Insecure** | Secretly fears they're an impostor |
| **Perfectionist** | Holds themselves to impossible standards |
| **Loyal** | Once committed, hard to break away |
| **Observant** | Notices patterns others miss |

### The Journey

| Phase | State | Key Question |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| **The Honeymoon** | Euphoric | "I made it - this is my dream job" |
| **The Doubt** | Confused | "Am I good enough?" |
| **The Breakdown** | Broken | "What's wrong with me?" |
| **The Awakening** | Aware | "What's wrong with THEM?" |
| **The Exit** | Free | "Who do I want to be?" |

## Secondary Characters

### Marcus Webb - The Manager

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Position** | Senior Product Manager, Alex's direct report |
| **Age** | 35 |
| **Background** | Ex-Google, recruited to Meridian 3 years ago |
| **Role** | The primary manipulator |

Marcus is the architect of Alex's breakdown. He's not evil - he's a product of the system. He was broken down the same way, and now he perpetuates the cycle. He genuinely believes he's helping Alex "grow."

**Key Tactics:**
- Love bombing: "You're going to be a star here"
- Negging: "You're doing great, but I need you to be more strategic"
- Moving goalposts: "Actually, I need you to take on this additional project"
- Gaslighting: "I never promised you that promotion timeline"

---

### Sarah Kim - The Mirror

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Position** | Product Manager (peer to Alex) |
| **Age** | 26 |
| **Background** | MIT, joined Meridian 2 years ago |
| **Role** | The one who shows Alex the truth |

Sarah was the golden child before Alex arrived. She's been through the same manipulation cycle and is now in the "burnout" phase - going through the motions, too exhausted to fight back. When Alex starts to question things, Sarah becomes the mirror that shows them the pattern.

**Key Revelation:**
"He did the same thing to me. The love bombing, the subtle criticism, the moving goalposts. You're not crazy, Alex. You're being played."

## The System

### Meridian Tech Culture

| Element | Surface Level | Reality |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| **"We're a family"** | Belonging, support | Dependency, no boundaries |
| **"Work hard, play hard"** | Fun culture | Burnout disguised as passion |
| **"We change the world"** | Purpose | Guilt for not sacrificing enough |
| **"Unlimited PTO"** | Freedom | No one takes time off |
| **"We invest in you"** | Growth | Indentured servitude |

### The Pattern

1. **Recruitment**: Love bombing, promises, prestige
2. **Onboarding**: "Family" culture, belonging, investment
3. **The Setup**: High expectations, early wins
4. **The Crack**: First criticism, planted doubt
5. **The Erosion**: Moving goalposts, increasing demands
6. **The Isolation**: Work becomes life, support networks fade
7. **The Breakdown**: Crisis point, self-blame
8. **The Choice**: Accept the system or escape

## Design Notes for Writers

1. **Pacing**: The manipulation should feel gradual - each chapter adds one more layer
2. **Perspective**: Stay close to Alex's POV so the reader experiences the gaslighting
3. **Dialogue**: Marcus should always sound reasonable - that's what makes it insidious
4. **The Turn**: Chapter 8 is the pivot - from victim to survivor
5. **The Exit**: Not a dramatic confrontation, but a quiet choice to leave
