# Chapter 6: The Gaslight

Alex started to question their own mind.

**THE INCIDENT**

It started with a small thing.

Alex had a meeting scheduled with Marcus to discuss the new project. They'd prepared a detailed plan, anticipating his feedback.

But when they arrived at his office, Marcus looked confused.

"Alex, I don't have you on my calendar. Are you sure we had a meeting?"

Alex pulled out their phone. "Yes, we scheduled it last week. Tuesday at 2 PM. You said—"

Marcus shook his head. "I don't remember that. I've been in back-to-back meetings all day. I think you might be confused."

*Confused.*

Alex stared at their calendar. The meeting was there. They'd received a confirmation.

But Marcus was looking at them with concern.

"Are you okay, Alex? You've seemed stressed lately. Maybe you should take a mental health day."

*I'm not confused. I know we had this meeting.*

But Marcus's certainty made them doubt.

*Did I imagine it? Am I losing my mind?*

**THE PATTERN**

The incidents multiplied.

Alex would remember a conversation one way. Marcus would remember it differently.

"I told you I needed the report by Friday."

"No, you said Monday."

"I never said that. Are you sure you're remembering correctly?"

"I thought we agreed on this approach."

"We never agreed on that. You must have misunderstood."

*Did I misunderstand? Am I remembering wrong?*

**THE DOCUMENTATION**

Alex started documenting everything.

They took notes in every meeting. They sent follow-up emails summarizing conversations. They saved every Slack message.

*If I have proof, I can't be gaslit.*

But when they brought up the documentation, Marcus would say:

"Alex, why are you keeping all these records? It's like you don't trust me. That's not how a team operates."

*Not trusting. That's bad. I should trust my manager.*

But the documentation was the only thing keeping Alex sane.

**THE BREAKDOWN**

One evening, Alex was working late when they received a message from Marcus:

*Hey, I've been thinking about your performance. I'm concerned that you're not stepping up the way I hoped. Can we talk tomorrow?*

Alex stared at the message.

*Not stepping up. Concerned.*

They'd been working 15-hour days. They'd delivered the project on time. They'd done everything Marcus asked.

*What more does he want?*

They couldn't sleep that night. They replayed every interaction, every piece of feedback, every conversation.

*What am I doing wrong? What am I missing?*

**THE CONVERSATION**

The next day, Alex went to Marcus's office.

"Marcus, I got your message. I'm... I'm confused. I've been working so hard. I delivered the project. What am I doing wrong?"

Marcus's expression softened. "Alex, I know you've been working hard. But hard work isn't the same as impact. And frankly, I'm not seeing the impact I expected from you."

*Impact. Again with impact.*

"But the metrics improved. The executives were happy. What more impact do you need?"

Marcus sighed. "Alex, you're being defensive. That's not a good look. I'm trying to help you grow, and you're pushing back."

*Defensive. Pushing back.*

Alex felt tears forming. "I'm sorry. I'm just... I'm trying so hard. I don't understand what you want from me."

Marcus stood up and came around the desk. He put a hand on Alex's shoulder.

"Alex, I think you're burning out. You've been working too hard. Maybe you should take some time off."

*Time off? After you just said I'm not having impact?*

"I can't take time off. I have another project—"

"The project will be fine. Jordan can take it over. You need to take care of yourself."

Alex felt like they were in a nightmare.

*He's taking my project away. Because I'm burned out. Because I've been working too hard. Because he made me work too hard.*

**THE TIME OFF**

Alex took a week off.

They didn't want to, but Marcus insisted. "It's for your own good," he said.

Alex spent the week in a fog. They couldn't stop thinking about work. They couldn't stop replaying the conversations in their head.

*Am I burned out? Am I not having impact? Am I defensive?*

They called their sister.

"Rachel, I think I'm losing my mind."

"What's wrong?"

Alex told her everything. The contradictory feedback. The forgotten meetings. The project being taken away.

Rachel was quiet for a long moment.

"Alex, this isn't you. This is them. They're gaslighting you."

*Gaslighting.*

"I don't know. Maybe I really am burned out. Maybe I'm not performing well."

"Alex, you've been working 15-hour days for months. You delivered a successful project. And they're telling you you're not having impact? That's not feedback. That's manipulation."

Alex wanted to believe her. But Marcus's voice was louder in their head.

*You're not stepping up. You're defensive. You're burned out.*

**THE RETURN**

Alex came back to work after a week.

Jordan had taken over their project. The team had moved on. Alex was assigned to a smaller, less visible project.

"This will be good for you," Marcus said. "Less pressure. A chance to recover."

*Recover. From what? From working too hard? From trying to prove myself?*

Alex sat at their desk, staring at their screen.

*What am I doing here?*

**THE MIRROR**

Sarah stopped by Alex's desk.

"Hey. How are you doing?"

Alex considered lying. But they were too tired.

"I don't know. I feel like I'm losing my mind."

Sarah nodded slowly. "Let me tell you something. Two years ago, I was where you are. Working 15-hour days. Desperate to please Marcus. Questioning my own sanity."

"What happened?"

"I burned out. Hard. Took three months off. Came back and... just stopped trying so hard."

Alex stared at her. "Stopped trying? But what about your career?"

Sarah laughed bitterly. "What career? I've been here two years and I'm doing the same work I was doing when I started. Marcus isn't going to promote me. He's not going to promote anyone. He keeps us all just insecure enough to work hard, but not secure enough to leave."

*Not secure enough to leave.*

The words hit Alex like a physical blow.

"Is that... is that intentional?"

Sarah shrugged. "I don't know if it's conscious. But it's the result. Look around, Alex. Everyone on this team is either new and eager, or burned out and going through the motions. No one is thriving. No one is growing. We're all just... surviving."

**END OF CHAPTER 6**
