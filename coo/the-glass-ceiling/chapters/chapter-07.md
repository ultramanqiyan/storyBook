# Chapter 7: The Breaking Point

**MONTH 6**

Alex hit rock bottom.

---

**THE SMALL PROJECT**

The new project was... small.

A minor feature enhancement. Something a junior PM could handle. Something that wouldn't be noticed by anyone important.

Alex tried to tell themselves it was a chance to recover. But they knew the truth: they'd been demoted.

*You're not having impact. You're burned out. You need less pressure.*

Marcus's words echoed in their head.

---

**THE EFFORT**

Alex threw themselves into the small project.

They worked just as hard as before. They wanted to prove they could still deliver. They wanted to earn back Marcus's trust.

But the project was too small to matter. No one noticed. No one cared.

At the next all-hands, the VP didn't mention Alex's name.

---

**THE ISOLATION**

Alex felt invisible.

They'd been moved to a desk in the corner. They weren't invited to important meetings. Their input wasn't sought on strategic decisions.

They'd gone from rising star to nobody in six months.

*What happened?*

---

**THE CALL**

One night, Alex's mom called.

"Alex, I haven't heard from you in weeks. I'm worried."

Alex felt tears forming. "I'm fine, Mom."

"You don't sound fine. You sound exhausted."

"I'm just... work has been hard."

"Then quit. Find another job. No job is worth this."

Alex wanted to explain. To tell her about the opportunity, the potential, the investment Marcus had made in them. But they couldn't find the words.

"It's not that simple, Mom."

"It is that simple. You come home every night exhausted. You never call. You've lost weight. This job is destroying you."

*Destroying me.*

The words hit too close to home.

---

**THE CRISIS**

The breaking point came on a Tuesday.

Alex had been working late - again - when they received an email from HR.

**Subject: Performance Improvement Plan**

Alex stared at the words.

*Performance Improvement Plan.*

They clicked open the email.

---

**THE PIP**

**MERIDIAN TECH**
**Performance Improvement Plan**

**Employee:** Alex Chen
**Manager:** Marcus Webb
**Date:** January 15, 2025

**Summary:**
Alex Chen has been placed on a Performance Improvement Plan due to concerns about their performance and impact. This plan outlines specific goals and expectations for the next 60 days.

**Areas of Concern:**
1. Strategic thinking - Alex has not demonstrated the ability to think beyond immediate tasks
2. Visibility - Alex has not positioned themselves as a leader within the team
3. Impact - Alex's work has not moved key metrics
4. Communication - Alex has been defensive when receiving feedback

**Goals:**
1. Demonstrate strategic thinking by proposing three new initiatives
2. Increase visibility by presenting at team meetings and all-hands
3. Deliver measurable impact on at least one key metric
4. Respond constructively to feedback without defensiveness

**Consequences:**
Failure to meet these goals may result in further action, up to and including termination.

---

Alex read the email three times.

*Strategic thinking. Visibility. Impact. Communication.*

The same vague, impossible-to-measure criteria that had been haunting them for six months.

*And now I have 60 days to fix it or I'm fired.*

---

**THE BREAKDOWN**

Alex broke down.

They sat at their desk, in the empty office, and cried. Not quiet tears - ugly, heaving sobs that shook their whole body.

Six months of exhaustion, confusion, and self-doubt poured out.

*What is wrong with me? Why can't I succeed here?*

---

**THE MOMENT**

After the tears stopped, Alex sat in the silence.

And something shifted.

*Wait.*

They pulled out their phone and opened the notes app. They'd been documenting everything for months.

They read through their notes.

*Meeting with Marcus - said I was too detail-oriented.*

*Meeting with Marcus - said I was too high-level.*

*Meeting with Marcus - said I needed to be more visible.*

*Meeting with Marcus - said I was scheduling too many meetings.*

*Meeting with Marcus - said I was defensive.*

*Meeting with Marcus - said I was burned out.*

*Meeting with Marcus - said I wasn't having impact.*

The contradictions stared back at them.

*This isn't me. This is him.*

---

**THE CONVERSATION**

The next day, Alex went to Sarah.

"I got a PIP."

Sarah's expression didn't change. "I know. Marcus told me."

"He told you?"

"He tells everyone. It's part of the playbook. Isolate, undermine, document, remove."

Alex felt cold. "This has happened before?"

"To three people in the last two years. All high performers. All eager to please. All pushed out."

*Pushed out.*

"Why?"

"Because they were threats. Or because they asked too many questions. Or because Marcus needed to show he was 'managing performance' to get his own promotion."

Alex felt like they'd been punched.

"This is... this is a system."

Sarah nodded. "Now you're getting it."

---

**THE CHOICE**

Alex sat with the information for a day.

They could accept the PIP and try to meet impossible goals. They could quit and walk away. Or they could fight.

*Fight.*

But what would fighting look like?

---

**THE DOCUMENT**

Alex started writing.

Not for work. For themselves.

They wrote everything they remembered. Every contradictory piece of feedback. Every impossible expectation. Every moment of gaslighting.

They wrote about the love bombing in the beginning. The isolation that followed. The burnout that was weaponized against them.

They wrote about Sarah's warning. About Jordan's complicity. About the pattern of high performers being pushed out.

When they finished, they had a 20-page document.

*This is what's happening to me. This is what's happening to everyone.*

---

**THE DECISION**

Alex made a choice.

They weren't going to accept the PIP. They weren't going to quit quietly. They were going to expose what was happening.

But first, they needed to understand the full scope.

---

**END OF CHAPTER 7**
