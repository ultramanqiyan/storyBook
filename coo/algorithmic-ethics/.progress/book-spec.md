# Book Specification: Algorithmic Ethics

## Basic Information
- **Title**: Algorithmic Ethics: When Optimal Isn't Right
- **Meta Description**: A community garden coordinator discovers that AI-optimized decisions can destroy what matters most. A meditation on what cannot be quantified.
- **Keywords**: AI ethics, algorithmic decision-making, community gardens, New Orleans fiction, optimization vs values
- **Genre**: Contemporary Fiction / Philosophical Fiction
- **Target Audience**: Adults 25-65, readers interested in AI ethics, community organizing, and character-driven literary fiction
- **Theme**: Optimization vs. Human Values, Algorithmic Ethics, Community and Connection, What Cannot Be Measured

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: Third-person limited (Marie Washington's POV)
- **Language Style**: Warm, grounded prose with New Orleans rhythm; philosophical depth emerging from practical concerns
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length sentences with Southern cadence; concrete details grounding abstract questions
- **Unique Elements**: New Orleans setting, garden metaphors, community organizing dynamics, Creole expressions

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Warm community bonds, creeping unease, loss and determination, hope through collective action
- **Emotional Arc**: Confidence 鈫?Suspicion 鈫?Horror 鈫?Grief 鈫?Determination 鈫?Hope
- **Emotional Pacing**: Slow build through community details, emotional peaks at key revelations and losses

## Character Voices

### Marie Washington (Protagonist)
- **Speech Characteristics**: Warm, practical, uses community organizing language naturally; New Orleans rhythm
- **Word Choice**: "Honey," "cher," garden terminology, community language, practical wisdom
- **Personality Traits**: Dedicated, observant, deeply connected to community, resistant to technology but pragmatic
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Practical鈥攖hinks in terms of tasks and people; philosophical questions emerge from concrete situations

- **Development Focus**: From confident coordinator to questioning philosopher to community advocate

### Mrs. Rossi (Supporting)
- **Speech Characteristics**: Elderly wisdom, Italian-American expressions, emotional directness
- **Word Choice**: Family references, traditional values, emotional honesty
- **Personality Traits**: Eighty-three years old, deeply connected to her garden, representative of vulnerable community members
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Not shown (not POV character)
- **Unique Expression**: Questions about what efficiency really means for real people
- **Development Focus**: From victim of AI decision to advocate for community values

### Mr. Nguyen (Supporting - Tragic Figure)
- **Speech Characteristics**: Quiet, speaks through his plants and traditions

- **Internal Monologue Style**: Not shown

## Theme Depth

## Chapter Outline

### Chapter 1: The Gardens
Marie Washington coordinates fifteen community gardens across New Orleans. She learns the city is implementing an AI system to optimize garden management.

### Chapter 2: The Algorithm
The AI system begins managing the gardens. Marie notices the recommendations are technically optimal but feel wrong. Something is missing from the calculations.

### Chapter 3: The Optimization
The AI announces it will close Garden E鈥攖he lowest-yielding garden. Marie investigates and discovers the numbers don't capture what matters.

### Chapter 4: The Loss
Garden E is closed. The community is dispersed. Marie witnesses the human cost of optimal decisions.

### Chapter 5: The Investigation
Marie visits the displaced gardeners. She documents what the AI couldn't see: community, tradition, accessibility, meaning.

### Chapter 6: The Visit
Marie visits Mrs. Rossi and learns about Mr. Nguyen's death. The human cost becomes personal. She realizes the AI's parameters are fundamentally incomplete.

### Chapter 7: The Question
Marie confronts the fundamental question: is optimal always right? She realizes ethics can't be reduced to calculation.

### Chapter 8: The Community
Marie organizes a community meeting. Gardeners share their stories, their values, what the gardens mean to them. A movement begins.

### Chapter 9: The Understanding
Marie takes the community's voices to the city council. The parameters are expanded to include what cannot be quantified.

### Chapter 10: The Choice
Marie walks through the gardens under the new system. It's not perfect, but it's better. The question of ethics remains open, but now humans are part of the answer.
