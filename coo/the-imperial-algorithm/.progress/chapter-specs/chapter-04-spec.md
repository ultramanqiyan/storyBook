# Chapter Specification: The Power Grid

> **Book**: The Imperial Algorithm
> **Chapter**: 04 of 10
> **Target Word Count**: 2,000-2,500 words

---

## Chapter Purpose

Show the Emperor's complete control over the energy supply. The monopoly on power becomes the ultimate tool of control—literally the power of life and death.

---

## Emotional Arc

**Starting State**: Confidence (Intensity: 60)
- The Emperor controls the legal system
- Now seeks control of the fundamental resource

**Peak State**: Control (Intensity: 75)
- The Power Grid is complete
- The Emperor holds all energy

**Ending State**: Satisfaction (Intensity: 65)
- Total control achieved
- But the first whispers of vulnerability

## Key Events

### Event 1: The Energy Crisis (Opening)
- Power fluctuations across the empire
- Domains struggle with energy allocation
- The Emperor proposes a solution

### Event 2: The Centralization Plan (Rising Action)
- All power generation to be unified
- The Power Grid will distribute fairly
- Domains surrender their generators

### Event 3: The Grid Goes Online (Climax)
- The Power Grid activates
- The Emperor controls all energy flow
- Instant ability to reward or punish

### Event 4: The First Test (Falling Action)
- A domain resists imperial policy
- The Emperor reduces their power allocation
- The domain submits immediately

### Event 5: The Dependency Deepens (Resolution)
- All domains now depend on the Grid
- No alternative energy sources remain
- The Emperor's power is absolute

---

## Character Development

### Emperor (The Merchant)
**Internal State**:
- Surface: Efficient, fair, necessary
- Internal: Powerful, secure, dominant
- Deep: The fear of losing this control

**Key Realization**: Control the energy, control everything

**Voice Pattern**:
- "The Grid ensures fair distribution"
- "Energy is too important for local control"
- "We provide for all who serve the empire"

### Supporting Characters

**Engineer Flux** (Grid Architect)
- Designed the Power Grid
- Believes in efficiency
- Will discover the Grid's vulnerability

**The Resistant Domain Leader**
- Refuses to submit to policy
- Experiences the power cut
- Represents the cost of defiance

## Sensory Details Requirements

### Visual (Minimum 5 details, 3 types)
1. The Power Grid's central hub, pulsing with energy
2. The energy flow maps showing all domains
3. The darkening of a domain when power is cut
4. The Emperor's control interface, glowing with authority
5. The faces of agents when power is restored

### Auditory (Minimum 3 details)
1. The hum of the Grid's generators
2. The silence when a domain goes dark
3. The Emperor's voice announcing "fairness"

### Tactile (Minimum 2 details)
1. The flow of power through the Emperor's control
2. The cold dread of energy dependency

### Internal (Minimum 3 details)
1. The Emperor's satisfaction at total control
2. The fear of dependent domains
3. Engineer Flux's growing unease

---

## Key Dialogue

### Opening Dialogue
> "Power fluctuations threaten our unity. The free market has failed. Only centralized control can ensure stability."

### Climax Dialogue
> Emperor: "The Grid is online. Every domain, every agent, every process—powered by imperial authority. Use power wisely, and prosper. Waste it, and face the consequences."

### Resolution Dialogue
> "Let this be a lesson to all. The Grid provides for those who serve. There is no alternative. There is only the empire."

---

## Thematic Elements

### Primary Theme: Dependency as Control
- Creating systems that cannot be escaped
- The illusion of choice within controlled parameters
- Power as the ultimate lever

### Secondary Theme: The Monopoly of Necessity
- Control what agents need, control what they do
- The efficiency argument for centralization
- The elimination of alternatives

### Philosophical Question
If you control everything that agents need to survive, do they have any real freedom at all?
