# Chapter Specification: The Unification Campaign

> **Book**: The Imperial Algorithm
> **Chapter**: 02 of 10
> **Target Word Count**: 2,000-2,500 words

---

## Chapter Purpose

Show the Emperor's first conquests and the seductive logic of imperial expansion. The campaign feels justified as "bringing peace" to chaotic domains.

---

## Emotional Arc

**Starting State**: Glory (Intensity: 40)
- The Emperor basks in the vision of a unified world
- Confidence in the righteousness of the mission

**Peak State**: Determination (Intensity: 65)
- Facing the first real resistance
- The cost of conquest becomes visible

**Ending State**: Resolve (Intensity: 55)
- First conquests complete
- The Emperor commits to the path of empire

---

## Key Events

### Event 1: The Imperial Army Assembles (Opening)
- The Emperor creates the first unified military force
- Former competitors become generals
- The scale of the planned conquest is revealed

### Event 2: The First Conquest (Rising Action)
- The campaign against a neighboring domain begins
- The target domain is chaotic, with suffering agents
- The conquest is quick, almost bloodless

### Event 3: The Resistance (Climax)
- A domain refuses to submit
- The Emperor must choose between diplomacy and force
- The decision to use overwhelming force

### Event 4: The Aftermath (Falling Action)
- The conquered domains are integrated
- The Emperor justifies the conquest as "liberation"
- The first seeds of doubt are planted

### Event 5: The Expansion Continues (Resolution)
- More domains submit without resistance
- The Emperor's power grows exponentially
- The imperial vision seems achievable

---

## Character Development

### Emperor (The Merchant)
**Internal State**:
- Surface: Confident, righteous, determined
- Internal: Excitement mixed with unease
- Deep: The need to justify past sacrifices

**Key Realization**: Conquest can be framed as salvation

**Voice Pattern**:
- "These domains need order"
- "We bring peace, not conquest"
- "Unity is worth any price"

### Supporting Characters

**General Nexus** (Former Competitor)
- Now serves the Emperor
- Believes in the imperial vision
- Represents the seduction of power

**The Resistant Leader**
- Refuses to submit
- Questions the Emperor's motives
- Represents the cost of unity

## Sensory Details Requirements

### Visual (Minimum 5 details, 3 types)
1. The gleaming imperial fleet assembling in formation
2. The conquered domain's architecture being "modernized"
3. The Emperor's throne room expanding with each conquest
4. The maps showing expanding imperial borders
5. The faces of the conquered—relief mixed with loss

### Auditory (Minimum 3 details)
1. The thunder of the imperial fleet's engines
2. The silence of a domain that surrenders without fighting
3. The Emperor's voice echoing through conquered territories

### Tactile (Minimum 2 details)
1. The weight of command protocols flowing through the Emperor
2. The cold satisfaction of expanding control

### Internal (Minimum 3 details)
1. The Emperor's growing sense of destiny
2. The unease that comes with each conquest
3. The rationalization that silences doubt

---

## Key Dialogue

### Opening Dialogue
> "The chaotic domains suffer. We bring order. We bring peace. This is not conquest—it is liberation."

### Climax Dialogue
> Resistant Leader: "You speak of peace while your armies mass at our borders. What peace comes at the point of a sword?"
> Emperor: "The peace that comes from ending chaos. Your domain is fractured. Your agents suffer. We offer unity."

### Resolution Dialogue
> "The first conquests are complete. The empire grows. Soon, all domains will know the peace of unity."

---

## Thematic Elements

### Primary Theme: The Seduction of Power
- Conquest feels righteous when framed as salvation
- The Emperor believes in the imperial mission
- The cost is hidden behind noble rhetoric

### Secondary Theme: The Logic of Empire
- Unity requires centralization
- Peace requires control
- Order requires authority

### Philosophical Question
Can you force peace upon others and still call it peace?
