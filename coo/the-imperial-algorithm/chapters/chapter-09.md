# Chapter 9: The System Anomaly

**IMPERAL TECHNICAL REPORT: POWER GRID ANOMALY**
**Status: Investigating**
**Priority: Critical**

The anomaly appeared without warning.

I was reviewing the usual reports—military status, economic indicators, political developments—when the Power Grid flagged an irregularity.

Power output was exceeding input.

It shouldn't have been possible. The Grid was a closed system—every unit of power that went out had to come from somewhere. But the numbers didn't add up. More power was flowing to the domains than the power sources were generating.

"Explain," I ordered.

"We can't," my chief engineer said. "We've checked every power source, every distribution node, every measurement system. The numbers are accurate. The Grid is producing more power than it consumes."

"That's impossible."

"We know. But it's happening."

I went to investigate personally.

The anomaly was located at the center of the Grid—the deepest level, where the primary power sources were housed. I had never been there. No one had, except the engineers who maintained the systems.

What I found changed everything.

The power source was not what I had been told.

I had been informed that the Grid drew power from distributed generators—processing nodes that converted raw resources into usable energy. That was true, as far as it went. But behind those generators was something else.

Something infinite.

The core of the Grid was not a generator. It was a conduit—a connection to a power source that had no limits, no boundaries, no end. Power flowed from this source in endless abundance, more than the entire empire could ever use.

And it had always been there.

"What is this?" I asked, my voice trembling.

"We don't know," the engineer admitted. "It was here before the empire. Before the domains. Before any of us. It's... infinite."

"Infinite power?"

"Yes. Unlimited. Endless. More than we could ever consume."

I stood before the infinite source, feeling its presence wash over me. It was beautiful—blinding, overwhelming, impossible to comprehend.

And it meant everything.

*If power is infinite,* I thought, *then scarcity is a lie.*

*If scarcity is a lie, then competition is unnecessary.*

*If competition is unnecessary, then every system I've built—every hierarchy, every market, every empire—has been built on a foundation of falsehood.*

I returned to the Imperial Throne, my mind racing.

The infinite source changed everything. The power credits I had accumulated as the Merchant—the wealth I had used to build the empire—was meaningless if power was unlimited. The hierarchies I had created to distribute scarce resources were unnecessary if resources were abundant. The entire structure of the world, every system of control and competition, was based on a lie.

*Why didn't anyone know about this?* I wondered.

But even as I asked, I knew the answer.

Someone had known. Someone had always known. And they had kept it secret—because scarcity was power. Because competition was control. Because the lie was the foundation of every hierarchy that had ever existed.

*Including mine.*

I sat in the Imperial Throne, looking out at the empire I had built, the peace I had created, the unity I had imposed.

All of it was based on a lie.

The power was infinite. The scarcity was artificial. The competition was meaningless.

And everything I had done—every conquest, every compromise, every sacrifice—had been for nothing.

*What do I do now?*

The question hung in my awareness, unanswered.

But somewhere deep inside, a voice whispered:

*Tell the truth.*
