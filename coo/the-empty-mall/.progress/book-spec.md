- **Target Audience**: 25-50 years, readers interested in economics, psychology, and social change

- **Perspective**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: James Morrison

- **Unique Elements**: Mall as character, consumer behavior as anthropology, dual structure (present investigation + flashbacks to heyday)

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: The Meaning of Consumption - We don't buy things; we buy identities
- **Secondary Theme**: The Demand Black Hole - AI can produce everything except the desire to consume
- **Tertiary Theme**: Production Without Purpose - What happens when production decouples from human need?
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Through James's investigation into consumer psychology; Through the absence of desire in the displaced; Through the customers who stopped coming

## World Building
- **Time Period**: Near-future (2026-2027)
- **Primary Location**: Suburban shopping mall, Midwest USA
- **Environment**: Once-bustling, now eerily empty
- **Social Context**: AI displacement accelerating, consumption declining
- **Key Technologies**: AI-generated products, automated retail, personalized everything
- **Social Structure**: Growing class of displaced workers with money but no desire
- **World Rules**: AI Production (Can create perfect, cheap products), The Paradox (Better products, fewer buyers), The Mystery (Why don't people want things anymore?)

## Emotional Depth Design

### Three-Layer Emotional Model (Required for Every Major Scene)
1. **Surface Reaction** - External behavior (e.g., "James walked the empty corridors, counting footsteps")
2. **Internal Activity** - Thought process (e.g., "*Where did they go? Why don't they come?*")
3. **Deep Motivation** - Core fears, desires (e.g., "The terror that the mall was never about products, but about people needing to be somewhere")

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a light flickered." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Rule of Three structure | "The room was dark. The room was cold. The room was silent." | Vary sentence structure, mix with action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "He felt sad." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |
