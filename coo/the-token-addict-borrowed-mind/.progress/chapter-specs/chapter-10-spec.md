## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
The question had been building for weeks, months maybe, and now it demanded an answer. Marcus sat at his desk in the empty office, the glow of his monitors the only light, and let the question finally surface: *Who is thinking now?* It wasn't a new question. He'd asked it before, in different forms, at different moments. But now, after everything—the dependency, the failed attempt, the revelation, the mask—it felt different. More urgent. More terrifying. Because he didn't have an answer. And the silence where the answer should be was the scariest thing he'd ever encountered.

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
The question remained, echoing in the silence of his mind: *Who is thinking now?* He still didn't have an answer. Maybe he never would. But sitting there, in the glow of the AI that had become his mind, Marcus felt something shift. The question itself—the asking, the wondering, the not-knowing—felt human. Felt like his. And maybe that was the beginning. Not an answer, but a question. Not a solution, but a search. Not a self, but the possibility of one. He closed his laptop, the CodeForge interface disappearing, and for a moment, sat in the dark. The question was still there. He was still there. And tomorrow, he would try again. Not to find the answer, but to live the question. *Who is thinking now?* He didn't know. But he was asking. And maybe, for now, that was enough.
