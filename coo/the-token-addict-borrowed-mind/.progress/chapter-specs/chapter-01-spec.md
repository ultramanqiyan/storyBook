### 2.3 Emotional Beats Breakdown

#### Beat 1: The Morning Routine (Position: 0-15%)
- **Trigger**: Morning commute to Palo Alto
- **Surface Reaction**: Checking emails, planning the day
- **Internal Process**: *Another day, another sprint. At least the architecture review went well yesterday.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Pride in his competence, identity as "the guy who solves hard problems"
- **Sensory Anchor**: The familiar weight of his backpack, the Caltrain rhythm

#### Beat 2: The Problem Solver (Position: 15-35%)
- **Trigger**: A colleague asks for help with a complex bug
- **Surface Reaction**: Confidently walking through the code, explaining the solution
- **Internal Process**: *This is what I do. This is who I am.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Validation of his expertise, fear of becoming obsolete
- **Sensory Anchor**: The satisfying click of keyboard, the glow of multiple monitors

#### Beat 3: The Introduction (Position: 35-55%)
- **Trigger**: Jennifer mentions CodeForge in a team meeting
- **Surface Reaction**: Polite interest, asking questions
- **Internal Process**: *Another AI tool. They come and go. Probably just hype.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Skepticism born from experience, slight anxiety about "keeping up"
- **Sensory Anchor**: The conference room's recycled air, the glow of the presentation screen

#### Beat 4: The First Prompt (Position: 55-80%)
- **Trigger**: Alone at his desk, he decides to try CodeForge
- **Surface Reaction**: Typing a prompt, watching the output
- **Internal Process**: *Wait. This is... actually good. Better than I expected.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Surprise mixed with a flicker of something he can't name—relief? Fear?
- **Sensory Anchor**: The cursor blinking, the code appearing character by character

#### Beat 5: The Realization (Position: 80-100%)
- **Trigger**: He realizes how much faster he could work with this
- **Surface Reaction**: Smiling, planning how to integrate it into his workflow
- **Internal Process**: *This could change everything. I could be so much more efficient.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Ambition, the drive to excel, the unexamined assumption that efficiency = good
- **Sensory Anchor**: The late afternoon light through the office windows

### 6.2 Dialogue Segment Specifications

#### Segment 1: The Help Request

**Participants**: Marcus, junior developer (let's call him Alex)

**Surface Content**: Alex asks for help with a bug

**Subtext**: Alex is insecure about his abilities; Marcus enjoys being the expert

**Emotional Undercurrent**:
- Marcus: Patient, slightly pleased to be asked
- Alex: Nervous, grateful, slightly embarrassed

**Power Dynamics**: Marcus has clear power; he's the expert

**Dialogue Beats**:
1. Alex approaches hesitantly
2. Marcus asks clarifying questions
3. Marcus explains the solution
4. Alex thanks him profusely
5. Marcus returns to his work

**Action Beats Required**: Marcus turns in chair, points at screen, Alex nods

**What Must Be Revealed**: Marcus's expertise and teaching style

**What Must Be Concealed**: Any hint of Marcus's future dependency

**Forbidden Expressions**:
- [ ] "What do you mean?"
- [ ] "You know what I mean"
- [ ] Direct emotion statements
- [ ] Over-explanation
