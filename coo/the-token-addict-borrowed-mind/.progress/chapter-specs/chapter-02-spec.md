## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Action/Continuation

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
The next morning, Marcus arrived at the office thirty minutes early. The parking garage was half-empty, the elevators quiet. He'd spent the previous night thinking about CodeForge—not just the tool itself, but what it could mean for his work, his career, his standing in the company. If this was the future, he wanted to get there first. His desk was exactly as he'd left it, dual monitors glowing with the screensaver. He woke them up, opened CodeForge, and began to configure it for serious work. The interface was clean, intuitive, almost inviting. He typed his first prompt of the day, watching the cursor blink as the AI processed his request. Within seconds, code began to appear—not just functional, but elegant. Marcus smiled. This was going to be a good day.

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Type**: Determination/Setup

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
The office was dark now, most of his colleagues long gone. Marcus sat at his desk, the glow of his monitors the only light. He'd stayed late to plan—to think about how to push CodeForge even further. What if he used it for architecture decisions? What if he let it handle all the boilerplate? What if he could double his output again? The questions felt exciting, not alarming. He was a software engineer, after all. Optimizing systems was what he did. Why shouldn't he optimize himself? He closed his laptop and stood, the familiar ache in his shoulders from hours of sitting. Tomorrow, he'd try something new. He'd let CodeForge handle a problem he would normally think through himself. Just to see what happened. The thought felt like a small adventure, a step into unknown territory. He didn't notice the slight quickening of his pulse, the way his mind had already begun to delegate the thinking to tomorrow's AI session.
