He'd been scrolling through his CodeForge history for an hour, trying to find the moment when an idea had become his. But the more he looked, the less he could tell. Had he thought of the architecture first, or had the AI suggested it? Had he written that algorithm, or had he just approved the AI's version? The lines were blurred, smudged, erased.

In the reflection of his dark screen, Marcus saw his own face. But for a moment—a terrifying, frozen moment—he didn't recognize the person looking back. *Who is that?* he thought. *Who is thinking now?* The question hung in the air, unanswered, unanswerable. He was still there, somewhere. He had to be. But where did Marcus end and CodeForge begin?

## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
After the failed attempt, Marcus returned to AI-assisted work with a new heaviness. The shame was there, but something else was growing beneath it—a question that wouldn't leave him alone. He typed his prompts, reviewed the output, made adjustments. The familiar rhythm. But now, each solution felt different. Foreign. He'd look at the code and wonder: *Is this what I would have written?* The question gnawed at him, growing louder with each passing hour. By the end of the day, he couldn't ignore it anymore. He opened his CodeForge history and began to scroll.

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
The question echoed in his mind, growing louder with each passing moment: *Who is thinking now?* He wanted to believe the answer was simple—that he was still there, still thinking, still in control. But the evidence on his screen told a different story. Month after month of prompts and responses, suggestions and approvals, until the line between his thoughts and AI's had dissolved completely. He was still Marcus Webb. He still had memories, preferences, a life outside of work. But when it came to thinking—really thinking, the kind that had once defined him—he wasn't sure anymore. The AI had become his mind, and he'd let it happen willingly, eagerly, in the name of efficiency. Now he sat in the dark, staring at his own reflection, and wondered if there was anything left of the person he used to be.
