## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
The morning after David's layoff, Marcus made a decision. He would work without AI for one day. Just one day, to prove to himself that he still could. The rationalizations from before felt hollow now, exposed by David's departure. If he was truly in control, truly just "using a tool," then he should be able to set it aside. He sat at his desk, took a deep breath, and closed the CodeForge tab. The interface disappeared, leaving only his blank editor. *I can do this,* he told himself. *I've been coding for sixteen years. AI has only been part of my life for a few months.* He cracked his knuckles, placed his fingers on the keys, and began to type.

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
The solution appeared on his screen, elegant and complete, and Marcus felt nothing but shame. He'd tried. He'd really tried. And he'd failed. Not because the problem was hard—it wasn't—but because he couldn't think anymore. Somewhere in the past few months, he'd lost the ability to solve problems on his own. The AI had become his mind, and he'd let it. He stared at the code, at the proof of his dependency, and wondered what was left of him. What was he without AI? Just a prompt engineer? A translator between humans and machines? The questions from before came rushing back, louder now: *What if I can't think anymore? Who is thinking now?* He didn't have answers. He only had the code on his screen, the AI that had written it, and the hollow feeling of a man who'd given away something essential without even realizing it.
