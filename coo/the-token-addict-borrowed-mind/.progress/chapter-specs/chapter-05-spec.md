## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Internal/Continuation

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
The doubt from yesterday lingered like a hangover. Marcus sat at his desk, staring at the CodeForge icon, the question still echoing in his mind: *What if I can't think anymore?* But as he watched his colleagues begin their day, he noticed something. Everyone was using AI. Not just some people—everyone. The developer to his left had a chat open with an AI assistant. The one across the aisle was copying code from a suggestion. Even the senior architect, who Marcus had always admired for his independence, was using AI to design a system. *Maybe I'm overthinking this,* Marcus told himself. *Maybe this is just... normal.*

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Type**: Suppression/Setup

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
By the end of the day, Marcus had successfully buried the question. He had rationalized, justified, and dismissed. He had compared himself to others and found himself not just normal, but ahead. He had watched David struggle and felt validated in his choice. He had returned to the comfortable rhythm of AI-assisted work, and the doubt had faded to a distant whisper. But the question was still there, buried deep, waiting. It would come back. It always comes back. For now, though, Marcus was fine. He was productive. He was successful. He was in control. He opened his laptop the next morning and began again, the question safely buried under layers of rationalization and productivity and the comfortable, seductive ease of not having to think.
