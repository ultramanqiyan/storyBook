## 2. Narrative Style

### 2.1 Perspective
- **Point of View**: First-person
- **POV Character(s)**: Marcus Webb
- **POV Consistency**: Single, consistent throughout

### 2.2 Narrative Structure
- **Dual Structure**: Yes
- **Structure Type**: Present timeline + Internal philosophical questioning
- **Interweaving Method**: External events trigger internal existential questioning; the narrative reveals the gradual blurring of Marcus's thoughts and AI's suggestions

### 2.3 Language Style
- **Overall Tone**: Confident early, increasingly uncertain, ultimately questioning
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Short analytical statements (early), hesitant and fragmented (late)
- **Vocabulary Level**: Technical terminology mixed with philosophical vocabulary
- **Unique Stylistic Elements**: The gradual intrusion of AI-like phrases into Marcus's internal monologue; moments where he can't distinguish his thoughts from AI outputs

### 2.4 Distinctive Features
- **What Makes This Book Unique**: First-person psychological journey into cognitive dependency, with the narrator's reliability becoming increasingly uncertain
- **Comparative Titles**: "The Circle" by Dave Eggers, "Klara and the Sun" by Kazuo Ishiguro, "Exhalation" by Ted Chiang

He's been coding since he was 14, takes pride in his craft, and has always been the "go-to" person for difficult problems. When he first encounters CodeForge, he's skeptical—but the efficiency gains are undeniable.

**Core Wound**: He believes he's using AI as a tool, but slowly realizes he's becoming dependent on it. The question "Who is thinking now?" haunts him.

**Evolution Through Book**:
- Chapters 1-3: Confident, sees AI as enhancement
- Chapters 4-6: First doubts, rationalization
- Chapters 7-9: Fear, shame, hypocrisy
- Chapter 10: Confusion, existential crisis

## 5. Thematic Depth

### 5.1 Core Themes
1. **Cognitive Outsourcing**: When we delegate thinking to AI, what happens to our minds?
2. **The Illusion of Enhancement**: Are we becoming more capable, or just more dependent?
3. **Identity in the AI Era**: If AI does the thinking, who am I?

### 5.2 Philosophical Exploration
| Question | Exploration Approach |
|----------|---------------------|
| What is independent thought? | Marcus's journey from confident thinker to confused borrower |
| Can borrowed thoughts be "mine"? | The blurring line between his ideas and AI's suggestions |
| What is the self without thinking? | The existential crisis when he can't distinguish his thoughts |

## 7. Sensory Detail Requirements (Quantified)

### 7.1 Required Sensory Types
Each chapter MUST include sensory details from at least **3 different categories**:

| Sense | Specific Types | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Visual** | Screen glow, code colors, office lighting, facial expressions | "The code completion suggestions appeared in soft gray text, almost ghostly against the black terminal" |
| **Auditory** | Keyboard clicks, notification sounds, meeting voices, silence | "The mechanical keyboard clattered as CodeForge auto-completed another function" |
| **Tactile** | Keyboard texture, chair discomfort, sweating, tension | "His fingers hovered over the keys, uncertain, waiting for the AI's suggestion" |
| **Olfactory** | Office coffee, stale air, ozone from electronics | "The break room smelled of burnt coffee and quiet desperation" |
| **Gustatory** | Coffee taste, dry mouth from stress | "His coffee had gone cold, bitter on his tongue" |
| **Internal** | Heartbeat, breathing, mental fog, anxiety | "His chest tightened as he stared at the blank screen without AI assistance" |

### 7.2 Quantified Standards
- **Minimum per chapter**: 5 sensory details
- **Minimum categories per chapter**: 3
- **Distribution target**: Visual (40%), Others (60% combined)

### 7.3 Sensory Detail Functions
Each sensory detail should serve one or more functions:
- [ ] Establish atmosphere (tech office, late-night coding)
- [ ] Reveal character state (anxiety, dependency, doubt)
- [ ] Advance plot (AI notification triggers decision)
- [ ] Create contrast (with AI vs. without AI)
- [ ] Trigger memory (comparing past self to present)

## 9. Prohibited Elements (Explicit List)

### 9.1 AI Writing Patterns (STRICTLY FORBIDDEN)
These phrases and patterns must be completely eliminated:

| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Vague location endings | "And somewhere..." | Specific location: "In the server room three floors below..." |
| Omniscient foreshadowing | "Neither of them knew..." | Character's intuition: "He felt a chill but couldn't say why..." |
| Cliche transitions | "will never be the same" | Specific change: "The notification tone sounded different after that" |
| False beginnings | "just the beginning" | Concrete continuation: "Three more messages arrived before noon" |
| Rule of Three | "It was X, it was Y, it was Z" | Varied structure with specific details |
| Abstract emotions | "He felt sad" | Physical manifestation: "His shoulders dropped. He didn't reach for his coffee." |
| Template dialogue | "What do you mean?" / "You know what I mean" | Subtext-rich alternatives |
| Repetitive structures | Similar sentence openings | Varied rhythm and structure |
| Overused conjunctions | "Moreover", "Furthermore", "In addition" | Direct connection or implication |
| Generic descriptions | "The room was nice" | Specific, character-relevant details |

### 9.2 Narrative Prohibitions
- [ ] Simple moral preaching (show complexity instead)
- [ ] Excessive technical descriptions (maintain accessibility)
- [ ] Protagonist suddenly "awakening" to solve problems (gradual growth only)
- [ ] Giving readers simple answers (leave room for interpretation)
- [ ] Deus ex machina resolutions
- [ ] Perfect character consistency (allow realistic contradictions)

### 9.3 Dialogue Prohibitions
- [ ] Direct statement of emotions ("I feel sad about...")
- [ ] Over-explanation ("The reason I'm doing this is...")
- [ ] Perfect responses (characters always say the right thing)
- [ ] Uninterrupted speeches (use action beats, interruptions)
- [ ] Immediate understanding (characters should misunderstand each other)

## 11. Open Ending Design (Chapter 10)

### 11.1 Unresolved Questions
1. Can Marcus ever reclaim his independent thinking?
2. Is the dependency reversible, or has he fundamentally changed?
3. What does "thinking" even mean in the AI era?

### 11.2 New Suspense
- Marcus receives a new project that requires original thinking
- Sarah asks him a question he can't answer without AI
- He stares at a blank screen, waiting for a suggestion that doesn't come

### 11.3 Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: Confused, questioning, uncertain of his own mind
- **Key Relationship**: Sarah notices something is wrong, but Marcus can't explain

### 11.4 Reader Engagement
- **Questions for Reader**: What would you do in Marcus's position? Where is the line between tool and dependency?
- **Emotional Resonance**: The unsettling feeling of not knowing your own mind
- **Thematic Echo**: "When thinking becomes borrowing, who is thinking?"
