### Opening
James stood before the board, his notes in hand. He had spent weeks preparing this moment, wrestling with what to say. He had no certainty. He had no faith. But he had a responsibility.

### Ending
"I recommend we proceed with the algorithm," James said, "but with significant modifications. We must diversify the criteria to value care work, creativity, and community contribution. We must build in ongoing oversight to detect and correct bias. We must acknowledge that no algorithm can be truly neutral—that all judgment reflects values, and we must choose our values consciously." He paused. "I used to believe in divine judgment. I lost that faith. But I still believe in judgment—in the necessity of discerning what is right, what is fair, what is just. That judgment cannot be automated. It must be human, and it must be humble."
