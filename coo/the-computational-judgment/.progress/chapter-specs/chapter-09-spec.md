### Ending
James walked home through London, his mind clear for the first time in weeks. He had lost his faith in divine judgment. But he had found something else: an understanding that all judgment—divine, human, or algorithmic—reveals more about the judges than the judged. The algorithm was a mirror. And what it reflected was not God's will, but human values—imperfect, biased, but capable of being examined and improved. That was the work. That was the faith he had left: not certainty, but the commitment to keep trying to be fair, to keep examining his own biases, to keep building systems that reflected the best of human values rather than the worst.
