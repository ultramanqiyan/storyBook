### Opening
James couldn't sleep. The board's questions echoed in his mind, triggering memories he usually kept buried. He had been a pastor for fifteen years. He had preached about judgment, about sin, about divine justice. He had believed he knew what was right. And then Sarah had died.

### Ending
Sarah had come to him for counseling about her relationship. He had told her, gently but firmly, that living with her partner outside of marriage was against God's will. He had urged her to change her life. A month later, she was dead by suicide. James had never recovered. His certainty had died with her. Now he taught ethics without certainty, trying to do good without knowing if he was right. And now he would help design an algorithm that would judge millions. The irony was not lost on him.
