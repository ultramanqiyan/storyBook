### Opening
The debate began immediately. "We should optimize for social contribution," one board member argued. "Productivity, community involvement, civic engagement." "That discriminates against the disabled, the elderly, the marginalized," another countered. "We need to optimize for need, not contribution." James listened, his mind racing. These were the questions he had studied for twenty years. But now they weren't theoretical. Now they would be encoded into an algorithm that would judge millions.

### Ending
The meeting ended without consensus. The criteria remained undecided. But James had glimpsed the fundamental problem: any criteria the board chose would reflect values, and values were not neutral. They would be shaped by who was in the room, by what they believed, by what they thought mattered. The algorithm would be fair only if the values it encoded were fair. And who got to decide that?
