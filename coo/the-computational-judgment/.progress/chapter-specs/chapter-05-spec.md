## 5. Key Paragraph Specifications

### Opening
The test cases were meant to validate the algorithm, to show that it worked as intended. But as James watched the results populate the screen, he felt a growing sense of horror. The algorithm was making decisions that seemed fair on the surface but revealed deep biases underneath.

### Ending
James stared at the results. The algorithm consistently undervalued people who didn't fit traditional models of productivity—caregivers, artists, the disabled, the elderly. It penalized people for circumstances beyond their control. It encoded the values of the people who had designed it, and those values were not neutral. "This isn't fair," James said. Dr. Sharma looked defensive. "The algorithm doesn't have bias. It's math." "Math created by humans," James countered. "And humans have biases. The algorithm just hides them behind numbers."
