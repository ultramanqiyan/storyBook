### Opening
Dr. James Okonkwo stood before his ethics class at the University of London, discussing the trolley problem for the thousandth time. At forty-four, he had built a career on asking difficult questions without providing easy answers. He had been a pastor once, certain of right and wrong. Now he was an ethicist, comfortable with uncertainty. But the email waiting in his inbox would challenge even his comfort with ambiguity.

### Ending
James accepted the appointment that evening, knowing it would change everything. He would help design how AI judged human worth. He would decide what values guided algorithmic assessment. He would shape a system that would affect millions of lives. The weight of it pressed on him, but he could not refuse. This was what he had been training for his entire life—even if he no longer believed in the God who had called him to ministry.
