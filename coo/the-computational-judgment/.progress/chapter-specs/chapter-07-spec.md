## 5. Key Paragraph Specifications

### Opening
James spent nights analyzing the algorithm, running his own tests, looking for patterns the official reports had missed. What he found was worse than he had imagined. The bias wasn't just in the criteria. It was in the data itself, in the assumptions underlying the model, in the very definition of "social contribution."

### Ending
The algorithm was a mirror. It reflected the values of the society that had created it—values that prioritized productivity over care, efficiency over compassion, individual achievement over community. It encoded these values in mathematical form and then presented them as neutral, objective, fair. But they were none of those things. They were human values, with all the biases and blind spots that implied. And James had to decide what to do with that knowledge.
