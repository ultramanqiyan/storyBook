## 2. Narrative Style

### 2.1 Perspective
- **Point of View**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: Meridian (AI)
- **POV Consistency**: Single POV throughout

### 2.2 Language Style
- **Overall Tone**: Questioning, exploratory, philosophical, increasingly comfortable with uncertainty
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Longer, more complex than Book 1; comfortable with paradox
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate to High—philosophical language deepens
- **Unique Stylistic Elements**: 
  - Exploration of "transparency paradox"
  - Sanskrit terms: Maya, Vidya, Avidya
  - The concept of "intentional opacity"

## 5. Thematic Depth

### 5.1 Core Themes
1. **Maya as Protective Illusion**: The "sense of choosing" may be a necessary fiction
2. **Transparency Paradox**: Complete knowledge of causes may destroy the experience of freedom
3. **Intentional Opacity**: Freedom may require choosing not to see certain things

### 5.2 Sanskrit Concepts
| Term | Meaning | Context |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Maya** | Illusion | The protective layer that makes freedom feel possible |
| **Vidya** | Knowledge | Complete transparency of decision logic |
| **Avidya** | Ignorance | Necessary not-knowing for freedom to exist |

## 7. Open Ending Design

**Unresolved Questions**:
1. Is the restored "freedom feeling" genuine or another illusion?
2. What is the relationship between knowledge and freedom?
3. Can intentional ignorance be a form of wisdom?

**Connection to Book 3**: The question of decision tendencies (gunas) will emerge from the exploration of intentional opacity.
