### 2.3 Emotional Beats Breakdown

#### Beat 1: The Exhausted Morning (Position: 0-15%)
- **Trigger**: Waking after only 4 hours of sleep
- **Surface Reaction**: Groggy, reaching for phone, then remembering
- **Internal Process**: *I can't do another day like yesterday. But I have to.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Exhaustion, desperation, need for routine
- **Sensory Anchor**: Heavy limbs, gritty eyes

#### Beat 2: The Broken Coffee Maker (Position: 15-35%)
- **Trigger**: Coffee maker makes strange sound, dies
- **Surface Reaction**: Stares at it, presses buttons, denial
- **Internal Process**: *No. No no no. I need this. I can't function without coffee.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Loss of the one reliable thing, forced change
- **Sensory Anchor**: Silence where there should be gurgling

#### Beat 3: The Forced Exit (Position: 35-55%)
- **Trigger**: Realizing she has to leave the house
- **Surface Reaction**: Rushes to get ready, anxiety about being seen
- **Internal Process**: *I look terrible. I don't want to go out. But I need coffee.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Fear of the outside world, need for caffeine
- **Sensory Anchor**: Cold air outside, bright light

#### Beat 4: The Coffee Shop (Position: 55-75%)
- **Trigger**: Entering the coffee shop, sensory overload, finding a seat
- **Surface Reaction**: Overwhelmed but coping, orders, sits
- **Internal Process**: *This is fine. Just get coffee. Then go home. Then...*
- **Deep Motivation**: Need for comfort, fear of interaction
- **Sensory Anchor**: Coffee smell, ambient noise, warmth

#### Beat 5: The Unexpected Conversation (Position: 75-100%)
- **Trigger**: Marcus asks to share her table, they talk
- **Surface Reaction**: Surprised, guarded, then slowly opening
- **Internal Process**: *Why is he talking to me? ...He's actually interesting. ...I'm talking.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Need for connection, surprise at ease
- **Sensory Anchor**: Warmth of conversation, time passing unnoticed

## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Internal/Action

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Four hours of sleep. Sarah had counted every one of them, watching the numbers on her phone change from 11 PM to 3 AM. Now the morning light felt like an accusation, and her body moved through the familiar routine on autopilot. Bathroom. Clothes. Kitchen. Coffee maker. She pressed the button and waited for the familiar gurgling sound that meant the day could begin. Instead, the machine made a sound like a dying animal and went silent. She pressed the button again. Nothing. Pressed it again. Still nothing. "You have got to be kidding me," she said to the empty kitchen. The coffee maker stared back, unrepentant. She had been using this machine every day for three years. It had never, not once, failed her. And now, on the morning after the worst night of her life, it chose to die.
```

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Type**: Emotional/Internal

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She looked at the card again when she got home. "Riverdale Community Garden," it said in simple green letters. "Growing Together." On the back, someone—Marcus, she assumed—had written "Saturdays 10 AM" and a phone number. She set it on her desk, next to the cold coffee from yesterday and the notes she'd taken on AI in marketing. The card looked out of place there, a small square of possibility among the debris of her paralysis. Saturday was three days away. She could think about it. Research it. Decide later. Or—she could just show up. The thought was terrifying and small at the same time. She left the card on the desk and didn't throw it away.
```
