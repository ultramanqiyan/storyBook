### 2.3 Emotional Beats Breakdown

#### Beat 1: The Card on the Desk (Position: 0-15%)
- **Trigger**: Looking at the card throughout the week
- **Surface Reaction**: Picks it up, puts it down, thinks about it
- **Internal Process**: *Should I go? What if it's weird? What if I don't belong?*
- **Deep Motivation**: Fear of the unknown vs. hunger for change
- **Sensory Anchor**: The card's weight in her hand

#### Beat 2: Saturday Morning (Position: 15-30%)
- **Trigger**: Waking up on Saturday, the decision point
- **Surface Reaction**: Gets dressed, hesitates, almost doesn't go
- **Internal Process**: *I could just stay home. No one would know. No one cares if I show up.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Fear of failure, fear of judgment
- **Sensory Anchor**: Clothes that feel wrong, nervous energy

#### Beat 3: Walking to the Garden (Position: 30-50%)
- **Trigger**: Actually leaving the apartment
- **Surface Reaction**: Walks quickly, rehearsing excuses to leave early
- **Internal Process**: *What am I doing? I don't know anything about gardening. This is stupid.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Fear of being exposed as incompetent
- **Sensory Anchor**: Heart rate, cold morning air

#### Beat 4: Arriving at the Garden (Position: 50-70%)
- **Trigger**: Seeing the garden, the people, not knowing where to go
- **Surface Reaction**: Stands at the entrance, uncertain
- **Internal Process**: *I don't belong here. Everyone knows what they're doing. I should leave.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Imposter syndrome, fear of being an outsider
- **Sensory Anchor**: The smell of soil, the sight of green

#### Beat 5: Working in the Garden (Position: 70-100%)
- **Trigger**: Someone gives her a task, she starts working
- **Surface Reaction**: Hands in dirt, following instructions, focusing
- **Internal Process**: *This is... actually okay. I'm doing something. I'm here.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Relief at action, grounding in physical work
- **Sensory Anchor**: Soil texture, plants, physical exertion

## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Internal/Action

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The card sat on her desk for three days. Sarah moved it from one corner to another, placed it under her coffee mug, then moved it back. It was just a card—a small rectangle of cardstock with green letters that said "Riverdale Community Garden" and a phone number on the back. But it felt heavier than that. It felt like a question she hadn't answered yet. Thursday passed. Friday passed. She opened her laptop and searched for the garden's website, looked at photos of people working in raised beds, read about their mission to "cultivate community through cultivation." The word "community" made her chest tight. She didn't have community. She had Slack channels and LinkedIn connections and a phone full of apps. But the card stayed on her desk, and every time she looked at it, something in her stirred.
```

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Type**: Emotional/Internal

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She got home and went straight to the sink to wash her hands. The dirt came off easily—dark soil that had been under her fingernails, a smudge on her wrist, dust on her jeans. But something remained, something she couldn't wash away. She had gone. She had stayed for two hours. She had talked to strangers and put her hands in dirt and done something that wasn't work, wasn't research, wasn't paralysis. The apartment felt different when she sat down at her desk. The same Slack notifications waited for her, the same browser tabs from her AI research. But next to them sat the garden card, and now it meant something different. She had said she'd come back next Saturday. She had meant it. For the first time in months, she had made a commitment and meant it.
```
