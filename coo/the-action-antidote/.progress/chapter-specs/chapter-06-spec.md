**Dialogue Beats**:

#### Segment 2: Marcus Opportunity

**Participants**: Sarah, Marcus

**Surface Content**: Marketing help for garden

**Subtext**: Marcus sees value in Sarah; Sarah is surprised to be valued

**Dialogue Beats**:
1. Marcus: "Elena mentioned you're in marketing. We've been talking about getting the word out about the garden. Would you be interested in helping?"
2. Sarah: "Helping how?"
3. Marcus: "Whatever you think. Social media, maybe a newsletter, events. We have no idea what we're doing on that front."
4. Sarah: "You want me to... do marketing? Here?"
5. Marcus: "Why not? You know what you're doing. And you're here anyway."
6. Sarah: "I... I'll think about it."

**Action Beats Required**: Cleaning up tools, standing, looking around

**What Must Be Revealed**: Sarah has value, the garden needs her skills

**What Must Be Concealed**: The depth of her professional struggles

## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Internal/Action

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Saturday arrived with less fanfare than the first time. Sarah woke up, made coffee, and got dressed without the hour of hesitation that had plagued her before. The voice was still there—"what if they don't want you back?"—but quieter now, more like background noise than a shout. She looked at the card on her desk, the one with Elena's number, and the words she'd written beneath it: "Resistance is normal. The voice gets louder right before it loses." She'd read those words every day this week. They were starting to feel true. The morning light through her window was bright, almost aggressive in its optimism. She grabbed her keys and headed out. The garden was waiting. So was she.
```

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Type**: Emotional/Internal

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She got home and opened her laptop. Not to check Slack, not to spiral through research tabs, but to think about what she could offer. A marketing plan for a community garden. Social media. Events. The words felt strange in her mind—she'd spent so long feeling like she was barely keeping up with her actual job. But here was something different. Something small, something local, something where she could actually see the impact. She opened a new document and started typing. Just ideas. Just possibilities. For the first time in months, she was thinking about what she could give, not what she lacked. The ripple from one small action—showing up at a garden—had spread further than she could have imagined. And she was still moving.
```
