## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Saturday morning. Sarah woke up and didn't reach for her phone. For a full minute, she just lay there, breathing, watching the light filter through the blinds. The quiet that used to feel oppressive now felt... peaceful. She couldn't pinpoint exactly when the shift had happened. It wasn't one moment, one decision. It was the accumulation of small choices—showing up at the garden, calling Elena, saying yes to the project, saying yes again after it failed. Each action had been a brick, and somewhere along the way, she'd built something. A new foundation. A new rhythm. She got up, made coffee, got dressed. The garden was waiting. And she was ready.
```

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She didn't know what came next. A month ago, that uncertainty would have paralyzed her. Now it felt... open. The future wasn't a threat to be researched and controlled. It was a garden to be tended—day by day, action by action. She looked at the small collection on her desk: the garden card, Elena's words, David's wisdom, her own notes. Tools. Keys. Reminders. She'd needed them all. She'd needed the garden, the community, the setbacks, the small wins. She'd needed to fall forward to learn how to move. The voice was still there sometimes—quieter now, easier to recognize as noise. But she had practice now. She had tools. She had people. And she had herself—not the paralyzed version that had stared at screens for hours, but someone new. Someone who acted. Someone who was still becoming.
```
