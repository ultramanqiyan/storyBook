Later, she found Elena weeding the herb garden, her weathered hands moving with practiced ease. Sarah knelt beside her, pulling weeds from the thyme bed.

"How are you?" Elena asked, not looking up. "Really?"

"Better." Sarah considered. "Not perfect, but better."

"That's all any of us can be." Elena sat back on her heels, studying Sarah's face. "You've come a long way since that first Saturday. You know that?"

"I guess I have." Sarah pulled another weed. "I didn't expect the garden to change everything. I just came because I couldn't stand my apartment anymore."

"Sometimes that's all it takes. One small action. One moment of 'I can't stay here anymore.' And then you're in motion."

"Thank you," Sarah said quietly. "For everything. For answering the phone that night. For the wisdom. For being here."

Monday morning. Sarah opened her laptop to find her manager's message waiting: *Can we chat this morning? I have another opportunity to discuss.*

A month ago, those words would have sent her into a spiral. Another opportunity? Was this a test? Would she fail again? Should she research the project first?

"Yes." No hesitation. No spiral. Just yes.
