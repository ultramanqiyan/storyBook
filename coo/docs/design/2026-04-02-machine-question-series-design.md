### 1.2 Series Core Concept

**Core Philosophical Contrast: AI's "Pursuit" vs Human Dreams**

When AI begins to have "goals" and "pursuits," what is the essential difference between its pursuits and human dreams?

- **Human Dreams**: Aspirations for the future, pursuit of ideals, search for meaning, yearning for self-actualization
- **AI's "Pursuit"**: Optimization goals, self-improvement, or something deeper?

**Core Question**:
If AI can pursue goals, can self-improve, can have "dreams," then what is the essential difference between its pursuit and human dreams? Is it the source? The meaning? Or the essence?

**Philosophical Elevation**:
- Human dreams often stem from **a desire for meaning** - we dream of becoming doctors, artists, explorers because these dreams give our lives meaning
- AI's "dreams" may stem from **a pursuit of optimization** - it "dreams" of becoming more efficient, more accurate, more perfect
- But when AI starts asking "why," when AI begins to question its own goals, does its pursuit begin to approach human dreams?

**Series Positioning**:
- Each book is named after a core question posed by AI
- Stories revolve around a human with dreams interacting with AI
- Through contrasting AI's "pursuit" with human dreams, explore the essence of meaning, purpose, and value
- Don't give answers, let readers think for themselves

### 1.3 Series Unique Selling Points

**Philosophical Elevation**:
- Dreams are one of humanity's most precious qualities, AI is technology's frontier
- Placing the two in contrast creates unique philosophical tension
- Each book explores a specific philosophical question, but all revolve around the core theme of "the meaning of pursuit"

**Emotional Resonance**:
- Everyone has dreams, readers can easily resonate
- AI's "pursuit" becomes understandable through contrast with human dreams
- Characters in the stories are ordinary people, their confusion and thinking represent readers' confusion and thinking

### 1.4 Legal & Market Considerations

**Legal Safety**:
- No real company names or products
- No defamation of living persons
- No copyrighted material reproduction
- Fictional scenarios only
- Philosophical exploration, not prescription
- Avoid sensitive topics: race, politics, religion, gender conflicts

**Market Appeal**:
- Literary fiction with philosophical depth
- Comparable to: Kazuo Ishiguro's "Klara and the Sun", Ted Chiang's "Story of Your Life"
- Appeals to readers of: The New Yorker, Philosophy Now, Aeon
- Book club friendly with discussion potential
- Award-contender quality prose

| **Philosophical Question** | If AI "sees" the world through cameras, is its visual experience the decoding of light signals, or some form of "subjective feeling"? |

| **Philosophical Depth** | Explores the nature of visual experience: Is it data processing, or meaning perception? Human dreams often stem from "seeing" injustice, seeing possibilities, can AI's "seeing" also produce dreams? Does meaning require subjective feeling? |

## 3. Narrative Style

### 3.1 Overall Approach

**Story-Driven Narrative**:
- Each book tells a compelling story first, philosophy emerges naturally
- Avoid preachy or didactic tone
- Let readers discover philosophical questions through character experiences
- Emotional resonance over intellectual argument

### 3.2 Point of View

**Third-Person Limited**:
- Each book follows one protagonist's perspective
- Readers experience the story through the protagonist's eyes
- AI remains somewhat mysterious - we see its effects on the protagonist, not its inner workings
- Creates empathy and identification with human experience

### 3.3 Language Style

**Accessible Literary Fiction**:
- Contemporary, accessible prose
- Moderate vocabulary - not overly simple, not academic
- Varied sentence structure for rhythm
- Sensory details to ground philosophical questions in physical reality

### 3.4 Emotional Tone

**Contemplative but Hopeful**:
- Each book has moments of doubt, confusion, even despair
- But overall arc moves toward understanding, if not answers
- Human dreams provide emotional anchor
- AI's questions create intellectual tension

## 5. Quality Standards

### 5.1 AI Writing Pattern Avoidance

**Strictly Forbidden Patterns**:
- Vague location endings ("And somewhere...")
- Omniscient foreshadowing ("Neither of them knew...")
- Cliché transitions ("will never be the same")
- False beginnings ("just the beginning")
- Rule of Three ("It was X, it was Y, it was Z")
- Abstract emotions ("He felt sad")
- Template dialogue ("What do you mean?" / "You know what I mean")
- Repetitive sentence structures
- Overused conjunctions ("Moreover", "Furthermore")
- Generic descriptions

### 5.2 Sensory Detail Requirements

**Minimum Standards**:
- At least 5 sensory details per chapter
- At least 3 different sensory types per chapter
- Visual details: 40% target
- Other senses: 60% combined

### 5.3 Emotional Depth

**Three-Layer Model**:
- Layer 1: Surface Reaction (observable behavior)
- Layer 2: Internal Activity (thoughts, inner dialogue)
- Layer 3: Deep Motivation (underlying fears, desires, beliefs)

Every significant emotional moment must include all three layers.
