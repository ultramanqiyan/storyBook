## 1. Series Overview

### 1.1 Basic Information

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Series Name** | Algorithmic Philosophy Series |
| **Book Count** | 10 books |
| **Target Readers** | Adults 25-55, Western readers interested in philosophy, AI ethics, and thought-provoking fiction |
| **Naming Convention** | Algorithmic [Theme Keyword] |
| **Language** | English (US) |

### 1.2 Series Concept

A 10-book series exploring fundamental philosophical questions through the lives of ordinary people in a near-future world (2035-2050) where AI has become deeply integrated into everyday life. Each book tackles a different philosophical theme through a unique protagonist - not tech elites or scientists, but regular people whose lives are transformed by their encounters with AI.

**Core Philosophy**: Philosophy is not abstract theory but lived experience. These stories show how ordinary people grapple with extraordinary questions when technology challenges our deepest assumptions about consciousness, self, intention, humanity, aesthetics, free will, identity, ethics, mortality, and truth.

## 2. Shared World Building

### 2.1 Time Period

**Era**: 2035-2050

**Key Characteristics**:
- AI is ubiquitous but not yet fully sentient
- Society is at an ethical crossroads
- Human-AI relationships are being redefined
- Traditional institutions are adapting to technological change

### 2.2 Technology Landscape

| Technology | Status in 2035-2050 |
|------------|---------------------|
| **Emotion Simulation AI** | Can perfectly mimic human emotional responses |
| **Personal Analytics** | AI systems that know individuals better than they know themselves |
| **Intent Recognition** | Algorithms that can predict and interpret human intentions |
| **AI Art Creation** | AI-generated art that moves audiences emotionally |
| **Neural Prediction** | Technology that can predict decisions before conscious awareness |
| **Consciousness Digitization** | Experimental projects in uploading human consciousness |
| **Algorithmic Ethics** | AI systems making moral decisions |
| **Life Extension** | Technologies offering digital immortality |

### 2.3 Social Context

- **Trust in AI**: Society increasingly trusts algorithmic judgments over human intuition
- **Professional Displacement**: Many traditional professions are being augmented or replaced
- **Ethical Debates**: Ongoing public discourse about AI rights, human uniqueness, and moral responsibility
- **Institutional Adaptation**: Legal, medical, educational systems grappling with AI integration

## 3. Book Summaries

### Book 1: Algorithmic Consciousness

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Theme** | Phenomenal Consciousness vs. Functionalism |
| **Philosophical Question** | If an AI can perfectly simulate human emotional responses, does it truly "feel" those emotions? |
| **Protagonist** | Rosa Moretti, 68, retired Broadway actress, now teaching at community theater |
| **Background** | Italian-American, Brooklyn, NY |
| **Story Hook** | Rosa is hired as an "emotion trainer" for AI actors. When she discovers an AI's performance is more "authentic" than her own younger self, she questions: if AI can perfectly simulate emotion, is there any real difference between simulation and feeling? |
| **Unique Angle** | Performance art perspective on AI consciousness - not from developers but from artists |

### Book 2: Algorithmic Self

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Theme** | Self-Knowledge vs. Big Data Analytics |
| **Philosophical Question** | When algorithms know you better than you know yourself, does "self-knowledge" still exist? |
| **Protagonist** | Margaret "Maggie" Sullivan, 45, high-net-worth divorce lawyer |
| **Background** | Irish-American, Chicago, IL |
| **Story Hook** | Maggie uses a "divorce prediction AI" for clients but discovers it predicts her own marriage's trajectory more accurately than her own intuition. When she follows AI's advice to "fix" her marriage, she questions: is the self defined by introspection or by behavioral data? |
| **Unique Angle** | Marriage/intimacy perspective on self-knowledge - a lawyer who solves others' problems but can't solve her own |

### Book 3: Algorithmic Intent

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Theme** | Intentionality and Machine Intent |
| **Philosophical Question** | Can machines have "intentions"? If so, how do we verify their authenticity? |
| **Protagonist** | Dorothy "Dot" Kowalski, 52, community mediator |
| **Background** | Polish-American, small town Ohio |
| **Story Hook** | Dot's mediation work is assisted by an AI that can predict disputants' "true intentions." When the AI sometimes knows what people want better than they do, she questions: is intention a product of introspection or behavioral inference? Do we truly "own" our intentions? |
| **Unique Angle** | Community mediation perspective - small-town America grappling with intention in everyday disputes |

### Book 4: Algorithmic Humanity

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Theme** | Anthropocentrism vs. Strong AI |
| **Philosophical Question** | After the emergence of strong AI, is "human-centrism" still justified? |
| **Protagonist** | Angela Washington, 38, pet memorial photographer |
| **Background** | African-American, Atlanta suburbs, GA |
| **Story Hook** | Angela is asked to photograph an AI "pet" for a "end-of-life memorial." When she witnesses the owner's genuine grief, she questions: what defines a life worth mourning? If we can sincerely mourn digital beings, where is the boundary of life? |
| **Unique Angle** | Pet memorial photography perspective - exploring life's boundaries through mourning and memory |

### Book 5: Algorithmic Aesthetics

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Theme** | AI Art and Aesthetic Subjectivity |
| **Philosophical Question** | If AI-created art moves people, is its aesthetic capacity equivalent to humans? |

If AI can better "restore beauty," does aesthetic subjectivity still exist? |
| **Unique Angle** | Antique restoration perspective - a craftsperson who "guards history" confronting AI's ability to preserve it better |

### 6.2 Philosophical Depth

Each book explores its theme through three layers:

1. **Personal Layer**: The protagonist's individual struggle
2. **Social Layer**: How the question affects community and relationships
3. **Universal Layer**: The timeless philosophical implications

## 7. Quality Standards

### 7.1 AI Writing Pattern Prohibition

All books must strictly avoid:
- "And somewhere..." endings
- "Neither of them knew..." foreshadowing
- "will never be the same" clichés
- "just the beginning" false beginnings
- Rule of Three structures
- Abstract emotional descriptions
- Template dialogue patterns

### 7.2 Sensory Detail Requirements

- Minimum 5 sensory details per chapter
- Minimum 3 different sensory types per chapter
- Distribution: Visual (40%), Others (60%)

### 7.3 Emotional Depth

Every significant emotional moment must include:
- Layer 1: Surface Reaction (observable behavior)
- Layer 2: Internal Activity (thoughts, inner dialogue)
- Layer 3: Deep Motivation (underlying fears, desires, beliefs)
