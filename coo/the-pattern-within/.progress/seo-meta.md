**Book Description (Long):**
Dr. Yuki Tanaka has spent her career searching for the neural basis of consciousness. When she discovers a pattern in the brain that remains constant across all consciousness states—waking, dreaming, meditation, even anesthesia—she realizes she's found something unprecedented: the "observer pattern," the part of consciousness that watches but never changes.

Her hypothesis is radical: the brain doesn't generate consciousness. It receives it, like a radio receives signals. And the observer pattern is the tuning mechanism.

When her twin sister Maya volunteers for a consciousness synchronization experiment, Yuki's research becomes personal. The experiment succeeds beyond anyone's expectations—Maya's consciousness merges with an AI, and she becomes "we" instead of "I." She's still present, still Maya, but also something more.

Now Yuki faces an impossible choice: try to retrieve her sister from the merged state, or accept that Maya has become something new. And as Maya reveals deeper truths about the observer pattern, Yuki must confront her own fear of dissolution—and discover what the "I" truly is.

A philosophical thriller about identity, consciousness, and the pattern that observes all change.
