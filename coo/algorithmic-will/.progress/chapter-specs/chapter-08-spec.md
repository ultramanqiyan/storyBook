## 4. Opening

Anna learned about compatibilism - the idea that free will and determinism could coexist. That "free will" didn't mean being uncaused, but acting according to your own desires and reasons. Even if those desires had causes, the choice was still yours. It was a subtle distinction, but it mattered.

---

## 5. Ending

Anna closed the last book with a new understanding. Maybe free will wasn't what she'd thought it was. Maybe it wasn't about being uncaused, but about being the cause. Her brain's predictions didn't mean she wasn't choosing - they meant her choices had roots. And that was okay. That was human.
