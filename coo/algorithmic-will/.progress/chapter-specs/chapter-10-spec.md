## 4. Open Ending Design

### 4.1 Unresolved Questions
1. Is free will real or an illusion?
2. Can we make meaningful choices if our brains are predictable?
3. What does it mean to "choose"?

### 4.2 Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: Anna has accepted that free will may be more complex than she thought, but choice still matters
- **Key Insight**: The question of free will may be unanswerable, but that doesn't mean our choices are meaningless

---

## 5. Opening

Anna Nowak walked into her counseling center the way she had every weekday for ten years. The question of free will hadn't been answered. Her brain still predicted her choices before she was aware of them. But she was here, doing this work, helping people change. And that was a choice - whatever "choice" meant.

---

## 6. Ending

The question of free will would never be fully answered. Anna knew that now. But she also knew something else: people changed. They broke patterns. They found new reasons. Whether that was "free will" or not, it was real. And her work - helping people find their reasons - was real too. The mystery remained. And somehow, that was okay.
