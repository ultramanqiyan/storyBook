## 4. Opening

Anna spent the weekend designing tests to prove the researchers wrong. She would make choices that were unpredictable - random, spontaneous, surprising. She would prove that her conscious mind was in control, that she could choose freely, that free will was real.

---

## 5. Ending

By Sunday night, Anna had to admit the truth. Even her "random" choices showed patterns. Even her "spontaneous" decisions had precursors in her brain activity. She couldn't outsmart the prediction. Her choices were determined before she knew she was making them. And if that was true for her, was it true for everyone?
