## 4. Opening

Anna started tracking her own behavior. Every choice, every habit, every decision. What she ate, when she slept, how she responded to clients. And what she found terrified her: patterns. Predictable, consistent patterns. The same way she'd helped clients see their patterns, she now saw her own.

---

## 5. Ending

Anna sat in her office after her last client left. She'd spent the day helping people "choose" to change. But if their choices were determined before they were aware of them, what was she really doing? Was addiction counseling just helping people change their brain patterns? Was there any room for genuine choice?
