## 4. Opening

Anna took a week off work - the first time in ten years. She couldn't counsel people about "choice" when she didn't know if choice existed. She spent her days reading, thinking, trying to understand what her life meant if free will was an illusion.

---

## 5. Ending

By the end of the week, Anna had read dozens of books and articles. Philosophy, neuroscience, psychology. And she'd found something interesting: the question of free will had been debated for centuries. The neuroscience was new, but the philosophical problem wasn't. Maybe the answer wasn't in the data. Maybe it was in the philosophy.
