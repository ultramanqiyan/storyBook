# Book Specification: Algorithmic Will

## Basic Information
- **Title**: Algorithmic Will: When Choice Is Predicted
- **Meta Description**: An addiction counselor discovers her choices can be predicted before she's aware of them. Is free will real? A meditation on choice, determinism, and the self in contemporary philosophical fiction.
- **Keywords**: free will philosophy, neural prediction, determinism, addiction and choice, neuroscience and consciousness, algorithmic choice, choice vs fate
- **Genre**: Contemporary Fiction / Philosophical Fiction
- **Target Audience**: Adults 25-55, readers interested in free will, neuroscience, and philosophical questions
- **Theme**: Free Will, Determinism, Addiction and Freedom, Choice vs Prediction

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: Anna Nowak
- **Language Style**: Clinical but warm prose; increasingly introspective
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length, precise but human
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - counseling terminology mixed with everyday language
- **Unique Elements**: Addiction counseling scenes, neural prediction study, philosophical inquiry

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Clinical warmth, Intellectual curiosity, Existential doubt, Acceptance
- **Emotional Arc**: Routine → Discovery → Crisis → Understanding → Choice
- **Emotional Pacing**: Measured beginning, accelerating crisis, contemplative resolution

## Character Voices

### Anna Nowak (Protagonist)
- **Occupation**: Addiction counselor
- **Age**: 35
- **Speech Characteristics**: Patient, empathetic, uses questions to guide
- **Word Choice**: Counseling vocabulary, Polish expressions, practical language
- **Sentence Patterns**: Questions to clients, reflective internal monologue
- **Personality Traits**: Compassionate, dedicated, secretly struggling with her own habits
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Reflective, questioning, analyzing her own patterns
- **Unique Expression**: Uses "What if..." to explore possibilities
- **Growth Arc**: From belief in free will to acceptance of complexity

### Dr. Marcus Webb (Supporting Character)
- **Occupation**: Neuroscience researcher
- **Age**: 50s
- **Speech Characteristics**: Academic, precise, gentle
- **Word Choice**: Technical neuroscience terms, careful explanations
- **Sentence Patterns**: Clear, explanatory, patient
- **Personality Traits**: Brilliant, careful, philosophical

### Client Characters (Supporting)
- **Occupation**: Various struggling with addiction
- **Speech Characteristics**: Varied, authentic, vulnerable
- **Word Choice**: Real speech patterns of those in recovery
- **Personality Traits**: Struggling, hopeful, human

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: Free Will vs Determinism
- **Philosophical Exploration**: Do we have genuine choice?
- **Real-world Metaphor**: Modern anxiety about choice and agency in an algorithmic age

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Rule of Three structure | "The room was dark. The room was cold. The room was silent." | Vary sentence structure, mix with action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt afraid." | Physical manifestation + internal thought |
| "Little did they know" | "Little did she know..." | Remove entirely, maintain POV discipline |
| "It all started when" | "It all started when..." | Start in medias res with action |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Moreover," / "Furthermore," | 0 per chapter | Remove entirely |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |
| "As if..." comparisons | 2 per chapter | Use concrete similes or direct description |
| "Seemed to..." / "Appeared to..." | 1 per chapter | Be definitive or use character uncertainty |
| Adverb-heavy dialogue tags | 0 preferred | Use action beats instead |
| Repetitive sentence structures | Monitor | Vary sentence length and structure |
| Overuse of bold/italic emphasis | Minimal | Trust prose strength |

### Dialogue Tag Requirements
- **Minimum 5-6 different dialogue tags per chapter**
- **Maximum 2 "said" per conversation**
- **Use action beats instead of tags where possible**
- **Examples**: whispered, muttered, snapped, breathed, demanded, questioned, admitted, conceded, challenged

### Tension Vocabulary (2-3 words per 1000 words)
- **Physical**: clenched, trembling, rigid, frozen, coiled
- **Atmospheric**: suffocating, charged, brittle, heavy, electric
- **Psychological**: paranoia, disorientation, vertigo, dissociation, intrusion
