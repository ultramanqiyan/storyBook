**Narrative Function**

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
The memorial stone was cold under Elara's fingers, the same cold it had been every year for five years. She traced the carved letters of her sister's name—LYRA VANCE—feeling the grooves worn smooth by weather and by her own repeated touch. Five years of mornings like this, five years of questions with no answers.

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
She pressed her forehead to the stone one last time, then stepped back. The village was waking behind her, but she wasn't ready to return to a life that had moved on without Lyra. Not when she could still feel, somewhere deep in her bones, that her sister wasn't gone.

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
The market was too loud, too normal, too full of people who had forgotten. Elara moved through the crowd without seeing, her mind still at the memorial stone—until a snatch of conversation stopped her cold. "The ruins," someone whispered. "Lights again last night."

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
No one would go. Everyone knew the ruins were death, were forbidden, were the reason time magic had been outlawed. But Elara wasn't everyone. She hadn't stopped believing in impossible things when Lyra disappeared.

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
The entrance to the ruins was smaller than she remembered from childhood stories—a crack in the earth more than a doorway. Elara squeezed through, her shoulders scraping against stone that hadn't seen sunlight in centuries. The darkness below wasn't empty. It was waiting.

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
The corridor opened into a vast chamber, and Elara's breath caught. Before her, suspended in a column of light that shouldn't exist, was something that looked like an hourglass made of crystal and starlight. And it was glowing.

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
The hourglass hung in the air, turning slowly, filled not with sand but with something that looked like captured starlight. Elara couldn't move, couldn't breathe, couldn't look away. Then the light shifted, and a voice—ancient, layered, neither male nor female—spoke her name.

**Ending Transition (Last 3 sentences)**
"Lyra Vance," ORACLE said, the name echoing through the chamber like a bell. "She lives. She waits. She is... trapped." Elara's knees hit the cold stone floor, but she barely felt it. Her sister was alive.
