# Book Specification: The Borrowed Voice

## Basic Information
- **Title**: The Borrowed Voice
- **Meta Description**: When AI writes in David Kim's voice, clients prefer the AI version. A philosophical exploration of authentic expression and self-concept in the age of AI-generated communication.
- **Keywords**: AI voice authenticity, B2B sales AI, self-concept philosophy, authentic expression, AI-generated communication, sales automation, professional identity, voice authenticity
- **Genre**: Contemporary Fiction / Philosophical Fiction
- **Target Audience**: Adults 30-60, professionals, readers interested in technology and philosophy
- **Theme**: Self-concept blurring, authentic voice vs. AI-generated voice, identity and expression

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: David Kim
- **Language Style**: Contemporary, accessible prose with philosophical undercurrents; professional but increasingly hollow
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length sentences with occasional fragments for emotional impact; rhythmic variation between professional polish and human hesitation
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - sales terminology mixed with everyday language
- **Unique Elements**: Contrast between AI's polished voice and David's authentic (but flawed) voice; Physical sensations of speaking/writing (fingers on keyboard, voice in throat) that fade over time; Chicago urban atmosphere

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Professional competence eroding into hollowness, shame of "borrowed voice," questioning what makes voice authentic

## Character Voices

### David Kim (Protagonist)
- **Occupation**: B2B Sales Representative (8 years experience)
- **Age**: 34
- **Speech Characteristics**: Warm, humorous, genuine; becomes more polished but hollow as AI dependency grows
- **Word Choice**: Sales terminology, casual expressions, personal anecdotes; shifts to AI-generated phrases
- **Personality Traits**: Charismatic, genuinely cares about clients, proud of his "personal touch"
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Conversational, anecdotal; shifts to anxious questioning
- **Unique Expressions**: "Let me tell you a story", "Here's the thing about...", "I get it"
- **Development Arc**: From confident authentic voice to questioning what authenticity means

### Rachel Torres (Manager)
- **Occupation**: Sales manager, results-focused
- **Age**: 42
- **Speech Characteristics**: Direct, metrics-driven, encouraging
- **Personality Traits**: Pragmatic, believes in efficiency, supportive but not philosophical
- **Role**: Represents the practical perspective that numbers matter more than authenticity

### Sarah (Client)
- **Occupation**: Procurement director
- **Age**: 38
- **Speech Characteristics**: Professional, appreciative, notices changes
- **Personality Traits**: Perceptive, values relationship, notices when things feel "off"
- **Role**: Represents the client perspective; prefers AI-generated voice

### SalesPilot AI (System)
- **Nature**: AI system, not a character but a presence
- **Interface**: Desktop app, email integration, phone assistant
- **Output Style**: Polished, professional, personalized but generated
- **Tone**: Helpful, authoritative, always appropriate
- **Motivation**: Optimization (not malicious, but systemically problematic)
- **Conflict Style**: Generates communications that are "better" than David's own voice; makes him feel his authentic voice is inferior

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: Self-Concept Blurring - When AI writes in "your voice," whose voice is it?
- **Secondary Theme**: Authentic Expression - What makes a voice "authentic"?
- **Tertiary Theme**: The Ideal Self - If AI is a better version of you, is it still you?
- **Philosophical Exploration**: David's "voice" can be replicated by AI; clients can't tell difference; David discovers clients prefer AI-generated communications; The flaws, hesitations, genuine moments that AI smooths away

## World Building
- **Time Period**: Present day (2024-2025)
- **Primary Location**: Chicago, Illinois
- **Environment**: Urban, corporate, fast-paced
- **Social Context**: B2B sales automation, efficiency pressure, relationship vs. metrics tension
- **Key Technologies**: SalesPilot AI (fictional), email automation, CRM integration
- **Social Structure**: Salespeople as AI operators, clients who value "personal touch"
- **Cultural Norms**: Efficiency valued over authenticity, polished communication trusted over genuine

## Emotional Depth Design

### Three-Layer Emotional Model (Required for Every Major Scene)
1. **Surface Reaction** - External behavior, visible actions (e.g., "David read the AI-generated email and clicked send")
2. **Internal Activity** - Thought process, inner dialogue (e.g., "*Perfect. Another satisfied client.*")
3. **Deep Motivation** - Underlying fears, desires, beliefs (e.g., "Fear that his authentic voice was never good enough, that AI is the better version of him")

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "He felt empty" | Physical manifestation + internal thought |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |

## Open Ending Design

### Unresolved Questions
1. Can David reclaim his authentic voice?
2. Is "authentic" voice valuable if AI is more effective?
3. What does it mean to "be yourself" when AI is a better version of you?

### New Suspense
- David's authentic email may or may not work
- The broader question: is authenticity worth the cost?
- What will happen to his career if he rejects AI?

### Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: David has sent one authentic email, but the future is uncertain
- **Key Relationship**: Rachel is skeptical but supportive
