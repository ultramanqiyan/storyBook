| Field | Value |

| **Chapter Count** | 10 |

---

## 2. Narrative Style

### 2.1 Perspective
- **Point of View**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: David Kim
- **POV Consistency**: Single

### 2.2 Narrative Structure
- **Dual Structure**: Yes
- **Structure Type**: Reality line (present sales work) + Inner line (voice and identity)
- **Interweaving Method**: Sales communications trigger identity reflection; AI outputs contrast with authentic voice

### 2.3 Language Style
- **Overall Tone**: Contemporary, accessible prose with philosophical undercurrents; professional but increasingly hollow
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length sentences with occasional fragments for emotional impact; rhythmic variation between professional polish and human hesitation
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - sales terminology mixed with everyday language
- **Unique Stylistic Elements**: 
  - Contrast between AI's polished voice and David's authentic (but flawed) voice
  - Physical sensations of speaking/writing (fingers on keyboard, voice in throat) that fade over time
  - Chicago urban atmosphere

---

## 3. Emotional Tone (Quantified)

### 3.1 Overall Atmosphere
**Primary Emotions**: Professional competence eroding into hollowness, shame of "borrowed voice," questioning what makes voice authentic

### 3.2 Emotional Arc
```
Chapter Distribution:
Chapters 1-3: Competent professional using AI as tool; subtle signs of voice distance
Chapters 4-6: Growing dependency; clients prefer AI version; unease
Chapters 7-8: Crisis - client says "you've changed" but means AI version is better; identity crisis
Chapters 9-10: Philosophical confrontation; no easy resolution; questioning
```

## 5. Thematic Depth

### 5.1 Core Themes
1. **Self-Concept Blurring**: When AI writes in "your voice," whose voice is it?
2. **Authentic Expression**: What makes a voice "authentic"?
3. **The Ideal Self**: If AI is a better version of you, is it still you?

### 5.2 Philosophical Exploration
| Question | Exploration Approach |
|----------|---------------------|
| Is self essentially behavior pattern? | David's "voice" can be replicated by AI; clients can't tell difference |
| If AI is more "me" than me, who am I? | David discovers clients prefer AI-generated communications |
| What makes expression authentic? | The flaws, hesitations, genuine moments that AI smooths away |

## 7. Sensory Detail Requirements (Quantified)

### 7.1 Required Sensory Types
Each chapter MUST include sensory details from at least **3 different categories**:

| Sense | Specific Types | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Visual** | Email on screen, client faces, city through window | "The email glowed on his screen, perfect and polished" |
| **Auditory** | Client voices, phone calls, silence after sending | "Her voice was warm, appreciative - responding to words he hadn't written" |
| **Tactile** | Keyboard, phone, pen | "His fingers hesitated over the keyboard, then pressed send" |
| **Internal** | Voice in throat (fading), hesitation (fading) | "He used to feel his words before he wrote them. Now he just pressed generate." |
| **Olfactory** | Office coffee, Chicago air | "The lake wind carried that particular Chicago smell" |

### 7.2 Quantified Standards
- **Minimum per chapter**: 5 sensory details
- **Minimum categories per chapter**: 3
- **Distribution target**: Visual (40%), Others (60% combined)

## 9. Prohibited Elements (Explicit List)

### 9.1 AI Writing Patterns (STRICTLY FORBIDDEN)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Vague location endings | "And somewhere..." | Specific location |
| Omniscient foreshadowing | "Neither of them knew..." | Character's intuition |
| Cliché transitions | "will never be the same" | Specific change |
| Abstract emotions | "He felt empty" | Physical manifestation |

## 11. Open Ending Design (Chapter 10)

### 11.1 Unresolved Questions
1. Can David reclaim his authentic voice?
2. Is "authentic" voice valuable if AI is more effective?
3. What does it mean to "be yourself" when AI is a better version of you?

### 11.2 New Suspense
- David's authentic email may or may not work
- The broader question: is authenticity worth the cost?
- What will happen to his career if he rejects AI?

### 11.3 Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: David has sent one authentic email, but the future is uncertain
- **Key Relationship**: Rachel is skeptical but supportive
