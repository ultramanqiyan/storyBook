# Chapter 3: The Investigation

James decided to investigate.

He started with Case #1248—the obsidian mask. He contacted Dr. Elena Rodriguez directly.

"Dr. Rodriguez, this is James Mitchell from the National Truth Verification Agency. I have some questions about your claim."

"Of course," she said. "Is there a problem with the verification?"

"Not exactly. I noticed something in photograph #3. A shadow that appears to have been added digitally."

There was a pause on the line.

"Yes," she said finally. "I was wondering if anyone would notice."

"Can you explain?"

"The mask is real," she said. "The discovery is real. But the shadow... I added it. To protect something."

"Protect what?"

"The spirit that lives in the mask."

James closed his eyes. This was not what he expected.

"Dr. Rodriguez, are you claiming that the mask contains a... spirit?"

"I'm claiming that the mask contains something that shouldn't be seen. Something that would be dangerous if it were fully visible. The shadow was my way of... obscuring it."

"Obscuring what?"

"Truth that isn't meant to be known."

---

James moved to Case #1249—the deep-sea fish. He contacted Dr. Marcus Chen.

"Dr. Chen, this is James Mitchell from the National Truth Verification Agency. I have questions about your claim."

"The fish?" Dr. Chen asked. "What about it?"

"The behavior at timestamp 04:23.817. The fish's eye tracked the camera. It seemed to... recognize the observer."

Another pause.

"You noticed that too," Dr. Chen said quietly.

"Can you explain?"

"The fish is real," he said. "The species is real. But the behavior... it's not normal. I've studied deep-sea biology for twenty years. I've never seen anything like it."

"What do you think it means?"

"I think... I think the fish knew it was being observed. I think it responded to the act of observation itself."

"That's not scientifically possible."

"No," Dr. Chen agreed. "It's not. But it happened."

---

James added to his ANOMALIES.docx file:

*Case #1248: Authentic evidence with deliberate modification. Claimant states modification was to obscure "truth that isn't meant to be known." Scientific explanation: None.*

*Case #1249: Authentic evidence with anomalous behavior. Subject displayed recognition of observation. Claimant confirms anomaly. Scientific explanation: None.*

He stared at the screen.

Two cases. Two anomalies. Two claimants who acknowledged that something was... different.

*This is my job,* he thought. *To determine what's real.*

But what if reality itself was the problem? What if truth wasn't something that could be verified?

He needed to understand more.
