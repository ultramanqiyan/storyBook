**Primary Objective**: Lily visits Ruth and hears Yoruba for the first time with understanding.

**Secondary Objectives**:
- Show the grandmother-granddaughter bond
- Demonstrate Lily's difference from other children
- Plant seeds of the teaching relationship
- Show Ruth finding purpose

- **Raised**: What does Lily understand that others don't?

## 4. Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 4: Ruth Speaks Yoruba

**The Moment**:
"Kíni tó n ṣe e, omomi?" The words came without thought, rising from somewhere deep. And then something miraculous happened—the child's eyes widened, and she leaned forward, as if she understood.

"You're speaking the old words," Lily said. "I hear them in my dreams."

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The child appeared in the doorway like a question—small, dark-skinned, with eyes that seemed to see more than they should. Ruth looked at her, and something stirred in the fog of her memory. She knew this face. She knew these eyes. But from where? From when?
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
"Will you come back?" Ruth asked, the words thick in her throat. "Will you come back and listen?" Lily nodded, her hand still warm in Ruth's. "I'll come back. I want to learn." And in that moment, Ruth felt something she hadn't felt in a long time—purpose. She had something to give. She had something to teach. And someone was willing to learn.
```
