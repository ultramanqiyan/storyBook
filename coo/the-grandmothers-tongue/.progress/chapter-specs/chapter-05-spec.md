## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
They came at first in twos and threes—children who had heard about the grandmother who told stories, who spoke words that sounded like music. Then they came in larger groups, filling the small room, sitting on the floor, their eyes bright with curiosity. Ruth looked at them—at their faces, so young, so open—and felt something she hadn't felt in years. Purpose. She had something to give. And they were hungry to receive it.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
That night, Ruth lay in her bed, the silence of the facility pressing in on her. She knew what she was doing was dangerous. She knew the system would notice eventually. But she also knew that these children—these bright, curious, hungry children—were worth the risk. The words would survive. The stories would continue. And somewhere, in the minds of children who had learned to listen, the old language would live on.
```
