## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The memory came without warning, rising from the depths like a fish breaking the surface of still water. She was in her room, looking at the white walls, when suddenly she was somewhere else—somewhere long ago, somewhere full of color and sound and the smell of chalk and old books. She was in a classroom, and he was there, writing words on a blackboard.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
The children sat in silence, the story settling over them like a blanket. Ruth looked at their faces—at the wonder, the understanding, the hunger for more—and felt Chukwuemeka's presence beside her. He had been a teacher. He had believed in the power of words. And now, somehow, she was continuing his work. The story would live. The words would survive. And Chukwuemeka, in some small way, would never truly be gone.
```
