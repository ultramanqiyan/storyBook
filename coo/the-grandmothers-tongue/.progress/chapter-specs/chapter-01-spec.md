### 1.2 Chapter Purpose
**Primary Objective**: Introduce Ruth Okonkwo, an elderly woman with Alzheimer's in a LEXICON care facility, whose memory fragments contain forbidden words.

**Secondary Objectives**:
- Establish the care facility environment
- Show Ruth's confusion and moments of clarity
- Introduce the tension between her old language and Optima
- Plant seeds of her hidden resistance

### 1.3 Key Questions Answered/Raised

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
She woke to white. White walls, white ceiling, white sheets—the color of forgetting. For a moment, she didn't know who she was. The name came slowly, rising from somewhere deep: Ruth. She was Ruth. But Ruth who? Ruth where? The questions swirled, fish in murky water, and she closed her eyes, trying to find something solid to hold onto.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She looked at the photograph one more time—the young woman with the bright smile, the man beside her, the children at their feet. That woman had a name. That woman had a story. And somewhere, in the fragments of her mind, that story was still alive. She would find it. She would remember. And when she did, she would tell someone. Because stories only survive when they are told.
```
