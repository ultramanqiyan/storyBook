## 4. Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 2: Teaching the Technique

**The Teaching**:
"The system reads your thoughts through the interface," Ruth explained, her voice low. "But it cannot read what you do not think in the front of your mind. You must learn to hold the words in a different place—in the back, where dreams come from, where memories live. That is where the old words survive."

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
"Today I will teach you something important," Ruth said, her voice low but clear. The children leaned forward, their eyes bright with attention. "I will teach you how to hide the words—how to keep them safe from the system that wants to delete them." She looked at each of them in turn, seeing the hunger in their faces, the determination. They were ready. They were willing. And she would give them everything she had.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
By the end of the lesson, each child had hidden at least one word in the deep places of their mind—a word that the system couldn't reach, couldn't delete, couldn't optimize away. Ruth looked at their faces—at the wonder, the fear, the hope—and felt something settle in her chest. The words were safe. The stories would continue. And even when she was gone, even when her mind finally failed completely, something of her would survive in these children.
```
