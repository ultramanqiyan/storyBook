## 4. Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 3: Teaching the Words

**The Teaching**:
"There are five words you must carry," Ruth said, her voice weak but clear. "Ìmìnira—freedom. Ayafe—beloved. Omoluwabi—good character. Ìbàjé—to question. And Ìtàn—story. These are the words that hold everything. These are the words that will survive."

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Ruth could feel herself fading. The fog was thicker now, pressing in from all sides, stealing memories before she could catch them. But some things remained clear—Lily's face, the words she had to pass on, the stories that had to survive. She didn't have much time. She had to make sure the legacy was secure.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
"I will carry them," Lily said, her voice steady despite the tears. "I will remember. I will pass them on. I promise." Ruth smiled, feeling the weight settle—not the weight of the words, but the weight of their release. They were safe now. They would survive. And when she was gone, something of her would live on in the mind of this child who had learned to listen.
```
