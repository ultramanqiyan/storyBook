## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Lily came back the next day, and the day after that. Each time, she brought questions—questions that started with "why" and "how" and "what does it mean." And each time, Ruth answered with stories. Stories from her childhood, from her mother, from her mother's mother. Stories that had been passed down for generations, surviving colonization, surviving war, surviving everything. And now, somehow, they would survive this too.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
The caregiver's eyes lingered on the gathering for a moment too long. Ruth saw the calculation there—the assessment, the report that would be filed. But she also saw the children, their faces bright with attention, their minds open to something the system couldn't give them. She would keep telling stories. She would keep passing on the words. And if the system noticed, if the system came—well, she was old. She had lived a long life. And these children were worth the risk.
```
