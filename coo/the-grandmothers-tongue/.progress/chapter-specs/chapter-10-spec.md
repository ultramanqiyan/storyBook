## 4. Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 3: Ruth Passes

**The Moment**:
Ruth closed her eyes, feeling the fog lift for one last moment of clarity. She saw Chukwuemeka, young and strong, waiting for her. She saw her mother, her grandmother, all the women who had passed the words down through generations. And she saw Lily, carrying the words into the future. "The story continues," she whispered. And then she was gone.

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The fog was almost complete now. Ruth drifted in and out of consciousness, sometimes seeing the white room, sometimes seeing other places—a kitchen in Nigeria, a classroom in London, a garden where Chukwuemeka had proposed. The memories came and went like fish in deep water, surfacing for a moment before disappearing into the depths.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
In the days that followed, the children gathered in small groups, speaking the words Ruth had taught them. Ìmìnira. Ayafe. Omoluwabi. Ìbàjé. Ìtàn. The words spread from mind to mind, hidden in the deep places where the system couldn't reach. Ruth was gone, but she was not forgotten. Her stories lived on. Her language survived. And somewhere, in the minds of children who had learned to listen, the old words were growing—seeds of resistance, planted in the darkness, waiting for the light.
```
