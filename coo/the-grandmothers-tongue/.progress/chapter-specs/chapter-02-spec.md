## 4. Key Scene Specifications

#### Scene 3: Attempted Conversation

**Opening Hook (First 3 sentences)**
"They tell me you're doing well, Mama." Adanna's voice was warm but careful, the words optimized and precise. Ruth looked at her daughter—at the face that was so familiar, so loved—and felt the gap between them like a chasm.

#### Scene 4: Ruth Speaks Yoruba

**The Moment**:
"Nne m," Ruth said, the words coming without thought. "Kíni tó n ṣe e?" My mother, what is troubling you? But Adanna just looked at her, confusion in her eyes, and Ruth felt the grief rise like a tide.

## 6. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 6.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The caregiver's voice was gentle. "Ruth, you have a visitor. Your daughter is here." A daughter. The word surfaced slowly, bringing with it a flood of half-formed memories—a small hand in hers, a voice calling "Mama," a face that was both familiar and strange. She had a daughter. She remembered that much.
```

### 6.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
After Adanna left, Ruth sat alone in her white room, the silence pressing in on her. She had a daughter who loved her, who came to visit, who held her hand and spoke kind words. But the words were wrong—optimized, efficient, empty of the things that mattered. Her daughter spoke a language that had no room for the word "mother" as Ruth understood it. And Ruth spoke a language her daughter couldn't hear.
```
