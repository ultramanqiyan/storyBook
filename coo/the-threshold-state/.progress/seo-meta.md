## Book Description (200 words)

Dr. Sarah Chen has spent fifteen years helping others navigate their most profound transformations. Her threshold methodology has guided hundreds of clients through life's major transitions—career changes, relationship endings, identity shifts. She's an expert in the space between who people were and who they're becoming.

But there's one threshold Sarah has never crossed: her own.

When a new client—David Chen, a successful CEO who's achieved everything and found nothing—enters her practice, Sarah recognizes the patterns immediately. David is approaching his threshold, the critical point where transformation becomes possible. But as she guides him through the process, she begins to notice something unsettling: the same symptoms appearing in her own life.

Her methodology, which she's taught to hundreds of others, isn't working on herself. The threshold she's been mapping for others is now demanding to be crossed.

With the help of her brother Wei, a meditation master who has spent decades navigating thresholds of his own, Sarah must finally face what she's been avoiding. What she discovers in the threshold state—the moment of maximum freedom between past and future—will transform not only her understanding of her work, but her entire life.

The Threshold State is a meditation on transformation, the courage to change, and the discovery that the guide must sometimes become the seeker.
