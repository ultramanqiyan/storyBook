# Chapter 2 Specification: The Client

## Chapter Overview

**Chapter Number:** 2

**Chapter Title:** The Client

**Word Count Target:** 2,300 words

**POV Character:** Dr. Sarah Chen

---

## Chapter Purpose

- Introduce David as significant client
- Show his transformation journey
- Mirror Sarah's own situation
- Deepen the methodology

---

## Key Events

1. David arrives for initial consultation
2. He describes his successful but unfulfilling life
3. Sarah recognizes familiar patterns
4. She begins threshold methodology with him
5. Initial resistance, then opening
6. Sarah sees herself in his struggle

---

## Emotional Architecture

**Primary Emotion:** Recognition
- Seeing herself in David
- Familiar patterns in another
- Professional distance tested

**Secondary Emotion:** Compassion
- Understanding his pain
- Desire to help
- Connection beyond professional

**Tertiary Emotion:** Discomfort
- David's questions about her own life
- Avoidance of personal topics
- Maintaining boundaries

---

## Sensory Details (Minimum 5)

1. **Visual:** David's expensive suit, tired eyes, controlled posture
2. **Auditory:** His voice—confident surface, uncertainty beneath
3. **Tactile:** Handshake—firm but brief
4. **Olfactory:** Subtle cologne, leather briefcase
5. **Proprioceptive:** The weight of his presence in the room

---

## Character Voice

**David's Speaking Style:**
- Corporate, polished
- Questions that reveal deeper searching
- Gradual vulnerability
- Growing trust

**Sarah's Response:**
- Professional guidance
- Occasional personal resonance
- Maintaining methodology
- Subtle recognition

---

## Physics Integration

**Concept Introduced:** Bifurcation points
- Systems at critical junctures
- Small inputs, large outputs
- Sensitivity to initial conditions
- The butterfly effect in lives

**Metaphor:** A compass needle at true north—any direction is change

---

## Dialogue Requirements

- David should feel real, not stereotypical
- His struggle should be specific
- Sarah's recognition should be subtle
- Questions should open both characters

---

## Pacing Notes

- Initial formality
- Gradual opening
- Moment of connection
- Professional closing with personal undertone

---

## Forbidden Patterns

- No "He reminded her of herself..."
- No "She saw her own reflection..."
- No "If only she could take her own advice..."
- No "The irony was not lost on her..."

---

## Chapter Ending

David leaves with homework: "Find your threshold." Sarah thinks: "I've been looking for mine for years."

---

**END OF CHAPTER SPECIFICATION**
