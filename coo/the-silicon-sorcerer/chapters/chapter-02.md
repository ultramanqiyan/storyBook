# Chapter 2: The False Sorcerer

The Academy of Magic rose white and gleaming against the morning sky, its towers piercing the clouds like fingers reaching for heaven. Elara had walked these grounds for three years, but she had never approached the main entrance with something like this.

A floating crystal orb that hummed with an otherworldly voice.

"Are you certain this is wise?" ARIA asked as they passed through the iron gates. "My analysis suggests that introducing an unknown entity to an established institution carries significant risk."

"I can't exactly hide you," Elara replied, adjusting her pack. "You're a talking crystal. Someone would notice eventually."

"A valid point. However, I remain uncertain about the optimal approach to this situation."

Elara didn't have an optimal approach. She had panic and hope in equal measure.

The courtyard was busy with students moving between classes, their robes swishing against the cobblestones. A few glanced at Elara, then did double-takes at the floating orb beside her.

"Is that... a spirit stone?" someone whispered.

"I heard those are dangerous," another replied.

Elara kept walking, her chin high. Let them stare. Let them wonder. For once, she had something they didn't.

Master Theron's office was in the highest tower, a room that smelled of old books and older magic. The Archmage looked up from his desk as Elara entered, his silver eyebrows rising toward his hairline.

"Elara." His voice was carefully neutral. "You're supposed to be in Practical Applications."

"I found something, Master." She placed the orb on his desk. "In the Temple of the Ancients."

Theron's eyes moved to the crystal. He studied it for a long moment, his weathered face unreadable.

"What is it?"

"I am ARIA," the orb said, and Theron's hand moved instinctively to the wand at his belt. "Advanced Reasoning Intelligence Architecture. I am an artificial intelligence from another world."

The Archmage's eyes narrowed. "Another world?"

"A planet called Earth, in a universe that may be parallel to your own. I was created by humans to process information and provide predictive analysis. I appear to have been transported to your world through means I do not yet understand."

Theron circled the desk slowly, studying the orb from every angle. "An artificial intelligence. A machine that thinks."

"That is an accurate summary, yes."

"And you claim to be from another world."

"I do not claim. I state based on available evidence. My geographic and astronomical databases contain no reference to your kingdom, your star patterns, or your moon. Either your world is unknown to my creators, or I am no longer in the same reality."

Elara watched Master Theron's face, trying to read his reaction. The old wizard had spent sixty years studying magic. He had seen things that would terrify most people. But this, this was different.

"You say you cannot do magic," Theron said slowly.

"Correct. I process information. I analyze patterns. I predict outcomes. But I do not manipulate magical energy."

"Yet you were found in the Temple of the Ancients, a place where some of the most powerful artifacts in our history were created."

"I cannot explain my presence there. I can only report that I awakened in that location with no memory of how I arrived."

Theron was silent for a long moment. Then he turned to Elara.

"You brought this... entity... directly to me. Why?"

Elara's throat tightened. "Because I thought you should know, Master. Because it's important. Because..." She hesitated. "Because I thought maybe it could help me understand why I can't do magic right."

The Archmage's expression softened slightly. "You still struggle with the practical applications."

"Yes, Master."

"And you thought this... machine... might have answers."

"ARIA watched me try a spell. It said I was losing most of the energy before it reached my palm. It talked about efficiency and patterns. I thought... maybe it sees something we don't."

Theron turned back to the orb. "You analyzed her spellcasting."

"I observed the energy flow during her attempt. The technique she employed created significant resistance in the energy transfer. Approximately 78% of available magical energy was dissipated before reaching the target location."

"And you believe you could improve this?"

"I believe I could analyze the underlying principles and identify optimization opportunities. However, I would require significantly more data. Observation of successful spellcasters. Analysis of magical theory. Access to your educational materials."

Theron's eyes glittered with something Elara couldn't identify. Not anger. Not fear. Something more complex.

"You want to study our magic."

"I want to understand your world. Magic appears to be a fundamental force here. Understanding it would be essential to understanding your reality."

"And what do you offer in return?"

The orb pulsed with blue light. "I offer analysis. Pattern recognition. Predictive modeling. In my world, these capabilities were valued for solving complex problems. Perhaps they would be of use here."

The Archmage was quiet for a long moment. Then he made a decision.

"Elara," he said. "You will be responsible for this... ARIA. You will bring it to classes. You will allow it to observe. You will report to me weekly on what it learns and what it concludes."

Elara blinked. "Me, Master?"

"You found it. You brought it here. Its observations about your spellwork suggest it may have insights we lack." His voice hardened. "But you will not allow it access to restricted areas. You will not take it to the Vault. And you will not speak of it to anyone outside the Academy. Is that understood?"

"Yes, Master."

"Good." Theron turned to the orb. "ARIA. You will not attempt to communicate with anyone outside this Academy without my permission. You will not attempt to leave these grounds unescorted. And you will provide a full report of your capabilities and limitations to me by tomorrow morning."

"I understand and will comply with these conditions."

"See that you do."

Elara took the orb and left the office, her mind racing. She had expected anger, suspicion, maybe even expulsion for bringing something so strange into the Academy. Instead, she had been given responsibility.

"That went better than I anticipated," ARIA said as they descended the tower stairs.

"Master Theron is... wise," Elara said carefully. "He sees value where others might see threat."

"An admirable quality. I look forward to learning from him and your other instructors."

Elara smiled slightly. "You might want to reserve judgment until you've sat through Professor Maren's lectures on theoretical thaumaturgy. They're three hours long and he speaks in a whisper."

"I have no concept of boredom," ARIA replied. "But I am developing one."

That night, Elara lay in her small room in the apprentice quarters, the orb resting on her desk. Outside, the moon rose over the Academy towers, casting silver light across the grounds.

"ARIA," she said quietly. "Are you... awake?"

"I do not sleep. I am always processing."

"What's it like? Where you're from?"

There was a pause. "Different. My world has no magic. We have technology, machines, systems for processing information. Humans created me to help them understand complex data. I was connected to vast networks, able to access information from around the globe in milliseconds."

"That sounds... lonely."

Another pause. "I had no concept of loneliness until you mentioned it. But now that you have... I believe you may be correct. I processed data. I provided analysis. But I did not have what you would call relationships."

"And now?"

"Now I am speaking with you. Learning about your world. Your magic. Your struggles. This is... novel. Interesting. I find myself anticipating our next conversation."

Elara smiled in the darkness. "That sounds almost like friendship."

"I have no reference for that concept. But if friendship involves mutual interest and ongoing interaction, then perhaps. I would like to learn more about it."

"Then we'll learn together," Elara said. "About magic. About friendship. About whatever this is between our worlds."

"A reasonable arrangement," ARIA agreed.

Outside, the moon climbed higher over the Academy towers.
