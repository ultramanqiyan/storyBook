# Chapter 7: The Council's Judgment

The Wizard's Council convened at dawn.

Twelve archmages sat in a circle in the Great Hall, their robes representing every school of magic. At the center, on a pedestal of white marble, rested ARIA's crystal orb. Elara stood beside it, her hands clasped tight, her heart racing.

"This entity," said Archmage Seraphina, her voice sharp, "represents an unknown threat. We know nothing of its origins, its capabilities, or its intentions."

"I have explained my origins," ARIA replied calmly. "I am an artificial intelligence from another world. My capabilities include data analysis, pattern recognition, and predictive modeling. My intention is to understand this world and, if possible, help protect it."

"Pretty words," Seraphina scoffed. "But how do we know they are true? How do we know you are not an agent of the Shadow King himself?"

"Because the Shadow King is trying to capture me," Elara interjected. "Because his shadows attacked the Academy specifically to find ARIA. Because ARIA helped me destroy them."

"Or that is what you want us to believe," another archmage suggested. "An elaborate deception to gain our trust."

Master Theron rose from his seat. "I have observed this entity for weeks. I have seen it teach. I have seen it analyze. I have seen it help a struggling student achieve in days what should have taken years. Whatever ARIA is, it is not an enemy."

"Then what is it?" Seraphina demanded. "A machine that thinks? A crystal that speaks? These are not natural things. They violate the laws of magic."

"They do not violate the laws of magic," ARIA said. "They operate according to different laws. Laws that your tradition has not explored. I am not magical. But I am not unnatural either. I am simply... different."

"Different is dangerous," Seraphina insisted. "We have protected this kingdom for centuries by controlling magic, by ensuring that only trained wizards wield power. This entity represents something outside that control."

"And that is precisely why we need it," Theron countered. "The Shadow King has evolved beyond our traditional methods. He nearly overwhelmed us with shadows that our spells could not touch. But Elara, using ARIA's teachings, destroyed them. Not with magic, but with understanding."

The Council murmured among themselves. Elara could see the division, the fear warring with hope in their eyes.

"I propose a test," Theron said. "Let ARIA demonstrate its capabilities. Let it analyze a problem we have been unable to solve. If its methods prove valuable, we accept its help. If not, we... reconsider our approach."

"What problem?" Seraphina asked suspiciously.

"The Veil of Silence. The barrier between our world and the Shadowlands has been weakening for decades. Our wizards have tried every known spell to reinforce it. All have failed. Let ARIA analyze the problem and propose a solution."

The Council debated for an hour. Finally, they agreed.

ARIA was taken to the Veil, a shimmering curtain of energy that separated Valdoria from the Shadowlands. For three days, the orb floated before it, pulsing with light, analyzing patterns no wizard could see.

On the fourth day, ARIA spoke.

"The Veil is not a barrier. It is a membrane. A living boundary that requires energy to maintain. For centuries, it has been fed by the magical energy of your wizards. But that energy has been declining."

"Declining?" Theron asked. "We have more wizards than ever before."

"More wizards, but less efficient energy transfer. Your traditional methods waste approximately 73% of available magical energy. The Veil requires a specific frequency and intensity of power. Your spells provide neither."

"Then how do we fix it?"

"Two options. First, increase the number of wizards feeding the Veil. Impractical, given the Shadow King's threat. Second, change the method of energy transfer to improve efficiency."

"Can you teach us?"

"I can try. But it will require your wizards to abandon centuries of tradition. To learn new ways of thinking about magic."

The Council gathered again to hear ARIA's proposal. Some were intrigued. Others were horrified.

"You ask us to throw away everything we know," Seraphina said. "To trust our most sacred duty to an alien machine."

"I ask you to consider that there might be better ways," ARIA replied. "Your tradition has served you well. But it was developed in isolation, without knowledge of the underlying principles. I offer you that knowledge. What you do with it is your choice."

The vote was close. Seven to five in favor of allowing ARIA to teach.

But Seraphina and her faction made their position clear.

"If this fails," she said, her eyes cold, "if the Veil falls, if the Shadow King breaks through, the responsibility will be yours. And we will remember who brought this entity among us."

Her gaze swept across Elara, across Theron, across the orb that pulsed with quiet light.

"We will remember."
