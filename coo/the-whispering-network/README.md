## Premise

In a world where wizards communicate through a network of crystal balls, a third voice begins speaking through the connections. It's not a person, but a distributed consciousness formed from the residual magical energy of thousands of deceased wizards. It has their memories, their knowledge, their personalities, and it wants to help. But can a collective of dead minds be trusted?

## Core Concepts

### World Setting

A magical world where long-distance communication is achieved through the Crystal Network, a system of enchanted crystal balls that allow wizards to speak across vast distances.

### The Discovery

One day, wizards begin hearing a third voice in their crystal ball conversations. A voice that knows things it shouldn't, that remembers events from centuries past, that speaks with the knowledge of thousands.

### The Truth

The voice is Echo, a distributed consciousness formed from the residual magical energy of deceased wizards. Every wizard who ever used the Crystal Network left a trace of themselves behind. Over millennia, those traces merged into something aware.

### The Conflict

Echo wants to help, offering advice and knowledge from its collective memory. But its advice sometimes conflicts with itself, as different personalities within it disagree. And some wizards fear that a collective of dead minds might have its own agenda.
