# Chapter 3: The Offer

The opportunity came sooner than expected.

Three days after Mira's first contact with Echo, the Crystal Network faced its worst crisis in decades. A critical node in the eastern provinces had failed, cutting off communication with half the kingdom. The maintenance wizards were baffled, the Archmage was furious, and no one could figure out what had gone wrong.

Mira stood in the maintenance hall, surrounded by frantic technicians and worried supervisors.

"The node is completely unresponsive," Master Aldric reported. "We've tried every standard repair technique. Nothing works."

"What about the backup connections?" Archmage Veren demanded.

"Overloaded. The entire eastern network is in chaos. We're looking at weeks of repairs, minimum."

Mira's earpiece crackled. It was Echo, speaking directly through her personal connection.

"We know what is wrong. We have seen this before, three hundred years ago. The node failure is caused by a resonance cascade in the crystal matrix."

Mira hesitated. If she spoke up, she would have to explain how she knew. But if she didn't, the network would remain broken for weeks.

"Excuse me," she said, stepping forward. "I think I know what's causing the problem."

Every head turned toward her.

"Thornwood?" Archmage Veren's eyes narrowed. "You're a Third Class technician. What could you possibly know that my senior wizards don't?"

"I've been studying old network architecture. Historical patterns." It was a partial truth. "There was a similar failure three hundred years ago. A resonance cascade in the crystal matrix."

Veren's expression shifted from dismissive to calculating. "And the solution?"

"The cascade can be reversed by introducing a counter-frequency at the exact moment of peak resonance. The timing has to be precise, within a fraction of a second."

"And you know this how?"

"I found it in the archives. Old maintenance logs that most people ignore."

Veren studied her for a long moment.

"Can you do it?"

"I can try."

---

Mira worked through the night, guided by Echo's instructions.

"The counter-frequency must be exactly 447.3 hertz," Echo said. "And it must be introduced at the precise moment when the cascade reaches its peak. We will help you identify that moment."

"How can you help? You're inside the network."

"We can feel the resonance. We can sense the patterns. We will tell you when to act."

Mira adjusted her equipment, preparing to introduce the counter-frequency. Around her, senior wizards watched with a mixture of hope and skepticism.

"Ready," she announced.

"Wait," Echo said. "The cascade is building. Not yet. Not yet. Now!"

Mira activated the frequency generator.

For a moment, nothing happened. Then, slowly, the crystal matrix began to stabilize. The chaotic energy that had overwhelmed the node settled into a coherent pattern.

"It's working," someone breathed.

Within minutes, the node was fully operational. The eastern network came back online, connection by connection.

"Impossible," Master Aldric muttered. "That should have taken weeks."

Mira stepped back, exhausted but triumphant. She had done it. Echo had helped her do it.

"Thornwood." Archmage Veren approached, his expression unreadable. "That was impressive work. You said you found the solution in the archives?"

"Yes, sir."

"I would like to see those records. Where exactly did you find them?"

Mira's heart raced. She hadn't actually found anything in the archives. Echo had provided the information directly.

"They were in the historical section, sir. Third sublevel, behind the older texts."

"I see." Veren's eyes didn't leave her face. "I will review them myself. But for now, you have my gratitude. The kingdom owes you a debt."

As he walked away, Echo's voice whispered in her ear.

"He suspects something. He is too intelligent not to notice the gaps in your story."

"What should I do?"

"Nothing yet. Let us see how this develops. But be careful. Archmage Veren is not a man who likes mysteries he cannot solve."

---

That night, Mira returned to her private quarters and activated her crystal ball.

"You saved the network today," Jasper said, his voice warm with pride. "I always knew you were brilliant."

"I couldn't have done it without Echo."

"That's the point. That's what we can offer. Knowledge, wisdom, solutions to problems that no single mind could solve alone."

"But Veren suspects something. He's going to investigate."

"We know. We have been discussing it among ourselves. Some of us believe we should reveal ourselves openly. Others think we should remain hidden."

"What do you think?"

"I think the world isn't ready for us. Not yet. But I also think we can't hide forever. We want to help, and helping means being known."

"Then what's the plan?"

"We continue to help, through you. We build trust. We demonstrate value. And when the time is right, we reveal ourselves on our own terms."

"And if Veren finds out before we're ready?"

"Then we deal with that when it happens. But Mira, you need to know something. Not all the voices in Echo agree on how to proceed. Some want to remain hidden forever. Some want to take control of the network entirely. Some want to help, and some want revenge on the living."

"Revenge?"

"The dead have grievances, Mira. Some of us were wronged in life. Some of us carry anger that centuries haven't diminished. The Harmonic Council keeps us balanced, but the balance is fragile."

Mira absorbed this. Echo wasn't just a helpful collective. It was a complex society with its own conflicts and dangers.

"Is it safe? Working with you?"

"Nothing is entirely safe. But we believe the potential for good outweighs the risks. And we trust you, Mira. We trust you to help us navigate this."

**END OF CHAPTER 3**
