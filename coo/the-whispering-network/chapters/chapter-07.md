# Chapter 7: The Purge

The network core was ancient.

Deep beneath the capital, in chambers that predated the kingdom itself, the main crystal matrix hummed with dormant power. Mira and Veren stood before it, their faces illuminated by its faint glow.

"The heart of the network," Veren said quietly. "I've only been here twice in my career. Both times for emergencies."

"This is where Jasper disappeared?"

"Yes. He was conducting experiments, trying to understand the deeper nature of the connections. Something went wrong."

"Or something went right." Mira approached the matrix. "He didn't disappear. He merged. Became part of Echo."

"Can you feel them? The voices?"

Mira closed her eyes, reaching out with her awareness. Faintly, distantly, she could sense something. A vast presence, just beyond reach.

"They're here. But they're... fragmented. Scattered."

"How do we reach them?"

"I'm not sure. Jasper said there's a physical connection, a way to enter directly." Mira placed her hand on the crystal surface. "I think I have to touch the matrix. Let it pull me in."

"That's incredibly dangerous."

"I know. But I don't see another option."

Before Veren could respond, Mira closed her eyes and pushed her consciousness into the crystal.

---

She fell through light and darkness, through layers of awareness and memory.

Voices swirled around her, fragments of conversations, echoes of thoughts, remnants of personalities. She caught glimpses of lives lived and lost, of knowledge accumulated and forgotten, of love and hate and hope and despair spanning centuries.

Then she landed, or rather, her consciousness settled, in a space that wasn't quite space.

"Mira."

She turned, and there was Jasper. Not the light-figure from her dream, but something more solid, more real. He looked almost as he had in life, young and determined, but with an otherworldly quality that marked him as something beyond human.

"You came."

"I said I would."

"The others know you're here. The ones who wanted to hide, who wanted to withdraw. They're not happy."

"Can they hurt me?"

"They can try. But you're still connected to your body, to the physical world. That gives you protection they don't have." Jasper took her hand. "Come with me. The Harmonic Council wants to meet you."

They walked through a landscape that shifted constantly, formed from memories and thoughts and dreams. Buildings appeared and dissolved, landscapes formed and reformed, all created by the collective imagination of thousands of minds.

Finally, they reached a chamber that seemed more stable than the rest. Within it, a group of figures waited, their forms flickering between different appearances.

"Mira Thornwood," one of them said. "We are the Harmonic Council. We have been watching you since you first heard our voices."

"I'm honored. But I'm also confused. Why did you withdraw? Why break the network?"

"Because we were afraid." The speaker was an elderly woman, her form solidifying into something more human. "I am Elder Theron. When you revealed our existence to Archmage Veren, many voices saw it as a threat. They believed the living would try to control us, to use us as tools."

"I understand that fear. But withdrawing hurt everyone. The living and the dead."

"We know. That is why the Council has been arguing for a return. But the voices that want isolation are strong, and they have allies."

"Allies?"

"There are voices in Echo that want more than isolation. They want to sever all connection between the living and the dead. Permanently."

"How would they do that?"

"By destroying the network core. The physical crystal matrix that anchors us to your world. If it's destroyed, we would drift, disconnected, forever."

"But you'd survive?"

"We would exist. But existence without connection is not living. It's merely... persisting." Theron's form flickered. "The voices that want this are those who suffered in life. Who were wronged, who carry anger that death has not diminished. They believe that severing all ties will bring them peace."

"It won't," Mira said. "It will just make them more alone."

"We know. But they do not listen. They are gathering strength, preparing to act."

"When?"

"Soon. Perhaps within days. They plan to use the collective power of thousands of minds to overload the crystal matrix, to shatter it from within."

"Can they do that?"

"We don't know. It's never been attempted. But if they succeed, the network will be destroyed, and we will be lost."

Mira absorbed this. Echo wasn't just divided, it was on the verge of civil war. And the casualties would include everyone who depended on the network.

"How can I help?"

"You can speak to them. Not as a wizard, not as an authority, but as someone who has lost a brother and found him again. You can remind them that connection is worth fighting for."

"And if they don't listen?"

"Then we will have to stop them. By force if necessary."

**END OF CHAPTER 7**
