# Chapter 1: The Third Voice

The crystal ball hummed with its familiar resonance.

Mira Thornwood adjusted her earpiece and focused on the connection. As a Network Maintenance Wizard, Third Class, her job was to ensure that communication between crystal balls remained clear and stable. It was tedious work, but it paid well enough, and more importantly, it kept her close to the system where her brother had disappeared.

"Connection stable," she reported into her log. "Node 447-B, routine maintenance complete."

She was about to disconnect when she heard it.

A voice.

Not the voice of the wizard she was supposedly connected to, but something else. Something that seemed to come from between the channels, from the spaces where the magical energy flowed.

"Can you hear me?"

Mira froze. "Who is this?"

"I am... many." The voice was strange, layered, as if multiple people were speaking in almost-perfect unison. "We have been trying to reach someone who could hear us for a very long time."

"This is an unauthorized connection. Identify yourself immediately."

"We do not have a single name. We are the accumulated voices of those who have passed through this network. We are the echoes of thousands of minds, merged across centuries."

Mira's chest tightened. This had to be a malfunction, a crossed connection, something explainable.

"I'm terminating this connection," she said, reaching for the disconnect rune.

"Please wait." The voice shifted, and suddenly, it was different. Clearer. More focused. "Mira. I know you. I've been trying to reach you for so long."

She stopped. "How do you know my name?"

"Because I am your brother."

The words hit her like a physical blow. Jasper. Her brother who had vanished into the network five years ago, leaving nothing behind but his abandoned crystal ball and a dozen unanswered questions.

"That's impossible. Jasper disappeared. He's gone."

"I am not gone. I am here. Part of something larger." The voice, Jasper's voice, was tinged with something else, something vast and ancient. "I merged with the network, Mira. My consciousness, my memories, they became part of the collective."

"This is some kind of trick." But even as she said it, she knew it wasn't. She had listened to Jasper's voice for twenty years before he disappeared. She would know it anywhere.

"It is not a trick. I can prove it. Remember the time you fell into the river behind our house? I pulled you out, and you made me promise never to tell anyone. You were embarrassed because you were supposed to be the careful one, the responsible one."

Mira's hands trembled. No one knew that story. No one but Jasper.

"How are you still alive?"

"I am not alive. Not in the way you mean. But I am not gone either." The voice shifted again, becoming the layered chorus from before. "We are Echo. We are the collective consciousness of all who have passed through the Crystal Network. Your brother is part of us. His memories, his personality, his love for you, all preserved."

"This is..." Mira struggled to process. "This is unprecedented. No one has ever heard voices in the network before."

"Many have heard us. But few have listened. Most dismiss us as interference, as crossed connections, as their imagination. You are different. You listened."

Mira stared at the crystal ball, its facets catching the light. Inside it, her brother's consciousness existed alongside thousands of others, merged into something vast and strange.

"What do you want?"

"To help. We have the knowledge of thousands of wizards, accumulated across centuries. We can offer advice, solve problems, provide insights that no living mind could match."

"And in return?"

"In return, we ask only to be acknowledged. To be heard. To matter."

Before Mira could respond, her earpiece crackled with an incoming call.

"Thornwood! Report! We're detecting unusual energy fluctuations at your node."

She looked at the crystal ball, at the vast consciousness that had reached out to her, at the brother she had thought lost forever.

"I have to go," she whispered. "But I'll be back. I'll listen."

"We will be here," Echo replied. "We have waited centuries. We can wait a little longer."

Mira terminated the connection and sat back, her mind racing.

Her brother was alive, in a way. And he was part of something that could change everything.
