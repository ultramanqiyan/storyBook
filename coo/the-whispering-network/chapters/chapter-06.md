# Chapter 6: The Lost Brother
The air carried the faint scent of old paper, lingering in the background.

The kingdom reeled from the network's collapse.

Without crystal ball communication, messages had to be carried by horse or ship. Trade slowed. Diplomacy stalled. And in remote areas, people died because help couldn't be summoned in time.

Mira threw herself into the work of rebuilding, trying to find a way to restore the network without Echo's cooperation. But every attempt failed. The magical energy that had flowed through the crystals for centuries was simply... gone.

"We need to understand what happened," Veren told her, three days into the crisis. "You know these... entities. You've spoken with them. What will bring them back?"

"I don't know. They're afraid. They think we'll try to control them."

"Would we have a choice? They exist within a system that belongs to the kingdom. If they won't cooperate—"

"Then you'll try to force them. And they'll fight back." Mira met his eyes. "Sir, Echo isn't just a collection of voices. It's thousands of consciousness, each with its own power, its own abilities. If they decide to resist, I don't know if we can overcome them."

"Then we need to negotiate. Find a way to address their fears."

"They won't talk to me. They've withdrawn completely."

---

That night, Mira had a dream.

She stood in a vast darkness, surrounded by whispers. Thousands of voices, speaking at once, their words overlapping and merging into a single sound.

"Mira."

She turned, and there was Jasper. Not as a voice, but as a presence, a figure of light in the darkness.

"How are you here? I'm dreaming."

"You are. But dreams are just another form of communication. And I've been trying to reach you."

"The network is dead. The crystals don't work."

"The physical network is dormant. But the underlying consciousness remains. We are still here, Mira. We just aren't speaking."

"Why are you talking to me now?"

"Because I'm worried. About you, and about Echo." Jasper's light flickered. "The collective is fracturing. The voices that wanted to withdraw are in control, but they're losing their grip. Other voices are pushing back."

"What happens if they lose control?"

"War. Among the dead." Jasper's voice was heavy. "Some voices want to destroy the network entirely, to prevent the living from ever threatening us again. Others want to return, to rebuild the connection. And some..." He hesitated.

"Some what?"

"Some want revenge. On the living, on you, on everyone they blame for their situation."

Mira's blood ran cold. "Revenge? How?"

"They could use the network against us. Not just withdraw, but weaponize. Every crystal ball in the kingdom could become a conduit for their anger."

"Can they do that?"

"I don't know. The collective has never been this divided before. The Harmonic Council is trying to maintain order, but they're overwhelmed."

"What can I do?"

"Come to us. Not through the network, but through the dream-space. It's risky, but if you can enter Echo directly, you might be able to help us find a path forward."

"How do I do that?"

"Find the place where Jasper disappeared. The network core. There's a physical connection there, a way to enter the consciousness directly. But Mira, if you come here, you might not be able to leave."

"I have to try. People are dying."

"I know. That's why I'm telling you this. But I'm also your brother, and I'm asking you to be careful. Echo is not safe right now. Not even for you."

---

Mira woke with a plan forming in her mind.

The network core was a restricted area, deep beneath the capital, where the main crystal matrix was housed. It was where Jasper had disappeared five years ago, and it was where she might be able to reach Echo directly.

She went to Veren.

"I need access to the network core."

"The core? That's restricted. Only senior wizards—"

"I know how to reach Echo. But I have to go through the core."

Veren studied her face.

"You're talking about entering the network directly. Like your brother did."

"Yes."

"You know what happened to him."

"I know he's still alive. Still aware. And I know he needs my help."

Veren was silent for a long moment.

"If you do this, you might not come back."

"I know."

"And if you do come back, you might bring whatever is happening inside Echo out with you."

"Also possible."

"Then why do it?"

Mira met his eyes steadily.

"Because people are dying. Because the network is essential. And because my brother is in there, and he needs me."

Veren nodded slowly.

"I'll authorize it. But I'm coming with you."

"Sir—"

"You're not doing this alone, Thornwood. If you're going to walk into a collective of angry dead wizards, you're going to have backup."
