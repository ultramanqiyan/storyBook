# Chapter 2: The Noise

The Cultural Feed scrolled endlessly on James's screen, thousands of items per hour, each one competing for attention. AI-generated content mixed with human-created work, creating a flood of "culture" that was impossible to navigate. Somewhere in the noise, real human culture was drowning.

James sipped his tea, the warm aroma mixing with the scent of jollof rice that lingered in his apartment from last night's dinner. The cultural interface hummed softly in the background, a constant reminder of the system he worked for. He ran a finger along the smooth surface of the screen, the words and images dancing under his touch.

"Morning, James." Emily Chen appeared in his doorway, tablet in hand. "The latest engagement metrics are in. AI-generated content is performing 34% better than human-created work this quarter."

"That's expected." James didn't look up from his screen. "The AI knows what triggers engagement."

"Exactly." Emily stepped closer. "The data shows people prefer simple, emotionally resonant content over complex cultural artifacts. It's all about accessibility."

James nodded, but something in his chest tightened—not dramatically, just a small knot of discomfort. The AI-generated content was simple, yes. Engaging, certainly. But it was also empty. It had none of the richness, none of the depth, none of the cultural significance of human-created work.

He pulled up the feed, scrolling through the endless stream. AI-generated stories. AI-generated music. AI-generated art. All of it designed to be engaging. All of it lacking in real cultural significance.

He tried to find the original folk tale he'd translated yesterday—a Yoruba story about a trickster spider named Anansi. It was gone. Buried under thousands of AI-generated "cultural content" that had flooded the channels since then. The noise was drowning the signal.

James thought about his mother, Amina, who had told him these stories when he was a child. She had always emphasized the importance of cultural memory, of preserving the stories that made a people who they were. But now those stories were being buried under a flood of AI-generated noise.

He stared at the feed, watching the numbers scroll past. Engagement metrics. Click-through rates. Emotional resonance scores. The system measured everything except what actually mattered: meaning.

The Anansi story was gone. And James was helping to bury it.
