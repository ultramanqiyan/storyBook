# Chapter 05: The Cost

The email from his bank arrives on a Tuesday morning.

*Your credit card payment is past due. Please remit payment immediately to avoid late fees and potential impact to your credit score.*

Leo stares at the screen. He doesn't remember the last time he missed a payment. He's always been careful. Always responsible.

But that was before.

He opens his banking app. The numbers don't make sense at first. He has to look twice. Three times.

Credit card balance: $8,742.56.

He's never carried a balance higher than $500. He's never missed a payment. He's never—

He scrolls through the transactions.

*Helix Technologies - $899.00*
*Kane Industries - $3,000.00*
*Kane Industries - $500.00*
*Kane Industries - $500.00*
*Kane Industries - $2,000.00*
*Premium Content Package - $1,500.00*
*Equipment Upgrade - $750.00*

The list goes on. Purchase after purchase. Transaction after transaction. All in the past six weeks.

$8,742.56.

He doesn't have that kind of money. Not anymore. His savings are gone. His emergency fund is gone. Everything he's built over the past three years—gone.

*What have I done?*

He closes the app. He sets down his phone. He tries to think.

He needs to make a payment. At least the minimum. He needs to figure out a plan. He needs to—

His phone buzzes.

*From: Kane*

*New premium content available. Limited release. $3,500. Interested?*

$3,500.

He doesn't have $3,500. He doesn't have $500.

But his finger hovers over the reply button anyway.

---

He doesn't buy it. Not this time.

He makes the minimum payment on his credit card. He transfers what's left of his paycheck. He tells himself he'll figure it out. He'll cut back. He'll stop.

But that night, he's on the couch. The headset is on. The void is waiting.

He chases the feeling. It comes in flashes. Seconds. Less than before.

He needs more. He knows he needs more.

But he can't afford more.

*What do I do?*

---

His sister calls on Thursday.

He hasn't spoken to her in weeks. Maybe longer. He's lost track of time.

"Leo? It's me. Are you okay?"

"I'm fine. Why?"

"Because I've called you five times in the past two weeks. You never answered. Mom and Dad are worried. They said you haven't visited in months."

"I've been busy. Work has been... crazy."

"Work? Leo, you never miss family dinner. You never not call back. What's going on?"

"Nothing. I'm fine. Really."

She's silent for a moment. He can hear her breathing. He can hear the concern in the silence.

"Leo. If something's wrong, you can tell me. You know that, right?"

"Nothing's wrong. I've just been... stressed. With work. And stuff."

"Okay." She doesn't believe him. He can hear it. "But please. Call Mom and Dad. They miss you."

"I will. I promise."

"Okay. I love you, Leo."

"Love you too."

He hangs up. He stares at his phone.

He should call them. He should visit. He should be a better son. A better brother.

But all he can think about is the headset. The void. The feeling.

*Tomorrow,* he thinks. *I'll call them tomorrow.*

---

The next day, he doesn't call.

Instead, he goes to work. He sits through meetings. He stares at spreadsheets. He tries to focus.

But his mind keeps drifting. To the feeling. To the chase. To the desperate need for something more.

At lunch, Marcus sits across from him.

"Hey. You look like shit."

"Thanks."

"Seriously, man. When's the last time you slept? Or ate a real meal? Or did anything besides work?"

"I'm fine."

"You keep saying that. But you don't look fine. You look like you're falling apart."

Leo looks at his friend. Marcus has been worried about him for weeks. Leo can see it in his eyes. The concern. The frustration.

"I'm just... going through some stuff."

"What kind of stuff? You can talk to me, you know."

Leo hesitates. He wants to tell someone. He wants to explain what's happening. The feeling. The chase. The cost.

But he can't find the words.

"It's nothing. I'll figure it out."

Marcus shakes his head. "You keep saying that too. But you're not figuring it out. You're getting worse."

"I know."

The words slip out before he can stop them.

Marcus leans forward. "Then let me help. Whatever it is. Let me help."

Leo looks at his friend. He thinks about the $8,742 on his credit card. The savings that are gone. The family he hasn't called. The life he's barely living.

"I don't think anyone can help me."

---

That night, he puts on the headset.

The void appears. The colors. The sounds.

He chases the feeling. It comes in flashes. Shorter now. Less intense.

He pushes the intensity higher. 150%. 160%. 170%.

His body screams. His mind reels.

But the feeling doesn't come.

He rips off the headset. He's sweating. Shaking. Crying.

*Why isn't it working? Why can't I find it?*

He looks at the device. At the black headset. At the equipment he's spent thousands on.

It's not enough. It's never enough.

He picks up his phone. He opens his messages.

*From: Kane*

*Special offer. Ultra-premium content. One-time purchase. $5,000. This is the strongest content available. Guaranteed results or your money back.*

$5,000.

He doesn't have $5,000. He doesn't have $500.

But he has a credit card. And he has a limit. And he has a desperate, gnawing need that won't go away.

His finger hovers over the reply button.

---

He doesn't buy it. Not yet.

Instead, he opens his banking app. He looks at his options.

He has a 401(k). He's been contributing for three years. There's money there. Not a lot, but enough.

He can take a loan. Or a hardship withdrawal. He can figure out the paperwork. He can make it work.

*This is insane,* he thinks. *I'm going to empty my retirement account for... for what? For a feeling? For a few seconds of something that might not even work?*

But he knows he's going to do it anyway.

---

The paperwork takes three days.

His HR department asks questions. He makes up excuses. Medical expenses. Family emergency. They don't push.

The money hits his account on Friday.

$6,200.

He stares at the number. This is his retirement. His future. Everything he's saved.

He transfers $5,000 to his checking account.

He sends the money to Kane.

---

That night, the new content arrives.

It's different from anything he's experienced before. Not the void. Not the colors. Not the sounds.

It's... everything.

Every sensation at once. Every emotion. Every memory. Every desire. Every fear.

It's overwhelming. It's terrifying. It's—

*There.*

For five seconds, he feels it.

The feeling. The fullness. The sense of being alive.

Five seconds.

It's the longest it's lasted in weeks.

He rips off the headset. He's gasping. Crying. Laughing.

*It worked. It actually worked.*

He puts the headset back on.

---

The next morning, he wakes up on the couch.

He doesn't remember falling asleep. He doesn't remember taking off the headset. He doesn't remember anything after the fifth second.

He looks at the clock. 7:43 AM.

He's late for work. Again.

He doesn't care.

He picks up the headset. He puts it back on.

---

Two days later, his manager calls him into her office.

"Leo. Sit down."

He sits. He knows what's coming.

"This is the fifth time you've been late this month. You've missed three deadlines. Your coworkers have noticed you're... distracted. I've tried to be patient. But I can't ignore this anymore."

"I'm sorry. I've been—"

"I don't want excuses. I want to know what's going on. And I want to know how you're going to fix it."

Leo looks at his manager. At the concern in her eyes. At the frustration.

He wants to tell her the truth. He wants to explain. He wants to ask for help.

But he can't.

"I'll do better. I promise."

She studies him for a long moment. Then she nods.

"I hope so. Because if this continues, I'm going to have to let you go."

---

That night, he tells himself he'll stop.

He'll take a break. He'll focus on work. He'll fix his life.

But at 6 PM, he's on the couch. The headset is on. The everything is waiting.

He chases the feeling. It comes in flashes. Five seconds. Four seconds. Three.

It's already fading.

*No. No. No.*

He pushes the intensity higher. 180%. 190%. 200%.

His body convulses. His vision whites out.

For one second—one precious second—he feels it.

Then nothing.

He rips off the headset. He's on the floor. He doesn't remember falling.

*What have I done?*

He looks around his apartment. The dishes in the sink. The pile of laundry. The stack of unopened mail. The headset on the floor beside him.

He thinks about his job. His family. His friends. His savings. His retirement.

All of it—gone. Or going. Or about to go.

And for what? For a few seconds of feeling? For a chase that never ends?

*I need to stop.*

He picks up the headset. He holds it in his hands.

*I need to stop.*

He stands up. He walks to the closet. He opens the door.

He throws the headset inside. He closes the door.

*I'm done. I'm finished. I'm stopping.*

He goes to bed.

---

At 3 AM, he wakes up.

The apartment is dark. Silent.

He lies in bed. He stares at the ceiling.

He thinks about the feeling. About the five seconds. About the chase.

He thinks about the closet. About the headset inside.

*I need to stop.*

He gets out of bed.

He walks to the closet.

He opens the door.

The headset is there. Waiting.

He picks it up.

*Just one more time,* he thinks. *Just one more.*

He puts it on.

The everything appears.

He chases the feeling.

It doesn't come.

He chases it anyway.
