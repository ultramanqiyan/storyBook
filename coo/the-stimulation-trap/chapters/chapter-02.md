# Chapter 02: The High

The email arrives at 2:17 PM on a Wednesday.

Leo is in a meeting—something about Q3 projections, something about market trends, something about synergy. He stopped paying attention ten minutes ago. His phone buzzes on the table. He glances down.

*Exclusive Preview: The Apex Collection. Limited Release. Premium Tier Only.*

His thumb hovers over the notification. He's heard of the Apex Collection. Everyone has. It's the new line of experiences from Helix—the biggest name in AI entertainment. Rumors say it's the most intense, most immersive content ever created. Rumors say it costs more than most people's monthly rent.

He opens the email.

*Dear Valued Subscriber,*

*You have been selected for early access to The Apex Collection. This is content beyond anything you've experienced before. Stimulus levels that push the boundaries of what's possible. Sensory depth that blurs the line between virtual and real.*

*Are you ready to feel something new?*

*Click here to preview.*

His heart beats faster. This is it. This is what he's been waiting for. The Garden of Echoes was nice. Peaceful. Beautiful. But lately, he's been wanting something more. Something that makes him feel... more.

He clicks the link.

---

The preview loads.

He's still in the meeting. Someone is talking about quarterly variance. But Leo is watching his phone screen, where a 30-second teaser plays.

Colors explode. Music swells. A voice—deep, resonant—speaks directly to him.

*You've felt peace. You've felt beauty. But have you ever felt alive?*

The screen shows fragments of experiences. A mountain peak at dawn, the air so thin it burns. A concert where the bass vibrates through every cell. A feast where every bite is a revelation.

*The Apex Collection. Feel everything.*

The teaser ends. A button appears: *Pre-order now. $899.*

$899. That's almost his entire entertainment budget for the month. That's more than his car payment. That's...

He doesn't hesitate.

He clicks the button.

---

That night, he can't focus on anything.

He tries his usual content—*The Garden of Echoes*, *Neon Drift*, even a new historical drama that's getting rave reviews. But nothing holds his attention. His mind keeps drifting to the Apex Collection. To the preview. To the promise of something more.

*Three days,* the confirmation email said. *Your experience will be ready in three days.*

Three days feels like forever.

He lies on the couch, the device in his hands, scrolling through the same content he's seen a hundred times. Nothing excites him. Nothing pulls him in.

*This is ridiculous,* he thinks. *It's just content. It's just entertainment.*

But he knows it's more than that. He's been feeling it for weeks now—a restlessness. A hunger. The usual experiences don't satisfy him the way they used to. The peace he felt in the Garden of Echoes has faded. The beauty feels distant. He needs something stronger.

*Three days.*

He closes his eyes. He tries to sleep.

---

The first day passes slowly.

Work is the same. Spreadsheets. Meetings. Marcus asking if he's okay. Leo says yes. He's always fine.

He goes home. He eats dinner—something from a box, something he doesn't taste. He sits on the couch. He stares at the device.

He doesn't put on the headset. What's the point? Nothing will feel right until the Apex Collection is ready.

Instead, he watches the preview again. And again. And again.

*You've felt peace. You've felt beauty. But have you ever felt alive?*

The words echo in his mind.

*Have you ever felt alive?*

He thinks about that question. Has he? He's felt satisfied. Content. Entertained. But alive?

He's not sure.

---

The second day is worse.

He can't focus at work. His manager notices.

"Leo. The Henderson report. It was due yesterday."

"I know. I'm sorry. I'll have it by end of day."

"Is everything okay? You've been... distracted lately."

"I'm fine. Just not sleeping well."

His manager nods, unconvinced but unwilling to push. "The report. End of day."

Leo nods. He opens the Henderson file. He stares at the numbers. They don't make sense. Or maybe they do. He can't tell anymore.

He thinks about the Apex Collection. About the mountain peak. About the concert. About the feast.

*Tomorrow,* he thinks. *Tomorrow I'll feel something real.*

---

The third day.

He wakes up early. He can't sleep. His stomach is tight with anticipation.

He goes to work. He submits the Henderson report. He sits through meetings. He nods at the right moments. He smiles when he's supposed to smile.

But inside, he's counting the hours.

*Six hours until I can go home.*

*Five hours.*

*Four.*

At 5:00 PM, he packs his bag. He doesn't say goodbye to anyone. He walks out of the building. He takes the stairs instead of the elevator because it's faster.

He walks home. He doesn't notice the weather. He doesn't notice the people. He doesn't notice anything.

He opens his apartment door. He walks to the couch. He picks up the device.

The notification is there.

*The Apex Collection is ready. Begin your experience?*

His hand trembles slightly as he taps *Yes*.

---

The headset slides over his eyes.

The world shifts.

But this time, it's different.

Usually, there's a transition. A lobby. A menu. A moment to prepare.

This time, there's nothing.

He's just... there.

---

He's standing on a cliff.

The wind is fierce. It tears at his clothes, his hair, his skin. He can feel every gust. The cold bites into him. The air is thin—he's gasping, his lungs struggling to pull in oxygen.

Below him, a valley stretches to infinity. Mountains rise in the distance, their peaks piercing clouds of gold and crimson. The sun is setting—or rising, he can't tell—and the sky is on fire.

*Oh.*

The word escapes him. Not spoken. Just felt.

He takes a step. The rock beneath his feet is solid. Real. He can feel every grain, every crack, every imperfection.

He looks down. His hands are trembling. Not from cold. From something else. Something he can't name.

*This.*

He doesn't know what he means. But he feels it. A fullness in his chest. A tightness in his throat. A sensation that spreads from his core to his fingertips.

*This is it. This is what I've been looking for.*

---

The experience shifts.

He's no longer on the cliff. He's in a crowd. Music thunders around him. Lights flash. Bodies press against him—warm, real, alive.

He's dancing. He doesn't remember starting. But his body moves. The bass vibrates through him, shaking loose something that's been locked inside. He raises his arms. He throws back his head. He screams—not words, just sound. Just release.

The crowd screams with him. A thousand voices. A thousand hearts beating in time.

He feels them. Not physically. But something deeper. A connection. A shared moment of pure, unfiltered existence.

*I'm alive.*

The thought comes unbidden. But he knows it's true. In this moment, surrounded by music and light and people he'll never see again, he feels more alive than he has in months. Years. Maybe ever.

---

The experience shifts again.

He's at a table. Food is piled before him—dishes he doesn't recognize, aromas he can't identify. He picks up a fork. He takes a bite.

Flavor explodes.

It's not just taste. It's memory. It's emotion. Every bite carries something—a childhood kitchen, a grandmother's hands, a moment of perfect contentment. He eats. He can't stop. Each dish is a revelation. Each flavor unlocks something new.

He's crying. He doesn't know when he started. Tears stream down his face as he eats. Not from sadness. Not from joy. From something in between. Something too big to name.

*This is what food is supposed to be.*

He thinks of his dinners—reheated pasta, cereal, sandwiches he doesn't taste. He thinks of his kitchen, his empty refrigerator, his meals eaten alone.

*This is what I've been missing.*

---

The experience continues.

He flies through impossible landscapes. He swims in oceans of liquid light. He runs through forests where every leaf sings. He climbs mountains that touch the stars.

Each moment is more intense than the last. Each sensation pushes him further. Higher. Deeper.

He doesn't want it to end.

But eventually, it does.

---

The headset comes off.

Leo sits on his couch. His apartment is dark. The clock reads 4:47 AM.

He's been in the experience for eight hours.

His body aches. His eyes are dry. His throat is raw—from screaming, from crying, from laughing. He doesn't remember laughing.

He sits in the darkness. He doesn't move. He doesn't think.

He just feels.

The memory of the experience is still there. The wind on the cliff. The music in the crowd. The taste of the feast. The feeling of flying. It's all there, vivid and real, like a second life he just lived.

*That was...*

He doesn't have words.

He stands. His legs are weak. He walks to the kitchen. He drinks a glass of water. It tastes like nothing. Like pipes. Like reality.

He looks at his apartment. The gray walls. The empty kitchen. The dishes in the sink.

It all feels smaller now. Dimmer. Like someone turned down the contrast on the real world.

He goes to bed. He lies in the dark. He closes his eyes.

He dreams of mountains and music and feasts. He dreams of flying. He dreams of feeling alive.

---

The next morning, he wakes up tired.

But it's a good tired. A satisfied tired. Like after a long journey or a hard workout.

He gets ready for work. He eats his cereal. It still tastes like cardboard. But he doesn't mind.

Because he knows what's waiting for him tonight.

The Apex Collection.

He can do it again. He can feel all of it again. The wind. The music. The food. The flying.

*This is what I've been looking for.*

He walks to work. The gray hallway doesn't bother him. The gray cubicle doesn't bother him. The gray spreadsheets don't bother him.

Because he has something now. Something real. Something that makes everything else bearable.

At lunch, Marcus appears at his cubicle.

"Hey. You look... different today. Good different."

"Do I?"

"Yeah. Like you actually slept. Or won the lottery. Or something."

Leo smiles. "Maybe I did."

Marcus laughs. "Seriously though. You want to grab lunch?"

"Sure."

They walk to the cafeteria. Leo eats a sandwich. It still doesn't taste like much. But he doesn't care.

Because tonight, he'll taste something real.

---

That evening, he's on the couch by 6:00 PM.

The device is in his hands. The headset is ready.

He hesitates for just a moment.

*Should I pace myself? Should I save it for the weekend?*

But the thought is gone as quickly as it came.

He slides on the headset.

The world shifts.

He's back on the cliff. The wind. The mountains. The sky on fire.

He takes a breath. The thin air burns his lungs.

*This.*

He smiles.

*This is everything.*
