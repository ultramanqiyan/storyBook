# Chapter 07: The Void

The third time, it doesn't work.

Leo sits in Kane's office. The headset is on his head. He's waiting for the feeling.

60 seconds pass.

Nothing.

He rips off the headset. "What happened? Why didn't it work?"

Kane's expression is grim. "Your neural pathways are adapting. Building tolerance. The same thing that happened with the regular content, the premium content, everything else. Your brain is learning to filter it out."

"No. No, that can't be right. You said this was the edge. You said there was nothing beyond it."

"There isn't. That's the problem." Kane sets the headset down. "You've reached the limit. And your brain is already moving past it."

Leo's hands are shaking. "What do I do? There has to be something."

Kane is silent for a long moment. "There's one option. But I have to warn you—it's not safe. It's not recommended. And I've never seen anyone try it without serious consequences."

"What is it?"

"Frequency reduction. Instead of waiting 72 hours between sessions, you wait less. 48 hours. 24 hours. The more often you stimulate your neural pathways, the more they stay open. But..." He pauses. "The more you do it, the faster you burn out. And the faster you lose the ability to feel anything at all."

Leo thinks about the nothing he just experienced. The 60 seconds of emptiness.

He thinks about his life. His job. His family. His apartment.

All of it already feels like nothing.

"How often can I do it?"

Kane shakes his head. "You're not hearing me. Every time you reduce the interval, you accelerate the burnout. You could go from feeling everything to feeling nothing in a matter of weeks. Days, even."

"I don't care."

Kane looks at him. "You should."

"I don't." Leo's voice is steady. Desperate. "I'd rather feel everything for a few more days than feel nothing for the rest of my life."

Kane is silent. Then he nods slowly.

"Come back in 48 hours."

Leo goes home.

He lies in bed. He stares at the ceiling.

He thinks about the feeling. The 60 seconds of everything. Joy. Peace. Connection. Meaning. Love.

He thinks about the nothing. The 60 seconds of emptiness.

He thinks about his life. His job. His family. His apartment.

All of it gray. Flat. Empty.

*48 hours,* he thinks. *I can wait 48 hours.*

He doesn't wait 48 hours.

He waits 36.

He goes back to Kane's office. He lies about the time since his last session.

"It's been 48 hours."

Kane studies him. "You're lying."

"I'm not."

"Your eyes. Your hands. You're shaking. You haven't slept. It's been less than 48 hours."

Leo doesn't deny it. "I need it. Please."

Kane sighs. He picks up the headset.

"60 seconds. And then you wait the full 72 hours. No exceptions. If you come back early again, I won't let you use it."

"Okay."

Kane places the headset on Leo's head.


The world disappears.

The feeling returns.

But it's different this time.

The joy is there, but it's muted. Distant. Like hearing music through a wall.

The peace is there, but it's shallow. Incomplete. Like a shadow of what it was.

The connection is there, but it's fragmented. Broken. Like trying to hold water in his hands.

The meaning is there, but it's hollow. Empty. Like a promise that was never kept.

The love is there, but it's cold. Distant. Like a memory of something that never existed.

60 seconds pass.

Leo rips off the headset.

"It wasn't the same. It wasn't as strong."

Kane nods. "I warned you. Your neural pathways are adapting. The tolerance is building."

"How do I stop it?"

"You don't. Not unless you stop using the Edge entirely. Let your brain recover. Give it months. Maybe years."

"I can't do that."

"Then you'll burn out. Faster than you think."


Leo goes home.

He sits on his couch. He stares at the wall.

He thinks about the feeling. The muted, distant version of what it used to be.

He thinks about the nothing. The emptiness that's waiting for him.

He thinks about his life. His job. His family. His apartment.

All of it gray. Flat. Empty.

He picks up his phone. He opens his messages.

*From: Kane*

*Remember: 72 hours. No exceptions.*

He closes the message.

He counts the hours.

72 hours.

He can wait.

He has to.


He waits 60 hours.

He can't wait any longer.

He goes back to Kane's office.


Kane refuses to let him use the headset.

"I told you. 72 hours. No exceptions."

"Please. I need it."

"You need to wait. Your neural pathways need time to recover."

"They're not recovering. They're dying. I can feel it. Every hour I wait, I lose a little more. The gray is spreading. The emptiness is growing. I need to feel something. Anything."

Kane shakes his head. "I can't help you. Not like this."

"Then who can? Who else has this technology? Who else can give me what I need?"

Kane is silent.

"Tell me. Please."

"There's no one else. I'm the only one with the Edge. And I'm telling you—it's not working anymore. Your tolerance is too high. Your pathways are too damaged."

Leo feels something break inside him. Something he didn't know was still there.

"What do I do?"

Kane looks at him. "You stop. You walk away. You let your brain heal. It won't be easy. It won't be fast. But it's the only way."

Leo thinks about stopping. About walking away. About living without the feeling.

He thinks about his life. His job. His family. His apartment.

All of it gray. Flat. Empty.

Without the feeling, what's left?

"I can't."

"Then you'll burn out. Completely. Permanently. You'll lose the ability to feel anything. Ever again."

Leo looks at Kane. At the headset on the desk. At the door behind him.

He stands up.

He walks to the door.

He opens it.

He turns back.

"Is there anything else? Anything at all? Even if it's dangerous. Even if it destroys me. I need to feel something. I can't live like this."

Kane is silent for a long moment. Then he reaches under his desk and pulls out a small case.

"There's one more thing. But I've never offered it before. And I'm only offering it now because you're going to find it somewhere else if I don't."

"What is it?"

"It's called the Abyss. It's not an experience. It's not content. It's... a reset. It wipes your neural pathways clean. Removes all tolerance. All adaptation. Returns you to the state you were in before you ever used any of this."

"That sounds... perfect."

"It's not. The process is traumatic. It's painful. And there's a risk—significant risk—that you'll lose more than just the tolerance. You might lose memories. Emotions. Parts of yourself that you can't get back."

Leo doesn't hesitate.

"I want to try it."


The Abyss is a chair.

It looks like a dentist's chair, but with wires and cables and sensors attached to every surface. There's a helmet connected to the back—a heavy, industrial-looking device with more wires than Leo can count.

"Lie down," Kane says.

Leo lies in the chair. It's cold. Uncomfortable. The sensors press against his arms, his legs, his chest.

"This will take about 30 minutes," Kane says. "It will be painful. More painful than anything you've experienced. But at the end, your neural pathways will be reset. You'll be able to feel again. Everything. Like the first time."

"Okay."

Kane places the helmet on Leo's head. It's heavy. Tight. The wires press against his scalp.

"Ready?"

"Yes."

Kane flips a switch.


The pain is immediate.

It's not physical—not exactly. It's deeper than that. It's as if every nerve in his body is being torn apart and rebuilt at the same time.

Leo screams.

He can't help it. The pain is too much. Too overwhelming.

He tries to move. He can't. The sensors hold him in place.

He tries to close his eyes. He can't. The helmet forces them open.

He tries to think. He can't. The pain consumes everything.

Time loses meaning. Seconds feel like hours. Hours feel like years.

He's dying. He must be dying. No one can survive this.

But he doesn't die.

The pain continues. And continues. And continues.

And then…

It stops.


Leo opens his eyes.

He's still in the chair. The helmet is still on his head. Kane is standing over him.

"How do you feel?"

Leo tries to answer. He can't. He doesn't know how he feels.

He tries to remember. He can't. His mind is... empty.

"Leo? Can you hear me?"

He nods.

"Do you know where you are?"

He looks around. The office. The desk. The shelves. The server.

"Kane's office."

"Good. Do you know who I am?"

"Kane."

"Good. Do you know who you are?"

Leo thinks about the question. Who is he?

He's Leo. He's... a data analyst. He works at... somewhere. He lives in... an apartment.

He has a family. A sister. Parents.

He thinks about them. He can see their faces. He can remember their names.

But he can't remember how he feels about them.

"I'm Leo."

Kane nods. "Good. The reset worked. Your neural pathways are clear. You should be able to feel again."

Leo sits up. The helmet is removed. The sensors are detached.

He stands. He walks to the door.

"Where are you going?"

"Home. I need to... rest."

"Leo. Wait." Kane's voice is urgent. "You need to give yourself time to recover. Your brain has been through a lot. Don't rush into anything. Don't use any content for at least a week. Maybe more."

Leo nods. "Okay."

He walks out the door.


He doesn't go home.

He goes to his apartment. He picks up his old headset—the one he bought from Helix. The one that started all of this.

He puts it on.

He starts the cliff experience.


The world shifts.

He's on the cliff. The wind tears at his clothes. The mountains rise in the distance. The sky is on fire.

He waits.

The feeling comes.

Not muted. Not distant. Not broken.

Real. Full. Complete.

Joy. Peace. Connection. Meaning. Love.

He feels it all. Like the first time. Like before the tolerance. Before the chase. Before everything.

He's crying. Laughing. Sobbing.

*It worked. The Abyss worked. I can feel again.*

He takes off the headset.

He looks at the clock.

He's been in the experience for 30 seconds.

He puts the headset back on.


Three hours later, he takes it off again.

The feeling is still there. Still strong. Still real.

But something is different.

He can't remember what he did yesterday. Or the day before. Or the week before that.

He tries to remember his sister's face. He can't.

He tries to remember his parents' voices. He can't.

He tries to remember his job. His apartment. His life.

It's all... fuzzy. Distant. Like a dream he can't quite recall.

He picks up his phone. He opens his contacts.

He sees names he doesn't recognize. Numbers he doesn't remember saving.

He scrolls through the list.

*Mom. Dad. Sarah (sister). Marcus (work).*

He knows these are important. He knows he should call them.

But he can't remember why.

He sets down the phone.

He picks up the headset.

He puts it on.


The next morning, he wakes up on the couch.

He doesn't remember falling asleep. He doesn't remember taking off the headset.

He looks around his apartment. It's familiar. But distant. Like he's seeing it for the first time.

He walks to the kitchen. He opens the refrigerator. It's empty.

He walks to the bedroom. He looks at the bed. The sheets are rumpled. The pillows are scattered.

He walks to the bathroom. He looks in the mirror.

The face looking back at him is familiar. But wrong. Older. More tired. The eyes are hollow. The skin is pale.

*Who is this?*

The thought comes unbidden. He pushes it away.

He's Leo. He knows that. He's a data analyst. He works somewhere. He lives here.

He has a family. A sister. Parents.

He thinks about them. He can see their faces. But he can't remember their names.

He picks up his phone. He opens his contacts.

*Mom. Dad. Sarah (sister).*

He calls the one labeled *Sarah (sister).*


"Leo? Oh my god, are you okay? I've been calling you for days. I was about to come over there."

"I'm... I'm fine."

"You don't sound fine. You sound... I don't know. Different. What's going on?"

"I don't... I can't remember..."

"Can't remember what?"

Leo tries to answer. He can't. He doesn't know what he can't remember.

"Leo. I'm coming over. Don't go anywhere. I'll be there in 20 minutes."

"Okay."

He hangs up.

He sits on the couch.

He waits.


Sarah arrives 18 minutes later.

She bursts through the door. Her eyes are red. She's been crying.

"Leo. What's going on? You look terrible. Have you been eating? Sleeping? What's happened to you?"

Leo looks at his sister. He knows she's his sister. He knows she cares about him.

But he can't remember why.

"I don't know."

"What do you mean you don't know? Leo, talk to me. Please."

He tries to explain. About the headset. About the chase. About the Edge. About the Abyss.

But the words won't come. He can't remember the details. He can't remember the sequence. He can't remember anything except the feeling.

"I just... I need to feel something."

Sarah looks at him. At the hollow eyes. At the pale skin. At the headset on the couch beside him.

She picks it up.

"Is this what's been happening? This thing?"

Leo nods.

"Get rid of it."

"What?"

"Get rid of it. Throw it away. I don't care what it costs. I don't care what you've invested. Get rid of it."

Leo looks at the headset. At the black visor. At the promise of feeling.

"I can't."

"Leo. Please. You're destroying yourself. Can't you see that?"

He looks at his sister. At the tears in her eyes. At the fear in her voice.

He knows she's right. He knows he should stop.

But he can't.

Not when the feeling is waiting. Not when the headset is right there. Not when the only thing that makes him feel alive is so close.

"I'm sorry."

Sarah stares at him. Then she nods slowly.

"I'm calling Mom and Dad. We're going to figure this out. Together."

She pulls out her phone. She starts dialing.

Leo watches her.

He thinks about the headset. About the feeling. About everything he's lost.

He thinks about what he's about to lose.

He picks up the headset.

He walks to his room.

He closes the door.

He puts it on.


The world shifts.

The feeling returns.

For a few precious seconds, he forgets everything.

The sister he can't remember. The parents he can't name. The life he can't recognize.

All of it disappears.

All that remains is the feeling.

Joy. Peace. Connection. Meaning. Love.

He holds onto it as long as he can.

But he knows, even as he feels it, that it won't last.

The tolerance is building. The adaptation is starting.

The void is coming.

And this time, there's nothing left to reset.
