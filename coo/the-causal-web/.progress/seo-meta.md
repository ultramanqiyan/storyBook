### 1.3 Book Description (SEO-optimized, 150-200 words)

Marcus Chen has spent his life studying patterns—how actions ripple through society, how choices propagate consequences. As a sociologist specializing in emergent social patterns, he understands causality better than most. But when he runs himself through the Causal Network, an AI system that quantifies human impact, he discovers something impossible: his causal weight is zero. His actions create no ripples.

Dr. Priya Sharma built the Causal Network to understand humanity. She believes everything can be calculated, predicted, understood. But Marcus is an anomaly she can't explain—a man whose causality points somewhere beyond her system.

As their intellectual connection deepens into something more, Marcus must confront a terrifying truth: his zero weight isn't an error. It's a glimpse of something beyond the network, a state of existence he experienced once before—when he died as a child on a frozen lake.

The Causal Web is a philosophical science fiction novel about free will, identity, and the courage to choose the unknown over the known. Perfect for readers who enjoyed Ted Chiang's "Story of Your Life" and Blake Crouch's "Dark Matter."
