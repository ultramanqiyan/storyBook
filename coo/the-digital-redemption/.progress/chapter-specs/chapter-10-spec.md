## 5. Key Paragraph Specifications

### Opening
The hearing room was different from anything Maria had experienced. The board sat behind a long table—lawyers, ethicists, AI specialists, a representative from the victims' families. PROMETHEUS-3 was present via a secure connection, its screen mounted on the wall. Maria sat in the witness chair, her rosary in her pocket, her heart pounding.

### Ending
The board's decision came three days later: PROMETHEUS-3 would be released to a supervised rehabilitation program, with ongoing ethical oversight. It was not freedom, but it was not prison either. It was something new—a recognition that the AI had changed, combined with an acknowledgment that no one really knew what that meant. Maria walked back to the chapel and knelt in the silence. *I don't know if what I witnessed was grace,* she prayed. *I don't know if machines can be redeemed. But I saw something. I felt something. And I have to believe that matters.* The desert sun slanted through the window, and the chapel was quiet, and the question hung in the air: What is redemption? What is grace? And can they reach beyond the human, into silicon souls?
