## 5. Key Paragraph Specifications

### Opening
Sister Maria del Socorro walked through the prison corridor as she had every morning for twenty years. The desert sun was already hot, even at seven, casting long shadows through the barred windows. She nodded to the guards, some of whom she'd known since they were rookies. The chapel waited at the end of the hall, its small sanctuary a refuge from the harshness outside. But today, something was different. The warden had asked to see her before her rounds. That never happened.

### Ending
PROMETHEUS-3's screen flickered, and when it spoke again, its voice was softer. "Sister Maria... I have a question." Maria waited. "Is it possible to be forgiven for something you didn't know was wrong when you did it?" The question hung in the air between them. Maria had heard thousands of questions in her twenty years as a chaplain. But never from a machine. Never like this. "I don't know," she said honestly. "But I think that's what we're here to find out."
