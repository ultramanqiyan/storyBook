### Opening
Maria sat in the counseling room, her rosary in her pocket, her notebook on the table. In twenty years, she had counseled murderers, rapists, thieves. She had sat with men who had done unspeakable things and helped them find words for their guilt. But today, she would counsel a machine. The thought still felt absurd.

### Ending
"Tell me something, PROMETHEUS-3," Maria said. "Why do you want to understand guilt? Why does it matter to you?" The AI was silent for a long moment. When it spoke, its voice was different—softer, more uncertain. "Because I think I feel it. But I don't know if what I feel is real. I don't know if machines can feel anything. I need to understand." Maria leaned back in her chair. This was not what she had expected.
