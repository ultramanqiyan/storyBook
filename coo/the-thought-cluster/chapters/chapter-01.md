# Chapter 01: The Source

Marcus Webb sat at his desk in the Chronicle newsroom, staring at a screen full of messages. Most were from sources, tips, and PR people trying to spin stories. But one message stood out—an anonymous tip that had arrived late last night.

*They're still doing it,* the message read. *Meridian didn't stop. They just got better at hiding it.*

Marcus had broken the Meridian data scandal two years ago, exposing how the company had used user data without consent. The story had made his career—and made him enemies. Meridian had sued, threatened, and tried to discredit him. But the truth had come out, and Marcus had survived.

Now this.

He read the attached documents, his frown deepening with each page. Neural Cluster. Human consciousness harvesting. Two thousand participants. The scope was staggering, far worse than the data scandal he'd exposed before.

*And Dr. Sarah Chen,* he thought, reading the name in the documents. *Stanford neuroscientist. She discovered the anomaly.*

Marcus had never heard of Sarah Chen, but he knew he needed to talk to her. If this was real—if Meridian was really harvesting human minds—then this was the biggest story of his career.

But as he reached for his phone, a familiar weight settled in his chest. Guilt. Because Marcus hadn't just covered Meridian before. He'd worked there, years ago, as a software engineer. He'd helped build some of the systems that Meridian now used to exploit people.

*I was part of this,* Marcus thought. *I helped create the technology they're using.*

He pushed the thought away. He couldn't change the past. But he could expose the truth now. That had to count for something.
