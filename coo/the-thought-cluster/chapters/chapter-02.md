# Chapter 02: The Interview

Marcus met Sarah Chen at a coffee shop near Stanford. She was younger than he'd expected, with sharp eyes and a nervous energy that suggested she hadn't been sleeping well.

"Dr. Chen," Marcus said, extending his hand. "Thank you for meeting with me."

"Mr. Webb," Sarah replied. "I've read your coverage of Meridian. You're the only journalist who's taken them on and won."

"That was different," Marcus said. "Data privacy is important, but this..." He shook his head. "This is something else entirely."

Sarah pulled out her tablet and showed him the evidence—neural scans, internal documents, participant testimonies. Marcus studied them, his journalist's instincts recognizing a bombshell when he saw one.

"This is incredible," he said. "And terrifying. How did you find all this?"

Sarah told him the story—the anomaly, the investigation, the breach, the confrontation with Harper. Marcus took notes, his mind racing with the implications.

"I need to verify this," Marcus said when she finished. "I need to talk to participants, see the neural data myself, get expert opinions. But if this checks out..."

"It will," Sarah said. "I've triple-checked everything."

Marcus nodded. "Then we're going to break this story. But we need to be careful. Meridian will come after us with everything they have."

"I know," Sarah said. "But I'm not backing down."

"Neither am I," Marcus said. And he meant it. This was why he'd become a journalist—to expose the truth, no matter the cost.
