# Chapter 8: The Confrontation

Sarah decided to gather the community. She organized a town hall meeting at the community center—"Truth in the Age of AI: A Community Conversation."

Forty people showed up. Martha Chen came, along with other residents who had stories the algorithm couldn't verify.

Sarah opened the meeting. "We're here because something is happening to truth. An algorithm is deciding what's real, and it's using criteria that don't account for our lives. Our stories. Our experiences."

A man stood up. "My father passed away last year. The algorithm flagged my obituary because I couldn't provide official death records in time. It called my father's life 'unverified.'"

A woman spoke next. "I run a small business. The algorithm flagged my ads because it couldn't verify my business license. But I've been here for twenty years."

The stories continued. Each one was different, but they all shared a common theme: the algorithm didn't understand their lives.

Martha Chen spoke last. "I'm seventy-four years old. I've lived in this town my whole life. I've seen things the algorithm can't see. And I'm here to tell you: my truth doesn't need a machine to validate it."

The room erupted in applause.
