# Chapter 9: The Resolution

The town hall meeting was covered by regional news. The story spread—small-town journalism versus big-tech algorithms, community truth versus digital verification.

The newspaper consortium reached out to Sarah. They wanted to discuss the situation.

" We've received feedback," the consortium's communications director said. "People are questioning our policies. We're willing to reconsider how we apply the AI verification system."

"What does that mean?"

"We're implementing a new category: 'VERIFIED BY COMMUNITY.' Stories that can't be verified through digital channels but have strong community testimony can be labeled differently. Not disputed. Not false. Just... different."

Sarah felt a small victory. "And my story?"

"We'll remove the 'LIKELY FALSE' label. Replace it with 'VERIFIED BY COMMUNITY.'"

She called Martha Chen with the news.

"They're changing the label," Sarah said. "They're adding a new category for stories like ours."

Martha was quiet for a moment. "So we won?"

"We changed something. Whether it's a win..." Sarah paused. "The algorithm still exists. It still uses the same criteria. We just added another option."

"But it's something."

"Yes," Sarah said. "It's something."

**END OF CHAPTER 9**
