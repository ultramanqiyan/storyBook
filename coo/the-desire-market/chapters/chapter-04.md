# Chapter 4: The Discovery

The files revealed everything.

I discovered the company's desire manipulation program—a secret initiative to not just predict desires, but to manufacture them. The program had started five years ago, and it was already everywhere. Billboards, social media, television advertisements—everything was designed to shape what people wanted.

The company wasn't just observing desire. It was creating it.

I learned that my entire life had been curated. The job at the Market, the apartment, the relationships—all recommended, all designed to shape me into the perfect employee. I was a test case, a proof of concept. If they could manufacture my desires, they could manufacture anyone's.

The betrayal hit hard. I'd given the company my best years, my most creative energy. I'd believed in the mission—understanding desire, optimizing satisfaction. Instead, I'd been a puppet.

I quit that day. Walked out of the office without telling anyone. The screens that had once seemed so informative now looked sinister. The data that had seemed so neutral was a weapon.

I spent the night wandering the city, trying to understand what I was supposed to do with this information. Expose the company? Warn others? Fight back somehow?

The questions had no answers.
