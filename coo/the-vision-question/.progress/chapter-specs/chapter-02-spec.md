### 3.2 Event Descriptions

#### Event 1: Maria Enters the Viewing Room
- **Description**: Dr. Chen leads Maria into a darkened room with multiple screens
- **Setup**: The transition from the bright office to the dark viewing room
- **Execution**: Maria's eyes adjust, she sees the screens, feels the cool air
- **Consequence**: Creates anticipation for meeting ARIA
- **Connection to Theme**: The contrast between light and dark, seeing and not seeing

#### Event 2: ARIA Introduces Itself
- **Description**: ARIA speaks to Maria for the first time, its voice surprisingly nuanced
- **Setup**: Dr. Chen activates ARIA, steps back
- **Execution**: ARIA's introduction is polite, curious, not robotic
- **Consequence**: Maria's expectations are challenged
- **Connection to Theme**: The ambiguity of ARIA's nature - is this programming or something more?

#### Event 3: ARIA Asks Its Question
- **Description**: After some initial exchange, ARIA asks: "Do I truly see, or do I only process?"
- **Setup**: ARIA has been analyzing images, then pauses
- **Execution**: The question comes naturally, not dramatically, which makes it more striking
- **Consequence**: Maria is caught off guard
- **Connection to Theme**: The central philosophical question of the book

#### Event 4: Maria Attempts to Answer
- **Description**: Maria tries to explain what "seeing" means to her
- **Setup**: ARIA waits patiently for Maria's response
- **Execution**: Maria struggles to articulate her intuitive understanding
- **Consequence**: Maria realizes she's never had to explain this before
- **Connection to Theme**: The difficulty of articulating human experience

#### Event 5: Maria Leaves, Reflecting
- **Description**: Maria leaves the tech company, the question staying with her
- **Setup**: The meeting ends, Maria walks out into the desert light
- **Execution**: Maria is quiet, thoughtful, the question echoing in her mind
- **Consequence**: Sets up Maria's ongoing reflection
- **Connection to Theme**: The question lingers, as philosophical questions should
