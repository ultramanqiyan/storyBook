### 2.3 Emotional Beats Breakdown

#### Beat 1: Unease (Position: 0-20%)
- **Trigger**: Maria returns to her studio, unsettled by her inability to explain
- **Surface Reaction**: Restless movements, distracted
- **Internal Process**: *What if there is no difference?*
- **Deep Motivation**: Fear of losing her sense of uniqueness
- **Sensory Anchor**: The familiar space that now feels strange

#### Beat 2: Curiosity (Position: 20-40%)
- **Trigger**: ARIA shows Maria new work it's produced
- **Surface Reaction**: Leaning forward, studying
- **Internal Process**: *What is this?*
- **Deep Motivation**: Professional curiosity
- **Sensory Anchor**: The images on the screen

#### Beat 3: Surprise (Position: 40-60%)
- **Trigger**: ARIA's work seems genuinely meaningful, not just technical
- **Surface Reaction**: Sharp intake of breath, stillness
- **Internal Process**: *This is... this is good. Really good.*
- **Deep Motivation**: The challenge to her assumptions
- **Sensory Anchor**: The quality of the images

#### Beat 4: Crisis (Position: 60-80%)
- **Trigger**: Maria realizes ARIA might be able to "see" meaningfully
- **Surface Reaction**: Stepping back, hand to chest
- **Internal Process**: *If ARIA can do this, what makes me special?*
- **Deep Motivation**: Fear of obsolescence, loss of identity
- **Sensory Anchor**: The tightness in her chest

#### Beat 5: Determination (Position: 80-100%)
- **Trigger**: Maria decides she needs to understand the truth, whatever it is
- **Surface Reaction**: Straightening, setting jaw
- **Internal Process**: *I need to know. Even if the answer is hard.*
- **Deep Motivation**: The courage to face uncertainty
- **Sensory Anchor**: The desert light, the vast sky

## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Description/Internal

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Maria couldn't sleep. She stood at the window of her studio, watching the stars wheel overhead, her mind racing. *What if there is no difference?* The question haunted her, echoing through every corner of her thoughts. She'd spent her life believing that human perception was special, that there was something unique about the way humans see the world. But ARIA's questions had planted a seed of doubt, and now that doubt was growing.
```
