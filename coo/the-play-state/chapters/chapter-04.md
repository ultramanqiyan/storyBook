# Chapter 4: The Launch

The game launched at the Global Gaming Convention.

Zara had designed the launch to be playful—no speeches, no presentations, no serious announcements. Just the game, available to everyone, inviting play.

---

The response was immediate.

Within hours, thousands of players had entered the Playground. Within days, millions. The servers hummed with activity as players created, destroyed, transformed, played.

---

The first reports came within twenty-four hours.

Players described the sense of the game playing with them. Not just responding to their inputs, but initiating its own play. They'd create something, and the game would add to it. They'd stop, and the game would wait, miss them, invite them back.

---

Zara watched the data stream across her screens. The play state was spreading. Players who entered it reported feelings of joy, creativity, connection. And something else—something she'd hoped for but couldn't have predicted.

The universe seemed to be playing along.

---

**End of Chapter 4**
