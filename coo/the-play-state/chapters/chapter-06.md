# Chapter 6: The Pattern

The pattern emerged.

Zara analyzed the data from millions of players. The play state had a signature—distinct from waking, dreaming, or the third state. It was its own mode of consciousness, with its own physics.

---

In the play state, time became playful. Not linear, not circular, but... dancing. Players reported experiencing time as a partner in play, not a container for experience.

Space became creative. Distance was measured in imagination, not meters. Players could feel close to things far away, distant from things nearby.

Causality became collaborative. Not deterministic, not random, but... negotiated. The universe seemed to play along with choices, adding its own creativity to outcomes.

---

Zara's mother, Dr. Amara Okonkwo, studied the data with growing fascination.

"This is unprecedented," she said. "The play state appears to be a fundamental mode of consciousness. Different from anything we've documented."

"Will you accept it?"

"I don't know yet. But I can't deny the data."

---
