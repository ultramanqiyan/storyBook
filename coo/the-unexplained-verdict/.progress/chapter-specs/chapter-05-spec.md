### 2.3 Emotional Beats Breakdown

#### Beat 1: Morning Routine (Position: 0-25%)
- **Trigger**: Beginning of workday, familiar patterns
- **Surface Reaction**: Efficient movements, automatic signing
- **Internal Process**: *Another day. Another queue. Another set of signatures.*
- **Deep Motivation**: The seduction of efficiency, the avoidance of discomfort
- **Sensory Anchor**: The rhythm of signing, the absence of thought

#### Beat 2: Signing Without Reading (Position: 25-50%)
- **Trigger**: Realizing she signed a claim without reading it
- **Surface Reaction**: Pauses briefly, then continues
- **Internal Process**: *Did I just...? Does it matter? The AI is always right.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Awareness of dependency, suppression of concern
- **Sensory Anchor**: The pen in her hand, moving without her full attention

#### Beat 3: Self-Observation (Position: 50-75%)
- **Trigger**: Deliberately observing her own behavior
- **Surface Reaction**: Watches herself sign another claim
- **Internal Process**: *I'm a rubber stamp. I'm just a signature.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Horror at what she's become, but also acceptance
- **Sensory Anchor**: The hollow feeling in her chest

#### Beat 4: Suppression (Position: 75-100%)
- **Trigger**: Pushing away the uncomfortable observation
- **Surface Reaction**: Returns to work with renewed focus
- **Internal Process**: *The system works. Marcus is happy. I'm efficient.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Fear of what change would mean, comfort in the known
- **Sensory Anchor**: The deliberate return to the screen

## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Action with character state

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Jennifer signed her name forty-three times that morning. She knew this because the ClaimSmart dashboard counted them for her - a small number in the corner of her screen, ticking up with each approval, each denial, each decision that bore her signature but not her judgment. She didn't read a single claim. The AI's verdicts appeared, she clicked approve or deny, and her signature materialized on the document like a rubber stamp pressed to paper. This was efficiency. This was what Marcus wanted. This was what she had become.
```

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Type**: Question with imagery

She gathered her things, the familiar routine of leaving, but something felt different. The question from David Chen echoed in her mind: "You signed your name to this. Tomorrow there would be more claims, more signatures, more decisions she couldn't explain. The system worked. That was what mattered.
