## 1. Chapter Positioning

### 1.1 Position in Book
- **Overall Location**: Climax (first half)
- **Narrative Function**: AI makes an error Jennifer would have caught - trust cracks
- **Relationship to Previous Chapter**: Continues from peak dependency
- **Relationship to Next Chapter**: Sets up Jennifer's attempt to explain and her failure

### 1.2 Chapter Purpose
**Primary Objective**: Show the AI making an error that Jennifer's trained instincts would have caught

**Secondary Objectives**:
- Demonstrate that AI is not infallible
- Show Jennifer's panic when she realizes the error
- Reveal her inability to explain why it's wrong
- Plant the seed of doubt about her role

### 1.3 Key Questions Answered/Raised
- **Answered**: Is the AI always right? What happens when it's wrong?
- **Raised**: If Jennifer can't explain why it's wrong, can she fix it? What is her value now?

## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Type**: Action with character state

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
The morning passed in a blur of signatures. Jennifer's pen moved across approvals and denials with mechanical precision, her mind elsewhere, her body performing the routine it had learned. The ClaimSmart dashboard counted: 12, 13, 14... Each number represented a decision she hadn't made, a judgment she couldn't explain, a signature that bore her name but not her thought. She'd stopped feeling the weight of them months ago.
```

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Type**: Question with imagery

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
She could report it. She could flag it for review. But without an explanation, without a reason, what could she say? "I feel like this is wrong"? That wasn't how the system worked. That wasn't how she worked anymore. The Martinez claim glowed on her screen, approved, ready to pay out thousands to a fraudster she couldn't prove was fraudulent. Her stomach was tight now - finally, after months of silence - but that wasn't enough. Feeling wasn't explaining. And she had forgotten how to explain.
```
