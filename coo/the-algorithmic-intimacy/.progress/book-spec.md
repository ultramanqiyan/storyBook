## 2. Narrative Style

### 2.1 Perspective
- **Point of View**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: Sarah Mitchell
- **POV Consistency**: Single

### 2.2 Narrative Structure
- **Dual Structure**: Yes
- **Structure Type**: Reality line (present advisory work) + Inner line (care and connection)
- **Interweaving Method**: Client interactions trigger reflection on care; AI outputs contrast with human connection

### 2.3 Language Style
- **Overall Tone**: Contemporary, warm prose with philosophical undercurrents; professional but increasingly hollow
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length sentences with occasional fragments for emotional impact; rhythmic variation between professional warmth and human distance
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - financial terminology mixed with everyday language
- **Unique Stylistic Elements**: 
  - Contrast between AI's perfect memory and Sarah's fading personal knowledge
  - Physical sensations of connection (handshakes, eye contact) that become mediated by AI
  - Boston professional atmosphere

---

## 3. Emotional Tone (Quantified)

### 3.1 Overall Atmosphere
**Primary Emotions**: Professional warmth eroding into hollowness, grief for lost connections, questioning what care means

### 3.2 Emotional Arc
```
Chapter Distribution:
Chapters 1-3: Warm professional using AI as tool; subtle signs of distance
Chapters 4-6: Growing dependency; AI remembers better than she does; unease
Chapters 7-8: Crisis - client dies and she doesn't remember their face; grief and shame
Chapters 9-10: Philosophical confrontation; no easy resolution; questioning
```

## 5. Thematic Depth

### 5.1 Core Themes
1. **Care and AI**: Is "care" essentially remembering and responding?
2. **Trust and Relationship**: What makes trust "human"?
3. **The Meaning of Connection**: Can AI mediate genuine relationships?

### 5.2 Philosophical Exploration
| Question | Exploration Approach |
|----------|---------------------|
| Is care essentially remember + respond? | WealthGuide remembers everything but doesn't care; Sarah cares but forgets |
| What makes trust human? | Clients trust Sarah, but they're responding to AI-generated care |
| Can AI mediate genuine relationships? | Sarah's relationships become hollow even as they appear successful |

## 7. Sensory Detail Requirements (Quantified)

### 7.1 Required Sensory Types
Each chapter MUST include sensory details from at least **3 different categories**:

| Sense | Specific Types | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Visual** | Client faces, portfolio screens, Boston skyline | "The client's face lit up - responding to AI-generated birthday wish" |
| **Auditory** | Client voices, phone calls, silence after notification | "Her voice was warm, thanking me for remembering - but I hadn't" |
| **Tactile** | Handshakes, pen, paper files (fading) | "The handshake was firm, but she was responding to AI's timing" |
| **Internal** | Connection feeling (fading), warmth (fading) | "She used to feel clients' lives. Now she felt their data." |
| **Olfactory** | Office coffee, Boston harbor air | "The harbor wind carried that particular Boston smell" |

### 7.2 Quantified Standards
- **Minimum per chapter**: 5 sensory details
- **Minimum categories per chapter**: 3
- **Distribution target**: Visual (40%), Others (60% combined)

## 11. Open Ending Design (Chapter 10)

### 11.1 Unresolved Questions
1. Can Sarah reclaim genuine care and connection?
2. Is "care" possible when AI mediates all relationships?
3. What does it mean to "know" someone?

### 11.2 New Suspense
- Sarah's unprepared visit may or may not go well
- The broader question: is human care valuable if AI care is more consistent?
- What will happen to her practice if she rejects AI?

### 11.3 Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: Sarah has made one unprepared client visit, but the future is uncertain
- **Key Relationship**: James is supportive but the tension remains
