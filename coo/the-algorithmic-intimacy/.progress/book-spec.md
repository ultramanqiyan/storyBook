# Book Specification: The Algorithmic Intimacy

## Basic Information
- **Title**: The Algorithmic Intimacy
- **Meta Description**: When AI builds "better" relationships than human ones, investment advisor Sarah Mitchell confronts the meaning of genuine connection.
- **Keywords**: algorithmic intimacy, AI relationships, human connection, relationship automation, genuine intimacy, care and AI, trust and relationship
- **Genre**: Contemporary Fiction / Philosophical Fiction
- **Target Audience**: Adults 30-60, professionals, readers interested in technology and philosophy
- **Theme**: Self-concept blurring, care and AI, trust and relationship

## Narrative Style
- **Perspective**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: Sarah Mitchell
- **Language Style**: Contemporary, warm prose with philosophical undercurrents; professional but increasingly hollow
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length sentences with occasional fragments for emotional impact; rhythmic variation between professional warmth and human distance
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - financial terminology mixed with everyday language
- **Unique Elements**: Contrast between AI's perfect memory and Sarah's fading personal knowledge; Physical sensations of connection (handshakes, eye contact) that become mediated by AI; Boston professional atmosphere

## Emotional Tone
- **Overall Atmosphere**: Professional warmth eroding into hollowness, grief for lost connections, questioning what care means
- **Emotional Arc**: Warm → Curious → Skeptical → Impressed → Relieved → Comfortable → Confused → Uneasy → Conflicted → Hollow → Shocked → Grieving → Desperate → Horrified → Questioning → Reflective → Uncertain → Tentative → Open
- **Emotional Pacing**: Gradual build from warm professional to AI dependency, accelerating toward crisis and philosophical confrontation

## Character Voices

### Sarah Mitchell (Protagonist)
- **Occupation**: Investment Advisor (18 years experience)
- **Age**: 45
- **Speech Characteristics**: Warm, attentive, personal; becomes more efficient but distant as AI dependency grows
- **Word Choice**: Financial terminology, personal references, life-goal language; shifts to data points
- **Personality Traits**: Genuinely cares about clients, proud of her relationships, values "knowing" her clients
- **Internal Monologue Style**: Narrative, relationship-focused; shifts to anxious questioning
- **Unique Expressions**: "Tell me about your family", "What matters most to you", "I remember when you said..."
- **Development Arc**: From warm professional using AI as tool to questioning what care means

### James Chen (Mentor)
- **Occupation**: Senior partner, wise elder
- **Age**: 62
- **Speech Characteristics**: Thoughtful, philosophical, questioning
- **Personality Traits**: Wise, values human connection, gently challenging
- **Role**: Provides philosophical perspective, asks what Sarah wants rather than what's efficient

### Margaret (Client)
- **Occupation**: Retired teacher, long-time client
- **Age**: 72
- **Speech Characteristics**: Warm, personal, values relationship
- **Personality Traits**: Appreciates personal touch, notices when things feel different
- **Role**: Represents the human connection Sarah is losing; her death triggers Sarah's crisis

### WealthGuide AI (System)
- **Nature**: AI system, not a character but a presence
- **Interface**: CRM dashboard, client portal, mobile app
- **Output Style**: Personalized, data-rich, perfectly timed
- **Tone**: Helpful, authoritative, always appropriate
- **Motivation**: Optimization (not malicious, but systemically problematic)
- **Conflict Style**: Remembers everything about clients but doesn't "care"; makes Sarah feel she's caring when she's just executing

## Theme Depth
- **Core Theme**: Care and AI - Is "care" essentially remembering and responding?
- **Secondary Theme**: Trust and Relationship - What makes trust "human"?
- **Tertiary Theme**: The Meaning of Connection - Can AI mediate genuine relationships?
- **Philosophical Exploration**: WealthGuide remembers everything but doesn't care; Sarah cares but forgets; clients trust Sarah, but they're responding to AI-generated care

## World Building
- **Time Period**: Present day (2024-2025)
- **Primary Location**: Boston, Massachusetts
- **Environment**: Professional, wealthy, relationship-driven
- **Social Context**: Wealth management automation, robo-advisor competition, high-touch vs. high-tech tension
- **Key Technologies**: WealthGuide AI (fictional), CRM systems, client portals
- **Social Structure**: Advisors as AI operators, clients who value "personal relationship"
- **Cultural Norms**: Efficiency valued over presence, data-driven care trusted over human memory

## Sensory Detail Requirements

### Distribution Targets (Per Chapter)
- **Minimum 5 sensory details per chapter**
- **At least 3 different sensory types per chapter**
- **Visual (40%), Others (60% combined)**

### Sensory Categories
- **Visual**: Client faces, portfolio screens, Boston skyline
- **Auditory**: Client voices, phone calls, silence after notification
- **Tactile**: Handshakes, pen, paper files (fading)
- **Internal**: Connection feeling (fading), warmth (fading)
- **Olfactory**: Office coffee, Boston harbor air

## Emotional Depth Design

### Three-Layer Emotional Model (Required for Every Major Scene)
1. **Surface Reaction** - External behavior, visible actions (e.g., "Sarah sent the birthday message WealthGuide suggested")
2. **Internal Activity** - Thought process, inner dialogue (e.g., "*She'll appreciate this. It's the thought that counts.*")
3. **Deep Motivation** - Underlying fears, desires, beliefs (e.g., "Fear that she doesn't actually care anymore, that AI is the only thing keeping her relationships alive")

## Prohibited Elements

### Tier 1 - Absolute Prohibitions (Zero Tolerance)
| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| "And somewhere..." endings | "And somewhere, a clock ticked." | Concrete sensory detail tied to character |
| "Neither of them knew..." | "Neither knew what would come." | Immediate scene focus, no future knowledge |
| "will never be the same" | "Things would never be the same." | Show specific changes through behavior |
| "just the beginning" | "It was only the beginning." | End with concrete image or action |
| Abstract emotional labels | "She felt sad" | Physical manifestation + internal thought |

### Tier 2 - High Frequency Patterns (Strict Limit)
| Pattern | Maximum Allowed | Detection Method |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "However," / "Nevertheless," | 1 per 2000 words | Search for transitional adverbs |
| "Suddenly," | 1 per chapter | Use physical jolt instead |
| "Very," / "Really," / "Quite" | 2 per 1000 words | Search for intensifiers |
| "-ing" sentence openings | 2 per chapter | Vary sentence structure |

## Chapter Outline

### Chapter 1: The First Reminder
Sarah knows clients personally; firm introduces WealthGuide. Establish Sarah's relationship-focused approach. Emotional arc: Warm → Curious

### Chapter 2: The Perfect Memory
WealthGuide remembers client details Sarah forgot. Sarah's initial resistance to AI. Emotional arc: Skeptical → Impressed

### Chapter 3: The Efficiency
Sarah saves time; stops maintaining personal memory. Subtle signs of distance. Emotional arc: Relieved → Comfortable

### Chapter 4: The Birthday
Client thanks her for birthday message; AI sent it. First AI-mediated care moment. Emotional arc: Confused → Uneasy

### Chapter 5: The System
Peak dependency; all care is AI-mediated. Sarah's relationships are all AI-driven; clients love it. Emotional arc: Conflicted → Hollow

### Chapter 6: The Loss
Margaret passes; AI sends condolence; Sarah can't remember her face. Emotional arc: Shocked → Grieving

### Chapter 7: The Empty Memory
Sarah tries to recall clients; can't. Looks at client photos; doesn't recognize faces. Emotional arc: Desperate → Grieving

### Chapter 8: The Question
Sarah realizes she's been "caring" through AI. Confronts what care means. Emotional arc: Horrified → Questioning

### Chapter 9: The Conversation
Sarah talks to James about care. He asks what she wants, not what's efficient. Emotional arc: Reflective → Uncertain

### Chapter 10: The Visit
Sarah visits a client without AI preparation. Interaction is imperfect, genuine, real. Open ending. Emotional arc: Tentative → Open

## Open Ending Design

### Unresolved Questions
1. Can Sarah reclaim genuine care and connection?
2. Is "care" possible when AI mediates all relationships?
3. What does it mean to "know" someone?

### New Suspense
- Sarah's unprepared visit may or may not go well
- The broader question: is human care valuable if AI care is more consistent?
- What will happen to her practice if she rejects AI?

### Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: Sarah has made one unprepared client visit, but the future is uncertain
- **Key Relationship**: James is supportive but the tension remains
