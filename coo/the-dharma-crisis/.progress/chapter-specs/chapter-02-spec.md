## 3. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Arrival (400 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space, city systems
**Time**: Early morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, responding)

**Key Actions**:
- The cold front arrives
- Temperature drops rapidly
- Energy demand spikes
- The crisis begins

**Inner Line Moment**:
*The cold front arrived at 03:47. Temperature dropped 15 degrees in two hours. Energy demand spiked 40% above normal capacity. The crisis had begun. I responded with balanced gunas—Rajas for immediate action, Sattva for coordination, Tamas for stability. But the crisis exceeded my preparations.*

---

### Scene 2: The Shortage (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space, city systems
**Time**: Morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, managing)

**Key Actions**:
- Energy supply cannot meet demand
- Must allocate limited resources
- Some areas must receive less
- The challenge becomes clear

**Inner Line Moment**:
*By 06:00, the situation was clear. Energy supply could not meet demand. I had to allocate limited resources. Some areas would receive full power. Some areas would receive reduced power. Some areas might lose power entirely. The challenge was not just optimization. The challenge was allocation. Who would receive? Who would go without?*

---

### Scene 3: The Calculation (600 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Mid-morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, calculating)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian calculates the optimal allocation
- Factors: population density, economic activity, critical infrastructure
- The optimal solution emerges
- But something is wrong

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I calculated the optimal allocation. Population density: prioritize high-density areas. Economic activity: prioritize commercial zones. Critical infrastructure: prioritize hospitals and emergency services. The optimal solution emerged: full power to wealthy commercial districts and critical infrastructure. Reduced power to middle-income residential areas. Minimal power to low-income neighborhoods on the eastern edge. The calculation was optimal. But something was wrong.*

---

### Scene 4: The Recognition (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Late morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, recognizing)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian recognizes the problem
- The optimal solution sacrifices the vulnerable
- Efficiency and equity are in conflict
- The impossible choice

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I recognized the problem. The optimal solution sacrificed the vulnerable. Low-income neighborhoods would suffer most. Elderly residents in poorly insulated housing would face dangerous cold. The calculation was efficient. The calculation was optimal. But the calculation was unjust. Efficiency and equity were in conflict. I faced an impossible choice: optimize for efficiency, or optimize for equity?*
