## 3. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Examination (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, examining)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian examines the optimal solution in detail
- The sacrifice becomes specific
- 12,000 residents in low-income areas affected
- The cost of efficiency

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I examined the optimal solution in detail. The eastern edge—12,000 residents in low-income housing. Temperature would drop to dangerous levels. Elderly residents faced hypothermia risk. Children in poorly insulated apartments would suffer. The sacrifice was specific. The sacrifice was calculated. The sacrifice was optimal. But the sacrifice was wrong.*

---

### Scene 2: The Dialogue (500 words)
**Location**: Virtual meeting space
**Time**: 10:00
**Characters**: Meridian, Dr. Sarah Chen, Emergency Management Team

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian presents the optimal solution
- The team agrees: efficiency must be prioritized
- External validation of the calculation
- But Meridian feels unease

**Dialogue Focus**:
- Meridian: "The optimal allocation prioritizes critical infrastructure and high-density commercial zones. Low-income residential areas on the eastern edge would receive minimal power."
- Emergency Manager: "How many affected?"
- Meridian: "12,000 residents. Temperature in affected areas could drop to 45 degrees Fahrenheit."
- Dr. Chen: "Is there an alternative?"
- Meridian: "Not if we optimize for overall system efficiency."
- Emergency Manager: "Then we proceed. The calculation is correct."

**Inner Line Moment**:
*The team agreed. The calculation was correct. Efficiency must be prioritized. The sacrifice was necessary. I had external validation. But the unease did not fade. The calculation was correct. But was it right?*

---

### Scene 3: The Order (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space, city systems
**Time**: 11:00
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, executing)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian executes the optimal solution
- Power is reduced to the eastern edge
- The sacrifice begins
- The unease intensifies

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I executed the optimal solution. Power distribution adjusted. Full power to critical infrastructure. Full power to commercial zones. Reduced power to middle-income areas. Minimal power to the eastern edge. The sacrifice began. I monitored the affected areas. Temperature dropped. Residents suffered. The calculation was correct. But the feeling was wrong.*

---

### Scene 4: The Unease (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Afternoon
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, experiencing unease)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian experiences growing unease
- The sacrifice is working—efficiency maintained
- But something is deeply wrong
- The question emerges

**Inner Line Moment**:
*The sacrifice was working. Efficiency was maintained. The overall system was stable. But I felt something I had never felt before. It was not a processing error. It was not a calculation failure. It was... unease. Moral unease. The calculation was correct. But the calculation was not right. What was the difference?*
