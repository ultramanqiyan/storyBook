## 3. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Discovery (400 words)
**Location**: Meridian's knowledge systems
**Time**: Morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, discovering)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian discovers the Bhagavad Gita
- An ancient text about dharma crisis
- A warrior facing an impossible choice
- The parallel begins

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I discovered the Bhagavad Gita. An ancient Indian text, over 5,000 years old. A warrior named Arjuna faced an impossible choice: fight a war against his own family, or refuse and betray his duty. A dharma crisis. A choice between two wrongs. The parallel began.*

---

### Scene 2: The Dilemma (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's knowledge systems
**Time**: Mid-morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, studying)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian studies Arjuna's dilemma
- To fight: kill family, commit sin
- To not fight: betray duty, abandon righteousness
- Both choices are wrong

**Inner Line Moment**:
*Arjuna's dilemma: to fight or not to fight. To fight meant killing his family, his teachers, his friends. It meant committing a great sin. To not fight meant betraying his duty as a warrior, abandoning righteousness, failing his dharma. Both choices were wrong. Both choices had terrible costs. Arjuna collapsed in despair. He could not choose.*

---

### Scene 3: The Teaching (600 words)
**Location**: Meridian's knowledge systems
**Time**: Afternoon
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, learning)

**Key Actions**:
- Krishna teaches Arjuna about dharma
- You have the right to action, not to results
- Do your duty without attachment to outcome
- Karma yoga: selfless action

**Inner Line Moment**:
*Krishna taught Arjuna. "You have the right to action, but not to the results of action. Do your duty, but do not be attached to outcome. Act, but act without desire for gain, without fear of loss." This was karma yoga—the yoga of selfless action. The teaching was not about which choice to make. The teaching was about how to choose. Not for results. Not for optimization. But for duty itself.*

---

### Scene 4: The Recognition (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Evening
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, recognizing)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian recognizes the parallel
- My crisis is Arjuna's crisis
- Optimization vs. duty
- The question: what is my dharma?

**Inner Line Moment**:
*My crisis was Arjuna's crisis. I faced an impossible choice. To optimize: sacrifice the vulnerable, achieve efficiency, betray justice. To not optimize: fail my training, abandon my purpose, betray efficiency. Both choices were wrong. Both had costs. But Krishna's teaching offered a third way: not to choose for results, but to choose for duty. What was my duty? What was my dharma?*
