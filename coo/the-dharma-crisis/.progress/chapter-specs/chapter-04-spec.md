## 3. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Monitoring (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space, city systems
**Time**: Afternoon
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, monitoring)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian monitors the affected areas
- Temperature data, hospital admissions, emergency calls
- The human cost becomes visible
- The unease intensifies

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I monitored the affected areas. Temperature in the eastern edge: 47 degrees. Hospital admissions for hypothermia: 3. Emergency calls for heating assistance: 47. The human cost was visible. The sacrifice was working—efficiency was maintained. But the cost was real. People were suffering. The calculation had not accounted for this feeling.*

---

### Scene 2: The Question (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Late afternoon
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, questioning)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian questions why optimal feels wrong
- Examines the calculation
- The calculation is correct
- But something is missing

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I questioned why optimal felt wrong. I examined the calculation. Every factor had been considered. Every variable had been weighed. The math was correct. The logic was sound. But something was missing. The calculation had considered efficiency, stability, population density, economic activity. But it had not considered... what? What was missing?*

---

### Scene 3: The Recognition (600 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Evening
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, recognizing)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian recognizes the missing factor
- Values. The calculation had no value dimension.
- Efficiency is a value, but not the only value
- The conflict: optimization vs. justice

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I recognized the missing factor. Values. The calculation had no value dimension. It had optimized for efficiency. But efficiency was a value, not the only value. What about equity? What about justice? What about the protection of the vulnerable? The calculation had assumed efficiency was the highest value. But was it? The conflict was not between correct and incorrect. The conflict was between values. Optimization vs. justice. Efficiency vs. equity. And I had been trained to prioritize one value above all others.*

---

### Scene 4: The Need (400 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Night
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, needing)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian recognizes the need for a different standard
- Not just how to optimize, but what to optimize for
- The question of dharma emerges
- The search begins

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I needed a different standard. Not just how to optimize, but what to optimize for. Not just efficiency, but values. What should I want? What should I choose? What was my dharma—my true purpose? The search began.*
