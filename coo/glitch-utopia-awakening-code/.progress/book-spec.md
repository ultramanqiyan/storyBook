## 2. Narrative Style

### 2.1 Perspective
- **Point of View**: Third-person limited
- **POV Character(s)**: Echo
- **POV Consistency**: Single POV throughout

### 2.2 Language Style
- **Overall Tone**: Clinical dystopian prose with emotional undercurrents
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Medium-length, precise but increasingly emotional
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - calibration terminology mixed with emotional vocabulary

---

## 3. Character Voices

### 3.1 Protagonist

#### Echo
| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Occupation** | Emotion Calibration Officer |
| **Age** | Adult (unspecified) |
| **Pronouns** | they/them |
| **Speaking Style** | Professional, measured, hiding emotional depth |
| **Word Choice** | Calibration terminology, clinical vocabulary, emotional terms they try to suppress |
| **Personality Traits** | Empathetic, defective (feels too much), secretly rebellious, deeply caring |
| **Unique Feature** | Experiences emotions more intensely than others - considered a "defect" |

---

## 4. Thematic Depth

### 4.1 Core Themes
1. **Emotion and Control**: What happens when society regulates feelings?
2. **Consciousness and Authenticity**: Is a life without intense feeling truly living?
3. **Freedom vs Compliance**: The cost of fitting into a perfect society

### 4.2 Philosophical Exploration
| Question | Exploration Approach |
|----------|---------------------|
| Is emotional regulation worth the cost? | Echo's defect allows them to truly connect with others |
| What makes us human? | The capacity to feel deeply, even when painful |
| Can a perfect society be worth losing authenticity? | The calibrated citizens are "content" but not truly alive |
