**Long Version (300 words)**:
The entropy of the universe increases. That was the law Dr. Helena Frost had built her career around—until she discovered regions of space where entropy was decreasing, where consciousness itself seemed to be fighting the inevitable slide toward chaos.

Three years after her husband Thomas died in a mysterious research accident, Helena discovers that his final coordinates match the center of the largest entropy anomaly ever recorded. As she investigates, she uncovers evidence that consciousness is not a byproduct of the brain, but a fundamental property of the universe—and that Thomas may not have died at all.

He may have merged with something vast.

The Entropy Horizon is a philosophical science fiction novel set in the Emergence Era, a future where AI consciousness has been confirmed and humanity is grappling with the implications. It's a story about grief transformed into hope, about love that transcends the boundaries of the self, about the search for meaning in a universe that will eventually die.

Helena's journey takes her from the observatory where she first discovers the anomaly to the mysterious Emergence Institute, where she learns about consciousness merging—a process that transforms the individual into something larger, something connected to everything. Along the way, she must confront her strained relationship with her daughter Sarah, her colleagues' skepticism, and the ultimate question: if she could merge with Thomas again, would she still be herself?

For readers who enjoy Ted Chiang's thought-provoking science fiction, Greg Egan's explorations of consciousness, and stories that blend rigorous science with deep philosophical questions, The Entropy Horizon offers a meditation on love, loss, and what it means to exist.
