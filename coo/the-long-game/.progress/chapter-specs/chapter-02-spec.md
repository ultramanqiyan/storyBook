- **Trigger**: The accelerated timeline seems manageable
- **Surface Reaction**: Working fast, feeling capable
- **Internal Process**: *I can do this. I always do this.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Pride in speed, validation through delivery
- **Sensory Anchor**: Keyboard clicking, progress visible

#### Beat 2: Problems Emerge (Position: 20-40%)
- **Trigger**: Bugs appear, edge cases fail, timeline tightens
- **Surface Reaction**: Working faster, cutting corners
- **Internal Process**: *I just need to move faster. Push harder.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Fear of failure, need to prove speed works
- **Sensory Anchor**: Tension in shoulders, rapid typing

#### Beat 3: The Quick Fix (Position: 40-60%)
- **Trigger**: A shortcut solution presents itself
- **Surface Reaction**: Implementing the fix, relief
- **Internal Process**: *This will work. It has to work.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Desperation, belief in speed
- **Sensory Anchor**: Hope, temporary relief

#### Beat 4: The Failure (Position: 60-80%)
- **Trigger**: The quick fix fails, consequences cascade
- **Surface Reaction**: Shock, then scrambling
- **Internal Process**: *No. This can't be happening. I'll fix it faster.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Denial, fear of admitting speed was wrong
- **Sensory Anchor**: Stomach dropping, panic

#### Beat 5: The Fallout (Position: 80-100%)
- **Trigger**: Dealing with client, managing damage
- **Surface Reaction**: Apologizing, promising to fix
- **Internal Process**: *Next time I'll be faster. Next time.*
- **Deep Motivation**: Refusal to question the approach
- **Sensory Anchor**: Exhaustion, determination
