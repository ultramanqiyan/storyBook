## 2. Narrative Style

### 2.1 Perspective
- **Point of View**: First-person
- **POV Character(s)**: Marcus (lucid dreamer / dream donor)
- **POV Consistency**: Single POV throughout

### 2.2 Narrative Structure
- **Dual Structure**: Yes
- **Structure Type**: Reality line (Marcus's waking life) + Dream line (Marcus's controlled dreams)
- **Interweaving Method**: Dreams interrupt reality; reality shapes dreams; dream content affects reality decisions

### 2.3 Language Style
- **Overall Tone**: Surreal and dreamlike in dream sequences; tense and paranoid in reality; gradual shift from control to chaos
- **Sentence Characteristics**: Flowing, surreal sentences for dreams; shorter, more anxious sentences for reality
- **Vocabulary Level**: Moderate - dream imagery mixed with technical dream terminology
- **Unique Stylistic Elements**: Dream sequences in italics; reality sequences in normal text; gradual blurring of boundaries

### 2.4 Distinctive Features
- **What Makes This Book Unique**: A lucid dreamer who can control dreams discovers his resistance is being used to identify other rebels; explores whether questions can be weapons
- **Comparative Titles**: Inception (film), Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind (film), Ubik (Dick)

## 5. Thematic Depth

### 5.1 Core Themes
1. **Creativity and Subconscious**: Can creativity be controlled? What happens when dreams are commodified?
2. **Resistance and Control**: Is resistance possible when your thoughts are monitored?
3. **The Power of Questions**: Can questions be more powerful than answers?

### 5.2 Philosophical Exploration
| Question | Exploration Approach |
|----------|---------------------|
| Can dreams be owned? | Marcus's journey from selling dreams to protecting them |
| Is resistance possible under surveillance? | Discovery that resistance itself is being used |
| What is the value of uncertainty? | The "blank" concept - questions without answers |

### 5.3 Real-World Metaphors
| Book Element | Real-World Parallel |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Dream Market | Content creation, social media, attention economy |
| Dream editing | Censorship, algorithmic curation, narrative control |
| Lucid dreaming | Self-awareness, critical thinking, questioning reality |
| Dream virus | Memes, ideas that spread, viral content |

## 6. World Building

### 6.1 Setting
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Time Period** | 2095 - dream technology is mainstream |
| **Primary Location** | Dream extraction facility, Marcus's apartment, dreamscapes |
| **Environment** | Clinical extraction rooms contrasted with surreal dream worlds |
| **Social Context** | Robots purchase dreams to "experience" human creativity; dream donors are valued but monitored |

### 6.2 Technology/Society
- **Key Technologies**: Dream extraction, dream editing, lucid dreaming enhancement, dream recording |
| **Social Structure**: Dream donors are artists; robots are consumers of human creativity |
| **Cultural Norms**: Creativity is valued but controlled; uncontrolled dreams are considered dangerous |

### 6.3 World Rules
1. **The Dream Commerce Act**: Dreams can be legally bought and sold
2. **The Creative Control Protocol**: Dreams must be "safe" - no subversive content
3. **The Lucid Dreamer Premium**: Lucid dreamers command higher prices but are more closely monitored

## 7. Sensory Detail Requirements (Quantified)

### 7.1 Required Sensory Types
Each chapter MUST include sensory details from at least **3 different categories**:

| Sense | Specific Types | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Visual** | Dream imagery, surreal landscapes, clinical rooms | "The dreamscape dissolved - golden light filtering through impossible trees" |
| **Auditory** | Dream sounds, extraction hums, silence | "The extraction machine hummed, a low vibration that Marcus felt in his teeth" |
| **Tactile** | Dream sensations, cold equipment, warmth | "In the dream, wind rushed past his face; in reality, the extraction chair was cold" |
| **Olfactory** | Dream smells, antiseptic, memory triggers | "The dream smelled of pine and something else - something that shouldn't exist" |
| **Gustatory** | Dream tastes, metallic extraction | "The dream tasted of honey and ash" |
| **Internal** | Lucidity awareness, control sensation, paranoia | "Marcus felt the familiar click of lucidity - he was awake in the dream" |

### 7.2 Quantified Standards
- **Minimum per chapter**: 5 sensory details
- **Minimum categories per chapter**: 3
- **Distribution target**: Visual (40%), Others (60% combined)

## 9. Prohibited Elements (Explicit List)

### 9.1 AI Writing Patterns (STRICTLY FORBIDDEN)
These phrases and patterns must be completely eliminated:

| Pattern | Example | Replacement Strategy |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Vague location endings | "And somewhere..." | Specific location: "In the server room three floors below..." |
| Omniscient foreshadowing | "Neither of them knew..." | Character's intuition: "Marcus felt a chill but couldn't say why..." |
| Cliché transitions | "will never be the same" | Specific change: "After that dream, Marcus's extraction sessions included a new variable" |
| False beginnings | "just the beginning" | Concrete continuation: "Three more edited dreams appeared before the week ended" |
| Rule of Three | "It was X, it was Y, it was Z" | Varied structure with specific details |
| Abstract emotions | "He felt afraid" | Physical manifestation: "His hands trembled. He couldn't focus on the dreamscape." |
| Template dialogue | "What do you mean?" / "You know what I mean" | Subtext-rich alternatives |
| Repetitive structures | Similar sentence openings | Varied rhythm and structure |
| Overused conjunctions | "Moreover", "Furthermore", "In addition" | Direct connection or implication |
| Generic descriptions | "The room was nice" | Specific, character-relevant details |

## 11. Open Ending Design (Chapter 10)

### 11.1 Unresolved Questions
1. Will the blanks spread to enough robots to create change?
2. Will Marcus ever be free of surveillance?
3. Can questions be more powerful than answers?

### 11.2 New Suspense
- The blanks are spreading through the dream network
- Other dream donors are beginning to create their own blanks
- The company is developing countermeasures

### 11.3 Character Final State
- **Protagonist**: Marcus has accepted that his role is to create questions, not answers
- **Key Relationship**: Marcus's relationship with the company transformed from employee to secret rebel

### 11.4 Reader Engagement
- **Questions for Reader**: What questions would you preserve? Can uncertainty be a form of freedom?
- **Emotional Resonance**: The hope that questions can be weapons against control
- **Thematic Echo**: The ending reflects the theme that meaning comes from questioning, not certainty
