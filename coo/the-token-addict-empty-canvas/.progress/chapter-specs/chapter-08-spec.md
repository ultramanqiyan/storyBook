## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Preparation (400 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Morning, before the presentation

**Key Events**:
- Marcus reviews his authentically created work
- It's rough, imperfect, but original
- The fear: "Is this good enough?"
- The memory of Sarah's advice: "Create something bad. Just create something."
- The decision: present what he has

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: The sketches, the rough design
- Auditory: His own breathing
- Internal: Fear, the weight of authenticity

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "This is what I have. It's not polished. It's not AI-perfect."
- Marcus (internal): "But it's mine. It came from me."
- Marcus (internal): "Is that enough? Is original but imperfect enough?"

### Scene 2: The Presentation (800 words)
**Location**: Conference room
**Time**: Mid-morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus presents his work to Jennifer and the team
- He explains his design choices authentically
- "This direction came from thinking about..."
- The work is visibly rougher than his previous AI-assisted work
- He admits uncertainty: "I'm not sure this element works yet"
- The vulnerability of authentic presentation
- The team's mixed reactions

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Conference room, his work on screen, faces
- Auditory: His voice, questions, silence
- Internal: Vulnerability, the exposure

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "This is my concept for the project. I want to walk you through my thinking."
- Marcus: "This direction came from considering the user's emotional journey..."
- Team member: "It's... different from your usual work. Less polished."
- Marcus: "Yes. I created this myself. Without AI."
- Jennifer: "Walk us through this element. What's the thinking?"
- Marcus: "I'm not entirely sure it works yet. I was exploring the idea of..."
- Team member: "You're not sure? You usually have all the answers."
- Marcus: "I don't have all the answers. This is a work in progress. This is my process."
- Jennifer: "Your process. This is what your process looks like?"
- Marcus: "Yes. Messy. Uncertain. But authentic."

### Scene 3: The Questions (600 words)
**Location**: Conference room
**Time**: Continuing

**Key Events**:
- The team asks probing questions
- Marcus answers honestly, even when uncertain
- "I considered that, but I'm not sure yet"
- "That's a good question. I need to think more about it"
- The contrast with his previous AI-assured presentations
- The discomfort of not having all the answers
- The authenticity of genuine uncertainty

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Faces, his work, the room
- Auditory: Questions, his honest responses
- Internal: Vulnerability, the discomfort

**Character Voices**:
- Team member: "Why this color palette?"
- Marcus: "I was exploring emotional resonance. I'm not sure it's right yet."
- Team member: "What about the typography?"
- Marcus: "That's still in progress. I'm considering alternatives."
- Jennifer: "How does this compare to what AI would generate?"
- Marcus: "It's less polished. Less refined. But it's mine. The AI would give you something that looks better but means less."
- Team member: "Does meaning matter more than execution?"
- Marcus: "I think so. At least for this project. At least for me."

### Scene 4: The Response (500 words)
**Location**: Conference room
**Time**: End of presentation

**Key Events**:
- Jennifer gives her response
- She acknowledges the authenticity
- "This is rough, but it has something the AI work didn't"
- The team's mixed reactions
- Some appreciate the authenticity
- Others miss the polish of AI-assisted work
- The uncertain judgment

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Jennifer's face, team reactions
- Auditory: Jennifer's voice, team murmurs
- Internal: Uncertainty, the weight of judgment

**Character Voices**:
- Jennifer: "This is... different. Rougher than your usual work."
- Marcus: "I know. It's not AI-polished."
- Jennifer: "But it has something the AI work didn't. A point of view. A voice."
- Team member: "But is it good enough? Is it professional quality?"
- Jennifer: "That's the question, isn't it? What is professional quality in the AI era?"
- Jennifer: "I appreciate the authenticity, Marcus. I'm not sure about the execution yet."
- Marcus: "I understand. I'm not sure either. But I'm trying."

### Scene 5: The Aftermath (400 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: After the meeting

**Key Events**:
- Marcus processes the presentation
- The uncertainty of the response
- The question: "Was authentic but imperfect enough?"
- The realization: he doesn't know yet
- The first step: he presented something real
- The fear: will it be enough professionally?

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Office, his work
- Auditory: Silence
- Internal: Uncertainty, the question

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "They didn't love it. But they didn't reject it either."
- Marcus (internal): "Jennifer said it has a voice. Is that enough?"
- Marcus (internal): "The team misses the polish. They're used to AI-perfect."
- Marcus (internal): "But I can't go back to AI imitation. Not after this."
- Marcus (internal): "What happens now? Do I keep struggling? Do I keep creating badly?"
