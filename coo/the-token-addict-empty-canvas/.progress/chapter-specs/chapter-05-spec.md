## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: Sarah's Project (500 words)
**Location**: Home, Sarah's studio
**Time**: Weekend morning

**Key Events**:
- Sarah is working on a personal design project
- She's creating something from scratch
- Her process: sketching, thinking, struggling, iterating
- Marcus watches from the doorway
- The contrast with his own process

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Sarah's studio, her sketches, her focused face
- Auditory: Pencil sounds, her muttering, erasing
- Internal: Observation, the first comparison

**Character Voices**:
- Sarah: (sketching, erasing, thinking aloud)
- Marcus (internal): "She's struggling. Why doesn't she just use AI?"
- Sarah: "This isn't working. Let me try a different approach."
- Marcus (internal): "I would have generated ten options by now."
- Sarah: "No, that's not it either. What am I trying to say?"
- Marcus (internal): "She's searching for something. What is she searching for?"

### Scene 2: The Conversation (700 words)
**Location**: Sarah's studio
**Time**: Mid-morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus asks Sarah about her process
- She explains her creative journey
- "I'm trying to express something that only I can express"
- Marcus asks: "Why not use AI to speed it up?"
- Sarah's response: "Because then it wouldn't be mine"
- The philosophical difference in their approaches
- Marcus's emptiness: he doesn't have anything to express

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Sarah's face, her work
- Auditory: Sarah's voice, the quiet of the studio
- Internal: The growing emptiness

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "What are you working on?"
- Sarah: "A personal project. Something I've been thinking about for months."
- Marcus: "Why don't you use CreateFlow? You could finish it faster."
- Sarah: "Because then it wouldn't be mine. It would be the AI's interpretation of my idea."
- Marcus: "But you'd still be directing it. Curating it."
- Sarah: "Curation isn't creation, Marcus. When I make something, I want it to come from me. All of it."
- Marcus: "But what's the difference? The output is what matters."
- Sarah: "The output isn't what matters. The process is what matters. The struggle is what matters."
- Marcus: "I don't understand. Why would you want to struggle?"
- Sarah: "Because the struggle is where I find myself. Where I discover what I actually think. What I actually feel."
- Marcus (internal): "I don't have anything to discover. I don't have anything to express."

### Scene 3: The Comparison (600 words)
**Location**: Sarah's studio
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus compares his process to Sarah's
- His process: brief → AI → selection → done
- Her process: idea → struggle → discovery → expression
- The difference: she creates, he selects
- The emptiness: he has no inner vision to express
- The question: "What do I have to say?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Sarah's work, his memory of his own
- Auditory: Sarah's continued work
- Internal: The emptiness, the comparison

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Her process is messy. Inefficient. Painful."
- Marcus (internal): "But it's hers. Every mark on that page came from inside her."
- Marcus (internal): "My process is clean. Efficient. Painless."
- Marcus (internal): "But none of it comes from inside me. It all comes from the AI."
- Marcus (internal): "She has something to express. I have nothing to express."
- Marcus (internal): "When did I lose my inner vision? When did I stop having something to say?"

### Scene 4: The Question (500 words)
**Location**: Home, living room
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Marcus asks Sarah a difficult question
- "Do you think I've lost my creativity?"
- Sarah's honest response
- "I think you've outsourced it. There's a difference."
- The question: "Can I get it back?"
- Sarah's uncertainty: "I don't know. Maybe. If you're willing to struggle."
- The weight of the possibility

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Living room, Sarah's face
- Auditory: Sarah's voice, home sounds
- Internal: The weight of the question

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "Sarah, can I ask you something?"
- Sarah: "Of course."
- Marcus: "Do you think I've lost my creativity?"
- Sarah: (pause) "I think you've outsourced it. There's a difference."
- Marcus: "What's the difference?"
- Sarah: "Lost implies it's gone forever. Outsourced means you gave it away. You can take it back, maybe. If you want to."
- Marcus: "Can I? Can I get it back?"
- Sarah: "I don't know. Maybe. If you're willing to struggle. If you're willing to sit with the blank canvas and feel the emptiness until something comes."
- Marcus: "What if nothing comes? What if the emptiness is all there is?"
- Sarah: "Then you'll know. And you can decide what to do with that."

### Scene 5: The Night (400 words)
**Location**: Home, bedroom
**Time**: Night

**Key Events**:
- Marcus lies awake, thinking about the conversation
- The question: "Am I willing to struggle?"
- The fear: "What if nothing comes?"
- The alternative: continue with AI, accept the emptiness
- The choice he's not ready to make

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Dark room, ceiling
- Auditory: Silence, Sarah's breathing
- Internal: The question, the fear

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Sarah says I can take my creativity back. If I'm willing to struggle."
- Marcus (internal): "But what if I struggle and nothing comes? What if the emptiness is permanent?"
- Marcus (internal): "At least with AI, I have output. I have productivity. I have success."
- Marcus (internal): "But I don't have myself. I don't have anything that's mine."
- Marcus (internal): "Tomorrow, I have to present my 'original vision' project. It's not original. It's AI-generated."
- Marcus (internal): "What am I going to do?"
