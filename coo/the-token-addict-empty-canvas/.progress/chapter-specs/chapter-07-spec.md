## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Aftermath (400 words)
**Location**: Nexus Technologies, Marcus's office
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus deals with the professional consequences
- The team's changed perception
- The loss of creative authority
- The shame that lingers
- The question: "Can I recover from this?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Office, team's averted eyes
- Auditory: Silence, whispers
- Internal: Shame, the weight of exposure

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "They all know. They all know I'm a fraud."
- Team member: (avoiding eye contact)
- Marcus (internal): "I've lost their trust. I've lost my authority."
- Marcus (internal): "Can I ever get it back? Should I even try?"

### Scene 2: The Opportunity (600 words)
**Location**: Jennifer's office
**Time**: Mid-morning

**Key Events**:
- Jennifer calls Marcus in
- She gives him another chance
- A new project requiring genuine creativity
- "I need you to actually create this time"
- The pressure of redemption
- The warning: "No AI shortcuts. I'll know."

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Jennifer's office, her face
- Auditory: Jennifer's voice, the weight of her words
- Internal: Anxiety, the pressure

**Character Voices**:
- Jennifer: "Marcus, I'm giving you another chance."
- Marcus: "Thank you, Jennifer. I won't let you down."
- Jennifer: "This project requires genuine creativity. Original vision. No AI shortcuts."
- Marcus: "I understand."
- Jennifer: "Do you? Because last time, you presented AI imitation as your own work."
- Marcus: "I know. I'm sorry. I won't do that again."
- Jennifer: "I need you to actually create this time. Can you do that?"
- Marcus: "I... I'll try."
- Jennifer: "Trying isn't enough. I need to know you can do this. Can you?"
- Marcus (internal): "I don't know. I don't know if I can create anymore."
- Marcus: "Yes. I can do this."

### Scene 3: The Struggle (700 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus sits with the new brief
- He resists the urge to open CreateFlow
- He tries to think, to ideate, to create
- The creative void is still there
- He sketches, erases, sketches again
- The temptation to use AI is overwhelming
- The struggle between dependency and authenticity
- The fear: "What if I can't do this?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Blank paper, sketches, CreateFlow icon
- Tactile: Pencil, paper, mouse
- Internal: Struggle, temptation, fear

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "No AI. I have to do this myself."
- Marcus (staring at blank paper): ...
- Marcus (internal): "Think. Think of something original."
- Marcus (sketching): A shape. No. Erase.
- Marcus (internal): "This is all derivative. This is all empty."
- Marcus (looking at CreateFlow icon): "Just one peek. Just to get started."
- Marcus (internal): "No. Jennifer will know. I have to do this myself."
- Marcus (internal): "But what if I can't? What if there's nothing there?"
- Marcus (sketching again): Another attempt. Another failure.
- Marcus (internal): "Where is my creativity? Where did it go?"

### Scene 4: The Sarah Support (500 words)
**Location**: Home, living room
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Marcus tells Sarah about the new project
- He admits his struggle
- Sarah offers support
- "Maybe start smaller. Don't try to create a masterpiece."
- The advice: "Create something bad. Just create something."
- The permission to fail

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Living room, Sarah's face
- Auditory: Sarah's voice, home sounds
- Internal: The weight of the struggle

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "Jennifer gave me another chance. A new project. No AI."
- Sarah: "That's good. That's an opportunity."
- Marcus: "But I can't do it. I sat there all afternoon and nothing came."
- Sarah: "Maybe you're putting too much pressure on yourself."
- Marcus: "I have to create something original. That's the assignment."
- Sarah: "Maybe start smaller. Don't try to create a masterpiece. Just create something."
- Marcus: "Something bad?"
- Sarah: "Something real. Even if it's bad. Especially if it's bad. That's how you start again."
- Marcus: "I don't know if I can. I don't know if there's anything left."
- Sarah: "There's something left. You just have to be willing to find it. Even if it's messy. Even if it's painful."

### Scene 5: The Night Attempt (500 words)
**Location**: Home, Marcus's home office
**Time**: Night

**Key Events**:
- Marcus tries again, alone
- He gives himself permission to create something bad
- He sketches without judgment
- Something begins to emerge
- It's not good, but it's his
- The first spark of authentic creation
- The fear: "Is this enough?"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Sketches, the emerging idea
- Tactile: Pencil, paper
- Internal: The struggle, the first spark

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Sarah said to create something bad. Just create something."
- Marcus (sketching without erasing): Letting the marks stay.
- Marcus (internal): "This is terrible. This is not professional quality."
- Marcus (internal): "But it's mine. It came from me."
- Marcus (continuing to sketch): The idea begins to take shape.
- Marcus (internal): "It's not good. But it's something. It's a start."
- Marcus (internal): "Is this enough? Is this what Jennifer wants?"
- Marcus (internal): "I don't know. But it's all I have."
