## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Preparation (400 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Morning, before the presentation

**Key Events**:
- Marcus reviews his AI-generated "original vision"
- He tries to convince himself it's good enough
- The anxiety of the upcoming presentation
- The memory of Sarah's words about struggle
- The decision: present what he has

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: The design on screen, his notes
- Auditory: Keyboard sounds, his own breathing
- Internal: Anxiety, denial, the weight

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "This is good. It's original enough. It's the best I can do."
- Marcus (internal): "Sarah would say it's not original. But she doesn't understand the pressure I'm under."
- Marcus (internal): "I'll present it confidently. No one will know the difference."

### Scene 2: The Presentation (800 words)
**Location**: Conference room
**Time**: Mid-morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus presents his "original vision" to Jennifer and the team
- He describes the design with confidence
- Jennifer asks about his creative process
- Marcus struggles to explain his inspiration
- The questions become more probing
- "Where did this idea come from?"
- "What was your thinking behind this element?"
- Marcus's answers become vague, defensive
- The team senses something is wrong

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Conference room, presentation screen, faces
- Auditory: His voice, questions, the silence
- Internal: Anxiety building, the truth pressing

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "This design represents a new direction for our innovation initiative..."
- Jennifer: "Walk us through your creative process. How did you arrive at this vision?"
- Marcus: "I explored the creative space, considered multiple directions..."
- Team member: "What was the inspiration for this particular element?"
- Marcus: "It... emerged from the exploration. It felt right."
- Jennifer: "But what was your thinking? What problem does it solve?"
- Marcus: "It addresses the core challenge by... by creating a new paradigm..."
- Team member: "That sounds like marketing language. What's the actual design rationale?"
- Marcus: "The rationale is... it works. It's effective."
- Jennifer: "Marcus, I've seen this design before. Or something very similar."

### Scene 3: The Revelation (600 words)
**Location**: Conference room
**Time**: Continuing

**Key Events**:
- Jennifer shows a similar design from another company
- The similarity is undeniable
- The truth: Marcus's "original vision" is AI-generated imitation
- The AI combined existing designs, nothing original
- The exposure: Marcus didn't create anything
- The shame: he's been caught

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: The similar design, faces, his own work
- Auditory: Silence, Jennifer's voice
- Internal: Shame, the truth exposed

**Character Voices**:
- Jennifer: "Marcus, look at this. This design was published last month by a competitor."
- Marcus: "I... I didn't know. I've never seen this before."
- Jennifer: "But your design is almost identical. How is that possible?"
- Marcus (internal): "Because the AI generated it. Because it combined existing designs."
- Marcus: "I... I used CreateFlow. It must have... referenced this."
- Jennifer: "So this isn't your original vision? It's AI-generated?"
- Marcus: "I directed it. I curated the output."
- Jennifer: "But you presented it as original. As your creative vision."
- Marcus: "I... I thought it was. I thought the AI was helping me create."
- Team member: "The AI doesn't create, Marcus. It imitates. It combines. It doesn't originate."
- Marcus (internal): "And neither do I. Not anymore."

### Scene 4: The Aftermath (500 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: After the meeting

**Key Events**:
- Marcus retreats to his office
- The shame of the exposure
- The realization: his work is imitation, not creation
- The question: "What am I if I can't create?"
- The denial: "I'm still valuable. I'm still a leader."
- The truth: he's lost his creative identity

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Office, closed door, his own work on screen
- Auditory: Silence, his own breathing
- Internal: Shame, the truth, denial

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "They know. They know I can't create."
- Marcus (internal): "But I'm still productive. I'm still valuable. I can still lead."
- Marcus (internal): "Lead what? A team that creates while I select?"
- Marcus (internal): "That's still leadership. That's still valuable."
- Marcus (internal): "Is it? Is it valuable if I have nothing original to contribute?"

### Scene 5: The Sarah Call (400 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Late afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus calls Sarah
- He tells her what happened
- Her response is compassionate but honest
- "This was always going to happen"
- The question: "What are you going to do now?"
- Marcus's uncertainty

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Phone, office
- Auditory: Sarah's voice
- Internal: Shame, uncertainty

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "Sarah, I... I got caught. They know my design wasn't original."
- Sarah: "What happened?"
- Marcus: "Jennifer showed me a similar design from a competitor. My AI-generated work was almost identical."
- Sarah: "Oh, Marcus. I'm sorry."
- Marcus: "Are you? Or is this what you expected?"
- Sarah: "This was always going to happen. You can't present AI imitation as original vision forever."
- Marcus: "I know. I just... I didn't know what else to do."
- Sarah: "What are you going to do now?"
- Marcus: "I don't know. I don't know what I am if I can't create."
