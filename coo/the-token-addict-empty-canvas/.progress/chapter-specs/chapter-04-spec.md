## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Unusual Brief (500 words)
**Location**: Nexus Technologies, Jennifer's office
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Jennifer gives Marcus a special project
- An innovation initiative requiring "original creative vision"
- "We need something that doesn't exist yet"
- The brief is deliberately vague: no templates, no references
- Jennifer's expectation: "You're our creative leader"
- Marcus's confidence: "I can handle this"
- The seed of doubt: this requires original thought

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Jennifer's office, the brief document
- Auditory: Jennifer's voice, Marcus's confident responses
- Internal: Confidence, underlying doubt

**Character Voices**:
- Jennifer: "Marcus, I have a special project for you. Our innovation initiative needs original creative vision."
- Marcus: "Of course. What's the scope?"
- Jennifer: "That's the challenge. We need something that doesn't exist yet. No templates. No references. Pure original thinking."
- Marcus: "I can do that. I'm our creative leader."
- Jennifer: "I know you can. This is why you're in this role."
- Marcus (internal): "But what if I can't? What if I've forgotten how?"

### Scene 2: The Attempt (700 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Mid-morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus sits down to create something original
- He opens a blank canvas - no CreateFlow
- He tries to think, to ideate, to create
- Nothing comes - the creative void
- He sketches, erases, sketches again
- Everything feels derivative, empty
- The panic: "I can't do this"
- The realization: he's lost the ability to create from nothing

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Blank canvas, failed sketches
- Tactile: Pencil, eraser, paper
- Internal: Panic, the creative void, fear

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Okay. Original vision. I can do this."
- Marcus (staring at blank canvas): ...
- Marcus (internal): "Think. Think of something. Anything."
- Marcus (sketching): A shape. No. Erase.
- Marcus (internal): "That's just a variation of something I've seen before."
- Marcus (sketching): Another shape. No. Erase.
- Marcus (internal): "This is all derivative. This is all empty."
- Marcus (internal): "Where are my ideas? Where is my creativity?"
- Marcus (internal): "I can't do this. I can't create from nothing."

### Scene 3: The AI Temptation (500 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus considers using CreateFlow
- But the brief requires original vision
- He tries to use AI anyway, generates options
- Everything looks like something else
- The AI can only combine existing ideas
- The realization: AI can't create original vision
- The fear: neither can he

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: CreateFlow interface, generated options
- Auditory: Keyboard sounds
- Internal: Desperation, the truth

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Maybe CreateFlow can help. Just to get started."
- CreateFlow (output): "Design options generated based on existing patterns..."
- Marcus (internal): "These are all derivative. These are all combinations of what already exists."
- Marcus (internal): "The brief needs original vision. AI can't do that."
- Marcus (internal): "And neither can I. I've forgotten how."

### Scene 4: The Sarah Conversation (600 words)
**Location**: Home, living room
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Marcus tells Sarah about the project
- He admits his struggle
- Sarah asks about his process
- Marcus describes his creative block
- Sarah's question: "When did you last create something from nothing?"
- Marcus can't remember
- The weight of the realization
- Sarah's suggestion: "Maybe you need to relearn how to create"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Living room, Sarah's face
- Auditory: Sarah's voice, home sounds
- Internal: The weight of the realization

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "I have this project. Requires original creative vision. And I... can't do it."
- Sarah: "What do you mean, you can't do it?"
- Marcus: "I sat in front of a blank canvas today. And nothing came. Nothing."
- Sarah: "When did you last create something from nothing? Without AI?"
- Marcus: "I... I don't remember. I don't remember the last time I had an original idea."
- Sarah: "Maybe you need to relearn how to create. Start small. Practice."
- Marcus: "Practice? I'm a creative leader. I shouldn't need to practice."
- Sarah: "Everyone needs to practice. Creativity is a muscle. If you don't use it..."
- Marcus: "It atrophies. Like everything else."
- Sarah: "Yes. Like everything else."

### Scene 5: The Denial (400 words)
**Location**: Home, Marcus's home office
**Time**: Night

**Key Events**:
- Marcus tries again to create something
- The blank canvas still stares back
- He retreats to CreateFlow
- He generates options, picks the "best" one
- He tells himself it's "original enough"
- The denial: "I can still create. I'm just... using tools."
- The truth: he's given up on original vision

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Blank canvas, CreateFlow interface
- Auditory: Keyboard sounds
- Internal: Denial, the retreat

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "I'll try one more time. Just one more time."
- Marcus (staring at blank canvas): ...
- Marcus (internal): "Nothing. Still nothing."
- Marcus (opening CreateFlow): "Forget it. I'll use the tools I have."
- CreateFlow (output): "Design options generated..."
- Marcus (selecting): "This one. This is original enough."
- Marcus (internal): "It's not original. It's a combination of existing ideas."
- Marcus (internal): "But it's the best I can do. It's all I can do."
