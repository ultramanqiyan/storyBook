## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Design Role (500 words)
**Location**: Nexus Technologies, design studio
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus's new responsibility: architectural and design decisions
- He reviews a design brief for a new product
- The weight of creative expectation
- His confidence: "I've always had good design instincts"
- The context: AI is standard, but original thought is still valued

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Design studio, sketches, screens
- Auditory: Creative discussions, keyboard sounds
- Internal: Confidence, creative anticipation

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Design is just problem-solving with aesthetics. I can do this."
- Colleague: "Marcus, we need your creative vision on this project."
- Marcus: "Let me think through the approach."

### Scene 2: The Introduction (700 words)
**Location**: Company all-hands meeting
**Time**: Mid-morning

**Key Events**:
- Jennifer announces CreateFlow deployment
- The demo: AI generating design options in seconds
- Marcus's initial reaction: skepticism mixed with curiosity
- The promise: "CreateFlow will enhance your creative process"
- The pressure: "Our competitors are already using it"
- Marcus's internal debate: enhancement or replacement?

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Large screen, CreateFlow demo, audience faces
- Auditory: Jennifer's presentation, audience reactions
- Internal: Curiosity, skepticism, the creative question

**Character Voices**:
- Jennifer: "CreateFlow represents the future of design. It doesn't replace creativity - it amplifies it."
- CreateFlow demo: Generating multiple design variations in seconds
- Marcus (internal): "Amplifies? Or takes over?"
- Colleague: "This is going to change everything."
- Marcus: "We'll see. Tools are only as good as the people using them."

### Scene 3: First Use (600 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus opens CreateFlow for the first time
- He inputs his design brief
- CreateFlow generates multiple options
- Marcus's surprise: the designs are good
- He selects one, makes minor adjustments
- The feeling: "I created this" (but did he?)
- The rationalization: "I directed the AI, so it's still my vision"

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: CreateFlow interface, generated designs
- Tactile: Mouse, keyboard
- Internal: Surprise, excitement, the first rationalization

**Character Voices**:
- CreateFlow (interface): "Design options generated. Select your preferred direction."
- Marcus (internal): "These are... actually good. Better than what I would have come up with."
- Marcus: "I'll refine this one. Add my personal touch."
- Marcus (internal): "I'm still the creative director here. The AI is just a tool."

### Scene 4: The Success (500 words)
**Location**: Design review meeting
**Time**: Late afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus presents his CreateFlow-assisted design
- The team is impressed
- Jennifer praises his "creative vision"
- Marcus feels validated
- The first seed: he can't explain his design choices in detail
- He deflects: "It just felt right"
- The team accepts this as creative intuition

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Presentation screen, team faces
- Auditory: Praise, discussion
- Internal: Validation, the first small doubt

**Character Voices**:
- Team member: "This is excellent, Marcus. How did you arrive at this direction?"
- Marcus: "I explored the creative space and this felt like the strongest approach."
- Team member: "But what about the color palette? The typography choices?"
- Marcus: "They just... worked together. Design is intuitive, you know?"
- Jennifer: "Great work, Marcus. Your creative instincts are sharp."

### Scene 5: The Evening (400 words)
**Location**: Home, with Sarah
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Marcus tells Sarah about CreateFlow
- Sarah, a product designer, is interested
- She asks about his process
- Marcus describes the AI assistance
- Sarah's question: "But what did YOU bring to it?"
- Marcus's deflection: "Direction. Curation. That's creativity too."
- The first tension about creative ownership

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Home, Sarah's face
- Auditory: Sarah's voice, home sounds
- Internal: Validation, slight defensiveness

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus: "We got this new AI tool at work. CreateFlow. It's incredible."
- Sarah: "Oh? How does it work?"
- Marcus: "You give it a brief, it generates options. I picked the best one and refined it."
- Sarah: "But what did you bring to it? What was your creative contribution?"
- Marcus: "I directed it. I curated the output. That's creativity."
- Sarah: "I guess. It just seems... different from how I design."
- Marcus (internal): "Different how? Better, probably."
