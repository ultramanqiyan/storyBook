## 4. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Comparison (700 words)
**Location**: Marcus's office
**Time**: Morning

**Key Events**:
- Marcus tries to design something himself first
- He sketches ideas, feels uncertain
- He inputs the same brief into CreateFlow
- CreateFlow generates options that are clearly better
- The moment of truth: his ideas vs. AI's
- The realization: "I can't compete with this"
- The decision: why try when AI is better?

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: His sketches, CreateFlow designs, comparison
- Tactile: Pencil, paper, mouse
- Internal: Uncertainty, defeat, surrender

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Let me try to come up with something on my own first."
- Marcus (sketching): Uncertain lines, erased mistakes
- Marcus (internal): "This doesn't feel right. Let me see what CreateFlow comes up with."
- CreateFlow (output): Multiple polished design options
- Marcus (internal): "These are better. Much better. Why would I struggle when this exists?"
- Marcus: "I'll go with the AI options. They're stronger."

### Scene 2: The Habit (600 words)
**Location**: Design studio
**Time**: Afternoon

**Key Events**:
- Marcus's new routine: always start with CreateFlow
- He stops trying to generate original ideas
- The pattern: brief → AI → selection → minor tweaks
- The team notices his increased "productivity"
- The praise for his "creative vision"
- The hollow feeling of borrowed creativity

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: CreateFlow interface, design iterations
- Auditory: Team praise, keyboard sounds
- Internal: The hollow productivity

**Character Voices**:
- Team member: "Marcus, you're on fire lately. So many great designs."
- Marcus: "Thanks. The ideas just keep coming."
- Marcus (internal): "The ideas aren't coming from me. They're coming from CreateFlow."
- Team member: "What's your process? How do you come up with these?"
- Marcus: "I explore the creative space, see what emerges."
- Marcus (internal): "I don't explore anything. I select what the AI explores."

### Scene 3: The Challenge (500 words)
**Location**: Design review meeting
**Time**: Late afternoon

**Key Events**:
- A colleague asks for Marcus's design rationale
- "Why this color palette? Why this layout?"
- Marcus struggles to explain
- He deflects: "It just works"
- The colleague presses: "But what's the thinking behind it?"
- Marcus uses AI to generate an explanation
- The explanation is convincing but not his own

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Meeting room, design on screen
- Auditory: Questions, Marcus's careful responses
- Internal: Panic, the gap between selection and understanding

**Character Voices**:
- Colleague: "Marcus, walk us through your design choices. Why this direction?"
- Marcus: "The color palette creates emotional resonance with the target audience..."
- Colleague: "But how did you arrive at that specific palette?"
- Marcus (internal): "I didn't. CreateFlow did."
- Marcus: "I explored multiple options and this one felt strongest."
- Colleague: "But what was your thinking?"
- Marcus: "Design is intuitive. Sometimes you just know when it's right."

### Scene 4: The Sarah Contrast (500 words)
**Location**: Home, Sarah's studio
**Time**: Evening

**Key Events**:
- Marcus watches Sarah design
- She sketches, iterates, struggles, succeeds
- Her process is messy, human, original
- Marcus compares it to his AI-assisted process
- The question: "Is my process even design?"
- Sarah's satisfaction with her original work
- Marcus's hollowness

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Sarah's sketches, her focused face
- Auditory: Pencil sounds, her muttering
- Internal: Comparison, hollowness

**Character Voices**:
- Sarah: (sketching, erasing, rethinking)
- Marcus: "You're still doing it the hard way."
- Sarah: "This is how I design. It's my process."
- Marcus: "CreateFlow could do that in seconds."
- Sarah: "But then it wouldn't be mine. It would be the AI's."
- Marcus (internal): "Is that the difference? Is my work not mine?"
- Sarah: "There's something satisfying about creating something yourself. Don't you feel that?"
- Marcus: "I... feel productive. That's something."

### Scene 5: The Surrender (400 words)
**Location**: Marcus's home office
**Time**: Night

**Key Events**:
- Marcus reflects on the day
- The comparison with Sarah
- The question: "What have I lost?"
- The rationalization: "I'm more productive now"
- The surrender: "This is just how design works now"
- The seed of creative emptiness

**Sensory Requirements**:
- Visual: Dark room, screen glow
- Auditory: Silence
- Internal: The quiet surrender

**Character Voices**:
- Marcus (internal): "Sarah's way is the old way. My way is the future."
- Marcus (internal): "But why does her work feel more... hers?"
- Marcus (internal): "Because she made it. I just selected it."
- Marcus (internal): "Does that matter? The output is what counts."
- Marcus (internal): "But the process... the process is where creativity lives."
- Marcus (internal): "Maybe I've already lost that. Maybe it doesn't matter anymore."
