# Chapter 4: The Attempt

Marcus tried to create something on his own.

A simple solution—a problem he might solve. In the past, he would have checked CreateFlow for AI-generated ideas, suggested approaches, optimized solutions.

This time, he asked himself: *What can I create?*

---

The question was harder than it should have been.

He'd been given ideas for so long that he'd forgotten how to generate them himself. The AI had been creating his solutions, designing his approaches, generating his ideas.

But he tried anyway. *What can I actually create?*

The answer came slowly. Uncertainly. But it came.

*I can build something. Something of my own. Not optimized. Not AI-generated. Just... mine.*

---

The attempt was harder than it should have been.

Every idea felt uncertain. Every solution felt inadequate. Every creation felt amateur.

But he kept going. Not because it was easy—it wasn't. But because it was necessary. Because creativity required original thought. Because creation required self-expression.

*This is what it costs to reclaim your creativity*, he thought. *The uncertainty and the originality. The difficulty and the self-expression.*

---

By the end of the day, something had shifted.

Not dramatically. Not obviously. But he had an idea. A solution. A creation.

Not AI-generated. Not optimized. Just his.

*This is what creating feels like*, he realized. *Not efficient. Not optimized. But real. Mine. Original.*

The insight was small. But it was his. Not AI-generated. His. And that, he realized, was what mattered.
