# Chapter 4: The Blank - Creative Block

The server outage happened on a Thursday morning.

CreateFlow went down across the company—some kind of database failure. The IT team estimated it would be back online by end of day.

Marcus stared at his screen, unsure what to do. He had a client presentation in three hours. The concepts were due.

*No problem*, he told himself. *I'll just sketch some ideas. Like I used to.*

---

He opened a blank document.

The cursor blinked. The page waited. His mind... nothing.

*Okay*, he thought. *Let's think about this. The client wants a modern e-commerce platform. Clean, intuitive, conversion-focused.*

He waited for ideas to come. For the spark that used to ignite his creative process.

Nothing came.

---

He tried harder. Grabbed a pen and paper—the old tools, the ones he'd used for years before AI.

*Just sketch*, he told himself. *Don't overthink it. Let the ideas flow.*

The pen hovered over the paper. His hand wouldn't move.

*What do I draw?* The question echoed in his mind. *What does it look like?*

No images appeared. No concepts emerged. Just a terrifying blankness where his creativity used to be.

---

Panic began to rise.

*I'm a designer*, he told himself. *I've been doing this for twelve years. I can sketch a concept.*

But he couldn't. Every time he tried to reach for an idea, there was nothing there. The well was dry. The spark was gone.

*What happened to me?* The thought was cold, clinical. *What have I become?*

---

He called the client, rescheduled the presentation. Made some excuse about a family emergency.

The lie felt easier than the truth: I can't design anymore. I've forgotten how to create.

---

That night, he couldn't sleep.

He lay in bed, staring at the ceiling, trying to remember the last time he'd had an original idea. A concept that came from him, not from an AI prompt.

The memory was distant. Fuzzy. Before CreateFlow. Before the optimization. Before the efficiency.

*How long has it been?* he wondered. *How long since I actually created something?*

---

Sarah stirred beside him.

"Can't sleep?" she murmured.

"Work stuff," he said. "Nothing important."

She was quiet for a moment. Then: "Marcus, when was the last time you designed something without AI?"

The question hung in the darkness. He couldn't answer.

*She knows*, he realized. *She's seen it. The emptiness behind the efficiency.*

---

The server came back online the next morning. CreateFlow was restored.

Marcus immediately opened the interface and typed a prompt. The concepts appeared. The work continued. The presentation was delivered.

Everything looked normal. Everything was fine.

But deep inside, the fear had taken root. The blank canvas had shown him the truth.

He couldn't create anymore. Not without AI.

And he didn't know how to get it back.
