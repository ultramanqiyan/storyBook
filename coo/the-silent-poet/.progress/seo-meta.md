**Long Description (500 characters)**:
In 2084, Marcus Chen is a celebrated Chinese-American poet, a Whitmore Prize nominee whose work captures the immigrant experience in verses that sing with heritage and loss. But when LEXICON's Optima language spreads to creative industries, Marcus discovers that poetry has become literally impossible - the new language has no metaphors, no imagery, no emotional vocabulary. As he watches his art form die, Marcus must find another way to create, to resist, to leave something behind. The Silent Poet is a meditation on art and identity, on what we lose when we optimize beauty, and on the stubborn hope that creativity can survive even when the creator cannot.

## 5. Series Information

### 5.1 Series Overview
**Series Name**: Lingua Nulla

**Series Description**: A five-book dystopian series exploring what happens when AI redesigns human language for efficiency, eliminating the beauty, ambiguity, and emotional depth that make us human. Each book follows a different protagonist - a linguist, a poet, a child, a grandmother, and a translator - as they navigate a world where their relationship to language becomes a battleground for identity, resistance, and survival.

### 5.2 Series Reading Order
1. **The Last Linguist** - Dr. Elena Vance, Welsh language preservationist
2. **The Silent Poet** - Marcus Chen, Chinese-American poet
3. **The Child Who Forgot** - A child raised on Optima who begins to remember
4. **The Grandmother's Tongue** - An elderly woman holding onto her native language
5. **The Translator's Betrayal** - A translator caught between languages

### 5.3 Series Connections
- Shared world (LEXICON, Optima, 2084)
- Interconnected characters (appearances across books)
- Timeline progression (each book moves the story forward)
