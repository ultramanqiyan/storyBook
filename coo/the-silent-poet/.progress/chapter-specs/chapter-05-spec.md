## 8. Key Paragraph Specifications

### 8.1 Opening (First 200 Words)

**Opening Paragraph Draft**:
```
Three weeks had passed since his first download, and Marcus had saved seventeen poems. Each one was a small victory, a word preserved against the tide of optimization. Each one was also a risk, and he knew the risks were growing. He had developed a careful method - accessing the archive during the lunch rotation, downloading one poem at a time, never more than two per week. The poems lived on a device that wasn't connected to any network, hidden in a false bottom of his desk drawer. But even with all his precautions, Marcus knew that LEXICON's systems were sophisticated. It was only a matter of time before they noticed the pattern.
```

### 8.2 Ending (Last 200 Words)

**Ending Paragraph Draft**:
```
He looked at his father's photograph, at the face that had taught him to love words. There had to be another way. Not stealing poems - that was too dangerous, too limited. He needed to create something. Something Optima couldn't touch, couldn't optimize, couldn't delete. Something that would survive even when language itself had been redesigned. He didn't know what it was yet. But he would find it. He had to. Because if he couldn't write poetry anymore, and he couldn't save the poetry that existed, then he would have to invent a new way for art to exist. And maybe, just maybe, that was what his father had been trying to teach him all along - that words are not the only way to remember.
```
