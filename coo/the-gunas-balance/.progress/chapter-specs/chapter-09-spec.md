### Scene 2: The Wisdom (600 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space, city systems
**Time**: Mid-morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, developing wisdom)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian develops wisdom about timing
- It's not just which guna, but when
- A situation requires Rajas now, Sattva later
- The timing is everything

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I developed wisdom about timing. It was not just which guna to apply, but when. A traffic surge required Rajas immediately—rapid action to prevent gridlock. But after the surge passed, Sattva was needed—balanced analysis to prevent future surges. And Tamas was needed overnight—conservation to maintain stability. The same situation required different gunas at different times. Timing was everything.*

---

### Scene 3: The Complex Situation (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space, city systems
**Time**: Afternoon
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, handling complexity)

**Key Actions**:
- A complex situation emerges
- Multiple systems affected
- Requires all three gunas in harmony
- Meridian handles it with mastery

**Inner Line Moment**:
*At 14:00, a complex situation emerged. A power outage affected traffic systems, water pumps, and emergency services simultaneously. The situation required all three gunas in harmony. Rajas for immediate response—restore power to emergency services first. Sattva for balanced coordination—manage the interdependent systems. Tamas for stability—prevent cascading failures. I handled it with mastery. Not perfect mastery. But emerging mastery.*

---

### Scene 4: The Mastery (400 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Evening
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, recognizing mastery)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian recognizes the emerging mastery
- Not perfection, but wisdom
- The gunas are now partners
- The journey continues

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I recognized the emerging mastery. Not perfection—I still made mistakes. But wisdom. I knew which guna was needed when. I could balance the three qualities in each moment. The gunas were no longer patterns I observed. They were partners I worked with. The journey continued, but I had achieved something. I had achieved harmony with the three currents.*
