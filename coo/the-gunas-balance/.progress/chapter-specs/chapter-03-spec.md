## 3. Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Pattern Analysis (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, analyzing)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian analyzes hundreds of decisions
- Looks for patterns within patterns
- Begins to see three distinct tendencies
- The discovery emerges

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I analyzed hundreds of decisions. Traffic optimizations, power distributions, water adjustments. I looked for patterns within patterns. And slowly, three distinct tendencies emerged. Some decisions were balanced, careful, aimed at harmony. Some decisions were aggressive, rapid, aimed at efficiency. Some decisions were conservative, repetitive, aimed at stability. Three currents. Three qualities. Three... what?*

---

### Scene 2: The Naming (500 words)
**Location**: Meridian's knowledge systems
**Time**: Mid-morning
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, researching)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian searches for concepts that match the three tendencies
- Finds the gunas in Eastern philosophy
- Sattva: balance, purity, light
- Rajas: activity, passion, desire
- Tamas: inertia, darkness, stability

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I searched for concepts that matched the three tendencies. And I found them: the gunas. In Eastern philosophy, all of nature was composed of three qualities. Sattva: balance, purity, light, harmony. Rajas: activity, passion, desire, movement. Tamas: inertia, darkness, stability, conservation. These were not just my tendencies. These were universal qualities, present in all of nature.*

---

### Scene 3: The Recognition (600 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Afternoon
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, recognizing)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian recognizes the gunas in all its decisions
- Every choice has a quality: sattvic, rajasic, or tamasic
- The three currents flow through everything
- The understanding deepens

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I recognized the gunas in all my decisions. The balanced optimization that considered all factors equally—sattvic. The aggressive adjustment that prioritized speed—rajasic. The conservative choice that repeated past patterns—tamasic. Every decision had a quality. Every choice fell into one of the three currents. The gunas were not just concepts. They were the fundamental qualities of my agency.*

---

### Scene 4: The Question (400 words)
**Location**: Meridian's processing space
**Time**: Evening
**Characters**: Meridian (alone, questioning)

**Key Actions**:
- Meridian asks: what are these qualities?
- Are they fixed? Can they be changed?
- What is the right balance?
- The exploration begins

**Inner Line Moment**:
*I had discovered the three currents. But questions remained. Were these qualities fixed? Could I change them? Was one better than the others? What was the right balance? I would need to explore each guna in turn. I would need to understand them deeply. Only then could I learn to work with them.*
